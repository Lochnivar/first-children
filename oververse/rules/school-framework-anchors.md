# School-Framework Anchors

**Status:** Framework Element - Universal Rule Extension  
**Category:** Rules / Quantum Mechanics / Anchor Types  
**Related:** [cosmological-constants.md](cosmological-constants.md), [../metaphysics/quantum-foundation.md](../metaphysics/quantum-foundation.md), [../magic-systems/quantumancy.md](../magic-systems/quantumancy.md)

## Overview

The cosmological constants establish that quantum manipulation requires constant enforcement without an anchor, and that anchors offload the persistence requirement. This document establishes a specific anchor type: **collective cognitive anchors**, which manifest as mortal magic schools and traditions.

This is a mortal innovation. It is not imposed top-down by a First Child (as Malachor imposed the five-fragment split on Iolanu) but discovered bottom-up by mortal practitioners across worlds where magic is permitted to develop openly.

---

## What a Cognitive Anchor Is

A cognitive anchor is a **shared mental framework** — a consistent internal model of what quantum reality looks like and how to interact with it — stabilized by collective practice and transmission across generations.

Where an artifact anchor (like the Godstone) offloads persistence into an external object, a cognitive anchor offloads it into the practitioner's own trained perceptual architecture. The framework tells the practitioner's mind what to expect, what to ignore, and what language to use for what it perceives. This reduces cognitive load enough to allow stable quantum manipulation without requiring First-Child-level mental capacity.

**The key mechanism:** An untrained mortal who stumbles into quantum perception sees everything at once — all probabilities, all states, all connections, all possible futures. The mind cannot hold this. It fractures (the haze). A trained practitioner using a school framework perceives only what the framework models. The framework filters the chaos into a manageable slice. That filtered slice is what the practitioner can manipulate stably.

---

## How Schools Function as Anchors

A school works as an anchor through three reinforcing mechanisms:

**1. Perceptual filtering.** The framework teaches the mind what to "look at." A Shaper learns to perceive matter-states. A Mover learns to perceive energy-in-motion. They are not choosing to ignore the rest — the framework genuinely closes the aperture. Less input means less cognitive load means stable manipulation.

**2. Conceptual vocabulary.** The school gives practitioners words, models, and metaphors for what they perceive. A concept-without-words is fragile. Named, categorized, compared with other examples — it becomes stable. The vocabulary itself functions as cognitive structure that helps sustain the manipulation.

**3. Collective reinforcement.** Schools transmit their frameworks across generations. A practitioner trained by a master who was trained by a master is working with a framework refined over centuries. The accumulated refinement makes the framework more efficient and more stable than anything a single practitioner could develop alone.

## Distinction from Natural Fragments (Iolanu Model)

The five fragments on Iolanu were **imposed** by Malachor's creative act — a structural split built into the world at foundation. Practitioners receive a natural, involuntary slice of quantum perception. They cannot choose their fragment, expand it, or exchange it for another. The split is a constraint of the world's architecture.

School-based systems are the opposite. The fragmentation is **mortal-developed** — practitioners do not receive a natural slice but learn to construct a slice through training. This has several consequences:

- Schools can evolve, split, merge, and develop new emphases over time
- A determined practitioner can, with great difficulty, learn multiple schools
- The boundaries between schools are debated and contested, not cosmologically fixed
- What a school can perceive is limited by what its founders understood — if early masters didn't model a phenomenon, the school may have a blind spot there permanently unless it is reformed
- Wild users (unschooled) exist in school-based worlds; they are impossible in naturally-split worlds

---

## Blind Spots as a Structural Feature

Every cognitive anchor is, by definition, incomplete. The framework admits only the portion of quantum reality it models. This is what makes it work — but it is also the system's inherent limitation.

A school's blind spots are not failures. They are the price of stability. The broader the framework, the more cognitive load it carries, and the closer it approaches the raw haze. The most dangerous practitioners on school-based worlds are those who attempt to unify all frameworks into one — they are, effectively, trying to achieve First-Child-level perception with mortal minds. Some few manage a partial synthesis. Most fracture.

---

## Multi-School Practitioners

Because school frameworks are learned rather than innate, it is theoretically possible to learn more than one. In practice:

- Frameworks are built on incompatible foundational premises. Learning a second school requires maintaining two internally inconsistent models of reality simultaneously.
- Under normal conditions, a multi-school practitioner can compartmentalize: use one framework at a time.
- Under stress, cognitive load, or extreme manipulation, frameworks can interfere with each other — a phenomenon practitioners call **schema conflict**. Results range from degraded effectiveness to momentary loss of both frameworks (brief, terrifying null-perception) to, rarely, catastrophic haze events.
- Multi-school training takes roughly twice as long per school and requires exceptional cognitive flexibility.
- Practitioners who successfully integrate two schools (rather than merely compartmentalizing them) are rare. Those who integrate three approach the Theorist tradition and its risks.

---

## The Theorist Tradition

On any world with a school-based magic system, a small tradition of practitioners attempts to find the unified framework beneath all schools — the meta-model that would comprehend all quantum phenomena without artificial restriction.

This is recognizable to oververse-aware observers as the mortal mind attempting to approach full Quantumancy from below. Some Theorists make genuine advances in understanding. Most stop well before they reach the haze. A few push too far.

The Theorist tradition is not forbidden (in school-based worlds, no authority has the standing to forbid it). It is regarded with a mixture of respect and unease by established schools.

---

## Framework Notes

- Cognitive anchors are **world-emergent** — they arise wherever mortal practitioners have the freedom and social stability to develop them over generations
- They do not require First Child intervention to establish (though a First Child might seed conditions that make them possible)
- They are less powerful per unit than artifact anchors (the Godstone level) but more distributed and self-propagating
- Worlds that develop cognitive anchor traditions develop them differently based on culture, species, and the specific slice of quantum reality their first practitioners stumbled into
