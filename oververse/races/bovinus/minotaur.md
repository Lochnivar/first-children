# Minotaurs (Bos labyrinthus)

## Overview

Minotaurs (*Bos labyrinthus*) are bull-derived anthropomorphic people from the FC genetic repository. Self-designation: **Minotaurs** or "the Horned." Common names: **Bull-folk**, Path-keepers, Straight-Walkers, "the ones who don't get lost." No designated role; traits lead to emergent infrastructure work, navigation, and structural design. Passed trial by building order into chaos—they mapped the maze, they didn't get lost in it.

## Physical Characteristics

- **Height:** 7–9 feet; broad-shouldered, heavy build
- **Weight:** 400–700 lbs
- **Head:** Bull-derived—horns (curved or swept), broad muzzle, strong jaw
- **Build:** Powerful; built for sustained labor; not fast but relentless
- **Presence:** Imposing but not aggressive; "I know where I'm going"

## Core Trait: Path-Soul Orientation

Minotaurs perceive structure. Not in an obsessive way—it's simply how they think. A corridor has a beginning and an end. A problem has steps. Chaos is not wrong; it's exhausting. They don't dislike improvisation—they find it inefficient. Give them a clear route and they'll walk it. Give them a maze and they'll map it. Give them a room full of Gnolls and they'll find the door.

They are not rigid. They are *oriented*. Straight lines are efficient. Curves have purposes. Randomness has none. This is not philosophy; it's neurology.

## Natural Gravitation: Infrastructure, Navigation, Structure

**Why they become path-keepers:**
- Structural thinking → natural fit for corridors, boundaries, wayfinding
- Patience → can work long hours on repetitive tasks
- Never get lost → ideal scouts, guides, boundary surveyors
- Deliberate → good at planning; bad at improv

**Roles:** Corridor surveyors, boundary mappers, infrastructure maintenance, Gatelock logistics, "someone who knows where things are." On Earth trial: often contracted for corridor classification, wall-segment documentation, integration metric baseline surveys. The Wall Commission hires them when they need someone who won't get confused.

## Relationship with Gnolls

*"Straight lines are no fun." / "Circles are inefficient."*

Mutual incomprehension, not hostility. Gnolls find Minotaurs boring—why walk the same path twice when you could go sideways? Minotaurs find Gnolls exhausting—why make everything a bit when you could just *say* it? They occupy opposite ends of the chaos–structure spectrum. Gnoll warmth is genuine; Minotaur patience is genuine. They just don't speak each other's language. A Minotaur at a Festivus pole would stand there, confused. A Gnoll in a Minotaur survey team would make it a comedy routine. Both are correct about the other.

## Trial History & Deployment

- Passed trial by mapping, not wandering; built structure into entropy
- Deploy as independent contractors—infrastructure, navigation, boundary work
- Often found in corridor-heavy environments; less common in open chaos

## Story Role

The straight line in a crooked world. Contrast with Gnoll (chaos), Quedari (passive), Corvani (observational). Minotaurs: structural, deliberate, impossible to confuse. Useful when you need someone who knows where things are. Exhausting when you need someone to go with the flow.
