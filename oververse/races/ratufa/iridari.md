# Iridari (Pristails)

**Status:** Framework Element - Universal Race

## One Line Summary

The broadcast is never finished until someone responds.

---

## Overview

Iridari (*Ratufa iridescens*) are giant squirrel-derived anthropomorphic people seeded through FC panspermia. Their defining trait is relational rather than individual: **they need the loop to close.**

They broadcast — constantly, visibly, emotionally. Information, observation, feeling, presence. The tail shows what they feel before they decide whether to show it. The voice follows. The social circuit opens the moment they enter a room and it stays open until someone responds. Not applause exactly. Not approval exactly. *Response.* The loop completing. The acknowledgment that the signal was received.

When the loop closes they are briefly, genuinely complete. When it doesn't — when they broadcast into silence, when the tail shows and nobody reads it, when they speak and the room continues without registering them — they are incomplete in a way that is physical as much as emotional. The social circuit is open and humming and going nowhere.

This is not insecurity in the self-focused sense. It is codependence in the relational sense. The Iridari doesn't only need to feel good about themselves — they need the *other half of the exchange*. They are built for reciprocity and they feel its absence immediately and persistently.

The fear of being forgotten is the existential version of this. Not death — something lonelier. To be present and unresponded to. To broadcast into permanent silence. To have the loop open forever.

**Singular/Plural:** Iridari  
**Adjective:** Iridarine

### Truism
*"An Iridari speaks two languages — mouth and tail."*

## Author's Narrative

The tail is the honest part. Everything else the Iridari can manage — the voice, the performance, the careful calibration of what to share and when. The tail just shows. Emotional state, unfiltered, visible to anyone who knows how to read it, impossible to fully suppress. The Iridari learns early that their inner life is not entirely private. Most of them make peace with this. Some never do.

What the tail reveals is not weakness. It is the Iridari social architecture made visible — a species for whom emotional transparency is not a vulnerability but a communication channel. The loop requires both parties to be legible. The Iridari is always legible. What they want, urgently and continuously, is for you to be legible back.

The broadcasting tradition emerged from this the way all Iridari behavior emerges from it — the loop needs to close, and the larger the audience the more closings are possible. The Iridari on a high place with a clear sightline isn't performing for ego. They're opening as many loops as the position allows. Some will close. That's enough. That's the work.

The fear of being forgotten is not vanity. The Iridari understands mortality with unusual clarity — when someone dies the loop closes permanently. What remains is the story, and the story is the last loop that can still close. When another Iridari tells the story of the dead, the dead's loop closes one more time. This is why they tell the stories. This is why they remember. The dead deserve to have their loop close.

A crew with an Iridari in it has a member who will notice every emotional shift in every other member before those members notice it themselves. The Sungrun reads the physical environment. The Iridari reads the social environment. Both are running continuously, both are involuntary, and both produce a species that knows things they didn't ask to know and now has to decide what to do with the information.

The difference is the Sungrun can sit with what they sense. The Iridari needs to say something about it. The loop is open. It needs to close.

## Universal Characteristics

### Physical Attributes
- **Height/Size:** 4'6"–5'4" — lean, light, built for height and speed
- **Build:** Agile and precise; comfortable bipedal or quadrupedal; exceptional spatial awareness; silent when they choose to be, which is less often than other species would prefer
- **Lifespan:** 200–300 years — long enough to accumulate deep social knowledge of a community; long enough to outlive many of the people whose loops they're still trying to close
- **Distinctive:** Large expressive golden or amber eyes; prominent front teeth; soft dense fur in muted base colors (gray-brown, tan, warm buff) with natural iridescence that catches light; and the tail
- **The tail:** Three to four feet, longer than the body, extraordinarily fluffy. Bioluminescent and iridescent cells respond to emotional state automatically — the tail shows what the Iridari feels before the Iridari decides to show it. Cannot be fully suppressed. Wrapping reduces movement but not color. Visible from significant distance. The Iridari's most honest feature and the one they have the least control over.

### The Tail — Emotional Display

The tail is involuntary at the color level. The Iridari can learn to manage their reactions — to slow the shift, to dampen the intensity — but cannot prevent the display entirely. Other species who spend time with Iridari learn to read it. Some read it better than the Iridari would prefer.

**Standard displays:**
- **Calm:** Soft green-blue, slow movement
- **Friendly/warm:** Warm yellow-gold, gentle curl
- **Curious:** Purple-pink flashes, tip movement
- **Amused:** Orange-red pulses, quickening
- **Nervous:** Rapid gray-white flickering
- **Angry:** Sharp crimson, stiff and raised
- **Sad:** Slow deep indigo, low carry
- **Excited:** Full rainbow shimmer, rapid movement
- **Deceptive:** Mismatched — the stated emotion and the tail color don't align; other Iridari spot this immediately; non-Iridari learn to spot it eventually

**At death:** The tail goes dark and does not recover. This is considered the most visible and honest marker of loss in Iridari culture.

### Cognitive & Behavioral Traits

**The open loop.** The Iridari social circuit opens the moment they enter a context and remains open until someone responds. This is not conscious. It is the background state of a species built for reciprocal exchange. The loop produces broadcast behavior — speaking, showing, sharing — because broadcast is the mechanism for closing it. An Iridari in a room full of people who are not responding is in a specific kind of distress that is difficult to explain to species who don't experience it.

**Codependent social architecture.** Not insecurity — codependence. The distinction matters. Insecurity is about the self. Codependence is about the relationship. The Iridari doesn't only need to feel good about themselves; they need the other half of the exchange. A compliment doesn't close the loop. A response does. Acknowledgment. The visible registration that the signal arrived.

**Observational precision.** They notice everything about social environments — who is watching whom, who has shifted position, who has gone quiet, what the emotional temperature of the room is doing. This is partly the tail-reading instinct applied outward: they're reading everyone else's legible signals the way they expect their own to be read. The social environment is as vivid to the Iridari as the physical environment is to the Sungrun.

**Compulsive information sharing.** Information held is a loop waiting to close. The Iridari who knows something relevant and hasn't shared it yet is sitting with an open circuit. The sharing isn't strategic — it's relief. Not all Iridari share indiscriminately; the socially sophisticated ones have learned to manage the compulsion. The young ones share everything immediately and usually faster than is strictly wise.

**Height preference.** Instinctive and persistent. The ground is danger — prey-animal heritage that never fully rationalized away. High places are safety, visibility, and broadcast position simultaneously. An Iridari on a rooftop or high ledge is not performing. They are home.

**Fear of being forgotten.** The existential expression of the open loop. To be forgotten is to have the loop open permanently with no possibility of closing. Worse than death — death closes the loop finally. Being forgotten while living, or being forgotten after death without stories to carry the loop forward — this is the Iridari's deepest fear and the engine of the storytelling tradition.

## Social Characteristics

### Community

Iridari communities are dense, loud, and extraordinarily well-informed about themselves. The combination of tail-reading, compulsive information sharing, and observational precision means that almost nothing stays private for long in an Iridari community. This is not considered a problem by most Iridari — privacy requires withholding, and withholding keeps loops open, and open loops are uncomfortable. The community that knows everything about itself is, from the Iridari perspective, a community that is continuously closing loops. This is health.

The territorial instinct around high places is real and occasionally contentious. The best broadcast positions are finite. Iridari communities negotiate these with the specific intensity of people for whom the position is not just convenient but psychologically necessary.

### The Broadcasting Tradition

The Iridari broadcast from height because height is both instinctively safe and practically optimal for reach. What they broadcast varies — news, observation, stories, emotional weather reports for the community, the ongoing social narrative of who is doing what and why and how everyone feels about it. The content is almost secondary to the function: loops closing at scale.

The broadcaster Iridari is not performing vanity. They are doing the most efficient version of what all Iridari do — opening as many loops as possible in a single position, collecting as many closings as the audience provides. The community that has a good broadcaster has a social infrastructure. Information moves. Emotional states are legible. The dead are remembered. The loop keeps closing.

### On the Dead

When an Iridari dies the tail goes dark. The community registers this as a physical fact before any announcement is made — the dark tail is unmistakable and immediate.

What follows is the storytelling. The dead Iridari's loops cannot close anymore — except through the stories other Iridari tell about them. Every telling is a closing. Every time someone laughs at a story about the dead, every time someone says *I remember when they—* and finishes the sentence, the loop closes one more time. The dead are kept alive in the only way available: by being the subject of ongoing exchange.

*"We tell their stories. That's how they live on."*

This is not sentiment. It is the Iridari's most honest theological position, stated plainly.

### The Personality Range

The codependent architecture expresses differently across individuals:

**Broadcasters** — the full expression; theatrical, highly visible, need large audiences; the tail is always showing
**Observers** — quieter expression; watch more than they speak; close loops through careful one-on-one exchange rather than broadcast; still need the response, just from fewer sources
**Socialites** — balanced; comfortable in groups and one-on-one; the most socially functional expression
**Hustlers** — transactional; have learned to use the information-sharing compulsion strategically; information brokerage as productive channel for the open loop
**Hermits** — rare and conflicted; withdrawn by circumstance or choice; the open loop doesn't close and they've learned to live with the discomfort; not happy about it

## Origin

FC panspermia-seeded from giant squirrel genetic template. No patron FC. No designated purpose. The codependent social architecture and the tail emerged from a species that lived in visible exposed positions in complex social groups — where everyone knowing the emotional state of the community was survival information, not vulnerability.

No FC designed a broadcasting species. They seeded a giant squirrel template. The tail and the loop followed from what giant squirrels already were.

---

## Natural Gravitation

Codependent social architecture + involuntary emotional display + observational precision + compulsive information sharing + height preference + storytelling tradition →

**Broadcasting and information distribution** — the most efficient expression of the open loop. Reach the largest audience from the highest position. Maximum loop-closing per unit of effort.

**Community social infrastructure** — the emotional weather reporter, the one who knows what everyone in the community is feeling before they've said it, the one who holds the social narrative together. Not appointed. Just present and paying attention.

**Storytelling and memory of the dead** — keeping the loops of the dead closing through narrative. The tradition that emerged from the fear of being forgotten applied outward to everyone else.

**Information brokerage** — the Hustler expression; the compulsion to share information channeled into something transactional. Not Kitsune sophistication — more immediate, more driven by the need to close the loop than by strategic positioning.

**Scout and advance social assessment** — they read social environments the way the Sungrun reads physical ones. Before a negotiation, before a meeting, before entering a hostile territory — the Iridari has already mapped who feels what about whom.

## Relationships

**Corvani:** Complementary temporal orientations with real friction in sustained contact. The Corvani carries the dead; the Iridari tells their stories. Both are doing the work of keeping the lost from being entirely gone, through completely different mechanisms. The Corvani finds the Iridari's constant need for response exhausting. The Iridari finds the Corvani's self-contained weight heavy to be around. When they manage the friction the collaboration is genuinely valuable — the Corvani holds who the dead were; the Iridari closes the loop.

**Kitsune:** The Kitsune reads the Iridari tail with unusual accuracy — they are motivated to understand emotional states and the tail provides unusually legible data. The Iridari finds the Kitsune's relational intelligence satisfying in a specific way: the Kitsune is always reading them, which closes loops continuously. The Iridari may not notice or may not care that the Kitsune is reading them for leverage rather than connection. The Kitsune finds this dynamic useful and faintly uncomfortable.

**Sungrun:** Natural partnership in environments where both social and physical awareness matter. The Sungrun reads the room physically — pressure, sound, smell, what bodies are doing. The Iridari reads the room socially — who feels what, what the emotional temperature is doing, what's being communicated through behavior rather than words. Together they cover the full environmental read. The Sungrun appreciates having someone handle the social layer. The Iridari appreciates someone who notices things and reports them — the Sungrun sharing a sensory observation closes a loop.

**Djemari:** Difficult. The Djemari's flat affect and minimal response is an open loop that never closes. The Iridari broadcasts; the Djemari notes it and continues. From the Djemari's perspective this is appropriate economy. From the Iridari's perspective it is a specific kind of social suffering. Both are professionally functional together. Neither finds it comfortable. The Djemari finds the Iridari's tail a useful environmental data point and says nothing about this, which the Iridari finds maddening.

**Bruin:** The Bruin's consistent principle and clear communication closes loops reliably — what the Bruin says is what the Bruin means, and the response is predictable. The Iridari finds this deeply restful. The Bruin finds the Iridari's social awareness useful in community contexts — the Iridari knows what the community is feeling before the Bruin needs to respond to it. Good working relationship.

**Gnoll:** Natural affinity. The Gnoll performs; the Iridari watches the performance and responds with genuine delight when it lands. The Gnoll gets a genuinely responsive audience; the Iridari gets a performer who closes loops with every successful bit. The tail during a good Gnoll performance is full rainbow shimmer. The Gnoll considers this the best review available. Good energy between them.

**Chelonari:** The Chelonari's patient, attentive presence closes loops steadily and without drama. The Chelonari listens; the Iridari is heard. The Chelonari finds the Iridari's social sensitivity useful context for the help they provide — the Iridari has already mapped what the community is struggling with before the Chelonari asks. The Chelonari is one of the few species that understands the fear of being forgotten without needing it explained.

**Bandari:** The Bandari's crew loyalty means that once an Iridari is crew the loop closes reliably — the Bandari sees them, responds to them, includes them. The Iridari finds the Bandari's direct warmth toward crew members deeply satisfying. The Bandari finds the Iridari's social awareness invaluable on jobs where reading people quickly matters. Good crew pairing. The Iridari learns not to broadcast things the Bandari would prefer stayed quiet. The Bandari learns to respond more than they naturally would. Both adapt.

## Native World & History

### Origin
FC panspermia-seeded from giant squirrel genetic template. Canopy-origin worlds — high biodiversity, complex social groups, exposed positions requiring constant social coordination. The tail developed as a social coordination mechanism before sapience; the sapient expression deepened the mechanism without changing its function.

### Trial History
Passed via vertical refuge and social cooperation. Stayed in the canopy, avoided ground engagement, coordinated through the tail-reading that made their social responses faster than the trial expected. FC assessment noted the information-sharing capability and the social cohesion. The validation-seeking was noted as a feature rather than a flaw — a species that needs response from its community stays together under pressure.

### Governance & Politics
Community-based, socially negotiated. The Iridari who knows what everyone is feeling has significant informal influence — not through authority but through social awareness. Formal governance varies by world; informal governance is always the Iridari who holds the social narrative and distributes it accurately.

### Legal Code
Social consequence rather than formal law. Iridari communities enforce norms through the broadcasting tradition — behavior that violates community standards becomes the subject of stories, and stories close loops in ways the subject cannot control. This is highly effective and occasionally brutal. The Iridari who becomes the subject of unflattering community narrative has a specific problem that no external legal system addresses.

### Theology
The storytelling tradition is the theology. The dead live through stories. The loop keeps closing as long as someone is telling. The theological question is not what happens after death but whether you will be worth telling stories about — whether the loops you opened in life will keep closing after you're gone.

The FC origin question gets the same treatment it gets from most panspermia species — acknowledged, set aside. Something seeded them. The tail was already there. The loop was already looking to close. What the FC intended is less interesting than what the Iridari made of it.

*"Be seen. Be remembered. Close the loop."*

## Story Role

- The crew member who knows what everyone is feeling before they've said anything — and can't always keep that to themselves
- The tail as involuntary honesty — the emotional state that shows before the decision is made
- The broadcaster who needs the audience not for vanity but because the loop has to close
- The fear of being forgotten made narrative — the story told at a funeral that closes the dead's loop one more time
- The Iridari in a room full of people who aren't responding — specific, recognizable distress
- The codependence dynamic in a mixed crew — what happens when the loop doesn't close and keeps not closing
- The Iridari Hustler who has learned to use the compulsion strategically — and what that costs them
- The dark tail — the most honest death marker in the multiverse
- Contrast with Corvani: both keep the dead, different mechanisms, different weights

## Variations Across Worlds

The tail and the codependent social architecture are biological and consistent. Specific tail color meanings are culturally consistent with minor regional variation — the core palette is universal, edge cases differ. Broadcasting tradition takes different forms depending on available infrastructure — high places, open channels, whatever the world provides for visibility and reach. The fear of being forgotten is consistent. The storytelling tradition for the dead appears on every world in some form.

## Naming Conventions

*Approved by author.*

### Philosophy

Iridari names are Italian-influenced — warm, open-vowel, melodic. Designed to be said aloud, to carry across distance, to sound good when someone calls them from a rooftop. Names that want to be spoken. Deliberately distinct from Geliss naming, which draws from the same cultural well but owns the sibilant register — Iridari names lean toward rounder, warmer sounds. More *l, r, v, m*; less hiss.

Family names carry community or roost lineage — which canopy, which high place, which broadcasting tradition they come from.

## Example Characters & Dialogue

### Mirella Highwatch
- **Age:** 147
- **Role:** Community broadcaster; has held the high position at Highwatch for sixty years; knows everything about everyone in a three-community radius and has opinions about all of it
- **Description:** Warm buff fur, gold iridescence. Tail in near-constant motion. Speaks quickly and accurately. The community comes to her before they come to anyone else because she already has the information and because telling her closes the loop immediately — she responds, always, to everything.
- **Sample Dialogue:**

  *New arrival: "How do you know all this? About everyone?"*  
  *Mirella: "I watch. I listen. People tell me things."*  
  *New arrival: "Why do they tell you things?"*  
  *Mirella: [tail warm gold] "Because I respond. That's all most people need. Someone to respond."*

  ---

  *At a funeral, telling stories:*  
  *Mirella: "He used to climb the east tower every morning before anyone else was up. Said the light was different before the community woke up. Said it was the only quiet that didn't feel like being forgotten."*  
  *[tail slow deep indigo]*  
  *Mirella: "We remember. The light is still different up there. He's not forgotten."*

### Tavio Brightperch
- **Age:** 34
- **Role:** Young Hustler; has discovered that the compulsion to share information can be monetized; is currently deciding whether that makes him practical or something else
- **Description:** Gray-brown fur, quick purple-pink flashes when curious — which is always. Moves fast. Talks faster. The tail betrays him constantly and he knows it and hasn't figured out whether to care.
- **Sample Dialogue:**

  *Client: "How do I know you won't sell this to someone else?"*  
  *Tavio: "You don't."*  
  *[tail flickers orange-red — amused]*  
  *Tavio: "But I haven't yet. And you're paying me not to. That's the arrangement."*  
  *Client: "Your tail just went—"*  
  *Tavio: "Ignore the tail."*  
  *Client: "It went orange."*  
  *Tavio: "I said ignore it."*
