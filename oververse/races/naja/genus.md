## Definition

Genus Naja is defined by the cobra-derived genetic template from the FC genetic repository. The non-sapient baseline is the cobra — a sit-and-wait predator defined by absolute stillness, environmental sensitivity, and the capacity for sudden precise action after extended patience.

### Physical
- **Upright bipedal** — arms and legs; not a serpentine crawler; the body plan is humanoid in structure
- **Scaled throughout** — fine scales on face and hands, larger across back and arms; coloration typically deep jewel tones (blues, greens, blacks) with individual pattern variation
- **Hood** — folds flat in neutral state; deploys under threat or extreme emotional condition; inner hood pattern is individual and considered deeply personal
- **Eyes** — fixed pupils, lidless; the permanent wide-open gaze never stops registering as attentive
- **Tongue** — forked; used in environmental sensing; active in observation mode, socially suppressed in most contexts

### Temperament
- **Sit-and-wait patience** — elevated from predator reflex to methodology; stillness is the default, motion is considered
- **Environmental attunement** — continuous low-level sensing of the immediate environment; difficult to surprise; difficult to deceive about physical states

### Warm-Blooded
Naja sapiens are warm-blooded — a departure from the non-sapient cobra baseline. Cold-blooded dependency on ambient temperature is incompatible with the consistent, environment-independent function that defines the sapient expression.

### Framework
- **Non-sapient baseline:** Cobras — Indian cobra (*Naja naja*), king cobra (*Ophiophagus rex*) and related species (Earth template)
- **Sapient expression:** [Maturi](maturinensis.md) (*Naja maturinensis*) — traits tend toward naturalist observation, physical healing, analytical patience, cross-context linguistic facility
