## One Line Summary

They noticed before you knew there was anything to notice.

---

## Overview

Sungrun (*Lepus acutis*) are rabbit-derived anthropomorphic people seeded through FC panspermia. Their defining trait is simple and consistent across every world and context they inhabit: **they sense almost everything.**

Not magic. Not telepathy. Biology — extraordinary hearing, smell, vibration sensitivity, and pressure awareness, all running simultaneously, all synthesized into an environmental read most species couldn't produce with instruments. The Sungrun standing in a room isn't passively present. They are actively receiving an enormous amount of information about that room, the people in it, the building around it, and what's happening outside. They've been doing this since they arrived. They already have opinions.

This doesn't make them any one thing. The sensory capability is a platform, not a vocation. A Sungrun scout is extraordinary at it. A Sungrun courier is extraordinary at it. A Sungrun physician, investigator, navigator, sentinel — extraordinary. The context determines the job. The senses come with the species.

The other thing that comes with the species is the music.

**Singular/Plural:** Sungrun  
**Adjective:** Sungrunne

### Truism
*"You didn't sneak up on a Sungrun."*

## Author's Narrative

The prey-animal heritage is worth taking seriously — not as weakness but as design. The Sungrun didn't survive by being stronger than what hunted them. They survived by never being caught by surprise, which required sensory architecture sophisticated enough to read an environment before the threat in it declared itself.

Apply that at sapient scale and you get a species that is simply *more aware* of the room they're in than anyone else in it. Not smarter. Not more strategic. Just receiving more signal. The Sungrun who walks into a tense negotiation already knows someone in the room is frightened, someone is angry, someone had something bad for lunch, and the ventilation system is struggling. They didn't investigate this. They just noticed.

The "already knew" quality is real and occasionally socially complicated. The Sungrun who waits politely for you to share information they already have is doing you a courtesy. The Sungrun who can't quite hide that they knew before you told them is being honest. Both are doing their best with a situation they didn't choose.

What they *did* choose — or what emerged from the same biological heritage — is the music. The thump is the hind-leg communication their non-sapient cousins use to signal danger. The Sungrun took that instinct and turned it into art. Folk percussion. Ground-rhythm singing. The thump as bass note beneath everything cultural. Not a signal system. Not information. Just: *this is what we are, this is how we celebrate it, and if you feel the beat in your chest that's the point.*

The thump says: we are here. We know you're there. We are not afraid. Come listen.

## Universal Characteristics

### Physical Attributes
- **Height/Size:** 5'4"–6'2" — lean and longer than the non-sapient baseline suggests; most of the height is in the legs
- **Build:** Lean muscle built for speed and endurance rather than power; powerful haunches; light but quick hands; surprising reach
- **Lifespan:** 180–250 years — long enough to accumulate deep environmental knowledge of a region
- **Distinctive:** Large mobile ears that swivel independently and communicate significant emotional information; wide-angle eyes with excellent motion detection and low-light capability; whiskers providing tactile environmental sensing; strong digitigrade legs; short dense fur in region-variable coloring; a small tail that is more expressive than it looks

### Sensory Attributes

This section exists because the sensory suite is the species. It deserves specificity.

**Hearing:** The ears swivel independently and triangulate sound sources with high precision. The Sungrun can isolate individual conversations across a crowded room, detect footsteps through walls, hear structural stress in a building, and track the location of multiple moving objects simultaneously. The range extends into frequencies most species don't register.

**Smell:** Highly developed chemical sensing. The Sungrun reads emotional states — fear, anger, arousal, illness — through scent involuntarily. They can't turn it off, only learn to not visibly react to what they're receiving. They can read the recent occupancy history of a space and detect chemical traces of substances, events, and people.

**Vibration:** Sensed through feet, hands, and whiskers. Ground vibrations indicate footstep patterns, vehicle movement, structural integrity, and at close range the heartbeat of something nearby. In contact with a ship's hull or a building's floor, the Sungrun is receiving a significant amount of structural data.

**Pressure/Weather:** Barometric sensitivity that registers atmospheric changes hours before other species notice anything. The Sungrun who says "weather's changing" is not guessing.

**Vision:** Wide-angle and motion-optimized. Excellent peripheral awareness and outstanding low-light performance. Not specialized for fine detail or color richness at range — they see *more* of the environment but not necessarily with greater precision at the center. Most visually acute at dawn and dusk.

### Cognitive & Behavioral Traits

**Continuous environmental synthesis.** The senses aren't discrete channels the Sungrun switches between. They run simultaneously and the synthesis is automatic. The Sungrun isn't consciously analyzing the room — they're receiving it. The processing runs below conscious thought and surfaces as environmental awareness. They often can't fully explain why they know something. They just do.

**Reflexive information sharing.** Culturally and instinctively, the Sungrun shares what they sense. Sitting on sensory data — particularly threat-relevant data — is uncomfortable at a deep level. This isn't compulsive disclosure. It's the species-level habit of a social architecture where shared awareness was survival. The Sungrun who has information and no one to tell yet is noticeably restless.

**Calibrated sensory management.** High-stimulation environments require active management. The Sungrun develops habits of sensory triage — what to consciously process, what to hold in background, what to actively filter. This is a skill that takes time to develop and that the Sungrun manages as a normal part of adult life. Environments that overwhelm the triage capacity are genuinely unpleasant rather than merely distracting.

**Crepuscular acuity pattern.** The Sungrun's senses sharpen measurably at dawn and dusk. Not that they're impaired at other times — just that the liminal hours produce peak performance. Many Sungrun schedule demanding work accordingly.

**The "already knew" quality.** The Sungrun often knows something before it's been formally shared — because the senses told them before the information arrived. The diplomatic management of this is an adult skill. The young Sungrun who can't hide they already knew is charming. The adult Sungrun who chooses when to reveal prior knowledge and when to let you feel like you brought them news is navigating a social reality their species has dealt with for a long time.

## Social Characteristics

### Community

Sungrun communities are warmer and more physically dense than the sensory profile might suggest. The prey-animal heritage is a *social* heritage — warren life is communal life. The Sungrun who lives alone is the exception. The default is a household of several, frequent contact with an extended community, and genuine discomfort with prolonged isolation.

This creates an interesting tension. The Sungrun genuinely likes being around people. Being around *many* people in *dense* environments requires the sensory management skills described above. Most Sungrun communities have developed physical and cultural architectures that balance the social need against the sensory cost — good acoustics, scent-aware spaces, clear sightlines. A Sungrun-designed building is noticeably different from a generic structure in ways that are hard to articulate but immediately felt.

### The Music

The thump is where non-sapient instinct became culture.

The hind-leg thump of the non-sapient rabbit is a warning signal — something is wrong, pay attention. The Sungrun took this and turned it entirely around. The thump is now percussion. It's rhythm. It's the bass line of their folk music tradition and it comes from the body itself — hind foot, heel, hands against a resonant surface. You don't need an instrument to participate in Sungrun music. You already have one.

The tradition is folk rather than formal. No conservatory culture, no complex notation, no priesthood of technique. Songs travel between communities through oral transmission. Everyone knows the standard songs. Everyone adds to them. The structure is simple enough to join mid-song and rich enough to sustain a lifetime of playing. Visitors who have never heard it before find themselves following the rhythm within a few measures. This is not accidental.

Thumping is celebration. Thumping is work accompaniment. Thumping is grief, is welcome, is farewell. The Sungrun sitting alone somewhere tapping a rhythm on their knee is not signaling anything. They're thinking with their body, the way their species does.

The leap from rhythm to voice is natural and usual. Sungrun singing over a thumped rhythm is the complete form — the beat from the body, the melody above it. The vocal range is wide. The preference is for harmonics over solo performance; the ideal is many voices over one excellent voice.

Other species find Sungrun music immediately accessible in a way that formal musical traditions from other cultures often aren't. The rhythm gets in before the unfamiliarity can stop it. This is considered a feature.

### The Sensory Social Contract

The Sungrun knows things about you they didn't ask to know. Your emotional state, your health, your stress levels. This is not something they can turn off. The social contract that emerged from this is worth understanding.

The Sungrun does not treat involuntary sensory information as actionable without permission. Knowing you're frightened doesn't mean they'll comment on it. Detecting illness doesn't mean they'll announce it in company. The information is received; what they *do* with it is a choice governed by relationship, context, and the Sungrunne understanding of what the information is *for*.

Close friends learn that the Sungrun already knows. This is often a relief. The Sungrun who says "I noticed you weren't yourself earlier" isn't prying — they're telling you the awareness was there and they respected your choice not to raise it.

Strangers and new contacts get the professional courtesy of not being visibly read. The first tell that a Sungrun trusts you is usually that they stop pretending not to have noticed things.

## Origin

FC panspermia-seeded from rabbit genetic template. No patron FC. No designated purpose. The sensory capability is not a design choice — it's what the biological substrate already was, scaled to sapience.

The non-sapient baseline was already extraordinary: wide-angle vision, sensitive hearing, smell-based social communication, vibration sensitivity, barometric awareness. The sapient Sungrun inherited all of it and added the cognitive architecture to synthesize and use it deliberately.

No FC designed a "sensor species." They seeded a rabbit template. The rest followed from what rabbits already were.

## Natural Gravitation

Extraordinary sensory suite + reflexive information sharing + calibrated environmental awareness + speed + crepuscular optimization →

The sensory capability is a **platform**. The Sungrun gravitates toward whatever work needs that platform. Context determines the role.

**Work where the senses are directly the asset:**
- Scout and advance assessment — they read the environment before the group enters it
- Sentinel and surveillance — continuous ambient awareness of a space; they notice before instruments do
- Investigation — they detect things; they can't always explain how; they're usually right
- Navigation — environmental reading includes weather, terrain, structural integrity, ambient signals
- Medical and diagnostic — chemical and sensory detection of physiological states
- Courier and messenger work — yes; speed plus awareness is a natural fit, though it's one fit among many

**Work where the senses are supporting capability:**
Effectively anything that benefits from unusually good situational awareness, which is most things. The Sungrun who became a merchant still reads the room better than anyone else at the table. The Sungrun who became a diplomat is still the first to know when the negotiation shifted. The work varies. The capability is constant.

## Relationships

**Bruin:** Mutual utility, different orientations. The Bruin is law at the frontier. The Sungrun is awareness at the frontier. The Bruin learns quickly to pay attention when the Sungrun gets edgy about something they can't yet articulate. The Sungrun appreciates the Bruin's consistency — a known quantity is easy to sense-manage. Good working combination in territories where both operate.

**Kitsune:** Complementary and occasionally friction-producing. The Kitsune gathers information strategically and shares it selectively. The Sungrun gathers information reflexively and shares it instinctively. The Kitsune finds the Sungrun useful (an allied Sungrun is an extremely hard-to-surprise partner) and occasionally alarming (the Sungrun knows things the Kitsune didn't plan to have known). The Sungrun finds the Kitsune's information hoarding instinctively strange — you have data that's relevant to the situation and you're *sitting on it*? The tension is mutual and usually professionally managed.

**Geliss:** Deep mutual respect. The Geliss achieves inside understanding of environments through immersion and time. The Sungrun achieves immediate environmental read through sensory synthesis. Both produce unusual situational awareness through completely different mechanisms. The Geliss's immersion knowledge is richer and more culturally nuanced. The Sungrun's sensory read is faster and physically grounded. They complement each other naturally — the Sungrun notices; the Geliss understands what the noticing means.

**Iridari:** Natural partnership. The Iridari broadcasts; the Sungrun detects. Information flows inward through the Sungrun and outward through the Iridari. The Iridari's emotional tail expressiveness is readable to the Sungrun at a level that occasionally makes the Iridari self-conscious. The Sungrun's sensory reads give the Iridari content worth broadcasting.

**Kibo:** Loud. This is the primary Sungrun experience of the Kibo. The Kibo's enthusiasm and full-commitment energy is sensory-intense. The Sungrun manages it because the Kibo is also exactly what they look like, which is restful in its own way — no hidden layers to sense through. The Kibo benefits enormously from having a Sungrun nearby who will notice the thing the Kibo didn't and tell them before it becomes a problem. Good partnership. Different volume settings.

**Corvani:** The Corvani patterns the past. The Sungrun reads the present. Complementary temporal orientations — between them, a situation can be understood through what it was and what it is right now. The Corvani's hyper-literal death-framed consistency is readable to the Sungrun as stable and trustworthy. The Sungrun's sensory reads occasionally hand the Corvani information that fits their pattern-recognition in ways that produce unsettling accuracy. Both find this useful.

**Djemari:** The Djemari's phlegmatic unbotheredness is sensory-restful in a way few other species manage. The Sungrun in a mixed crew often gravitates toward the Djemari in high-stimulation moments — not for comfort exactly, but because the Djemari is a stable sensory anchor. The Djemari neither notices this dynamic nor finds it worth commenting on. Noted.

**Gnoll:** The Gnoll is loud, theatrical, and completely legible to Sungrun senses in a way the Gnoll is faintly aware of and finds amusing. The Sungrun can't be deceived by the Gnoll's performance because they're reading underneath it — and the Gnoll knows this and thinks it's funny. The Gnoll in turn has an accurate read on when the Sungrun's sensory management is struggling, and is one of the few species who will quietly reduce their own output without being asked. Surprisingly functional pairing.

**Bandari:** The Bandari navigates by reading exits and loose panels. The Sungrun navigates by reading everything. Complementary in the field — the Bandari's pragmatic situational assessment and the Sungrun's sensory environmental read produce combined awareness that's hard to catch off-guard. The Bandari appreciates a partner who knows things without needing to explain how they know. The Sungrun appreciates a partner who doesn't make their sensory knowing weird.

## Governance & Politics

No formal universal structure. Sungrun communities organize around the warren-inheritance model in various expressions — extended household, nested community, fluid affiliation. The structure values consensus and shared information over hierarchy. Leadership tends toward competence and demonstrated reliability rather than formal authority — the Sungrun who is listened to is usually the one whose environmental reads have proven accurate over time.

External governance is engaged with pragmatically. The Sungrun follows the law of whatever jurisdiction they're in without deep ideological investment in any particular system.

---

## Legal Code

Pragmatic and community-based. Disputes are mediated through shared understanding rather than formal procedure. The Sungrun's sensory capability makes certain kinds of deception extremely difficult within Sungrun communities — not impossible, but the cost of maintaining a lie in close proximity to people who can smell your stress is high. This shapes a legal culture where honesty is both ethical expectation and practical default.

## Theology

The sensory tradition runs through Sungrun spiritual practice like the thump runs through their music — present in everything, not always named.

The liminal hours are sacred. Dawn and dusk — when the senses are sharpest and the world is between states — are when Sungrun religious practice concentrates. Not formal service so much as deliberate presence: going somewhere open, opening the senses fully, receiving what the world is doing without agenda. Awareness as practice rather than prayer.

The music is the other axis. The communal thump-and-voice tradition is where collective spirituality lives — not in doctrine or institution but in the shared physical experience of rhythm, the sensation of feeling others around you through the beat, the voice rising to find the harmony. The Sungrun congregation is a circle thumping. The Sungrun sermon is a song.

The FC origin question is acknowledged and set aside with characteristic pragmatism. Something seeded them. The senses are theirs now. What you receive, you're responsible for. Keep receiving.

*"Open your ears. Open your nose. Open your skin to the world. It's still talking. We're still listening."*

## Variations Across Worlds

The sensory capability is biological and consistent. The musical tradition travels with every Sungrun community — the thump is present wherever they are, though specific folk song repertoire varies by world and community. The physical palette (fur coloring, ear shape, size variation) varies by environment in ways that map to non-sapient rabbit baseline diversity. The warren community model appears in most worlds in some form.

## Naming Conventions

### Philosophy

Sungrun names are short, rhythmic, and comfortable in the mouth — designed to work in a work song as easily as in a formal introduction. The naming tradition reflects the musical culture: names that have a beat to them, that fit naturally into rhythm, that don't slow a sentence down.

Most given names are two syllables. Three is formal or ceremonial. One syllable is usually a nickname that stuck and became the working name.

A community name identifies the warren or settlement of origin. Formal address is given name plus community name. Casual address is given name alone, and usually arrives quickly after first meeting.

Gender sits loosely in Sungrun naming — many names lean but none are exclusive. The community name is shared across gender lines.

### Community Names

The community name identifies the warren or settlement of origin. Compound constructions, typically describing geography or a founding characteristic.

**Examples:**
Ironfen, Thornmere, Redhollow, Dustwatch, Greywood, Saltmarch, Bramblecroft, Ashfen, Coldrun, Riverwatch, Stonehollow, Darkfen, Highburrow, Moorend, Dawnwatch, Embervale, Quiethollow, Farrun, Whisperfen, Oldmarch

---

### Full Name Construction

**Formal:** Given Name + Community Name  
*Mira Dawnwatch. Bran Thornmere. Tamsin Coldrun.*

**Casual:** Given name only, usually from first contact onward.  
*"Ask Mira — she was there when it happened."*

**Nickname precedent:** One-syllable shortenings are common and persist.  
*Brenna → Bren. Tamsin → Tam. Corva → Corv. Finnegan → Finn.*

### Example Characters & Dialogue

**Mira Dawnwatch**
- **Age:** 82
- **Role:** Ship's navigator, currently; previously sentinel work on two frontier stations
- **Description:** Lean, amber-furred, ears that never quite stop moving even when she looks relaxed. Speaks less than she knows. The crew has learned that "something feels off" from Mira is worth taking seriously before she can explain why.
- **Sample Dialogue:** "We shouldn't dock here tonight." / *"Why not?"* / "The berthing master was frightened when he confirmed our slip. Whatever he's afraid of, it's inside the station."

**Bran Thornmere**
- **Age:** 194
- **Role:** Investigator-for-hire, primarily cold cases and missing persons
- **Description:** Old enough to have accumulated enormous sensory pattern library; still and deliberate in a way that reads as patience rather than age. Rarely startled. Has learned to let people arrive at conclusions he reached ten minutes ago.
- **Sample Dialogue:** "I already know what you're going to ask me. Tell me anyway. Sometimes what people choose to say is more useful than what they know."

**Finn**
- **Age:** 45
- **Role:** Courier; good at it; doesn't stay anywhere long
- **Description:** One name; the community name was left behind intentionally; not discussed. Fast. Cheerful on the surface. The ears don't stop moving.
- **Sample Dialogue:** "Package is in, package is out, I'm gone before noon. No, I didn't see anything. Yes, I'll remember it if it comes up."

**Tamsin Coldrun**
- **Age:** 31
- **Role:** Apprentice medic; the diagnostic instinct led her here
- **Description:** Young enough that sensory management is still a visible effort in dense environments. The one who says "something's wrong" before the instruments confirm it; still learning when to say it out loud and when to wait.
- **Sample Dialogue:** "His heartbeat is wrong. I don't — I can't tell you exactly wrong how. It just is. Can we run something?"
