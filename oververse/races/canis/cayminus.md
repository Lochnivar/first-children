# Cayminus (Coyote Folk)

**Status:** Framework Element - Universal Race

## Overview

Cayminus (*Canis cayminus*) are coyote-based anthropomorphic people. Common names: **Coyote folk**, Nummus's bankers. Nummus's banking staff at Mercara's Cross—banking vaults, quantum ledger, financial systems. Contractual and opportunistic; everything is a deal.

### Truism
*"Sound books, steady hands, and a great deal of patience."*

## Author's Narrative

The Cayminus started as a joke.

Specifically: what would it look like to staff a chaotic, provocative, boundary-ignoring FC's banking operations with people who were genuinely good at their jobs? The answer turned out to be more interesting than the joke.

Nummus is the most Trump-like of the First Children — chaotic, transactional, likely to offend, constitutionally incapable of leaving well enough alone. His banking infrastructure at Mercara's Cross required staff who could maintain immaculate books while simultaneously managing the political fallout from whatever Nummus had just done. The Cayminus didn't just survive that environment. They thrived in it.

Because here's the thing about chaos: it generates the most interesting financial conditions in the multiverse. Market disruptions, novel risk calculations, unprecedented contract structures. A Cayminus in a stable, predictable economy is a bored Cayminus. Working for Nummus is playing on the hardest difficulty setting — and they love every minute.

The coyote was always going to be this. In the mythologies of a dozen Earth cultures, Coyote is the trickster — chaotic, clever, amoral, funny. The joke was putting actual coyote people in suits and giving them a spreadsheet. What emerged was something more precise: a species for whom the *game* of acquisition is the point. Not the money sitting in the vault. The move that put it there.

They are not thieves in the pejorative sense, though they will steal. They are not bankers in the institutional sense, though they run the books. They are connoisseurs of the deal in all its forms — the signed contract, the lifted pocket watch, the hostile acquisition engineered through seventeen layered agreements. Different boards. Same game. The satisfaction is always in the elegance of the play.

What surprised me was the dignity that emerged from the comedy. The gentleman in the corduroy jacket with the silver-headed walking stick and the red bow tie isn't performing respectability — he *is* respectable, in the way that genuine mastery of anything eventually becomes. He has spent a very long time getting extraordinarily good at something he loves. The wealth is simply what competence looks like after sufficient time.

The Bruin give them a long leash, professionally speaking. The Cayminus pay promptly when caught, never whine about it, and keep the acquisition to a minimum in policed territory. It's an unspoken gentleman's agreement between two species who respect competence above almost everything else. Neither acknowledges it formally. That would require a conversation about feelings, which neither wants.

Nummus, for his part, probably doesn't fully understand what he has in them. That's fine. They understand it perfectly.

### Governance & Politics

Cayminus governance is simple in principle and ruthless in practice: laissez-faire within, protectionist without.

Inside Cayminus institutions the game runs free. No restrictions on method, approach, or competition. One Cayminus outmaneuvering another is expected, respected, and occasionally applauded if the play was elegant enough. The only enforceable internal rules are the ones that keep the institution functional:

- Contracts are sacred. Always.
- The books balance. No exceptions.
- Restitution is prompt. Don't make it complicated.

Clumsy interference in another's play is the closest thing to a social crime — not because it's harmful but because it's graceless. Elegance is the standard. Everything else is negotiable.

Externally the wall goes up immediately. Outside players don't receive the same access, the same information, or the same terms. Information asymmetry is the most valuable commodity a Cayminus institution holds; sharing it freely with outsiders is leaving money on the table. Individual Cayminus may compete fiercely among themselves, but they present a unified, impenetrable face to the outside world.

There is no formal government. There are houses, institutional networks, and the quiet weight of accumulated reputation. Status is earned through quality of play, not appointment. The oldest and most successful houses carry institutional authority not because anyone granted it but because nobody has successfully challenged it.

Nummus sits inside the wall. That was the deal. It remains the best deal either party ever made.

### Legal Code
- Contractual & opportunistic justice
- Everything is a deal. Breach a contract → restitution or servitude
- Disputes settled by negotiation or arbitration (they're good at it)
- Punishment: Debt slavery until repaid (with interest)
- "You entered the agreement. You pay the price. Simple."

### Theology

Nummus made an offer. The Cayminus negotiated terms. The deal was struck.

That's it. That's the theology.

Nummus would provide a home, resources, and the most financially interesting operating environment in the multiverse. The Cayminus would provide their talents, maintain the books, and keep his banking infrastructure running flawlessly regardless of what he'd just done. Both parties knew what they were signing. Both parties signed anyway.

The covenant holds. It has always held. The Cayminus uphold their end with characteristic precision and expect — with equal precision — that Nummus upholds his. When he doesn't, which is frequently, the response is not crisis of faith. It's a formally filed grievance, documented with evidence, delivered with professional disappointment and genuine affection.

Nummus finds this either hilarious or baffling depending on the day. The Cayminus find Nummus exhausting and irreplaceable in roughly equal measure. The arrangement continues.

There are no priests. No temples. No schools of theological thought. The cosmic questions — what Nummus actually is, what the FC represent, what happens after the ledger closes for the last time — are considered interesting dinner conversation and not much more. The deal was real. The work is real. The rest is speculation.

The candles get lit. Nobody asks why anymore. They're worth lighting regardless.

## Naming Conventions

### Philosophy

Cayminus names carry polished elegance and understated wealth — evoking 19th-century banking houses, Parisian salons, and gentlemen's clubs. They suggest old money, refined taste, and the quiet thrill of a well-played acquisition without being overt. A name is the first move in any game; it should be a good one.

Gender is largely irrelevant in naming. Names sound neutral or adaptable unless a character deliberately leans into a performative social role. Professional identity and reputation outrank gender in every social context.

### First Names

Drawn from Victorian British and French Belle Époque pools. Mostly gender-neutral or easily adaptable in sound and use.

#### Victorian Stock
Grounded, respectable — the financier across the desk. Associated with Cayminus operating inside legitimate institutions.

Henry, Edward, Oscar, Reginald, Theodore, Victor, William, Charles, James, Percy, Alfred, George, Florence, Violet, Clara, Ida, Louisa, Mabel

#### French Stock
Elegant, opportunistic charm. Associated with Cayminus who operate in the spaces between institutions — brokers, arbiters, consultants whose exact role is difficult to define.

Camille, Claude, Dominique, Émile, Henri, Louis, Maxime, Pierre, Rémy, Antoine, Auguste, Blaise, Léopold, Séraphin, Zéphyr, Aurélie, Amélie, Marguerite, Blanche, Fleur

#### Blended Stock
Most common. The adaptable player comfortable in both the boardroom and the back room.

Rémy Ashford, Henri Vale, Oscar Richelieu, Camille Beaumont, Louis Fontaine

### Last Names

Last names evoke inherited wealth, institutional legacy, and quiet financial power. They feel earned across generations — a house name that opens doors before a word is spoken.

#### Precious Metals & Currency Stock
The name itself does financial work. Understated but unmistakable.

Aurum, Argent, Sterling, Goldwyn, Silverton, Golding, Silvers, Aurelius, Doré, Or

#### Aristocratic & Old Money Stock
Victorian and French old-money flavor. Associated with established houses and long institutional presence.

Beaumont, Beauchêne, Cavendish, Devonshire, Fitzwilliam, Hamilton, Richelieu, Spencer, Talbot, Wentworth

#### Place & Fortune Stock
Subtle luxury or ancestral association. The family name references where the money came from without saying so directly.

Fontaine, Lafontaine, Lamontagne, Larivière, Lavallée, Roche, D'Aureville, Crossveil, Vale, Sovereign

### Accomplishment Names

Rare. The Cayminus equivalent of a knighthood — an ancestor's move so legendary it became the name itself rather than a house or metal. Most Cayminus have a *Sterling* or a *Beaumont*. The one with *Fivekeys* is someone people have heard of.

Fivekeys, Quickclose, Playmaster, Nightclose, Doubleterm, Slidekey

### Placeholder Names

A Cayminus without a house name is identified by financial state rather than physical appearance — because for them, that is the most fundamental descriptor. No house. No legacy play. No accumulated reputation.

#### Loss Stock
The name stings quietly. Something was lost or taken.

Broke, Spent, Null, Void, Bare, Naught, Zero

#### Neutrality Stock
Not shameful — settled. A Cayminus who closed all accounts and chose to begin fresh rather than carry a name they no longer wanted.

Even, Level, Flat, Square, Par, Wash, Clear

A Cayminus who earns their way from a placeholder name to a house name has played the most personal game of acquisition possible — and won.
