# Otter-Folk (Lutra sapiens)

**Status:** Framework Element - Universal Race

## Overview

Otter-folk (*Lutra sapiens*) are otter-derived anthropomorphic people from the FC genetic repository. Self-designation: **Otter-folk** or **Ottern** (singular). Common names: **Otters**, "the ones who make it okay to laugh," "check your pockets." No designated role; traits lead to emergent facilitation, bridge-building—and in some individuals, grift. Passed trial by cooperation under pressure: play as stress relief, tool use for survival, holding hands so no one drifts away.

### Truism
*"Don't be afraid to smile with an Ottern, but make sure to check your pockets."*

## Universal Characteristics

### Physical Attributes
- **Height:** 4–5 feet; lean, agile
- **Build:** Otter-derived head—small ears, whiskers, expressive
- **Lifespan:** Moderate (varies by world)
- **Digits:** Webbed; comfortable in water and on land
- **Fur:** Dense; varies by variant
- **Distinctive:** Carry favorite objects (rocks, stones); tool use is natural

### Cognitive & Behavioral Traits
- **Play-as-bonding:** Build trust through shared play; process stress through silliness. A serious negotiation that ends without at least one moment of shared play is incomplete. They'll turn a tense standoff into a game of "who can balance the rock longer" and everyone will feel better. The warmth is genuine and physical: touch, shared objects, proximity.
- **Charm-aware:** They know they're disarming. They're not accidentally charming—they're deliberately deploying it. Individual variation: some are genuine facilitators, some are con artists, some are both. Even the grifters can be warm—the unsettling part isn't "real vs. fake" but "is this warmth being used right now, or is it just warmth?" You never know which Ottern you're dealing with. Sometimes neither. Sometimes both in the same afternoon.

### Social Characteristics
- **Community:** Cooperative, physical, touch-based bonding
- **Culture:** Play-centered; shared objects as tokens of trust

## Natural Gravitation

**Why they become facilitators:** Play defuses tension. Charm opens doors. They make it safe to be vulnerable—or they make you *think* it's safe. The species you want in a room when two sides have stopped talking—or the species you should worry about when someone's about to get taken.

**Roles:** Bridge-builders, plaza facilitators, integration-metric contractors, "the ones who make it okay to laugh." Also: grifters, con artists, the charming kid who runs the long con. FC note: "Charm as weapon and tool. Noted. Use with caution."

## Relationships

- **Quedari:** Both patient. Quedari curl and wait; Otter-folk play and wait. Different energy.
- **Gnolls:** Both use humor. Gnolls puncture pretension; Otter-folk create connection—or the illusion of it. Gnolls might clock them: "You're good. I'm better. But I see what you're doing."
- **Kitsune:** Fellow operators. Kitsune use contracts; Otter-folk use charm. Mutual recognition. Sometimes partners, sometimes rivals.
- **Corvani:** Possibly immune. Hyper-literal, don't respond to charm. Otter-folk find them frustrating or refreshing.
