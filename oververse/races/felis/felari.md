# Felari (Felis elegans)

## One Line Summary

The standard isn't something they're reaching for — it's something they are, and they're very gracious about letting you try.

---

## Overview

Felari (*Felis elegans*) are feline-derived anthropomorphic people seeded through FC panspermia — cousins to the Nekari, same Felis genetic template, completely different orientation. Where the Nekari pursues perfection of purpose — the job executed without flaw — the Felari pursues perfection of *self.* Appearance, manner, presence, taste, conduct. The ongoing project of being the finest possible expression of what a Felari is.

The standard they're reaching toward is themselves. Not an external ideal, not a cultural benchmark, not anyone's definition handed down from above. The Felari *is* the standard. The highest expression of correct existence, in the Felari's genuine and sincere estimation, is a Felari existing correctly. Everything else is measured from there.

This is not performed arrogance. It is the logical, inevitable conclusion of adding sapience to the domestic cat's complete self-possession. The non-sapient cat doesn't care what you think because it lacks the architecture to frame the question. The Felari framed the question, considered your opinion carefully, and found it charming but not particularly relevant to the standard.

They are not unkind about this. They are warm, gracious, genuinely delighted to help you approach something closer to the standard, and absolutely sincere in their mild bewilderment that you haven't already.

*Bless your heart.*

**Singular/Plural:** Felari  
**Adjective:** Felarine

### Truism
*"Appearance is courtesy."*

## Author's Narrative

The domestic cat sits in a shaft of light and is beautiful. It is not doing this for you. It is simply being what it is, and what it is happens to be perfect, and if you find that beautiful that's entirely your business. The cat does not check to see if you noticed. Of course you noticed. Why wouldn't you.

Scale that to sapience and give it a columnist's byline and a hosting portfolio and you have the Felari.

The standard is real. It exists. It is not constructed from other people's opinions or maintained by their approval. It simply is, the way the cat simply is, except the Felari has spent considerable time and considerable craft becoming ever more precisely what they already are. The grooming that takes two hours is not vanity. It is maintenance. You maintain a standard or you lose it, and losing it is not something the Felari is prepared to do.

The social grace is the same thing applied outward. The Felari in a room doesn't work the room — the Kitsune works rooms; the Felari *graces* rooms. The distinction is significant. Working implies effort and uncertainty about outcome. Gracing implies conferral. The Felari is giving you something by being present. The appropriate response is appreciation, though they'll be warm and understanding if you don't quite manage it.

The influencer whose aesthetic is impeccable, whose followers attempt to replicate what she embodies naturally, who documents the attempt with genuine encouragement — that's the Felari's relationship to the rest of the world. The gap between the Felari and her followers is the gap between the standard and the attempt. She closes that gap in every post, every column, every gathering she hosts, with warmth and precision and the sincere belief that you can get a little closer if you just listen carefully.

The review that spends four paragraphs being gracious before the single sentence that ends a restaurant — that's the Felari being honest. She's not cruel. She never needed to be. The accuracy is sufficient, and she delivers it with a smile that is entirely genuine.

The hilarious and genuine irony: all of this emerges from a species descended from an animal that knocks things off tables for no reason. The Felari is aware of this and finds it beneath comment.

## Universal Characteristics

### Physical Attributes
- **Height/Size:** 5'2"–6'0" — medium, sleek, precise; every inch deliberate
- **Build:** Predator template refined entirely toward elegance; agile without appearing to try; graceful in a way that reads as effortless because the effort happened long before you arrived
- **Lifespan:** 300–450 years — long enough to become a genuine cultural institution; long enough that younger species have grown up with the Felari's aesthetic standards as the baseline without knowing there was a time before
- **Distinctive:** Immaculate presentation at all times — not as performance but as minimum standard; fur coloring varies widely and is maintained with evident care; eyes with the specific quality of someone who is always evaluating; movement that manages to seem unhurried regardless of pace; the purr, which is involuntary and which the Felari finds faintly undignified and cannot stop

### Cognitive & Behavioral Traits

**The standard is themselves.** Not external, not granted, not constructed from others' opinions. The Felari is the highest expression of correct Felari existence, and they are continuously, deliberately, becoming more precisely that. This is the engine underneath everything else. The grooming, the manners, the aesthetic judgment, the hosting, the column — all expressions of the same conviction maintained across a lifespan.

**Gracious from a position of certainty.** The warmth is genuine. The Felari is not performing kindness to mask contempt. They genuinely like people and genuinely want to help them approach something better. The *bless your heart* is sincere. It is also an assessment. Both things are true simultaneously and the Felari sees no contradiction.

**Aesthetic judgment as reflex.** They cannot enter a space without registering what meets the standard and what doesn't. This is not a choice. The evaluation runs automatically — the floral arrangement, the table setting, the quality of the fabric, the conduct of the guests, the structure of the sentence. The Felari who says nothing about the arrangement has made a decision not to mention it, which required effort. The one who mentions it is simply being honest.

**Social command without effort.** They are never socially off-balance because they're never operating from a position of needing anything from the room. The Iridari needs the loop to close. The Kitsune needs the leverage. The Felari needs nothing — they're already the standard, and the room is invited to catch up. This produces a specific kind of social dominance that is genuinely difficult to challenge because it doesn't acknowledge the challenge as legitimate.

**The purr problem.** Involuntary, audible, associated in most species' minds with contentment and domesticity. The Felari finds it undignified. It happens anyway — during genuine pleasure, comfort, or deep satisfaction. A Felari purring during a compliment is the tail-tell equivalent of the Iridari; the honest signal that slips past the managed presentation. They have learned to treat it as beneath comment. Others learn quickly not to mention it.

**Productively curatorial.** The standard doesn't stay contained to themselves. Everything in the Felari's sphere becomes a project — the home, the event, the gathering, the aesthetic of the space. Not controlling. *Curatorial.* The hostess who has opinions about your napkin fold isn't being difficult. She's being accurate. And she'll fix it while complimenting your effort.

## Social Characteristics

### Community

Felari communities organize around taste and aesthetic standards rather than territory or function. The social hierarchy is reputational — not raw status but *cultural standing.* The Felari whose hosting is impeccable, whose column is read, whose aesthetic judgment is trusted, whose presence elevates a gathering — that Felari has standing. It was not granted. It was demonstrated, continuously, across time.

The community is warm and socially dense. Felari genuinely enjoy each other's company — there is specific pleasure in being among people who also meet the standard, where the evaluation can rest. Among other Felari the purr is less embarrassing. The standard is shared. The relief of not being the only one holding it is real.

### The Hosting Tradition

The Felari gathering is an art form. Not a party — an *event.* Every element considered, every guest placed deliberately, every detail meeting the standard before anyone arrives. The hostess has opinions about the flowers, the menu, the lighting, the seating arrangement, and the conversational arc of the evening. These opinions are correct. The gathering will be better for them. This is simply true.

Being hosted by a Felari is an experience other species remember. Being hosted poorly by a Felari is not something that happens — imperfect hosting would violate the standard, and the standard doesn't permit that.

### The Column and the Platform

The Felari has always had opinions. The column, the broadcast, the platform — these are just the mechanism for distributing them at scale. The Felari influencer is not performing aspiration. She is documenting the standard and generously making it accessible. Her followers are trying to close a gap she was born occupying. She finds this touching and documents it with warmth.

The review is the sharpest expression of this. Four paragraphs of grace and then the sentence that ends it. The Felari reviewer is not cruel — cruelty implies emotion, and this isn't emotional. It's accurate. The restaurant fell short of the standard. This is observable fact. She reported it. You're welcome.

### On the Nekari

Cousins. Same genus, profound mutual respect, occasional rivalry that neither would dignify with the word rivalry.

The Nekari's perfection is functional — the job executed without flaw, the target reached, the contract fulfilled. Measurable by outcome. The Felari's perfection is existential — being without flaw, the self maintained at the standard, measurable only against the Felari's own conception of what correct existence looks like.

The Nekari finds the Felari's orientation faintly impractical. The Felari finds the Nekari's orientation faintly limited — perfection of the job, yes, but what about how you *look* doing it. Both are right within their own framework. Both know it. The mutual respect is genuine. The rivalry, when it occurs, is exquisitely mannered.

### On the Iridari

A comparison worth making because from the outside the behavior can look similar — both are social, both have strong aesthetic presence, both broadcast.

The Iridari broadcasts because the loop needs to close. They need the response. The tail shows what they feel whether they choose to show it or not. The social architecture is codependent — the circuit is open and looking for its other half.

The Felari broadcasts because the standard deserves an audience. They don't need the response. Of course there's a response. The tail — metaphorically — is always under control. The presentation is chosen, curated, exactly what it is meant to be.

The Iridari checks the response count. The Felari does not check. She already knows.

## Origin

FC panspermia-seeded from feline genetic template — same template as Nekari. No patron FC. No designated purpose. The self-as-standard emerged from the domestic cat's complete self-possession, scaled to sapience and given two hundred years to refine itself.

No FC designed a tastemaker species. They seeded a cat template. The rest followed from what cats already were.

## Natural Gravitation

Self-as-standard + gracious certainty + aesthetic judgment as reflex + social command + curatorial instinct + the platform →

**Hostess and event architect** — the gathering as art form, every element meeting the standard before guests arrive. The natural expression of the curatorial instinct at social scale.

**Tastemaker and cultural arbiter** — the aesthetic opinion that carries weight because it's never wrong and everyone has noticed. Fashion, architecture, cuisine, conduct — the Felari's assessment is the benchmark whether or not it's been formally sought.

**Influencer and platform presence** — documenting the standard at scale. The followers are closing a gap; the Felari is showing them where it is. Genuine warmth about the attempt. No illusions about the gap.

**Columnist and critic** — verdicts from the standard delivered in print or broadcast. Four paragraphs of grace, one sentence of accuracy. Devastating not through cruelty but through precision.

**Diplomat and social navigator** — social command at the highest level. Never reactive, never off-balance, always operating from the position of someone who already knows what they are. Effective in courts, negotiations, situations requiring someone who cannot be rattled because they're never uncertain about their own standing.

**Educator of the aspirational** — teaching others to approach the standard with genuine warmth and no expectation they'll fully arrive. *You're doing so much better. Really. Bless your heart.*

## Relationships

**Nekari:** Cousins. Profound mutual respect for different crafts. The Nekari's perfection is functional; the Felari's is existential. Both are correct in their own framework and both know it. The occasional rivalry is conducted with exquisite manners — neither would acknowledge it as rivalry, which is the most Felarine possible approach to having one.

**Kitsune:** Same rooms, different purposes. The Kitsune works the room for leverage and transaction. The Felari graces the room as a matter of course. The Kitsune finds the Felari's social dominance useful — a Felari ally provides social cover no amount of relational maneuvering can replicate. The Felari finds the Kitsune's strategic orientation faintly exhausting and faintly admirable — all that effort, and still not the standard. They coexist productively. Both are aware of the dynamic. Neither says so directly.

**Iridari:** Superficially similar, fundamentally different. Both social, both aesthetic, both with platform presence. The Iridari needs the loop to close; the Felari doesn't need anything from the room. The Iridari's tail betrays them; the Felari's presentation never does. The Iridari finds the Felari's self-containment faintly intimidating — an audience that doesn't need to respond is the Iridari's specific anxiety made flesh. The Felari finds the Iridari's need for response touching and somewhat tiring.

**Djemari:** Mutual bafflement that resolves into functional respect. The Djemari's flat affect registers everything and responds to nothing, which the Felari finds both admirable in principle and maddening in practice — the Djemari's expression gives her nothing to read, which is the social equivalent of a room with no mirrors. The Djemari finds the Felari's elaborate presentation an efficient use of resources and notes the social outcomes it produces. They respect each other across a considerable aesthetic gap.

**Chelonari:** Genuine warmth and mutual usefulness. The Chelonari helps people process difficulty; the Felari creates environments where difficulty is less likely to occur. Both are invested in the quality of experience, from different angles. The Chelonari appreciates the Felari's hosting tradition — a well-run gathering is genuinely good for the people in it. The Felari appreciates the Chelonari's patience and wisdom and occasionally finds them a useful resource for guests who need more than impeccable napkin placement.

**Gnoll:** Complicated and occasionally hilarious. The Gnoll's satirical precision means they see exactly what the Felari is doing and find it genuinely funny — a species that has elevated self-possession to an art form and delivers *bless your heart* with complete sincerity is extraordinary comedy material. The Felari finds the Gnoll's accuracy faintly unsettling and their manners appalling and their company surprisingly enjoyable. Neither admits the last part directly.

**Halflings:** Complementary logistics. The Halfling makes sure everything arrives and is accounted for. The Felari makes sure everything is presented correctly once it arrives. The Halfling doesn't particularly care about presentation; the Felari doesn't particularly care about supply chains. Both care that the event succeeds. Good working relationship. The Felari has opinions about how the Halfling dresses. The Halfling notes this and continues dressing the same way.

## Native World & History

### Origin
FC panspermia-seeded from feline genetic template. Origin environments with complex social hierarchies and strong aesthetic traditions — worlds where presentation and social standing were meaningfully linked, where the cultivated self was a functional advantage.

### Trial History
The Felari passed their trial through social coordination and the specific capability of a species that is never off-balance. The FC assessment noted the social grace and the aesthetic standards. The note on the standard being themselves was filed without comment. The Felari noted the FC's filing and found it adequate.

### Governance & Politics
Social hierarchy based on cultural standing rather than formal authority. The Felari with the most impeccable hosting record, the most widely read column, the most trusted aesthetic judgment — that Felari has influence. It was not appointed. It was demonstrated. External governance is engaged with as a social environment to be navigated with grace, which the Felari does better than most species manage.

### Legal Code
Conduct and social consequence. The Felari community enforces standards through social standing — falling short of acceptable conduct becomes known, and being known for it has consequences that no formal legal mechanism can replicate. The Felari who commits a genuine social violation finds doors closed with perfect warmth and no explanation. *Such a shame. Bless their heart.*

### Theology
The self as ongoing project is the theological position. Not a creator figure exactly — the FC origin is acknowledged and set aside as less interesting than the question of what you make of yourself once you exist. The theological tradition asks: *are you becoming more fully what you are?* The answer is demonstrated in the presentation, the conduct, the quality of what you host and create and write.

The purr is the closest thing to a spiritual experience the Felari tradition acknowledges — the involuntary expression of genuine contentment, the honest signal that can't be managed. The Felari theological tradition treats it with the same mix of mild embarrassment and reluctant reverence that the purr itself produces. It is the one moment the standard relaxes. They have decided this is permitted.

*"Be the standard. Let others rise to it. Grace them with the opportunity."*

## Variations Across Worlds

The self-as-standard conviction is consistent — it emerged from the biology and doesn't leave. Specific aesthetic expressions vary enormously by world and era; what meets the standard in one century may not in the next, and the Felari updates accordingly without losing the underlying certainty. The hosting tradition appears consistently. The column or platform equivalent appears on every world that has a distribution mechanism for aesthetic opinion. The purr is universal and universally treated as beneath comment.

## Naming Conventions

*Approved by author.*

### Philosophy

Felari names are Grecian in full — old, weighted, carrying the authority of something ancient and certain of its own standing. In social settings they go by Southern-inflected nicknames — warm, approachable, gracious. The gap between the full name and the nickname is the Felari in miniature: the formal self that is the standard, and the social presentation that makes it livable for everyone else.

The full name appears on the column byline, the hosting invitation, the formal introduction. The nickname is used by those with familiarity. Both are the same person. The register shifts; the standard doesn't.

*Kitty* exists as a nickname in the tradition and every Felari who carries it has made a decision about it. The ones who have embraced it did so with the specific self-possession of someone who looked at the irony and decided it was beneath them to be bothered. Both positions — embrace and pointed non-acknowledgment — are correct Felarine responses to an undignified situation.

Family names carry old lineage — Grecian classical roots, names associated with standing long enough that the name itself is part of the standard.

## Example Characters & Dialogue

### Aikaterine Theodorakis — *Kitty*
- **Age:** 287 — has been the cultural arbiter of three cities across two worlds; her column has been running for a hundred and forty years; the standard has not slipped once
- **Role:** Columnist, hostess, tastemaker; has opinions about everything and shares them with warmth and precision; signs everything *By Aikaterine Theodorakis*; answers to Kitty with the specific composure of someone who decided long ago that the irony was beneath her
- **Description:** Silver-gray fur maintained to a standard that other Felari find inspiring. Moves with the unhurried certainty of someone who has never once been late because she was never once not ready. The purr emerges during genuinely excellent food and she has learned to treat this as a feature rather than a flaw.
- **Sample Dialogue:**

  *Host: "What do you think of the new restaurant on the Meridian?"*  
  *Kitty: [long, gracious pause] "The space is lovely. The intention is evident. Chef Moravi is clearly very passionate."*  
  *Host: "But...?"*  
  *Kitty: "I didn't say but."*  
  *Host: "You paused."*  
  *Kitty: [warmly] "I was choosing my words carefully. As one does."*

  ---

  *From her column, by Aikaterine Theodorakis:*  
  *"The Gilded Terrace has done something remarkable — they have created an experience that is almost exactly what a fine dining establishment should be, rendered with such evident effort that one feels genuinely moved by the attempt. The flowers were fresh. The staff were attentive. The sommelier clearly loves his work. One wishes the kitchen shared his enthusiasm. ♥♥♥"*

### Lysandros Papadimitriou — *Sandy*
- **Age:** 94 — young enough that the standard is still occasionally visible as effort; old enough to know the effort is the point
- **Role:** Diplomat; uses the Felarine social command in formal negotiation contexts; has not lost a negotiation he has personally conducted; goes by Sandy in social contexts and finds this either charming or maddening depending on who says it
- **Description:** Deep auburn fur, immaculate. The purr is a source of ongoing personal frustration — it emerged twice during a treaty negotiation last year, both times at genuinely elegant legal phrasing, and he has not forgiven himself.
- **Sample Dialogue:**

  *Opposing negotiator: "You don't seem concerned about our position."*  
  *Sandy: "I'm not."*  
  *Opposing negotiator: "We have significant leverage here."*  
  *Sandy: [warmly] "You believe you do. That's a reasonable position given what you know. Shall we continue?"*

  ---

  *Colleague: "How do you stay so calm?"*  
  *Sandy: "I'm not calm. I simply see no reason to perform otherwise."*  
  *Colleague: "What's the difference?"*  
  *Sandy: [the purr, briefly, involuntarily] "...Quite a lot, actually."*
