# Felari (Felis elegans)

**Status:** Framework Element - Universal Race  
**Category:** Races / Species  
**Related:** [Genus Felis](genus.md), [Nekari](nekari.md), [Races README](../README.md)

## Overview

Felari (*Felis elegans*) are feline-derived anthropomorphic people created through FC panspermia—cousins to the Nekari, sharing the Felis genetic template. Where Nekari pursue perfection of purpose or action, Felari pursue perfection of appearance or manners. Same genus, different orientation: Nekari are about the job; Felari are about the presentation.

## Contrast with Nekari

| | Nekari | Felari |
|---|--------|--------|
| **Perfection of** | Purpose, action | Appearance, manners |
| **Focus** | The job gets done | How it looks when it's done |
| **Truism** | "Perfection is not vanity, it is duty." | "Appearance is courtesy." |

Both share Felis traits: predator template, solitary tendency, pride. Nekari channel that into precision work; Felari channel it into social grace, aesthetics, and the cultivation of presence.

## Universal Characteristics

### Physical Attributes
- Sleek, graceful (same Felis baseline as Nekari)
- Night vision, enhanced senses
- Grooming and presentation are paramount

### Emergent Tendencies
- Perfection of appearance → etiquette, fashion, aesthetics, social refinement
- Perfection of manners → diplomacy, hosting, cultural arbiters
- Often found in roles where presentation matters: courts, salons, high-end negotiations

### Personality
- Appearance-conscious (not vain—it's the craft)
- Manners as weapon and tool
- **Truism:** *"Appearance is courtesy."* — Double meaning: "Look your best" and "You're lucky we grace you with our presence."

## Truism

*"Appearance is courtesy."* — Double meaning: "Look your best" (polite) and "You're lucky we grace you with our presence" (edge).

## Variations Across Worlds

- Cultural expressions of "perfection" vary
- Relationship to Nekari: cousins, sometimes rivals; mutual respect for different crafts
- May overlap with Kitsune in social spaces (Felari: presentation; Kitsune: transaction)
