# Kitsune (Fox People)

**Status:** Framework Element - Universal Race  
**Category:** Races / Species  
**Related:** [Genus Vulpes](genus.md), [Races README](../README.md), [Nekari](../felis/nekari.md), [Kibo](../hippopotamus/kibo.md), [Geliss](../gekko/geliss.md), [Bandari](../procyon/bandari.md)

## Overview

Kitsune are fox-based anthropomorphic people created through FC panspermia. Their traits—clever, cunning, morally flexible—tend toward information, trade, and opportunism. Not a designated role; brokering, deal-making, and finding what others need emerge naturally from who they are. They won't help for free, ever. "Nothing personal, just business." They often form one vertex of the economy triangle with Nekari and Kibo. Legara recruits them for contract work at Mercara's Cross.

### Truism
*"Everything has a price, only Kitsune know theirs."*

## Author's Narrative

The Kitsune understand people. Not academically, not observationally — from inside the experience, as participants with genuine stakes in the outcome. They read what someone actually wants beneath what they're saying. They find the gap between stated position and genuine need. They know the precise moment when resolve begins to crack.

This makes them extraordinary contract specialists. It makes them equally extraordinary con artists. The skill set is identical — the difference is ethics, and Kitsune ethics are, by their own accounting, flexible.

The legitimate broker and the accomplished grifter are the same Kitsune wearing different hats. Both are reading the same signals, running the same calculations, finding the same pressure points. The contract is the professional instrument. The con is the same instrument without the paperwork. A Kitsune will tell you the paperwork is important. They're not wrong. They're also not always wearing the hat you think they are.

This is why Legara found them useful and why they found Legara's offer worth taking. The work at Mercara's Cross puts them in contact with every species, every motivation, every configuration of need and fear and commitment the multiverse generates. For a species that counts coup in relationships rather than gold, that's the richest environment imaginable.

The tail rank system measures what actually matters — depth of relational understanding, sophistication of read, breadth of network. A marking appears when mastery is demonstrated. How that mastery was developed is between the Kitsune and their conscience. The tail doesn't ask.

The nine-tails have spent 800 years watching mortals make and break commitments. They have seen most configurations of the question. They still find new ones. This is, from a Kitsune perspective, the whole point.

The truism says everything: *"Everything has a price. Only Kitsune know theirs."*

Not the financial price. The motivational price. What someone actually wants, what they'll actually pay, what they'll actually sacrifice when the moment arrives and the calculation becomes real.

That knowledge is the currency. The contract is just what reveals it.

## Universal Characteristics

### Physical Attributes
- **Height/Size:** Medium build; quick, agile
- **Build:** Attractive, charming; use appeal to negotiate
- **Lifespan:** Very long-lived (hundreds to thousands of years, varies by world)
- **Tails:** One tail (always—exactly one physical tail). Rank markings (bands, patterns) indicate mastery. See Tail Rank System below.
- **Forms:** May shapeshift between fox and humanoid forms (varies by world)
- **Appearance:** In humanoid form, often attractive and graceful

### Cognitive & Behavioral Traits
- **Clever:** Silver-tongued, quick-witted
- **Ruthlessly practical:** Morally flexible; practical over principled
- **Transactional:** Won't help for free (ever). "Nothing personal, just business."
- **Memory:** Long memories, especially for social interactions and precedent
- **Learning:** Quick learners, especially for social manipulation

### Social Characteristics
- **Community:** Trade networks; guild-like structures; rank hierarchy (tail markings)
- **Culture:** Contract culture; reputation is currency; rank earns respect

## Natural Gravitation

Clever + cunning + morally flexible → information work, brokering, deal-making. Traits lead naturally to trade, contracts, and finding what others need. Often form the economy triangle with Nekari (precision) and Kibo (muscle): Kitsune brokers, Nekari executes, Kibo protect. Kitsune takes cut, professionals get paid.

## Relationships

- **Nekari:** Professional respect; Kitsune broker, Nekari execute. Economy triangle.
- **Kibo:** Muscle for hire; Kitsune broker, Kibo protect. Economy triangle.
- **Otter-folk:** Fellow operators. Kitsune use contracts; Otter-folk use charm. Mutual recognition.

## Naming Conventions

### Philosophy

Kitsune names are relational instruments before they are identifiers. Every element conveys context, desired tone, and the type of interaction being offered or requested. A skilled reader can learn more from a Kitsune business card than most species learn from an hour of conversation.

Names are revealed in layers, each calibrated to the relationship and the moment. The full name is rarely deployed all at once — it is earned, offered, or withheld with deliberate precision.

### Given Names: Star and Mine

Every Kitsune carries two given names, hyphenated in formal use.

#### The Star Name
Bestowed in infancy by family or close elders. A quiet guiding prayer — the light they hope the child will walk toward. It may evoke clarity, renewal, hidden strength, growth through adversity, or quiet wisdom. The Star name precedes the Kitsune. It was there before they had opinions about themselves.

The Star name is who your family saw when they looked at you. Whether you grow into it, run from it, or spend 800 years finding your way back to it is your own story.

Drawn from Japanese pools — precise, nature-imagery, contained.

**Examples with meanings:**
Akira (bright clarity), Haru (spring / renewal), Hikari (light), Kaze (wind / unseen movement), Ren (lotus / growth through adversity), Sora (sky / open possibility), Tsukiko (moon child), Yoru (night / hidden paths), Yumi (reason / bow), Kai (sea / recovery), Miko (shrine insight), Tama (jewel / precious), Fuji (enduring), Izumi (spring / fountain), Suzu (bell), Mizuki (beautiful moon), Nami (wave), Kiri (mist), Shirou (clear / white), Hayate (swift wind)

#### The Mine Name
Chosen by the Kitsune themselves at the One-Tail ceremony — a coming-of-age ritual akin to a bar or bat mitzvah. The young Kitsune stands before their community and declares who they intend to be in relationship with the world. Warm, accessible, immediately social. The face they are choosing to show.

The Mine name is who you decided to be. Whether the warmth is genuine or instrumental is a calculation only the Kitsune is making.

Drawn from Creole and French Belle Époque pools — fluid, musical, socially open.

**Examples:**
Amelie, Céleste, Delphine, Fleur, Lisette, Rémy, Aurélie, Blanche, Camille, Émilien, Marcelin, Roseline, Séraphin, Théodore, Zéphyr, Clairvie, Bastien, Antoine, Marguerite, Émile

#### The Hyphenated Full Given Name
In formal use both names appear together. The Mine name always leads — the declared self before the guiding hope. The Star name follows, grounding the warmth in something more specific.

*Amelie-Akira. Fleur-Haru. Céleste-Tsukiko. Marcelin-Ren.*

The gap between the Mine name and the Star name is where the most interesting Kitsune story lives.

### Family Names: Dual Lineage

Every Kitsune carries two family names joined by the Spanish *"o"* connector — one from Japanese fox mythology, one from Creole and French cultural pools. Both are always present in the full formal name. Which leads depends on context.

#### Japanese Family Names
Formal, weighted, mythologically resonant. Announces lineage, depth, and cultural identity. The name that appears on contracts, formal documents, and introductions where rank matters.

Kuzunoha, Tamamo, Akatsuki, Kagemichi, Inari, Hakumen, Shirogane, Tenko, Byakko, Kitsunebi, Sessho, Kogitsune

#### Creole/French Family Names
Warm, socially fluid, culturally adaptive. Announces a line that embedded itself deeply enough in a relational culture that the name stuck. The name that appears on dinner invitations, casual introductions, and interactions where warmth is the primary instrument.

Lafleur, Fontaine, Thibodeaux, Moreau, Lavallée, Beaumont, Boudreaux, Fontenot, Arceneaux, Bellerose, Roche, Delacroix, Cormier, Trosclair, Duvalier

#### The Full Formal Construction
*Mine-Star [Japanese] o [Creole]*

*Amelie-Akira Kuzunoha o Lafleur*

The Japanese family name always leads in the full formal construction regardless of social context. The card convention governs abbreviated social use only.

### The Card Convention

Kitsune strategically present which family name leads depending on context — and on what they want the interaction to be.

**Creole name leading** — warm, relational, approachable. *"We are doing this as people."* The broker's card. Extended with warmth. Always.

**Japanese name leading** — formal, weighted, professional. *"We are doing this as principals."* The contract card. Expected in formal proceedings whether the Kitsune prefers it or not.

A Kitsune who inverts the expected order is making a deliberate statement. A grifter hands you whichever card they've already determined makes you most comfortable — which means they decided before they reached into their pocket, which means they were reading you before you knew the interaction had started.

Higher tail ranks deploy both with precision. The card is always the first move. Most people never notice it was a move.

### Placeholder Names: Orphaned & Disowned

A Kitsune without family has no one to bestow a Star name and no community to witness a Mine name ceremony. Their tail rank serves as family name — the only identifier the world gave them when no one else would.

*Fleur Onetail. Delphine Threetail. Rémy Fivetail.*

This is not a young Kitsune awaiting ceremony. This is absence made visible. For a species whose entire identity is relational — who counts coup in relationships, who reveals themselves in layers to those who've earned it — carrying your rank as your only family marker means the network that should surround you simply isn't there.

**No Star name** means no family prayer. Nobody looked at this Kitsune in infancy and hoped something for them. Or that person is gone. Or severed. The guiding light was never lit, or was extinguished.

**No Mine name ceremony** means no community witness. The declaration of self was never made publicly, or was made and subsequently rejected.

A Kitsune who introduces themselves as *Delphine Threetail* is telling you everything about what's missing without saying a word about it. In Kitsune culture, the name lands like a closed door.

A Kitsune who earns their way from a placeholder name to a full dual family name has rebuilt the most personal possible network from nothing. That story is always worth hearing.

### Rank and Naming Fluency

- **One-tail** — Mine name freshly declared at ceremony. Still learning what the card convention means and why it matters.
- **Two to three-tail** — Hyphenated given name in regular professional use. Beginning to understand the social power of which name and which card they present.
- **Four to six-tail** — Full fluency. Knows exactly which face to show, which card to hand, and why. The calculation is automatic.
- **Seven to eight-tail** — The full name carries weight before they speak. Non-Kitsune who can read names adjust their tone immediately.
- **Nine-tail** — *Amelie-Akira Kuzunoha o Lafleur.* The full name deployed in full. Rooms quiet. The Star name feels quietly prophetic in hindsight. The Mine name, chosen 800 years ago by a young Kitsune at their ceremony, either fits perfectly now or tells its own complicated story.

## Tail Rank System

**Physical reality:** Every Kitsune has exactly one tail. Markings (bands, patterns) indicate rank. Markings appear naturally when mastery is demonstrated—both time and achievement required. Permanent; cannot be faked or removed.

### Rank Progression

| Rank | Name | Tail Appearance | Status |
|------|------|-----------------|--------|
| One-tail | Novice | Plain, unmarked | Learning; supervised closely |
| Two-tail | Journeyman | First band near tip | Completed basic training |
| Three-tail | Practiced | Second band (mid-tail); geometric patterns | Handles standard contracts independently |
| Four–six-tail | Established | Additional bands toward base; silver/gold accents | Trusted professional; complex cases |
| Seven–eight-tail | Expert/Senior | Interconnected patterns; metallic threading | Highly respected; difficult negotiations |
| Nine-tail | Master/Elder | Full coverage; mandalas, sacred geometry; metallic threading; faint glow when active | Legendary; word carries weight across worlds |

### How Markings Appear (Hybrid)
- **Natural emergence:** Tail faintly outlines when mastery approaches (genetic/magical recognition of skill)
- **Formal recognition:** Kitsune petitions council when patterns are clear; ceremony confirms and "sets" the marking
- Result: Organic + community validation. Can't fake it; ceremony makes it official.

### Cultural Significance
- **Respect hierarchy:** Nine-tail commands rooms. One-tail defers. Mid-ranks navigate carefully.
- **Assignment by rank:** Simple contracts → one/two-tail. Impossible multi-world deals → seven/nine-tail only.
- **Social navigation:** Non-Kitsune learn to count bands and adjust tone. "That's a seven-tail. Choose words carefully."
- **Personal pride:** Each new band is a life milestone.

### Nine-Tail Rarity
- 20–30 alive at any time (across all Kitsune)
- 800+ years minimum—most are ancient
- Tail: Full coverage, intricate, metallic-threaded, faint glow when active
- Presence: Immediate respect (or fear). Rooms quiet when they enter.
- "You can feel a nine-tail before you see them. The tail announces them."
