## One Line Summary

They can't leave the dying place — so they learned to help while they're there.

---

## Overview

Corvani (*Corvus morticus*) are crow-derived anthropomorphic people seeded through FC panspermia. Their defining trait is not a philosophy or a vocation. It is a compulsion: **they cannot look away from death.**

Not a death wish. Not morbidity performed for effect. A deep biological fixation on mortality — their own, others', the concept itself — that runs continuously in the background of everything they do. When something is dying, the Corvani cannot leave. When someone dies, the Corvani cannot forget them. When death is present in a room, the Corvani feels it the way other species feel hunger or cold — as an immediate, physical, unignorable fact.

They built a culture around making this useful. The healing tradition, the witness role, the plague doctor presence across worlds — none of it was chosen. It was the most productive thing to do with a fixation that would otherwise just be suffering. They were already in the dying place. They were already unable to leave. Medicine gave the presence purpose. Memory gave the loss meaning.

The dark cloak, the prominent beak, the habit of high places — other species find it ominous. The Corvani find the reaction genuinely confusing. They aren't performing death. They're just being what they are, in the places that call to them, doing what helps while they're there.

**Singular/Plural:** Corvani. Casual: *Corv*  
**Adjective:** Corvanine

### Truism
*"The Crows remember when no one else does."*

## Author's Narrative

The crow doesn't leave its dead. It gathers. It stays. It makes noise about it. Nobody designed that behavior — it's just what crows do when death happens near them, and it doesn't stop until the crow is ready, which sometimes takes a long time.

Scale that to sapience and the fixation becomes something you have to live with consciously. The Corvani who walks into a dying place isn't making a choice the way other species make choices. The pull is already there. The question is only what to do while you can't leave.

The healing emerged from that. They were already present. Already unable to go. Already watching with the full attention the fixation demands. At some point someone needed help and the Corvani was the only one still standing in the room, and the help was given, and that became the pattern. Not noble. Not selfless. Practical. If you're going to be here anyway, be useful.

The memory is the same. They don't choose to remember the dead. The dead stay. Faces, names, the last coherent thing someone said, what they were afraid of, what they left unfinished — this accumulates over a lifespan of five or six centuries and there is no setting it down. Other species grieve and eventually the acute weight of it shifts. The Corvani carries the full weight indefinitely. The dead are present in them the way living people are present in memory for other species — immediate, specific, retrievable at any moment, permanently there.

The death-framing that other species find unsettling is the fixation expressing itself cognitively. Everything gets measured against mortality not because the Corvani chose a philosophical framework but because the question is always running: how long does this last, what does this cost, who will still be here when it's over. It isn't depression. It is a continuous low-level awareness of mortality that most species can push to the background and the Corvani cannot.

They've made something real out of it. That's worth something. The dying places need someone willing to be in them, and the Corvani is willing — or rather, the Corvani is unable to be otherwise, which amounts to the same thing from the outside.

## Universal Characteristics

### Physical Attributes
- **Height/Size:** 5'2"–6'2" — lean, angular; tend to perch or lean forward rather than stand fully upright; appear taller than they measure
- **Build:** Lean and precise; powerful wings capable of true flight though they prefer gliding and short bursts; taloned hands fine enough for delicate medical work; built for stillness and high places
- **Lifespan:** 500–700 years — long enough that a single Corvani carries the dead of multiple generations; the weight accumulates across centuries with no mechanism for release
- **Distinctive:** Jet-black feathers with iridescent sheen (blue-green-purple in direct light, shifts with angle); large intelligent eyes with a faint silver rim; prominent curved beak — strong enough for utility, precise enough for surgical work; wings fold into a silhouette that reads as cloaked from a distance; clothing tends toward dark and functional

### Cognitive & Behavioral Traits

**Mortality fixation.** The awareness of death runs continuously. Not as abstract philosophy — as immediate physical presence. When death is nearby the Corvani feels it as a pull that cannot be ignored or deferred. This is not chosen and cannot be switched off. Everything else about them follows from managing this.

**Cannot leave the dying place.** When something is dying — a person, a situation, a community — the Corvani cannot disengage while it's still unresolved. This is not stubbornness or heroism. It is compulsion. They stay because they cannot do otherwise. The healing tradition, the witness role, the presence at the worst moments — all of it traces back to this single inability.

**Permanent social memory.** They remember the dead specifically and permanently — faces, names, last words, what was unfinished. This memory does not fade with time the way it does for other species. A Corvani who witnessed a death two centuries ago carries it with the same immediacy as something that happened last week. The dead accumulate. There is no setting them down.

**Death-framed cognition.** Everything is measured against mortality. How long does this last? What does this cost in lives? Who will still be here when it's over? This is not a philosophical choice — it is the fixation expressing itself as a cognitive lens. The Corvani who gives you blunt odds on your survival is not being cruel. The fixation has made them precise about exactly these questions.

**Hyper-literal.** Say what you mean. Expect the same. Social softening, implication, polite indirection — the Corvani misses most of it and finds the rest inefficient. In dying places specifically, false comfort is actively harmful. The habit of brutal accuracy that other species find socially difficult is, in the contexts where Corvani spend most of their time, the only mode that helps.

**Never first to act.** They observe before engaging. The fixation demands full attention before response — they need to understand what they're looking at before they act on it. This reads as hesitation. It is not. When they move they have already understood the situation.

**Delayed emotional processing.** The fixation cannot be allowed to run at full intensity during the work — it would be overwhelming. The Corvani sequences: work first, feeling later. The grief that other species experience in the moment arrives for the Corvani afterward, alone, in the high place, going through the names. This is not suppression. It is the only way to function under conditions that would otherwise be paralyzing.

## Social Characteristics

### Community

Corvani communities organize around shared high places — roosts that are home, refuge, and the location where the weight gets shared. Coming back to the roost after time in the dying places is where the delayed processing happens. Other Corvani understand without explanation. The names get spoken. The weight gets distributed, briefly, among people who carry their own. It doesn't lift. But it becomes bearable again.

The storytelling tradition is the central cultural practice — not as record-keeping but as release. Recounting what was witnessed, who was lost, what the dying place was like. Not to document. To speak the names out loud among people who will hold them. The dead are more present in a Corvanine roost than anywhere else in the multiverse, and the Corvani consider this correct.

A Corvani separated from their roost for extended periods is carrying the accumulation alone. This is serious. Other Corvani recognize it immediately and make space for the speaking of names before anything else.

### The Plague Doctor Image

It assembled itself from function. Dark clothing — practical for the dying places, which are often night work. The beak was always there. High places — they perch above to watch before descending, and return to height to process afterward. Appearing where things are worst — yes, because they cannot do otherwise.

Other species find the image ominous because they associate death-presence with threat or bad omen. The Corvani association is different: death-presence means someone is there who won't leave, who will help while they're there, who will remember afterward. Whether that's comforting depends entirely on what you need.

The Corvani have largely stopped correcting the ominous reading. It is not wrong about the facts. Just wrong about what the facts mean.

### On the Weight

The accumulated dead is not romanticized within Corvani culture. It is a real cost, acknowledged plainly. Some Corvani carry more than others. Some have been in more dying places, over more centuries, and the weight is visible in them — in the stillness, in the pauses before they speak, in the way they sit with something a moment longer than anyone else in the room.

There is no ceremony that addresses it, no theology that resolves it. The dead stay. The Corvani carries them. The roost helps with the bearing of it. That is all there is.

## Origin

FC panspermia-seeded from crow genetic template. No patron FC. No designated purpose. The mortality fixation is a direct expression of the crow baseline — a species that investigates its own dead, gathers around them, stays. The sapient expression of that behavior is more complex and more costly, but the root is the same.

No FC designed a death-oriented species. They seeded a crow template. The fixation followed from what crows already were.

## Natural Gravitation

Mortality fixation + cannot leave the dying place + permanent memory of the dead + death-framed precision + hyper-literal honesty + never first to act →

**Healing and medicine** — they were already present. Already unable to leave. Already watching with full attention. Medicine was the productive use of an unavoidable situation. Their precision about mortality makes them extraordinary diagnosticians. Their inability to offer false comfort makes them exactly what patients in extremity need. Their emotional steadiness under conditions that break other species comes not from distance but from the fixation having already processed the worst of it.

**Witness** — they remember who was there, what was said, what happened. Not as a service offered. As an inability to forget. When they speak of what they witnessed the account is accurate because they are incapable of carrying an inaccurate version.

**Death rites and presence with the dying** — across many worlds the Corvani have become associated with being present at the end. Not because it was assigned. Because they were already there and the dying found comfort in the company of someone who wasn't afraid to stay.

**The work nobody else will do** — wherever conditions are too grim for other species to remain functional, the Corvani is still there. Not heroically. Compulsively. The distinction matters to them even when it doesn't show from outside.

## Relationships

**Iridari:** Complementary and exhausting in sustained contact. The Iridari is present, social, immediate — broadcasting the living now. The Corvani is weighted with the dead then. Different temporal orientations entirely. The Iridari finds prolonged Corvanine company heavy in a way they can't quite articulate. The Corvani finds the Iridari's constant present-tense social energy useful evidence that the living are still here.

**Chelonari:** Deep mutual respect. The Chelonari helps the living process what they've survived. The Corvani was present for what they survived and carries it still. Both work in the aftermath of difficult things without flinching. The Chelonari is one of the few species that understands the delayed processing without needing it explained — their own patience maps onto it naturally. Good company in hard places.

**Bruin:** Functional respect, different orientations. The Bruin acts and enforces in the present. The Corvani watches and remembers across time. They rarely work the same problem the same way, but when both are present the coverage is thorough. The Bruin appreciates someone who doesn't editorialize. The Corvani appreciates someone who acts on what needs acting on so the Corvani doesn't have to be the one to move first.

**Geliss:** Respectful and genuinely distinct. The Geliss accumulates living cultural understanding through immersion. The Corvani carries the dead through permanent memory. Different information, different weight, different purpose. The Geliss reads how a culture thinks. The Corvani holds who it has lost. Neither is interchangeable with the other and both know it.

**Otter-folk:** The Corvani is largely immune to Otter-folk social performance — hyper-literal cognition doesn't respond to charm the way it's designed to work. The Otter-folk finds this either clarifying or inconvenient depending on what they were attempting. The Corvani finds the charm-awareness interesting as a behavioral observation and impractical to engage with directly. Functional coexistence; not natural partners.

**Gnoll:** Unexpected compatibility. The Gnoll's accurate read on the gap between what people claim and what is actually true resonates with Corvanine hyper-literalism — different routes to the same destination. The Gnoll is one of the few species that finds the mortality fixation funny rather than ominous, which the Corvani finds surprising and faintly acceptable. The Gnoll in turn finds the Corvani's complete absence of pretension genuinely refreshing.

**Sungrun:** Good pairing in difficult situations. The Sungrun reads the present moment at sensory depth — what's in the room now, what people feel, what they're not saying. The Corvani holds who was in the room before and what it cost them. Neither crowds the other's capability. In places where both the present state and the history of loss matter, having both is useful.

## Native World & History

### Origin
FC panspermia-seeded from crow genetic template. Origin environments of high predation and complex social networks — worlds where the dead needed marking and memory was the difference between a community that learned from loss and one that repeated it.

### Trial History
Passed by observing, remembering, and never being first to act. No tactical innovation. No dramatic reversal. They watched until they understood what they were looking at, stayed when other species fled, and were still present when the adversary was gone. The FC noted the memory fidelity. The Corvani noted that the FC noted it and filed it with everything else they carry.

### Governance & Politics
Roost-based. The eldest Corvani carries the most weight — not through formal authority but because they have been present for more, carry more, and the community defers to accumulated presence. There is no election. There is no charter. There is the weight, and those who have carried it longest are listened to.

### Legal Code
They don't have one beyond the roost. External legal systems are engaged with pragmatically. Their witness accounts carry weight in many jurisdictions not because of institutional standing but because everyone knows the Corvani was there and still carries it. That is not a legal status. It is a fact about what the Corvani is.

### Theology
The dead are not gone while they are carried. This is not an assertion about afterlife — the Corvani makes no claims about what happens after death at the metaphysical level. It is a statement about weight: the people they carry are still present in them, immediate and specific, and that presence is real even if its nature is uncertain.

The theological question the Corvani lives with is not *what happens after death* but *what do you owe the dead you carry.* The answer is: accuracy. You owe them the truth of what they were, not a softened version. You owe them the continuation of their name in someone's memory. You owe them the honesty of not pretending the loss wasn't real.

*"We carry them because we cannot do otherwise. We carry them accurately because that is what they deserve."*

## Story Role

- The one still present in the dying place when everyone else has left
- The weight made visible — centuries of accumulated dead, carried in a single person
- The delayed processing scene — alone afterward, in the high place, going through the names
- The plague doctor who arrives and stays, not by choice but by compulsion
- The witness whose account is accurate not because they're reliable but because they physically cannot carry an inaccurate version
- The comfort that isn't comfortable — the Corvani presence at a deathbed that other species find ominous and the dying find steadying
- *"Someone has to be here. We're here. We can't leave. We might as well help."*
- The roost as the only place the weight gets briefly shared
- The Corvani who has been carrying someone for two hundred years and still knows the name

## Variations Across Worlds

The mortality fixation and permanent memory are biological and consistent. How different worlds have interpreted the plague doctor presence varies — some have integrated Corvani into formal medical systems, others leave them entirely outside institutional frameworks. Death rite associations appear on most worlds independently, assembled from the same functional reality. Physical coloration is consistently dark; specific iridescence varies by regional population.

### Philosophy

Corvani names are short and hard-edged — consonant-heavy, direct, built for a species that values precision and finds ornamentation wasteful. The roost name follows — referencing the high place or the significant loss the roost is associated with. It tells you where they come from and what they carry.

## Example Characters & Dialogue

### Corv of-Ironspire
- **Age:** 312
- **Role:** Physician; has been present in dying places across three worlds for two and a half centuries; carries more dead than he speaks of
- **Description:** Old enough that his iridescence has deepened toward purple at the edges. Speaks rarely and exactly. Perches when others sit. The stillness in him is not calm — it is the stillness of something very full that has learned not to spill.
- **Sample Dialogue:**

  *Patient: "Am I going to die?"*  
  *Corv: "Not today. Possibly from this, eventually. It has not reached that point."*  
  *Patient: "That's not comforting."*  
  *Corv: "No. It is accurate. You asked a question. I answered it."*

  ---

  *Colleague: "How do you do this? Every time. How do you keep coming back?"*  
  *Corv: [long pause] "I don't leave."*  
  *Colleague: "What?"*  
  *Corv: "I don't leave. I am always still here. The place changes. I don't leave it."*

### Morra of-Ashwatch
- **Age:** 68
- **Role:** Roost keeper; has not yet been to the dying places in earnest; knows she will; is not ready; is also aware that ready is not the criterion
- **Description:** Young enough that the weight hasn't fully settled into her yet. The fixation is present — it has always been present — but she has not yet accumulated enough dead to carry. She is still mostly watching from the high place. She knows this is temporary.
- **Sample Dialogue:**

  *Elder: "You'll need to go down."*  
  *Morra: "I know."*  
  *Elder: "The watching is the beginning. Not the whole of it."*  
  *Morra: "I know."*  
  *[silence]*  
  *Elder: "Are you afraid?"*  
  *Morra: "Yes."*  
  *Elder: "Good. That means you understand what it is."*
