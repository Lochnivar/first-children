## Definition

Genus Corvus is defined by the crow-derived genetic template from the FC genetic repository. Independent generalist species (like Chelonari, Pachi, Quedari); no designated role. Origin unclear—natural evolution or panspermia-seeded on a world of high predation, shifting seasons, and complex social networks. Survived trial by **observing, remembering, and never being the first to act**.

### Physical
- **Crow-derived** — 5–6 feet tall; jet-black feathers with subtle iridescent sheen (blue-green-purple in light)
- **Wings** — Flight capable; prefer gliding and short bursts
- **Beak** — Prominent, curved, strong; used for precise manipulation, defense, feeding
- **Taloned** — Hands and feet for grasping, climbing, delicate work

### Temperament
- **Hyper-literal** — Say what you mean; expect the same
- **Death-framed** — Measure everything against mortality
- **Pattern-obsessed** — See patterns everywhere; must analyze
- **Brutally honest** — Lying is inefficient and confusing
- **Detail fixation** — Notice and mention everything
- **Social obliviousness** — Miss emotional subtext
- **Delayed emotional processing** — Feel deeply, but later, privately

This is the species norm, not a disorder. Among themselves: perfect clarity. Other species find it strange.
