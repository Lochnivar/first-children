# Tinkari (Gnomes)

**Status:** Framework Element - Universal Race  
**Category:** Races / Species  
**Related:** [Races README](../README.md), [Dwarves](dwarves.md), [../../mechanics/trials.md](../../mechanics/trials.md), [../../FRAMEWORK.md](../../FRAMEWORK.md)

## Overview

Tinkari are First Children purpose-built for operations—Quantum DevOps. Where Dwarves build and Hobgoblins record, Tinkari keep things running. They monitor anchor networks, patch quantum instabilities, maintain transit systems, and program the crawl. Same role everywhere: production support. Mercara's Cross trains, Earth's transformation, theway systems, rift mitigation—if it runs, they run it.

## Framework Role: FC Staff

### Origin
Created by First Children as purpose-built operators for divine infrastructure.

### Role
- Monitor and maintain quantum systems (anchors, theway, crawl parameters)
- Operational support for FC infrastructure
- Transit maintenance (Mercara's Cross trains, gateways, conduits)
- Incident response (quantum instabilities, rift spawns, decay)
- Deploy fixes, rollbacks, patches—"fix it in production"
- Automation and self-healing systems

### Physical Traits
- Smaller than humans (gnome-sized)
- Nimble fingers, sharp eyes
- High metabolism (always moving, always checking)
- Can work in tight spaces (conduits, crawl-spaces, quantum lattices)

### Personality
- Frantic, helpful (highly caffeinated energy)
- Pragmatic over proper ("ship it, we'll patch")
- On-call mentality (something's always about to break)
- Improvisers and adapters (Dwarves build it right; Tinkari make it work)
- Obsessed with automation (fewer 3am pages)
- Friendly but distracted (half-listening to the monitoring feed)
- "It's not a bug, it's a feature—until it's a bug again"

### Culture
- On-call rotations and incident postmortems
- War stories about the time the crawl almost inverted
- Highly caffeinated—or is it that coffee is highly gnomed up? (Tinkari have made caffeine their sacrament; their brews are legendary, their tolerance higher)
- Respect Dwarves (they built it) but don't understand why they won't just hotfix
- Get along with Hobgoblins (they document; Tinkari need the runbooks)

### Story Function
- Operational exposition (how things actually run vs. how they're supposed to)
- Tension (something's degrading, they're racing the clock)
- Humor (chaotic good energy vs. Dwarf rigidity)
- Bridge between "how it was built" and "how it's breaking"

### Example Character
Brixi Quickpatch, Crawl Operations—89 years old. Perpetually has one earpiece in, listening to the Earth crawl metrics. "The Dwarves built it to spec. Spec was wrong. We've been patching it for forty years. It's fine. Probably." Will deploy a fix at 2am without waking anyone if it means avoiding an incident report.

### Example Contexts
- **Mercara's Cross:** Maintaining the trains, scheduling, gate alignment, platform quantum stability
- **Earth:** Programming the crawl—transformation parameters, phase transitions, mortal interface calibration
- **Trial worlds:** Monitoring anchor decay, rift suppression, theway wetware patches

## Universal Characteristics

### Physical Attributes
- **Lifespan:** Long-lived (typically 150-250 years, varies by world)
- **Size:** Smaller than humans (gnome-sized)
- **Build:** Nimble, quick, built for crawling through infrastructure
- **Senses:** Sharp eyes for dashboards and subtle quantum drift

### Cognitive Abilities
- **Pattern recognition:** Spot degradation before it cascades
- **Improvisation:** Fix things with what's available
- **Multitasking:** Monitor six systems while explaining the problem to a Hobgoblin

## Variations Across Worlds

- Operational scope varies (trains vs. crawl vs. anchors)
- Relationship to Dwarves and Hobgoblins may differ by FC
- Some Tinkari specialize (transit vs. quantum vs. theway); others are generalists
