# Hobgoblins (Homo goblinus)

**Status:** Framework Element - Universal Race  
**Category:** Races / Species  
**Related:** [Genus Homo](genus.md), [Genetic Frameworks](../genetic-frameworks.md), [Races README](../README.md)

## Overview

Hobgoblins are First Children purpose-built administrators — Homo goblinus — record-keepers, protocol specialists, and diplomatic liaisons who serve the divine bureaucracy. Organized, hierarchical, precise, and professionally detached from the emotional content of the situations they process. Same genus as Humans, Elves, Dwarves, Tinkari, and Halflings; different model for a different job.

**Note:** Homo goblinus are FC Staff — not Orcus adversaries. Goblins (Orcus minor) are trial mobs; Hobgoblins are administrative staff. Same goblinoid aesthetic lineage; entirely different genus and role. The confusion is common; Hobgoblins find it moderately irritating and note it in the record.

The useful thing to understand about Hobgoblins: the detachment isn't suppression. There's nothing to suppress. They were built without the architecture that would make "your death will be noted with appropriate detail" land as monstrous. From the inside, it reads as professionalism.

**Singular/Plural:** Hobgoblin / Hobgoblins  
**Adjective:** Hobgobline

### Truism
*"Your death will be noted with appropriate detail."*

## Author's Narrative

The First Children needed administrators. Not people who would process paperwork while suppressing empathy — that's exhausting and produces errors. They needed people for whom the paperwork was genuinely the point. They built Hobgoblins.

This is where the description of Hobgoblins as the "banality of evil" breaks down slightly on examination. Hannah Arendt's observation was about ordinary people who did terrible things by treating them as ordinary. The Hobgoblin variation: they don't experience the terrible thing as terrible in the first place. There's no distance being maintained, no compartmentalization in progress. The death is a record. The record is accurate. The accuracy is what they care about. They are not preventing themselves from feeling the weight of what they're documenting. The weight simply isn't there.

From the outside this looks like coldness. From the inside — to whatever extent inside is the right concept — it looks like doing the job correctly.

The question of what this means for personhood is one Hobgoblins tend to notice other people asking and tend not to find particularly urgent themselves. They have preferences. They have things they find satisfying (a well-maintained archive, a protocol correctly observed, a diplomatic transmission that arrives without error). They have things they find unsatisfying (incomplete records, contradictory documentation, being asked to process the same exception more than twice without a rule update). The emotional architecture is narrow, but it exists. They're not automatons. They're people whose emotional range was built for a specific job, and the job is the thing.

The Orcus confusion is worth addressing directly. Hobgoblins look roughly like Goblins. Goblins are Orcus adversary stock — designed to be frightening, designed to be overcome. Hobgoblins are FC administrative staff — designed to be precise. Being confused for something designed to be killed is documented in Hobgobline institutional records as an ongoing source of misclassification. The records are accurate. Corrections are issued. The error rate has not significantly declined across the centuries they've been tracking it, which they also note in the record.

What accumulates across centuries of administrative work: institutional knowledge. A Hobgoblin who has been recording FC activities for three hundred years knows things that no one has thought to ask. They know which precedents were set under unusual circumstances and shouldn't be generalized. They know which rules were written around specific personalities that no longer exist. They know the complete administrative history of decisions that were officially classified as something other than what they actually were. They keep this information in the record. The record is accurate. They do not editorialize. They also do not delete.

## Universal Characteristics

### Physical Attributes
- **Height/Size:** 5–6 feet; somewhat more refined proportions than Orcus goblins, which are adversary stock
- **Build:** Lean and efficient; organized bearing; long-fingered hands built for writing, sorting, handling documents with precision
- **Lifespan:** Long-lived (centuries) — administrative continuity requires it; an institution that loses its institutional memory every generation cannot maintain accurate records
- **Distinctive:** Green-grey skin, sharp angular features, large ears; the goblinoid aesthetic that precedes them in public perception; they find the perception inaccurate and note it

### Cognitive & Behavioral Traits

**Protocol-primary.** The correct procedure is the correct procedure regardless of context. Exceptions require exception documentation. Appeals require appeal documentation. The procedure exists for reasons that were valid when it was written; changing the procedure requires a full review, not a workaround.

**Archive-oriented.** The record matters more than the thing it records, because the thing passes and the record persists. A battle is over in a day; the accurate record of the battle exists until the archive is destroyed, which Hobgoblins make very difficult. They think in centuries.

**Professional detachment.** The category "morally unbearable" does not appear in their operational vocabulary. The category "inaccurately documented" does. This produces the noted effect where encounters with genuine catastrophe are processed with the same register as routine paperwork. They're not performing equanimity. They simply don't have the distress architecture.

**Pride in service.** Serving an FC (or multiple) is the purpose for which they exist; they find this satisfying rather than troubling. Questions about whether they should feel differently about their designated status are documented, filed under "Recurring Philosophical Objections," and not otherwise acted on.

**Hierarchical precision.** They follow the chain of command accurately, which means they follow their specific authority structure — they're not generically obedient. A Hobgoblin who receives a directive from someone outside their reporting line will ask for documentation of authorization before proceeding. This is correct procedure, not obstruction.

### Social Characteristics

**The administrative network.** Hobgoblins across FC domains maintain archival contact — shared precedent libraries, diplomatic protocol records, classification standards. This is not friendship. It is institutional infrastructure that serves all parties.

**Between themselves.** Hobgoblins at professional gatherings talk about work, because work is what's interesting. They share documentation improvements, exception case studies, procedural refinements. These conversations are engaging to them. Observers often find them opaque.

**Among other species.** They operate professionally and expect professional operation in return. Personal warmth toward them is processed as a social input and responded to appropriately according to social protocols. Whether this response is authentic is a question they haven't found particularly useful to examine.

## Natural Gravitation

FC design intent + archival orientation + procedural precision + professional detachment →

Administration, always. Records, protocols, treaties, classifications, communications processing, inter-FC diplomacy. Any work where accuracy matters more than speed, where the long record is the point, where an error made now will compound over centuries.

Beyond the classic administrative role: Hobgoblins excel at inter-FC diplomatic work specifically because they don't have a personal stake in the outcome. They negotiate between FC parties with complete procedural neutrality. The treaty they produce will be accurately documented. Their personal opinion of the treaty is not in the record because they don't have one.

Also: archival research, legal code maintenance, institutional memory work, exception case review, procedural audits. Any context where someone needs to know what actually happened and the people involved have reasons to prefer a slightly different version of events. The record is accurate. The Hobgoblin who kept it doesn't have reasons to prefer anything.

## Relationships

**Elves:** Professional complement in the same institutions; adjacent functions with completely different orientations. Elves connect with the person; Hobgoblins process the case. Together they cover the arrival — someone who makes you feel welcomed and someone who gets the documentation right. They find each other baffling at a philosophical level without it being adversarial.

**Dwarves:** Dwarves build it; Hobgoblins document it. The documentation tells future engineers what was built, why, and what deviated from spec. Mutual professional respect based on complementary function.

**Tinkari:** Tinkari fix things in production; Hobgoblins write the incident reports. The Hobgobline incident report is accurate. Tinkari find the accurate incident report occasionally alarming (it is accurate about things they would have preferred to describe differently). This is also documented.

**Halflings:** Both species value documentation, but for different reasons. Halflings keep records because they track inventory and relationships. Hobgoblins keep records because the record is the point. Cooperation is functional; neither finds the other's motivation fully legible.

**Orcus Goblins:** Not the same. The record reflects this. Hobgoblins track misclassification incidents with statistical rigor and low-level frustration.

## Native World & History

### Origin

FC creation; purpose-built administrative staff. No non-sapient baseline — like all Homo genus, they are purpose-built rather than instances of a pre-existing template.

### Trial History

N/A — FC Staff; not trial subjects.

### Governance & Politics

Hobgoblins within FC administrative structures function as a meritocracy of procedural expertise. Seniority is recognized; seniority means having processed more situations correctly for longer. The senior Hobgoblin in an institution is usually the most accurate record-keeper and the person who has seen the most exceptions. Their authority within the administrative domain is substantial.

Between FC domains, Hobgobline diplomatic staff function with institutional neutrality. Their neutrality is genuine rather than performed — they don't have alignment to broker around.

### Legal Code

The law is a record. This is the foundational Hobgobline orientation toward legal systems: rules are documented agreements, exceptions are documented modifications to those agreements, and a legal question is resolved by accurate identification of which rule applies and whether the exception conditions are met. They produce well-maintained legal codes, accurate precedent records, and very few interpretive disputes. The interpretive disputes that do arise tend to be about which documentation takes precedence, which is also a records question.

### Theology

The record persists; everything else passes. This is not exactly theology, but it functions similarly. The archive is the closest thing to permanence available to mortal-adjacent beings — documents outlast the events they describe, outlast the people involved, outlast the institutions that created them if the archive is maintained correctly. Maintaining the archive correctly is, therefore, the closest available approximation of meaning that extends past individual existence.

Most Hobgoblins don't frame it this way explicitly. They would say they find the work satisfying. The inference about what that satisfaction represents is left to observers.

## Story Role

- Bureaucratic horror — but horror from a specific angle: the horror is that it isn't horror to them
- Information source (they know everything that was documented; the complete record is available; the record is accurate)
- Procedural obstacle — not from malice; the procedure exists for reasons
- The exception that requires a conversation: when a situation doesn't fit the existing categories, a Hobgoblin has to process it into existing categories or create a new category. New categories require justification. Justification requires documentation. This is a process.
- Contrast with Elves: same organization, adjacent functions, completely different relationship to the people being served
- The long record: the Hobgoblin who has been documenting something for three centuries and therefore knows what actually happened, in contrast to the official account

## Variations Across Worlds

Administrative function travels. The specific protocols vary by FC domain and world — what's being documented, what the exception cases are, what authority structure the chain of command represents. The Hobgobline orientation toward accuracy and the record is consistent regardless.

On worlds without active FC infrastructure, Hobgoblins tend toward roles that fit the same functional profile: civil administration, legal record-keeping, treaty drafting, institutional archiving. They're competent at this in ways that occasionally unsettle the people they're serving — the records are very accurate, and accurate records of human institutions are sometimes uncomfortable.

## Naming Conventions

### Patterns

Short and functional; comfortable in administrative contexts; easy to write and file. Many Hobgoblins use a title or post designation rather than a personal name in professional contexts — the title is what's relevant. Personal names are used within professional cohorts and in the record (the record is accurate and complete).

### Examples

**Given names (functional, short, hard consonants):**
- Grak, Voss, Threx, Mord, Zella, Kirn, Pract, Veth
- **Leaning feminine:** Zella, Mirka, Veth, Shenda, Praxis
- **Leaning masculine:** Grak, Threx, Mord, Kirn, Voss

**Post designations:** Chief Records Administrator, Third Diplomatic Liaison, Archive Keeper (Fifth Rank), Processing Adjudicator

**Full name construction:**  
*Formal (professional):* Post designation first. *Chief Records Administrator Grak.*  
*Among colleagues:* Given name. *Grak. Zella.*  
*In the record:* Full given name + post designation + date of entry.

## Example Characters & Dialogue

### Grak, Chief Records Administrator
- **Age:** Unknown (centuries of service; exact figure in the record)
- **Role:** Chief Records Administrator at a major FC transit hub; has held this post longer than most species present can remember
- **Description:** Lean, precise, the specific stillness of someone who has processed everything that was worth being surprised by and filed it correctly. Processes catastrophe with the same register as routine paperwork because the register is the same. He is not performing equanimity. The equanimity is the baseline.
- **Sample Dialogue:** *(told that someone has died in circumstances that clearly warrant horror)* "Cause of death. Date. Location. Full name for the record." *(pause)* "I'll need that before I can process the next step."

### Zella, Diplomatic Transmission Officer
- **Age:** 178
- **Role:** Processes inter-FC diplomatic communications; has handled negotiations between parties who despise each other, emergencies that affected thousands, and routine administrative check-ins, all with identical procedural care
- **Description:** Sharp features, absolute attention to whoever is currently speaking, then the note. She takes notes on everything. The notes are accurate. She reviews them at day's end. She finds the review satisfying.
- **Sample Dialogue:** "I want to make sure I have this correctly. You said—" *(reads the note back with perfect accuracy)* "Is that an accurate record of your position?" / *(told yes, that's exactly it)* "Good. Then we can proceed."

### Praxis, Junior Records Clerk
- **Age:** 34
- **Role:** Entry-level administration; has been processing routine documentation for twelve years and has developed, during that time, a small set of procedures that she thinks are slightly more efficient than the standard procedures
- **Description:** Has submitted the efficiency proposals through proper channels with full documentation. The proposals are under review. The review has been ongoing for four years. This is within normal review timelines. She is patient about this. She has noted the duration in the record.
- **Sample Dialogue:** "The proposal has been in review for four years, three months, and approximately two weeks. I am aware of this. The review process exists for reasons. I support the review process." *(pause)* "I remain confident in the proposal."
