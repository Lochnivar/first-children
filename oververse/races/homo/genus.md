**Status:** Framework Element - Universal  
**Category:** Races / Genus Definition  
**Related:** [Genetic Frameworks](../genetic-frameworks.md), [../README.md](../README.md)

## Definition

Genus Homo is defined by the bipedal humanoid genetic template. Unlike other genera, Homo has no non-sapient baseline in the repository — FC staff species are purpose-built job models, not instances of a shared repository template. All Homo expressions share the same bipedal humanoid baseline; each is a specialist variant on that baseline for a specific function.

The one exception: Homo sapiens (humans) are the generalist expression. Where every other Homo species was built optimized, humans were built unoptimized — which is itself a design choice, for a specific purpose.

## Shared Genus Traits

### Physical
- **Bipedal humanoid** — Standard two-legged, two-armed form; the baseline the other expressions diverge from
- **Variable lifespan** — Model-dependent: humans moderate (70–90 years); elves extended (centuries to low thousands); others in between
- **HAI range** — 7–10; all Homo expressions read as human or near-human at distance; none carry obvious non-human animal markers except Homo bovinus (bovine-derived; HAI 6, visibly non-human)
- **ConfAlpha D–E** — FC design intent placed all Homo staff toward the humanoid-equivalent end; social compatibility in human-adjacent operational environments was a design specification

### Temperament (Role-Defined)
Each Homo species was built for a specific function; the temperament was built for the function. The variation between species is dramatic because the functions are different. What they share: professional orientation toward their designated role; recognition of the chain of command; awareness that they are FC staff (except humans, who are not aware of this).

### Framework
- **Non-sapient baseline:** None — FC purpose-built; not derived from a pre-existing repo baseline
- **No trial:** FC Staff species are not trial subjects; they are the staff who run trials

## The Generalist Problem

Across the genetic repository, generalist variants tend to fail trials. Specialists survive because trials are designed for specific conditions; a species optimized for those conditions passes. A generalist — adequate at many things, exceptional at none — hits the specific threshold the trial demands and stops.

Homo sapiens is the latest generalist attempt. Previous sapiens deployments have failed. The Earth trial is active. FC keeps attempting the generalist model because the outlier success category — when a generalist produces a response that no specialist would have arrived at — is occasionally extraordinary. The failure rate doesn't change the value of the outlier.

The other Homo expressions are the specialists. Each one was built to solve a specific part of what generalist humans can't reliably do: communication, administration, engineering, operations, logistics, security. Taken as a system, the Homo genus covers what sapiens can't cover alone. What sapiens provides is the adaptation layer — the thing that happens when the situation isn't covered.

## Species Interactions

**Professional family.** Same genus = family; different models = different jobs. The shared baseline is visible in physical similarity (all Homo expressions read as humanoid; several are visually nearly identical to humans). The functional differences are significant.

**Humans and the rest.** Humans on Earth are managed by the other Homo species without knowing it. Elves at integration desks, Halflings running commissaries, Hobgoblins processing administrative cases, Tinkari maintaining the crawl, Dwarves building infrastructure, Bovinus standing security — all of this happens around and for and adjacent to humans who have no idea what they're part of. The other Homo expressions know. This asymmetry is structurally significant.

**Intra-Homo professional relationships:**
- **Dwarves and Tinkari:** Built it vs. keeps it running; philosophical difference that produces regular friction and necessary collaboration
- **Elves and Hobgoblins:** Connection vs. documentation; adjacent functions in the same systems; mutual incomprehension
- **Halflings and Tinkari:** Supply and demand; Halflings keep the coffee stocked; this is a real service
- **Hobgoblins and Dwarves:** Document it and build it; professional tolerance that deepens when the records are accurate
- **Bovinus and everyone:** Large, quiet, specific; the other species have learned to read the signals; most find them more approachable than they expect

## Why People

The question that applies to the entire genus: these are purpose-built FC staff species. Does that make them people?

The position the framework takes: yes, but in different ways. Elves were built to care and care. Hobgoblins were built for detachment and are detached. Dwarves were built to value craft and the craft is genuinely their identity. Tinkari were built for operational awareness and are genuinely aware. Halflings were built to provision and genuinely want to know if you've eaten. Bovinus were built for the line and hold the line for real reasons.

The design is the starting point, not the limit. What each species does with their design — how they live inside the function, what they make of it across centuries — is their own. The function doesn't define the personhood; it shapes the context in which personhood develops.

Humans are the exception: they don't know their function, which means they can't be defined by it. Whether this produces more authentic personhood or just more confusion is the question the trial is intended to answer.
