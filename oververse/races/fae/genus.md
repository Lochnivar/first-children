# Genus Fae

**Status:** Framework Element — Classification Incomplete / Disputed  
**Category:** Races / Genus Definition  
**Related:** [Genetic Frameworks](../genetic-frameworks.md), [../README.md](../README.md)

## Definition

"Fae" is a placeholder genus name. FC cannot classify these beings through standard taxonomic methods — no genetic repository ancestry can be traced, no non-sapient baseline exists on record, no creator is on file. The name is borrowed from the oldest documented word for things that don't fit the system.

That the genus name is itself a shrug is, taxonomically speaking, unusual enough to note.

---

### Physical (general tendencies — inconsistent across species)

- Tend toward the aesthetically extreme — very beautiful or very strange; rarely unremarkable
- Quantum-adjacent without being quantumancers; reality responds differently to them than to repo-derived species in ways that resist measurement
- Physical characteristics resist standardization; no shared baseline template is apparent across species

### Temperament (general tendencies — inconsistent across species)

- Associated with liminal states, edges, and transitions — physical, temporal, conceptual
- FC find them unsettling in a specific way they struggle to articulate; current working theory is that fae proximity to the substrate produces something that resembles looking at a reflection and seeing it look back

### Framework

- **Non-sapient baseline:** Unknown / None on record
- **Sapient expressions:** [Ilvari](ilvari.md), [Lumari](lumari.md), [Margari](margari.md) *(all stubs)*

## Authorial Note (Framework Level)

The cosmological premise is that the Prime Author — absent from conscious interaction — is the substrate the universe operates within. Fae may be substrate-direct expressions: not made by FC or PA intentionally, but arising from the medium itself. This is not in-universe consensus. FC have no theoretical framework for this interpretation and would likely reject it as untestable. It is the authorial truth underlying the undefined-origin classification.

The distinction from other species: every other race is an *anchored pattern* shaped by FC or PA deliberate action. Fae may simply be what the substrate does when left entirely to itself.
