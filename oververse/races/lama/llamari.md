## Overview

Llamari (*Lama sapiens*) are llama-based anthropomorphic people created through FC panspermia. Their defining trait is hypervigilance as competence — the anxiety is not a flaw in the system, it is the system. Prey animal instincts elevated by sapience into a cognitive mode that scans, checks, rechecks, and prepares for every failure mode simultaneously. The ones who checked survived. The ones who didn't, didn't. Several hundred generations later, the checking is load-bearing.

They are exhausting to be around. They are also the most prepared entity in any room they enter.

### Truism
*"There's always time for one last check."*

## Author's Narrative

Picture a Llamari girl in full Carnival costume — bright dyed wool, intricate patterns she wove herself, baton in hand — stepping into formation with forty others. Watch her perform.

This is the only time she is graceful. Not despite the anxiety. Because of it. The hypervigilance that makes her difficult to be around in conversation — the checking, the spiral, the *what if what if what if* — is doing exactly what it always does. Scanning. Correcting. Adjusting in real time. In this context, pointed at the formation, calibrated against forty other bodies doing the same thing, it produces something extraordinary.

She is not escaping who she is. She is the most fully herself she will ever be.

The rest of the time she is carrying cargo manifests, checking route waypoints, rechecking the cargo manifests, asking if anyone checked the cargo manifests, and doing the job with a thoroughness that borders on compulsive and results in a flawless delivery record.

This is also who she is. The grace and the anxiety are the same engine. The baton and the manifest are the same instinct. She would not trade either.

## Universal Characteristics

### Physical Attributes
- **Height/Size:** 5–6 feet tall; stocky, sure-footed
- **Build:** Thick woolly coats — their own wool, often dyed and woven into elaborate textiles; split hooves, perfect balance; low center of gravity on difficult terrain
- **Lifespan:** Moderate to long (varies by world)
- **Distinctive:** Large lung capacity — efficient oxygen usage at altitude and under sustained exertion

### Cognitive & Behavioral Traits
- **Hypervigilant:** Constant environmental scanning — not paranoia, biological inheritance. One mistake was death. This is the cognitive mode of survivors.
- **Worry spiral:** What if, what if, what if. Every failure mode catalogued before the first step is taken. Exhausting for observers. Extraordinarily useful in practice.
- **Compulsive checking:** Things must be verified. Then verified again. Verbal processing is common — the anxiety thinks out loud.
- **Competent because of it, not despite it:** The anxiety IS the preparation. A relaxed Llamari would miss things. The checking is the methodology.
- **Curl of a different kind:** When the spiral becomes unmanageable, the outlet is communal — not withdrawal but performance. The anxiety needs somewhere productive to go.

### Social Characteristics
- **Community:** Close-knit; communal care and mutual checking. A community of Llamari is a community of people monitoring each other, which produces remarkable collective awareness.
- **Carnival:** Impromptu coordinated performance — music, synchronized movement, baton work, pageantry. Not scheduled; deployed when needed. When community anxiety levels have been running high, someone starts, and within minutes there are forty Llamari in formation. Visitors who stumble across it think they've found something legendary. The Llamari think they're taking the edge off. Both are correct.
- **Workers by day, performers by season:** Most Llamari maintain regular occupations. Carnival is maintenance, not career. The few who perform professionally are outliers.
- **Textile tradition:** Wool — their own, shed and gathered — is woven into elaborate patterns. The weaving requires the same constant checking as everything else they do. The textiles are extraordinary.
- **The alternative:** The Carnival is not the only pressure valve available. It is simply the one that works. Most Llamari choose the Carnival.

## Natural Gravitation

Hypervigilant + sure-footed + efficient endurance → anything requiring sustained vigilance and thorough preparation: logistics, transport, route-planning, cargo management, quality inspection, anything where missing a detail has consequences. Not designated — the trait deploys wherever checking matters.

The anxiety is not a liability in these roles. It is the qualification.

## Relationships

- **Djemari:** The natural long-route pairing. Phlegmatic calm meets perpetual anxiety across hundreds of miles of difficult terrain. A seasoned Djemari learns quickly that giving the Llamari a finite, specific task to check — the cargo manifest, the waypoints, the load balance — channels the anxiety into something productive and calms the spiral considerably. The Djemari does not do this out of kindness. They do it because a calm Llamari is easier to travel with. The Llamari does not mind the motive. The checking needed doing anyway.
- **Chelonari:** Patient in completely different ways. Llamari check everything because something might go wrong. Chelonari wait because everything will resolve. Occasional friction; mutual respect for thoroughness.
- **Kethi:** Anxious in completely different ways. Kethi anxiety is quantum and existential. Llamari anxiety is logistical and immediate. They recognize something in each other but it does not make them easy companions.

## Variations Across Worlds

- Carnival traditions vary in form — baton work, synchronized music, coordinated pageantry, marching formations — but the impromptu communal pressure-release function is consistent across all recorded populations
- Textile patterns and dyeing traditions vary by world and community
- The Djemari pairing on long routes appears on every world where both species are present
- Solo performance exists but is rare and considered somewhat unusual — the group is the instrument

## Naming Conventions

### System
Given name + family lineage name. Two to three syllables each. Warm open vowels, soft consonants — the names carry something of the Carnival phonetics; they sound well when called across a formation.

Gendered pools; family names follow lineage.

### Given Names

**Female:** Kaya, Sisa, Tika, Mika, Pawi, Kori, Nima, Chaska, Wayra, Suma

**Male:** Tupak, Rimak, Waman, Sinchi, Wayno, Kunti, Yupan, Kachi, Pallqa, Rikra

### Family Names
Sumaqa, Wayracha, Pachari, Tinkuy, Rimachi, Pallqari

### Full Name Examples
- Kaya Sumaqa, Sisa Tinkuy
- Tupak Pachari, Rimak Wayracha

## Example Characters & Dialogue

### Kaya Sumaqa
**Age:** 34  
**Role:** Cargo route specialist

Junior on a long-haul route. Has checked the manifest four times since departure. Is currently asking whether anyone checked the manifest. Is also the reason the last three deliveries arrived without a single item misplaced, a record her senior partners have not managed to match.

**Sample Dialogue:**
> "The delivery — yes, okay, I can do this. Which route? You said the main route, right? Assuming main route. Did we check the cargo? We should check the cargo. What if something shifted? — *yes I know I checked it already*, what if it shifted *since then* — okay. Okay. We're fine. Are we fine? We're fine."

*(She delivers perfectly. She always delivers perfectly. She has not yet noticed the correlation.)*

### Tupak Rimachi
**Age:** 180  
**Role:** Senior logistics coordinator; Carnival drum lead

Old enough to have made peace with the spiral — not ended it, made peace with it. Knows which worries are load-bearing and which are noise. Still checks everything. Has simply gotten faster at the check.

Leads the drum line at every Carnival his community holds. Has been doing it for ninety years. The younger Llamari time themselves against him.

**Sample Dialogue:** *(to a junior who is spiraling)* "Write it down. All of it. Every what-if. Now check each one. How many actually need action?" *(pause)* "The rest can wait. Let's move."

*He does not tell them the anxiety will go away. It won't. He tells them what to do with it.*
