# Quedari (Manis sapiens)

**Status:** Framework Element - Universal Race  
**Category:** Races / Species  
**Related:** [Genus Manis](genus.md), [Races README](../README.md)

## Overview

Quedari (*Manis sapiens*) are pangolin-based anthropomorphic people created through FC panspermia. Their defining trait is patience as philosophy — not as a virtue they practice, but as a settled conviction about how the world works. Time resolves most problems. Sufficient is optimal. Everything else is wasted effort.

They passed their trial by curling into armored spheres and waiting. 847 years. FC verdict: "Boring — do not replicate."

The Quedari did not dispute this assessment. They were already doing something else.

### Truism
*"Everything passes. Wait long enough."*

## Author's Narrative

There is a door in the craftsman's district with no sign on it. There has never been a sign on it. The Quedari who works behind it knows where it is. That has always been sufficient.

The sign nailed to the wall beside it was put there by someone else — an innkeeper, a town elder, someone who got tired of giving directions three times a day. The Quedari walked past it on the way in, registered that it existed, and went back to the watchwork. The sign remains. This also has never required comment.

This is the Quedari in full: present, competent, indifferent to your need to locate them. They will do the work. The work will be exceptional. When it is done and you have paid, the conversation is over.

They are not rude. Rudeness requires investment in how you are perceived. They simply do not maintain social performances that cost effort and return nothing they need.

## Universal Characteristics

### Physical Attributes
- **Height/Size:** 4–5 feet tall; stocky, low center of gravity
- **Build:** Chitinous plates (brown/gray); optimized for defensive curling; built for stillness not speed
- **Lifespan:** 400–600 years
- **Tongue:** Long, prehensile, extraordinarily sensitive — the primary precision tool; used for fine manipulation, fitting, testing joins by touch. More useful for craft than for speech.
- **Distinctive:** The defensive curl — tuck limbs, head, tail into a complete armored sphere. Shed plates periodically; the raw material of their trade.

### Cognitive & Behavioral Traits
- **Patient:** Not as discipline — as worldview. Time is the most powerful force available. Most problems resolve themselves if you wait long enough.
- **Deliberate:** Slow movement, minimal speech, no wasted motion. Economy of effort in everything.
- **Curl when uncertain; uncurl when certain:** Behavioral tell as reliable as a clock.
- **Sufficient is optimal:** Once needs are met, continued effort is waste. This applies to income, conversation, and ambition equally.
- **No performance:** They do not maintain social signals for an audience's benefit. Not cold — simply not expending effort that returns nothing they require.

### Social Characteristics
- **Community:** Loose collective; minimal governance; no hierarchy. Disputes resolved by waiting — either the issue resolves or it stops mattering.
- **Family:** Present and fed. Ambition on behalf of family is no more compelling than personal ambition. Enough is enough.
- **Signage:** Quedari do not mark their workspaces. You know where they are or you don't. Both outcomes are acceptable. Signs that appear on their behalf were put there by someone else entirely.
- **The gender question:** Matronymic and patronymic naming conventions exist because the species they do business with insisted on being able to tell. The Quedari adopted the minimum viable solution and stopped thinking about it.

## Natural Gravitation

The tongue is precise. The patience is extreme. Everything else about them militates against combat, heavy labor, and extended speech. Fine work is what remains — and fine work pays enough to fund the preferred state of not working.

Jewelers, watchmakers, engravers, armor-smiths, surgeons, instrument-makers, calligraphers — any occupation where patience and fine manipulation are the primary requirements. The work is exceptional not from craft pride but from the logic of sufficiency: exceptional work commands a price that requires fewer commissions to meet the same need.

They do not over-deliver. The work will be exactly as good as the fee justifies. Then it will be done.

## Relationships

- **Chelonari:** Also patient, also shelled. Different orientation — Chelonari help others; Quedari help themselves. Comfortable silence between them. Neither requires the other to explain.
- **Hobgoblins:** Understand procedures; minimal conversation required; compatible for business. Responsible for most of the administrative conventions the Quedari have adopted, including the naming system.
- **Kethi:** Too loud, too anxious. Avoid when possible.
- **Humans:** Active, curious, exhausting. Pay fair prices without excessive haggling. Tolerable.

## Native World & History

### Origin
FC panspermia-seeded from pangolin genetic template.

### Trial History
- **Strategy:** Survived rifts, predators, scarcity, and conflict by curling and waiting
- **Duration:** 847+ years
- **Repository:** Active; approved but deprioritized
- **FC Verdict:** "Boring — do not replicate"

The Quedari did not pass despite their strategy. They passed because of it. The FC's failure to appreciate this is the FC's problem.

### Governance & Politics
Loose collective. No leaders, no hierarchy. Decisions emerge by waiting until consensus appears or the issue becomes irrelevant. No known case of the second outcome being considered a failure.

### Legal Code
Dispute resolution: wait. If both parties wait long enough, the dispute resolves or stops mattering. No known formalized legal tradition beyond this.

### Theology
*To be developed.*

## Variations Across Worlds

- Population size and settlement patterns vary; they favor quiet locations
- Fine craft specialization varies by world's needs — a world without mechanical timekeeping produces engravers and jewelers; a world with it produces watchmakers and instrument-builders
- The philosophy is consistent across all recorded populations
- The no-sign convention appears universal; local communities have independently solved for it in various ways

## Naming Conventions

### System
Single syllable given names. As few characters as necessary.

**Males** use the father's given name as a second name (patronymic).  
**Females** use the mother's given name as a second name (matronymic).

Since given names are drawn from a shared pool with no gender markers, the full name does not signal gender to outside observers. This is not an oversight. The Quedari do not maintain public gender signals. If you need to know, you may ask. You will receive a one-word answer.

The naming convention exists because the species the Quedari do business with required it. The Quedari adopted the minimum viable solution.

### Given Name Pool (shared)
Vel, Fen, Mor, Bel, Tor, Wal, Nen, Del, Kor, Ros, Wul, Mer, Dov, Tin, Gol, Nas, Bos, Rul, Sig, Far

### Full Name Examples
- Vel Fen (Vel, son of Fen)
- Mor Bel (Mor, daughter of Bel)
- Tor Wal, Del Sig, Kor Nas

## Example Characters & Dialogue

### Vel Fen
**Age:** 210  
**Role:** Watchmaker

Third generation in the same workspace. The sign on the door was installed by the previous town council. Vel has never acknowledged it. Has a waiting list of eighteen months and takes new commissions at his discretion. Has declined commissions from three sitting heads of state, not as a statement — it simply wasn't a convenient time.

**Sample Dialogue:** *(without looking up)* "Eighteen months. Half up front. Leave it there."

*If you ask about the sign he will not answer. Not because he objects to the question. He simply has nothing to say about it.*

### Ros Tor
**Age:** 340  
**Role:** Armor-smith

Works exclusively in her own shed scales. Each piece requires two to four years depending on the commission. Has never advertised. Has never needed to. Is currently three commissions behind because she took six months off when the work felt sufficient for the year.

**Sample Dialogue:** "It will be done when it is done. You will know because I will send for you."

*She will send for you. The piece will fit perfectly. You will attempt to compliment her on it. She will wait for you to finish, then tell you the balance due.*
