## Definition

Genus Equus is defined by the horse-derived genetic template from the FC genetic repository. The non-sapient baseline is the horse in its many forms: wide-ranging herd animal, migratory grazer, pattern-memorizing navigator of enormous territories. The horse survives through herd cohesion, spatial memory, and the kind of environmental reading that tells a herd when the range ahead is good before the herd reaches it.

The FC took that spatial memory — the horse's composite pattern recognition of terrain, seasons, resource distribution, and herd movement — and made it the foundation of a sapient species.

The result is a people who know where things should go before the transaction is proposed.

### Physical (general tendencies)

- **Equine-derived, bipedal** — 5.8 to 7 feet tall; proportioned for endurance rather than power; built to cover ground
- **Head:** Clearly equid — elongated, mobile, expressive; large eyes positioned for wide field of view; prominent ears that rotate and signal mood
- **Mane:** Present in both sexes; runs from crown to mid-back; color and texture vary significantly by individual; considered expressive of identity in most Equan cultures
- **Tail:** Full equine tail; involuntary emotional signaling (position and movement) that the Equan cannot fully suppress and have largely stopped trying to
- **Hooves:** Feet end in solid hooves; digitigrade stance; the movement is quiet and characteristic — you learn the sound of an Equan walking before you learn their voice
- **Coat:** Short body coat over the torso and limbs; coloration follows horse patterns — solid, dappled, marked, patterned; individual variation significant
- **Lifespan:** 80–120 years

### Temperament (general tendencies)

- **Herd-oriented** — social structure organized around small cohesive groups with clear membership; the herd is the primary unit of identity, safety, and decision-making
- **Pattern memory** — spatial and temporal pattern recognition above baseline sapient capacity; they remember routes, resource cycles, and distribution patterns across long periods
- **Range-seeking** — the pull toward movement is biological before it is cultural; settled life is learned behavior, not a default
- **Emotionally outward** — emotional states legible at range; the mane and tail are involuntary signals; concealment is learned and effortful
- **Circulation instinct** — a felt discomfort when resources are static; things should be moving; surplus should be going somewhere

### Framework

- **Non-sapient baseline:** Horse (*Equus ferus* and domesticated descendants); herd animal, wide-range migrant, pattern-navigating grazer with strong spatial memory and herd cohesion
- **Sapient expression:** [Equan](equan.md)
