## Definition

Genus Cervus is defined by the deer-derived genetic template from the FC genetic repository. Created as an ecological sensitivity framework — a species tuned to read the health of living systems at a biological level no instrument currently replicates.

The non-sapient baseline is the deer in its many forms: herd animal, forest browser, seasonal migrant, prey species that survives through environmental awareness rather than strength. The FC took that environmental awareness — the deer's composite reading of pressure, chemistry, vibration, humidity, every signal the landscape broadcasts — and made it the foundation of a sapient species.

The result is a people who know what a landscape needs before the landscape can say so.

### Physical (general tendencies)

- **Cervid-derived, bipedal** — 5.5 to 7 feet tall; digitigrade legs ending in cloven hooves; torso and arms proportioned for fine manual work
- **Head:** Clearly cervid — softened muzzle (modified for sapient vocalization; present but mobile, not flat); large lateral eyes; prominent independently mobile ears
- **Antlers:** Both sexes; females carry smaller, simpler forms year-round; males carry larger branching structures shed and regrown annually; the velvet growth phase is a period of heightened ecological sensitivity
- **Coat:** Dappled or spotted; seasonal variation common; fine and short in warm months, denser in cold
- **Lifespan:** 120–180 years

### Temperament (general tendencies)

- **Ecologically attuned** — receive continuous biological signal from their environment; a stressed ecosystem registers as discomfort; a healthy one as ease
- **Tending instinct** — the discomfort of ecological wrongness creates a compulsion to respond; they tend, garden, restore. Not from philosophy — from felt necessity
- **Quiet observers** — the same sensory orientation that reads landscapes reads rooms; they notice more than they announce
- **Seasonal awareness** — internal rhythms track ecological cycles; the antler shedding and regrowth cycle is personally and culturally significant

### Framework

- **Non-sapient baseline:** Deer (Cervidae — multiple species); prey species, forest browser, herd/family social structure, extraordinary environmental sensitivity
- **Sapient expression:** [Tzvari](tzvari.md)
