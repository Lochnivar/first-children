# Stratara

**Status:** Framework-Level Summary - Proposed World  
**Category:** Contested/Collaborative Creation  
**Related:** [World Creation](../mechanics/world-creation.md), [First Children](../cosmology/first-children.md), [../FRAMEWORK.md](../../FRAMEWORK.md)

## Overview

Stratara is a proposed world that demonstrates **collaborative world creation** and how such collaborations can spiral into conflicts over ownership and control. The world functions as a cosmic "game" among First Children siblings, highlighting framework concepts of peer judgment, contested creations, and the moralistic gray areas of First Children interactions.

## Framework Elements Demonstrated

### Collaborative Creation
- **Multiple First Children:** 4-6 First Children siblings collaborated to create Stratara jointly
- **Ownership Questions:** The collaboration raises questions of "whose world is it?"—demonstrating how collaborative creations can become contested
- **Conflict Escalation:** What began as an amicable experiment in "controlled imbalance" devolved into a conquest game

### Peer Judgment
- **External Observation:** Other First Children (not involved) observe from afar, debating if this "game" violates moralistic rules
- **Moralistic Gray Areas:** Some argue it's fine if balanced and educational, while others see it as immature sibling squabbling
- **Potential Liberation:** If things get too destructive, it could trigger a liberation operation—external First Child might sneak in souls from another world to tip the scales

### Quantum Foundation
- **Quantum Substrate:** The world's "rules" are baked into its quantum substrate
- **Territories as Energy Patterns:** Territories aren't just land; they're coherent energy patterns that First Children can subtly influence
- **Mortals Perceive as Magic:** Mortals perceive quantum manipulation as "fickle fates" or magical boons/curses, filtered through cultural lenses

### World-Specific Expansions

**Territory System:**
- World divided into ~42 regions (territories), each a self-sustaining quantum node
- Natural borders created by unstable rift zones that spawn chaotic energy patterns (chokepoints)
- First Children don't directly intervene (to avoid breaking physical laws like causality)
- Instead, they seed influences by granting fragmented quantum abilities to mortal factions

**Game Mechanics (World-Specific):**
- **Cycles:** Time flows normally, but First Children impose "cycles" via anchored quantum constructs (e.g., celestial events like comet passes that recharge energies)
- **Reinforcements:** At each cycle, First Children allocate "troops" by infusing raw quantum energy into regions, manifesting as population booms, technological surges, or rift-spawn allies
- **Quantum Artifacts:** Stable anchors from conquered territories grant bonuses (e.g., Reader fragments for spying)
- **Victory Conditions:** First First Child to control all territories "wins" the round, but the game resets periodically (world-specific exception: built-in quantum loop that reforms the board without full destruction)

**Mortal Awareness:**
- **Open Cosmology:** Stratara is "open"—mortals know about the First Children "players" (called "The Strategists")
- **Varied Responses:** Some worship them as patrons, others resent being pawns, leading to underground resistance movements
- **Quantum-Gifted Mortals:** Rare fragments manifest in mortals, who become heroes or villains, persecuted by rival factions

**Races:**
- Draws from universal races (Humans, Elves, Orcs, Kitsune, etc.)
- Each race adapted to different territories and roles in the "game"
- Societies evolve around conquest dynamics

## Themes

- **Balance and Imbalance:** Conquest creates growth (new tech/magic from merged cultures) but risks total haze (world-ending instability if one First Child dominates too hard)
- **Mortal Agency:** Mortals could spark their own sub-games, like rebelling to force a "draw" and attract liberation souls from other worlds
- **Sibling Rivalry:** Demonstrates how First Children conflicts play out through proxy wars
- **Moral Complexity:** Is it abusive to mortals, or educational? Framework allows for gray areas

## Potential Story Hooks

- A mortal with full-ish Quantumancy fragments (rare exception) disrupts the game, drawing external First Child attention
- Sibling betrayal: One First Child cheats by violating a minor moralistic rule, triggering peer intervention
- Crossover: Souls from Iolinus (or another world) get pulled in for a liberation operation, bringing foreign quantum interfaces that glitch the board
- **Dual Board Meta-Game:** See [Hegemon-Stratara Connection](hegemon-stratara-connection.md) for exploration of interconnected game boards where First Children play both worlds simultaneously

## Framework Conformance

**Conforms to Framework:**
- All "magic" is quantum manipulation (no separate realms)
- First Children are glorified waveforms (background detail)
- Physical laws cannot be violated (no causality breaking)
- Mortal quantum abilities are fragments (world-specific split)
- Soul transfer requires mechanisms (greyrose-like anchors)
- Peer judgment determines legality (debated by external First Children)

**World-Specific Expansions:**
- Territory system as quantum nodes
- Cycle mechanics via anchored constructs
- Game reset mechanism (quantum loop exception)
- Open cosmology (mortals aware of First Children)

**World-Specific Exceptions:**
- Built-in quantum loop that reforms the board without full destruction (works within framework, demonstrates creative use of quantum mechanics)
