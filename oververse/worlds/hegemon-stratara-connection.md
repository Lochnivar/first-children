# Hegemon-Stratara Connection: The Dual Board Meta-Game

**Status:** Framework-Level Concept - Proposed Connection  
**Category:** World Relationships / Meta-Game  
**Related:** [Hegemon](hegemon.md), [Stratara](stratara.md), [Soul Transfer](../mechanics/soul-transfer.md), [World Creation](../mechanics/world-creation.md), [../FRAMEWORK.md](../../FRAMEWORK.md)

## Overview

Hegemon and Stratara can function as two interconnected game boards where First Children play simultaneously, creating a meta-game of strategic resource management across multiple worlds. This demonstrates framework concepts of cross-world connections, soul transfer, and the escalation of collaborative creation conflicts.

**Core Concept:** Two boards, two games running simultaneously with interaction potential between them.

## The Two Boards

### Hegemon: The "Serious" Board

**Characteristics:**
- Established game with refined strategies
- Mortals aware of the contest (open cosmology)
- Slow, strategic gameplay
- Cultural depth and established rules
- First Children as distant manipulators
- Geopolitical focus

**Framework Alignment:**
- Open cosmology variant (framework allows)
- Strategic conquest mechanics (world-specific)
- Mortal awareness of First Children (framework variant)

### Stratara: The "Experimental" Board

**Characteristics:**
- Newer creation, different ruleset
- Mortals may be unaware (closed cosmology variant possible)
- Fast, chaotic gameplay
- Raw conquest focus
- First Children more hands-on
- Risk-style territory control

**Framework Alignment:**
- Different cosmology variant possible (framework allows)
- Territory-based mechanics (world-specific)
- Different First Children involvement levels (framework allows)

## Potential Relationships

### Option 1: Practice Board Model

**Concept:** Stratara is where First Children test strategies before deploying them on Hegemon

**Mechanics:**
- Strategies tested on Stratara's faster cycles
- Successful tactics refined for Hegemon's slower game
- Failed experiments discarded
- Stratara as literal practice board for the real game

**Framework Alignment:**
- First Children can test strategies (framework allows)
- Cross-world learning possible (framework allows)
- Different world rules enable experimentation (framework allows)

### Option 2: Resource Purchase Model

**Concept:** Winning on Stratara is the price for making moves on Hegemon

**Mechanics:**
- Need to conquer X territories on Stratara to "purchase" major intervention on Hegemon
- Resource management across two fronts
- Sacrifice position on one board to secure the other
- Strategic trade-offs between boards

**Framework Alignment:**

- Strategic decision-making (framework allows)

### Option 3: Dual Victory Model

**Concept:** Need to win BOTH boards to truly "win"

**Mechanics:**

- Higher stakes, more complex strategy

**Framework Alignment:**

### Option 4: Overlapping Players Model

**Concept:** Same First Children playing both boards simultaneously

**Mechanics:**

**Framework Alignment:**
