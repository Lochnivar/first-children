# City-State Survival — Reference Study

How Iolanu's segregated city-states remain viable under sustained monster pressure.
Status markers: **LOCKED** = decided and stable; **PENDING** = established but needs development; **OPEN** = not yet resolved.

*Related:* [monsters_and_rifts.md](monsters_and_rifts.md) · [Wrinomo.md](../cultures/Wrinomo.md)

---

## The Core Tension

Monster pressure makes unprotected settlement impossible. Only fortified city-states survive.
But fortified city-states require food. Agricultural land cannot fit inside walls at any scale
that sustains a real population. The territory outside city walls is dangerous expedition
territory for intercity travel.

This appears to be a contradiction. It resolves through a single mechanical distinction.

## The Congregation Mechanic

**Source:** [monsters_and_rifts.md](monsters_and_rifts.md)

**LOCKED** — Monsters are drawn to settlements — persistent, fixed, overnight human presence.
Congregation is not instantaneous. Individual monsters or small groups arrive first, over
hours or days. The horde builds toward a signal over the course of days, not minutes.

**LOCKED** — What triggers congregation: consistent overnight occupation. A village that is
inhabited every night is a fixed, readable signal. The congregation finds it and grows until
it overwhelms the defenses.

**LOCKED** — Sapience is defined as the capacity to diverge from natural programming.
Signal strength is a spectrum of divergence: monsters exhibit one specific non-entropic
behavior (targeting) and generate a weak signal; humans diverge across many axes and
generate a strong signal. Any human settlement completely overrides monster-to-monster
congregation signals within range. For full sapience mechanics, see
[monsters_and_rifts.md](monsters_and_rifts.md).

**LOCKED** — Monsters target intelligent life specifically. Non-intelligent life — wildlife,
livestock, birds, the full wilderness ecology — generates no congregation signal. This is
why fauna exists in Iolanu's wilderness at all, and why livestock in open fields do not
draw hordes. A flock of sheep is invisible to the congregation mechanic. The shepherd
with that flock is not — but alone, they generate minimal signal.

**LOCKED** — The signal scales with the number of intelligent beings in fixed overnight
occupation. One person draws so slowly that wandering monster traffic is the greater
threat. Two people together draw meaningfully faster. The threshold is two or more
intelligent occupants in consistent overnight presence. This is the basis for wilderness
homesteading — a lone person outside city walls is not suicidal, merely accepting a
different risk profile. It is also why lone shepherds overnight with large flocks are
viable in ways that two-shepherd enclosures are not without active perimeter management.

**LOCKED** — What does not trigger congregation: empty agricultural land, unoccupied
fields, livestock without intelligent handlers present. Workers who arrive at dawn and
return inside walls at dusk do not generate the sustained signal that builds a horde.
These are real dangers — monsters wander through, incidental attacks happen. They are
not congregation events.

**This is the resolution.** The farm survives because it is not a settlement. The farmer
survives if they make it back inside before nightfall.

**LOCKED** — The congregation mechanic produces two distinct survival strategies operating
on opposite principles:

- **Stay below the threshold.** A lone homesteader, a single shepherd, a skeleton-crew
  tulou rotation. Survive by never generating a signal strong enough to build meaningful
  congregation. Risk is individual wandering monsters, not hordes. Fails when occupant
  count rises — a second person joins, the signal strengthens, congregation begins.

- **Outpace congregation.** The Thohalla caravan model. A caravan is many intelligent
  beings together — a strong signal, well above threshold, drawing congregation rapidly
  from the moment it stops. Survives not by staying below the threshold but by moving
  before congregation peaks. The horde builds toward the last location. The caravan is
  already somewhere else. Fails when the caravan stops too long: weather, injury,
  illness. Every additional night in the same place is a night the congregation has
  closed more of the gap.

City-states use the first strategy for agricultural infrastructure — tulou, livestock
enclosures, skeleton shepherd rotations. The Thohalla use the second for the caravan
itself. Both follow from the same mechanic. Both have the same failure mode: the
strategy breaks down when its core condition is violated.

## The Tulou Model

**LOCKED** — City-states maintain farming zones outside their walls using agricultural
redoubts — small, purpose-built fortified structures. These are work-shelters, not villages.

**Primary functions:**
1. **Crop protection** — physical barrier keeping monsters from trampling fields incidentally
2. **Hold point** — shelter for workers caught in the field when a monster passes through,
   until city militia can sally and extract them

**What the tulou is not:** a settlement. It is occupied only during working hours and
growing seasons. It does not generate the consistent overnight presence that triggers
congregation. It is infrastructure, like a tool storage shed that happens to have thick
walls and a bar on the door.

**What the tulou needs to survive:** hours, not days. It holds until a report reaches
the city and a response force rides out. It does not need to survive a siege. It needs
to be defensible long enough for the city's reach to arrive.

**Construction principle:** single massive structure where the building face is the wall —
no perimeter to defend, just the exterior. Thick walls, high windows, barred gate,
interior courtyard. Capacity for a working party. Nothing that reads as permanent habitation.

**LOCKED** — The same model applies to ranching and animal husbandry. Livestock graze in
open fields during the day under guard and are returned to fortified enclosures at dusk.
The enclosure serves the same function as the tulou — thick walls, barred gate, nothing
that reads as permanent human habitation — and operates on the same principle: the animals
are not generating a congregation signal if they are not sleeping in the open. Monsters
that wander through grazing land are a real danger to livestock and herders. They are not
a congregation event. The herd survives because the animals are inside before nightfall.

## Sally Range as Agricultural Perimeter

**LOCKED** — The agricultural zone is defined by how far the city's military can respond
before a congregation event becomes unmanageable. This is the practical boundary of
food security.

A strong city with organized militia and effective cavalry has a large agricultural perimeter.
A city under pressure — internal conflict, resource depletion, plague, Church extraction of
military capacity — loses tulou at the edge of its response range first. Food stress follows
military stress. The two are the same measurement.

**The siege implication:**

**LOCKED** — An enemy who suppresses a city's willingness to sally does not need to breach
the walls. Controlling the space between city gate and agricultural perimeter collapses the
food supply. The city starves itself. This is the Iolanu model of siege warfare: make the
sally too costly, hold the perimeter at standoff, wait.

---

## Active Perimeter Management

Maintaining the agricultural zone requires more than passive tulou. Without regular culling,
congregation builds in zones between city patrols. Without deliberate baiting when congregation
accelerates, a horde can consolidate faster than the city can respond.

**LOCKED** — The Wrinomo perimeter-warden and bait-keeper specializations handle this
function for cities that can contract them. They read congregation rates the way they read
trail sign for caravan work — the same expertise, fixed territory instead of moving corridor.
Municipal contracts rather than episodic caravan fees.

**LOCKED** — A city that loses its perimeter management — through financial collapse,
political disruption, or loss of key Wrinomo personnel — faces congregation buildup within
seasons. Declining perimeter management is one of the earliest legible indicators of
city-state decline, visible to anyone paying attention before any other symptom shows.

*For full Wrinomo perimeter-warden detail, see [Wrinomo.md](../cultures/Wrinomo.md).*

## The Maritime Supplement

**LOCKED** — Cities on water have an additional food source that operates under a different
threat model. Fishing and harbor aquaculture do not require agricultural land and do not
depend on sally range.

Monster congregation behavior is oriented toward fixed settlements. Open water has none.
Fishing boats are mobile and do not sleep in a fixed location each night. This is a
different threat profile from the overland congregation problem — real dangers, different
mechanics, not directly resolved by the same solutions.

**LOCKED** — Coastal and river city-states supplement land-grown food with maritime harvest.
Inland cities cannot. This creates a structural food advantage for coastal city-states:
larger sustainable populations, less dependency on military capacity for food security,
and more resilience under siege conditions.

## Agricultural City-States

**LOCKED** — Not every city-state is a trade hub or a political center. Some are primarily
food producers — walled agricultural communities whose surplus is their export, traded to
larger cities that cannot fully feed themselves from their own sally range alone.

These communities are smaller, politically dependent, and structurally tied to the larger
cities that purchase their output and provide military guarantee when congregation events
exceed local capacity. They have not appeared prominently in the narrative yet because
the story has not needed them — they are background infrastructure, not protagonist locations.

They are politically significant:

- A city that controls the agricultural communities feeding it has a structural food security
  advantage that compounds over time
- A city dependent on distant food communities is vulnerable to convoy disruption — the
  Wrinomo insurance model applies here
- The Locquodian Order's mission to disrupt connective tissue between city-states creates
  a complex relationship with food dependency networks: agricultural interdependency is
  exactly the kind of structural binding the Order is meant to prevent, but attacking food
  supply lines causes the civilian suffering that draws attention the Order prefers to avoid

## Summary — What Keeps a City Fed

| Mechanism | Function | Dependency |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| Congregation delay | Farmland is not a settlement target | Core mechanic — no dependency |
| Tulou redoubts | Hold point + crop protection | Requires construction investment |
| Sally range | Defines agricultural perimeter | Requires military capacity |
| Active culling | Suppresses congregation buildup | Wrinomo contract or equivalent |
| Livestock enclosures | Same tulou model for animal husbandry | Requires construction investment |
| Maritime harvest | Supplements land agriculture | Coastal/river geography |
| Agricultural city-states | Surplus food import | Trade routes + convoy security |

A city that loses any one of these can compensate with the others. A city that loses several
simultaneously faces famine on a timeline proportional to its reserves. The interplay between
these mechanisms — and what happens when they fail in sequence — is the agricultural
substructure of city-state politics on Iolanu.

## Open Decisions

- **LOCKED** — Agricultural communities have no universal political category. The relationship
  is defined by the culture of the dominant city-state, not by any cross-cultural standard.
  Known variations:
  - **Maneth:** Feudal peonages. The agricultural community owes obligation to a lord;
    the lord owes military protection in return. Clear hierarchy, inherited status.
  - **Llosca:** Collective responsibilities. The agricultural community is understood as
    a shared civic obligation — the city feeds itself through communal stewardship, not
    through ownership of dependent communities.
  - **Astelli:** Family plantations. Agricultural land is clan or family-held, worked by
    family labor and family clients, passed through inheritance. The city's food supply
    is an aggregate of private family holdings rather than a civic or feudal structure.
  - Other cultures: **PENDING** — to be determined as each culture's relationship to
    agriculture and land is developed.
- **LOCKED** — The Locquodian Order treats agricultural communities as internal city
  infrastructure. A city feeding itself from its own farms — regardless of whether those
  farms are inside walls or in dependent communities outside them — is not a threat to
  fragmentation. It is a city being a city.
  The threshold is cross-city resource sharing. If two city-states begin sharing farms,
  pooling agricultural output, or creating interdependent food networks between them, that
  is exactly the connective tissue the Order is mandated to sever. Internal food security
  is invisible to them. Shared food security is a consolidation event.
- **LOCKED** — Wrinomo perimeter contracts are a city-state decision with no unified
  procedure. Some cities have a dedicated official whose remit includes perimeter
  management; others fold it under general militia command; others handle it through
  civic administration or merchant guild infrastructure. The Wrinomo negotiate with
  whoever holds the relevant authority in that city, and they have learned to identify
  that person quickly.
- **LOCKED** — There is no legal or formal cap on agricultural perimeter size. The limit
  is military and practical: as far as the city can reasonably project force and respond
  to a congregation event before the tulou is overwhelmed. A city expands its perimeter
  by expanding its military reach, not by negotiating a boundary. A city's perimeter
  shrinks when its military capacity does. The perimeter is a live measurement of power,
  not a fixed geographic line.
