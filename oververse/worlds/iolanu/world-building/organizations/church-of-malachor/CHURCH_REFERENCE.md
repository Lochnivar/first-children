# Church of Malachor — Reference Document

Quick-reference for Church structure, theology, personnel, and open decisions.
Status markers: **LOCKED** = decided and stable; **PENDING** = established but needs development; **OPEN** = not yet resolved.

## The God

**Source:** [zeta_god.md](../../cosmology/zeta_god.md) · [malachor_faith.md](../../cosmology/malachor_faith.md)

- **LOCKED** — Malachor (called Zeta in earlier ages) is a little-'g' god: one of the First Children of the big-G God; essentially a forbidden demiurge.
- **LOCKED** — Created Iolanu against explicit prohibition, then tied his life-force to the world. He cannot be killed without extinguishing all life on Iolanu. The entire population is a hostage.
- **LOCKED** — Personality: clever, immature, spoiled. Lacks compassion and the desire to develop it.
- **LOCKED** — Currently absent — distracted by matters on off-continent landmasses. The Church runs on autopilot. He is aware of direct divine intervention on Iolanu; the mission against him must remain quiet.
- **LOCKED** — Has Quantumancy (all five quantum abilities combined).
- **LOCKED** — The Shattering: his Godstone (direct connection to Iolanu) was shattered into seven Godshards, ending the God-King era. He no longer rules directly.
- **LOCKED** — Blackmoor is where Malachor was residing when the Shattering occurred at a separate location (the ancient city above Dwarhoffen, where the Grand Rift permanently resides). The landscape was not damaged by the Shattering itself — it was damaged by Malachor's *reaction* to it. What scarred Blackmoor is what an immature, spoiled god does when he loses his direct connection to his creation. The moorland is the aftermath of a divine tantrum. The Church built on top of it as a sacred site — not the site of the Shattering, but the site of Malachor's grief and rage at the moment his world changed. Both sites carry theological weight; they are not the same.
- **OPEN** — Does anyone in the Church know the Exarch-level autopilot problem? Who knows Malachor is effectively unreachable right now?
- **OPEN** — Godshards: who holds them? Does the Locquodian Order guard them to prevent reassembly?

## The Six Hierarchies

**Source:** [church_hierarchies.md](church_hierarchies.md)

The Church has six hierarchies: five organized around cardinal sins, plus the Ebon Hierarchy which holds cross-territorial authority.

### Governance Principle

**LOCKED** — Sin hierarchies have significant sway in their host city but do not rule it. The hierarchy is a power inside the city — influential, politically weighted, capable of applying pressure — but the civic structure remains local. The Church is a tenant with leverage, not the landlord.

**LOCKED** — Hierarchy presence does not require cultural endorsement. The Church establishes chapters because it has the authority and resources to do so, not because it was invited. The culture's resistance — or outright hostility — is the story, not an obstacle to the placement. Some hierarchies have worked with the grain of local culture; others are occupying hostile territory. Both are valid.

**Exception:** The Ebon Hierarchy holds Blackmoor outright. It is the one city in the world where the Church is effectively the government.

### Five Sin Hierarchies

| Sin | Location | Status |
|-----|----------|--------|
| Pride | Alephis (Takomai imperial capital) | **LOCKED** — in-world name: **Hierarchy of the Upright Soul** |
| Greed | Kitzigen Falls (Kitzengai heartland) | **LOCKED** — in-world name: **Hierarchy of the Vigilant Steward** |
| Lust | Burmallosca (Llosca capital) | **LOCKED** — in-world name: **Hierarchy of the Loving Heart** |
| Wrath | Stridence (Khalesar highland city) | **LOCKED** — in-world name: **Hierarchy of the Open Fist** |
| Envy | A'Thohalla (Thohalla heartland ruin) | **LOCKED** — hostile imposition; in-world name: **Hierarchy of the Reclaimed Heritage** |

**Design decision logged:** Five sins are Pride, Greed, Lust, Wrath, Envy. No expansion to seven. Envy is the mechanical lever for Church interference with Rathen/quantum-gift populations.

**Cultural pairings — confirmed:**
- **Pride → Takomai** *(Hierarchy of the Upright Soul).* The name reaches past the examination record to claim the soul underneath it. You're not proud of your achievements — your soul is upright, and your standing is its outward expression. Pride reframed as certainty: the conviction that you are correct, your record proves it, and the Church certifies what the examination system can't measure. The Takomai find this flattering and quietly alarming — the hierarchy inserted itself into the one gap the examination system can't fill.
- **Greed → Kitzengai** *(Hierarchy of the Vigilant Steward).* The name does the doctrinal work of converting the sin into a virtue — you're not hoarding, you're being a responsible custodian of Malachor's abundance. Excess isn't greed; it's evidence of good stewardship. The Kitzengai find this flattering and immediately suspicious in equal measure — they know when a framing is too convenient, and they know when they're being watched.
- **Lust → Llosca / Burmallosca** *(Hierarchy of the Loving Heart).* (Developed — see section below.)
- **Wrath → Khalesar** *(Hierarchy of the Open Fist).* The most contested. The name is a pointed reminder embedded in a gesture — an open fist is the moment before, readable immediately by any martial culture as readiness held in check. The Church calls their military devotion Wrath; the Khalesar call it covenant faithfulness. The hierarchy has been trying to redirect that martial energy into Church crusade service for generations. The tamadas have been refusing. Individual warriors are susceptible. The civic structure holds.
- **Envy → Thohalla / A'Thohalla** *(Hierarchy of the Reclaimed Heritage).* Envy at its core is the desire to claim the object being desired — and the name doesn't hide it. "Reclaimed" is the act of claiming dressed as restoration. The Church isn't just envying the Thohalla's prior relationship with Malachor; it's taking it, administratively. From the Church's framing: that heritage was neglected, mismanaged, allowed to drift — the hierarchy restores it. From the Thohalla's perspective: our heritage was never lost. We kept it in the saltwater and the Morning Roll while you weren't watching. You didn't reclaim anything. You arrived and called seizure restoration. The Thohalla drink the Remembrance alone in the dark while a chapter house manages their "reclaimed heritage" fifty yards away. Caravan mobility is the primary defense. A'Thohalla stays ruins to deny the Church an anchor.

**LOCKED** — All six seats confirmed: Pride = Alephis, Greed = Kitzigen Falls, Lust = Burmallosca, Wrath = Stridence, Envy = A'Thohalla, Ebon = Blackmoor.

### Hierarchy of the Loving Heart — Burmallosca (developed)

- **LOCKED** — Seat: Burmallosca, Llosca capital.
- **LOCKED** — Significant sway; does not rule. The Llosca civic structure remains local — the hierarchy is a major power inside the city, not the government.
- **PENDING** — Appropriated and shaped the Llosca body-positive culture; possibly operates on an inverted sin model (regulates/licenses rather than condemns). High Church presence but distracted — which is why Loisa operates there.
- **OPEN** — Exactly how the inversion works doctrinally. Does the Lust Hierarchy frame desire as sacred aspiration (Plenty/Surrender) rather than sin? That reading would fit the Five Aspirations framework.

### Ebon Hierarchy — Blackmoor (seat)

**LOCKED** — The Ebon Hierarchy holds Blackmoor. The city is effectively theirs — the exception to the governance principle above. Blackmoor is where Malachor was residing when the Shattering occurred elsewhere (at the ancient city above Dwarhoffen). The landscape was scarred not by the Shattering itself but by Malachor's reaction to it — his grief and rage at losing his Godstone, expressed at the location where he stood when it happened. The black moorland is what a god's tantrum leaves behind. The Church built here because this is where Malachor was — the site of his response, not the site of the event. The Ebon Throne sits at the center of that response.

**OPEN** — What does Blackmoor look like as a city governed by inquisitors? Who lives there voluntarily? What is exported from Blackmoor that makes other cities maintain trade with it despite the Church's grip on it? Does the blast site retain any residual properties — divine, physical, or both?

- **LOCKED** — Cross-territorial authority. Not pinned to a city-state; operates wherever heresy exists.

## Priesthood Rank Structure

**Source:** [priesthood.md](priesthood.md)

### Training Sequence (no independent authority)

| Rank | Function |
|------|----------|
| Novate | Initiate; basic catechumen training |
| Novarch | Junior cleric; recordkeeping, supervised rites |
| Acolyarch | Senior acolyte; trains novices, enforces ritual precision |
| Cantarch | Parish priest; public worship, local stewardship |
| Praeparch | Steward; manages resources, audits; final mentorship gate |

### Power Tier (irrevocable authority)

| Rank | Function |
|------|----------|
| Terarch | Tribunal commander and enforcer; first independent authority |
| Axiarch | Provincial governor and institutional architect |
| Hierarch | Doctrinal authority and legal arbiter |
| Exarch | Supreme head; final political authority |

### The Terarch Threshold

**LOCKED** — The single most structurally important transition in the Church. Below Terarch: training, mentorship, supervised service. At Terarch and above: independent command, legal weight, political exposure, binding oaths that cannot be unwound without tribunal action.

Promotion requires: time-in-rank (3 years as Praeparch), service record, two sponsors (at least one Axiarch+), ratified action with witnesses, written doctrinal exam, public oath before a Hierarch, and **Noctarch clearance**.

### The Binding of Office

**LOCKED** — Promotion ritual. Public citation of record, sponsor testimonies, recitation of oath, sealing of ledger entry, issue of insignia (iron gauntlet or tribunal ring). Every field is auditable; records can be investigated, forged, and surfaced.

### The Noctarch

**PENDING** — Side appointment available during Praeparch phase (Shadow Arc). Provides background clearance function at Terarch promotion (pass/fail flag). Also acquires blackmail leverage and secrecy skills. Not a rank in the main sequence — a parallel appointment with significant covert utility.

**OPEN** — Full Noctarch definition: what is their organization? Who do they report to? Are they Ebon-adjacent or a separate internal intelligence function?

## Structural Reach

**LOCKED** — The Church is six fortresses, not an empire. Real institutional power exists only at its anchor cities — the six hierarchy seats. Everything else is presence without force.

**LOCKED** — Parishes exist in virtually every significant city across both continents. The Cantarch running a parish in a foreign city-state has presence, performs worship, maintains the record. He does not have enforcement capacity. He knows this. His career depends on knowing this.

**LOCKED** — Why the mission model fails here. The Spanish mission system worked through geographic continuity — fixed points within a day's walk of each other, roaming priests connecting them, a colonial apparatus backing them when needed. None of that infrastructure exists in Iolanu:
- Travel between cities is armed expedition, not pastoral itinerancy
- The Locquodian Order actively destroys connective tissue — roads, alliances, communication corridors are exactly what they disrupt
- The city-states accept no external administration
- Monster pressure consumes the resources that would build the network

**LOCKED** — The apathetic roil. Where the Church is physically present and enforcing, populations comply. When it moves on or relaxes pressure, populations drift back to local culture, local practice, local indifference. Not apostasy. Not rebellion. Human nature under intermittent institutional pressure. This is the Apostasy's operating environment — the gaps the Church cannot sustain.

**LOCKED** — The bull as aspiration, not order. Official decrees travel from Blackmoor with full formal weight. Local clergy receives them, records receipt, and reads them in two registers: what they formally require and what is realistically enforceable. There is an art to this reading, developed over years in a specific city.

**LOCKED** — The two-language system. The Church operates in formal language (universal primacy, Malachor's dominion across both continents, the full doctrinal weight of every bull) and practical language (which three sentences in this bull can actually be acted on, and what "actioned" means in a report back to Blackmoor). Every clerk above Cantarch is fluent in both. The gap between them is where the institution's real politics happen.

**LOCKED** — Institutional wisdom as career survival. The wise Hierarch with twenty years in a Kitzengai trade hub knows exactly how much pressure the Vigilant Steward's authority can bear. He is useful, present, respected within his limits, and still there. The young Hierarch who tested those limits is reassigned. The one who pushed hard enough to cause a political crisis learns, in the words of the institution, how not to fly. The Exarch's bulls claim universal reach. The back-chamber reality is six anchor cities and correspondence that nobody is compelled to obey.

**LOCKED** — The Church mirrors its god. Malachor asserts creation and dominion over Iolanu while being effectively absent. The Exarch asserts universal primacy across both continents while lacking the infrastructure to enforce it. The performance of authority and the capacity to exercise it have been separated for long enough that the institution has adapted around the gap.

**LOCKED** — The internal tension: zealots vs. moderates. This is the Church's defining fault line.

*Zealots* demand the dial at 11. Malachor's authority is absolute; the gap between claim and capacity is a failure of faith and force, not a structural ceiling. Push harder, enforce more, the backing will come. They are not wrong about what the authority *should* be — if Malachor were present and active, they would be correct. He's not present. They believe this is temporary.

*Moderates* have quietly accepted that the dial has a hard cap and built the institution around it. Legitimacy is spent when you test it and fail. Every overreach that gets pushed back publicly lowers the ceiling — you've now demonstrated where it is. Stay well below it, project confidence, never let anyone confirm the ceiling exists. They may be equally devout. They have stopped needing Malachor to answer to run the institution.

Neither faction says the god is unreachable. The zealot believes he isn't. The moderate knows that saying so would collapse the entire structure. Both stay silent on the question, for opposite reasons. The silence of Malachor is the thing no one names in the back chamber, and everyone in the back chamber knows.

**LOCKED** — The evolution of zealotry over time. The paleozealots of the immediate post-Shattering Church were a categorically different creature. They had a reference point — living memory of what Malachor's active presence felt like, or one generation removed from people who did. They were trying to restore something they had personally experienced. Their overreach was fueled by genuine adrenaline of recent loss and the reasonable belief that backing was coming soon.

Current zealots have no reference point. Their fervor is entirely inherited — doctrine, hagiography, institutional tradition. They have never experienced what they are fighting to recover. That is a fundamentally different grade of conviction. The economics reflect it: paleozealot overreach could sustain itself on the energy of immediate loss; current zealotry runs on doctrine alone, which depletes faster and replenishes slower.

The Church's current moderation is not restraint by preference. It is scar tissue — the institutional memory of what paleozealot overreach cost, built up over generations of watching zealots spend capital the god never replaced. The moderates didn't win the argument. The math won it for them, repeatedly, until the lesson was structural.

Today's zealot is a domesticated descendant of the paleozealot. Still dangerous. Still capable of causing institutional crises. But operating with inherited conviction rather than experiential certainty, and unconsciously aware — if never consciously admitting — that the backing the paleozealots expected has not arrived in a very long time.

## Church Cultural Identity

**LOCKED** — The Church of Malachor is the Crusades, darker, with significantly better PR.

**What it kept from the Crusades model:**
- Divine mandate as the ultimate argument-ender. Malachor wills it. The argument is over.
- Holy violence as service rather than cruelty — the Ebon Hierarchy is the Inquisition, but disciplined. Solon's precision over butchery is exactly this ethos: punishment is Malachor's right, not the Knight's pleasure. The butchers have lost sight of purpose.
- The transactional relationship with power — indulgences, legitimacy, the Church as the institution that can ratify your claim if you are useful enough to it.
- The gap between stated mission and actual conduct, structurally maintained rather than accidentally concealed.

**What the better PR changes:**
- **Absorb rather than eliminate.** The Loving Heart is the template — co-opt what you cannot suppress, build doctrine around it, give it a hierarchy. The culture you absorb cannot become a martyr. It becomes a chapter.
- **The names do the work.** You cannot argue against the Hierarchy of the Loving Heart without sounding like you oppose love. The institution named itself the argument before the argument began.
- **The Ebon Hierarchy stays surgical.** Heretics disappear, recant, or are never heard from again. Public burnings create martyrs and news. The threat of the Ebon Hierarchy does more work than the Ebon Hierarchy itself, which is the point.
- **The parish provides genuine value.** Record-keeping, dispute resolution, education, community structure. Removing the Church creates a vacuum. Resistance becomes costly even for populations that despise the institution. You don't fight what you depend on.
- **The hagiography runs ahead of the culture.** Control what virtue looks like before anyone else gets to define it, and the culture will eventually produce people who look like the hagiography. The PR department wrote the casting call.
- **The two-language system keeps the worst in the back chamber.** The public face is always the aspirational one. The formal language is always the language of the Five Aspirations. The back-chamber language is where the actual decisions live, and the back chamber does not issue press releases.

**The darkest part:**
The PR is good enough to produce genuine devotion. Many believers are sincere. The faith is real even when the institution is corrupt. Sincere devotion is the best cover an evil institution can have — it is not performed, it cannot be exposed, and it generates defenders who are not lying when they defend it. The Church did not plan this. It is simply what happens when you run a sophisticated enough operation for long enough.
