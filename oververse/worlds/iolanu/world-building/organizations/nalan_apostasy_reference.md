# The Nalan Apostasy — Reference Document

Quick-reference for Apostasy structure, doctrine, recruitment, and open decisions.
Status markers: **LOCKED** = decided and stable; **PENDING** = established but needs development; **OPEN** = not yet resolved.

*Related:* [CHURCH_REFERENCE.md](church-of-malachor/CHURCH_REFERENCE.md) · [greyroses.md](greyroses.md) · [chapterhouse_nala_ibelin.md](chapterhouse_nala_ibelin.md)

## What It Is

**LOCKED** — The Nalan Apostasy is not a conventional religious underground. It is an
accumulated toolkit built by Earth Souls who understood how power structures change and
applied that knowledge to Iolanu. Nine centuries of calibrated institutional design,
each contribution building on the last, most of it now invisible as Apostasy work —
it has become the water Iolanu swims in.

**LOCKED** — Operational doctrine: calibrated friction, not confrontation. The Apostasy
does not preach heresy or a message of discontent. It does not recruit for rebellion.
It introduces systemic instability into Malachor's perfect system — slowly, deliberately,
permanently below the threshold that would trigger Church mobilization or Locquodian
Order action. The discipline is knowing exactly where that threshold is and never
crossing it.

**LOCKED** — The Apostasy's invisibility is its primary defense. It must not look like
an organization from the outside — or from most of the inside.

---

## The Cover: University of Ibelin

**LOCKED** — The public face of the Apostasy's operational core is the University of
Ibelin — a genuine institution of higher learning in the ancient sense. Not a shell.
Not a fake. A real university with real students, real scholars, real scholarship. The
cover works because it is not false.

**LOCKED** — Inside the University, invisible to most of its faculty and students, is
the Chapterhouse — the hidden apparatus where Earth Souls are received and acclimated,
where Greyroses are managed, and where the innermost operational layer functions.

**LOCKED** — Who knows: the Lord of Ibelin; Earth Souls; a small number of trusted
inner-circle agents. Below that level, the university genuinely is a university.

**LOCKED** — The University sits on deliberately cultivated thin theological ice with
the Church. Knowledge as near-heresy is an engineered position, not an accident. When
something Apostasy-adjacent surfaces, the Church reaches for the box it already has —
"those scholars, always pushing limits" — rather than constructing a new and more
accurate one. The pre-existing bounded suspicion is the camouflage. Genuine slips are
lost in the heretical noise.

**LOCKED** — The Church cannot move against the University without offending the Lord
of Ibelin, attacking scholarship without evidence of heresy, and triggering political
costs that outweigh the uncertain benefit. The University's genuine function is its
best defense.

## Operational Structure

**LOCKED** — Hybrid covert identity / ambient model. The Apostasy has no single
organizational shape that can be mapped and decapitated.

**LOCKED** — At the street level, most people connected to the Apostasy's work do not
know they are connected to it. They know a person, or belong to an institution, or
participate in a system — the University, AJ's accreditation network, the Knights of
the Lustrous Lance — without knowing the deeper layer. The ambient model means Nala
works on individuals quietly. Some get drawn deeper. Most never do.

**LOCKED** — The Apostasy has no mirror relationship to the Church. It does not
organize around the Church's five sins, five hierarchies, or any other Church
structural principle. Axiarch Solon's claim in scene that the Apostasy has five
orders mirroring the Church reflects Church intelligence that is wrong — he is
projecting his own institution's framework onto something that does not work that
way, or using rhetoric to unsettle his subject. The Apostasy's actual structure
is deliberately unmappable.

**LOCKED** — The Apostasy's internal culture has outgrown the "good goddess" recruitment
pitch. Earth Souls are not dumb, and they are not devotees. They understand what Nala
actually is — a powerful entity running a centuries-long grudge operation against her
brother — and they have made their own assessment of the work independent of her
motivations. The cause holds up without the theology. Malachor genuinely is a tyrant
holding a world hostage. The work genuinely does good. That is sufficient. The "good
goddess" framing is Nala's pitch at the transit moment; it is not the internal culture
of people who have been on Iolanu long enough to think it through. Senior Earth Souls
probably do not discuss Nala's motivations reverently.

**OPEN** — What the organizational structure actually looks like below the innermost
circle. How decisions are made. How information flows. What operational authority
looks like in practice without a formal hierarchy.

**LOCKED** — Nala does not communicate with the operation. The Apostasy runs on
inherited doctrine, accumulated institutional knowledge, and the judgment of Earth
Souls — not ongoing divine direction. She set the operation in motion. It runs
without her. The silence is mutual: Nala isn't talking, and the Apostasy isn't asking.
Earth Souls who have been on Iolanu long enough have made their own assessment of
the work. They do not need, and are not particularly interested in, input from the
goddess who recruited them.

**LOCKED** — The operational reason for silence: any direct communication with
living people on Iolanu risks detection by Malachor. He is aware that direct divine
intervention on Iolanu is detectable; Nala operates on the assumption that he is
watching for exactly that. The Apostasy's nine centuries of progress would be exposed
if Malachor traced a communication back to its source. The mutual silence serves the
operation. The fact that it also suits both parties is beside the point operationally
— but it is true.

**LOCKED** — The one known exception: Nala whispered to Chance Hodgkins, exploiting
Malachor's hubris to influence what became the Shattering. She took a calculated
risk — the Shattering was worth it. Malachor's confidence in his own sealed system
created the blind spot she used. She does not assume that blind spot still exists
or that she could use it again without consequence.

**LOCKED** — Soul interception at death is Nala's primary safe communication window.
The recruitment pitch, the orientation, whatever she communicates about Iolanu and
the work — it all happens in that transit moment. Once the soul arrives in Iolanu,
Nala is silent. Earth Souls cannot ask for guidance or confirmation. They are on
their own, which is how they prefer it.

**LOCKED** — Having a Greyrose does not make someone an Earth Soul. Many Greyrose-
bearers are Iolanian-born dedicated members — like the Greyrose Knights — who will
never host an Earth Soul. The Greyrose is a prerequisite for soul transfer, not
synonymous with it. It creates a pool of candidate bodies Nala can draw from at the
transit moment. Most bearers will serve the Apostasy for a lifetime in their own body.

**LOCKED** — Greyroses have no role in ongoing communication. They are a soul-transfer
mechanism: technically preparing the body to receive an incoming Earth Soul, and
buffering the previous soul through the transition. Once the Earth Soul is seated, the
Greyrose's transfer function is complete. Nala has no ongoing channel through it.

## Recruitment: Earth Souls

**LOCKED** — Nala intercepts souls in transit between Earth death and the Earth
afterlife. She does not recruit from living candidates. The soul must be in transit —
dying is the condition of availability, not the selection criterion.

**LOCKED** — Selection criterion: an inherent quality visible in the soul in transit.
Not trained skills, not ideological alignment, not specialized knowledge. A cognitive
or temperamental disposition that Nala recognizes as applicable to Iolanu's specific
needs. Nine centuries of watching souls has refined her eye.

**LOCKED** — The quality produces the contribution. The Earth Soul does not arrive
with a predetermined role. The quality, applied to Iolanu's context, grows into
specific work that could not have been fully predicted in advance.

**LOCKED** — Earth Souls were dying when Nala made her offer. The prologue establishes
that the conversation is real — Nala speaks to the soul in transit and the choice is
genuine. But consenting while dying, under a pitch from a goddess, is not the same
as understanding what you agreed to once you're in the body. The disorientation is real.
The buy-in to the specific work of the Apostasy is not assumed — it has to be earned
by what they find when they arrive. Earth Souls who have been on Iolanu long enough
do not tend to remain devotees. They become pragmatists who have made their own
assessment of whether the work is worth doing.

## Historical Depth

**LOCKED** — The first Earth Soul arrived in 1119 (Earth calendar). The operation is
approximately 900 years old by Gabby's arrival. The accumulated toolkit spans nearly
a millennium of Earth history — medieval covenant theology, guild organization,
chivalric codes, Enlightenment institutional thinking, contemporary systems analysis.
These frames do not replace each other. They layer.

**LOCKED** — The oldest Apostasy work is no longer visible as Apostasy work. It has
become infrastructure — ways things are done in Iolanu that nobody traces back to
source. The Apostasy's most durable contributions are the ones nobody recognizes.

## Relationship to the Church

**LOCKED** — The Church does not know the Apostasy exists. "Apostasy" as the Church
uses it is a generic theological term for falling from the faith — not a named
organization. The capital-A Apostasy is an internal designation unknown outside
the innermost circle. Solon and his order investigate individual suspected heretics
and extract fragments — a name, a contact, a practice that doesn't fit doctrine —
but have never assembled those fragments into a picture of something organized and
deliberate. The absence of a visible target is the operation's primary defense.

**LOCKED** — The Church cannot crusade against something it cannot see. The Apostasy
produces no preaching, no visible recruitment, no organizational shape that can be
mapped. Parish clergy notice occasional oddities — a scholar who asks uncomfortable
questions, a traveler whose background doesn't quite add up — and file reports that
go nowhere because the fragments never connect.

**LOCKED** — The University of Ibelin's cultivated theological edginess absorbs
those occasional oddities into a bounded, manageable channel. "Another scholar
pushing limits" is a category the Church already has. It leads nowhere dangerous.

---

## Relationship to the Locquodian Order

**LOCKED** — The Locquodian Order sees the University of Ibelin as a cross-city-state
institution producing unusually networked graduates. This is below their action
threshold — intellectual networks read differently than political ones — but they are
watching.

**LOCKED** — The Apostasy's work produces local fragmentation effects (portable
credentials atomize feudal structures) while building diffuse networks at the macro
level. It looks like neither consolidation nor resistance. It is designed to fall into
the Order's blind spot.

**LOCKED** — A party from the University moving organized through multiple city-states
crosses a visibility threshold for both factions simultaneously — hence the embedded
agents in Loisa's tour party.

## The Embedded Agents Problem

**LOCKED** — At least one Church watcher and one Locquodian operative are embedded
in Loisa's eight-person tour party. Neither knows what they have found. Both are
responding to institutional pattern-recognition, not targeted intelligence.

**OPEN** — Which party members are the agents. How they got inside the party.
Whether Loisa knows or suspects. What each agent thinks they are looking at.
When and how they are discovered.
