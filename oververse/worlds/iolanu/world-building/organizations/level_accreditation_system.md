# The Level Accreditation System

**Category:** Cultural Institution / Organization  
**Status:** Active; beyond any single point of control  
**Origin:** Ibelin Academy  
**Related Files:** [A.J.](../../characters/supporting/aj.md), [Knights of the Lustrous Lance](knights_of_the_lustrous_lance.md)

---

## What It Is

A skills accreditation framework operating across Iolanu's city-states. Practitioners are assessed in a named competency — their class — and assigned a numerical level from 1 to 10 reflecting demonstrated ability. The credential is portable, legible to strangers, and independent of birth, guild membership, or political affiliation.

A Level 8 Fighter from a Thohalla caravan holds the same credential as a Level 8 Fighter from a noble house. The system does not care about the distinction. This is not incidental. It is the point.

---

## The Vocabulary

**Classes** are the named competency areas. Fighter, Healer, Archer, Scout, Navigator, Physician, Engineer — these were among the first. The list has grown well beyond its origins. Classes are not fixed by any authority. If a practitioner can make a reasonable argument for a class and demonstrate meaningful skill within it, assessors have wide latitude to certify it. The framework welcomes expansion. The point is not the taxonomy.

**Levels** run from 1 to 10 and reflect demonstrated ability:

- **1–3:** Developing competency. Hireable for basic work. Still learning.
- **4–6:** Solid practitioner. Reliable, respected, commands steady employment.
- **7–9:** Expert. Sought after. Commands premium rates. Regional reputation.
- **10:** Exceptional. Few per class in any given region. The credential alone is a form of introduction.

The language is A.J.'s, introduced wholesale and adopted without modification. Iolanu people now say *"he's a Level 6 Fighter"* and *"she's leveling in navigation"* without any awareness that the vocabulary came from somewhere specific. The words are simply how people talk about skill now.

---

## How Certification Works

A candidate presents to an assessor, demonstrates their skill through practical evaluation, and receives a credential if they meet the threshold for a given level. The assessment is direct — performance, not pedigree.

Assessors were originally trained at Ibelin. They are no longer exclusively there. Cities across Iolanu now have their own assessors, trained by assessors trained by other assessors. The system has propagated far beyond its origin point. A.J. is not involved in most certifications and has no desire to be. He has not attempted to maintain central control. An institution he controls can be targeted. An idea in the wild cannot.

**Physical credential:** A compact stamped token indicating class and level, carried by the holder and recognized by any party familiar with the system — mercenary companies, city guard recruiters, guild masters, ship captains. Simple enough to verify quickly, difficult enough to counterfeit that fraud carries reputational risk.

Higher-level practitioners often find the token redundant. Their reputation precedes the credential. They carry it anyway because the system runs on consistency.

---

## The Spread

The system began at Ibelin Academy as a martial accreditation. It did not stay martial for long.

Guild members saw what fighters were doing and applied the framework to their trades. Merchants. Navigators. Physicians. Herbalists. Negotiators. Cartographers. A.J. did not push it into these domains — people took the logic and ran with it because the logic is genuinely useful. A portable credential that says what you can do, legible to strangers, independent of who you know. That has value anywhere.

The result: *leveling* is now a verb in Iolanu. People say they are leveling in something. The word is in the language. The concept it carries — that skill is improvable, measurable, and demonstrable — traveled with the word into every domain that adopted it.

---

## The Actual Disruption

The classes and the numbers are not the disruption. The disruption is the assumption embedded in using them.

A feudal world runs on fixed circumstances. You are born to your lot. The village healer's daughter is a healer's daughter. The caravan guard's son is a caravan guard's son. Movement between circumstances requires extraordinary luck, extraordinary violence, or extraordinary patronage. The system does not reward effort with legible progress. It does not show you how far you have come or how far you could go.

The Level system does. It gives people a visible answer to *am I getting better?* and *does it matter?* Both answers are yes. Both answers are visible to strangers. Both answers travel with the person who earned them across city-state borders without requiring a letter of introduction or a patron's seal.

The serf who is leveling in something is a serf who believes their circumstances are not fixed. That belief, multiplied across a population across decades, is A.J.'s actual weapon.

The Church cannot ban it because there is nothing to ban. Trying harder is not heresy. Measuring improvement is not sedition. The words are simply words.

---

## A.J.'s Current Relationship to the System

He watches it from Ibelin with satisfaction and no desire to reclaim it. His success metric for both the Level system and the KLL is the same: the operation no longer needs him. Anything that still needs him can be stopped by stopping him. Anything that has outgrown him cannot.

He occasionally hears something the system has produced that surprises him — a class he never anticipated, a level threshold that has become culturally meaningful in a city he has never visited, an assessor three generations removed from Ibelin training who has developed their own refinements. He finds all of this correct. The idea belongs to Iolanu now. It never needed to belong to him.

## Practical Examples

### Class Examples

**Fighter**
- **Level 2:** Has weapon training, can handle themselves in a brawl. Hireable for low-risk guard work where presence matters more than ability.
- **Level 5:** Professional soldier quality. Reliable in formation or single combat. The baseline most mercenary contracts specify.
- **Level 8:** The kind of fighter a company builds around. Opponents assess them before engaging. Commands significant rates.
- **Level 10:** Few per region. The credential alone is a form of announcement. Stories get told.

**Healer**
- **Level 3:** Treats wounds, sets bones, knows which remedies work for which complaints. Village-healer competency.
- **Level 6:** Battlefield triage. Knows which wounds to prioritize, which to close now and which to manage later. What city infirmaries hire.
- **Level 9:** The person a governor sends for when someone important is dying. Has seen things most healers haven't and adapted accordingly.

**Navigator**
- **Level 4:** Reliable on known routes in fair conditions. Coast-hugging competent.
- **Level 7:** Open-water capable, reads weather and current with confidence, can work from stars alone. Ship captains advertise their navigators' levels.
- **Level 10:** Has routes others don't know exist. Periodically hired by city-states, not just ships.

**Negotiator** *(an unexpected class — formalized by a Llosca merchant who made the argument to an assessor and it stuck)*
- **Level 3:** Gets a fair deal more often than not.
- **Level 6:** Walks into a room knowing what the other party needs and leaves having given them less of it than they intended to accept.
- **Level 9:** Is requested by name. Has ended disputes that were heading toward conflict. Cities have paid to borrow one.

**Monster-Reader** *(emerged from necessity — someone had to study horde behavior patterns, and eventually enough people did it seriously that it became a class)*
- **Level 4:** Identifies species, estimates horde size, knows general behavioral patterns. Useful to caravan leaders.
- **Level 7:** Reads movement and concentration patterns, anticipates where a horde will be in two days. What Thohalla riftwalker teams build around.

---

### Multi-Class Examples

Multi-classing is common and often tells you more about a person than any single credential.

**Level 6 Fighter / Level 4 Scout** — The shape of an experienced caravan guard. Can handle a fight and find trouble before it finds the caravan. Standard hire for serious overland routes.

**Level 7 Merchant / Level 5 Negotiator / Level 3 Navigator** — A Llosca trading house principal. The navigation credential is modest but it means they've made the crossing themselves and know what they're asking of their captains.

**Level 5 Healer / Level 5 Monster-Reader** — Emerging from Thohalla culture. Knows what a creature does to a body and how to undo it. Specialized and sought after.

**Level 4 Fighter / Level 4 Navigator / Level 4 Scout** — The shape of a competent generalist. No single credential impressive; the combination suggests someone who has done many things seriously. Sailors who've been in trouble, Thohalla who've left the caravans, former city guards who went looking for more.

**Level 8 Fighter / Level 3 Negotiator** — A KLL knight, often. The fighting credential is legitimate and documented. The negotiation credential reflects that running your own career involves contracts.

---

### The System in Use

A mercenary company posts requirements: *Level 5 Fighter minimum, Level 4 Healer preferred, escort contract, two months, northern route.* The credential replaces "experienced soldiers from good families" — a requirement that once filtered by birth now filters by demonstrated ability. The noble-born Level 3 Fighter is a harder argument than he used to be.

A city governor negotiating for repair of a damaged outpost road specifies Level 6 Engineers minimum. Three cities away, an assessor who has never met A.J. certifies a Level 6 Engineer who will take that contract.

A Thohalla caravan leader presents credentials at a city gate. The gate captain checks levels, adjusts the entry assessment accordingly. The caravan leader is assessed on what they can demonstrably do, not on being Thohalla.

A lord who used to rely entirely on family reputation and personal recommendation now occasionally says, out loud, without apparent awareness of what has happened to him: *"I need Level 7s for this."*

---

### Unexpected Classes

The framework was never meant to stay contained. Among the classes that emerged without A.J.'s design:

- **Tide-Caller** — Caelori coastal navigation specialist, distinct from general Navigator
- **Rift-Scout** — Thohalla assessment of rift proximity and behavior; the assessors are almost exclusively Thohalla
- **Memory-Keeper** — Caelori Echo practitioners, carefully worded to avoid Church attention
- **Poison-wright** — Exists. Several cities certify it. A.J. has no notes on this.
- **Crowd-Weaver** — Formal speaking and mass persuasion; emerged in three cities independently

## On Standardization

A.J. is not a standards body. A Kitzengai Level 4 Fighter and a Manchire Level 4 Fighter are probably not identical. Regional assessors develop their own thresholds, emphases, and refinements. Experienced practitioners know this and adjust — the credential is a starting point for conversation, not a final word. Mercenary companies that hire across regions run their own assessments and treat the credential as prior information, not conclusion.

A.J. is aware of this variance and is not concerned. Others argue about it. He watches them argue about it and considers the argument itself evidence that the system matters to them — which is the point.

He has produced a small number of **Guiding Principle documents** from Ibelin — advisory texts on assessment philosophy, class definition, level threshold thinking. They are not mandates. Cities and assessors read them, argue with them, adopt the parts they agree with, and ignore the rest. A.J. considers this correct behavior.

When Mauwood introduced a Level 20 track — an entire upper tier beyond the original framework — A.J. received the news, noted that Mauwood now has practitioners motivated to hit Level 20, and moved on. More leveling. More ruts disrupted. The system is doing what it was designed to do, in a direction he did not design. This is not a problem.

The game theory is simple: the more people who use the vocabulary, hold credentials, and think in terms of levels, the more the embedded assumptions spread. Standardization would make the system easier to argue with. Variation makes it impossible to contain.

## Notes

The Level system is not an Apostasy program. It is not run by the Apostasy, staffed by the Apostasy, or accountable to the Apostasy. It originated with an Apostasy operative at an Apostasy-adjacent institution. That is the full extent of the connection.

The assessors in a dozen city-states have no idea who A.J. is. The practitioners carrying Level credentials have no idea where the vocabulary came from. The mercenary captains who hire by level and the guild masters who require certification do not think of themselves as participants in anything larger than a useful system.

They are not wrong. The system is useful. It is also, quietly and irreversibly, eating the ruts that have kept people in place for generations.

A.J. designed it that way. Then he let it go.
