# Locquodian Order

**Category:** Organization / Faction  
**Status:** ACTIVE — major faction; one of three global pressures driving world events

---

## Founder: Caladon

**Status:** LOCKED

Caladon is a wolf-headed human hybrid created by a Quantumancer during the early Empire era. He served as majordomo, personal butler, and servant to his Quantumancer master — a proximity that gave him firsthand witness to the dangers of unchecked Quantumancy and the Empire that enabled it.

His master was among the Quantumancers involved in the Shattering alongside Chance Hodgkins. The master was killed, but QM souls do not transition cleanly — too entangled with the universe to dissolve into it. The Haze that empowered them in life traps them after death, conscious but unable to act. Caladon knows his master's soul persists in this state. It is unresolvable. He has made his peace with it.

The hybridization that made Caladon what he is also made him immortal — an unintended side effect. He is unique in the world. He has tried to end his life and cannot. He is very, very old and very, very alone.

He has personally met Malachor in the god-king persona.

### Character
Caladon is urbane and refined. Personally very warm — warmth is a chosen discipline, less corrosive than hatred and resentment over this timescale. He holds himself emotionally aloof because connections create complications he has been through too many times. He is genuinely likeable, someone people want to confide in. He keeps one hand on the door.

### His Role in the Order
Caladon is a myth to the rank and file — a founder-figure believed long dead. He is real, alive, and involved in daily decisions. The myth serves two purposes: protection for Caladon, and protection for the cellular structure (captured operatives cannot reveal what they do not know).

He sets vision and mission. He does not run local operations. His perspective is too ancient for current conditions — when he last intervened directly (~1,000 years ago), he applied old methods to modern problems and the blowback confirmed what he should have already known. He explicitly relies on the cellular system for everything. His value is pattern recognition across centuries, not ground-level judgment.

**His direct intervention threshold is extreme.** It has not been crossed since the failed intervention ~1,000 years ago.

## Core Mission

**Status:** LOCKED

Prevent the reformation of the Empire. The Shattering is the proof case: the Empire created the conditions that allowed Quantumancers to operate at the scale that caused it. Fragmentation is not an end in itself — it is the structural prevention of catastrophe.

The Order's opposition to unification is therefore not ideological balkanization for its own sake. It is a specific, practical conclusion: concentrated Imperial power enables Quantumancy at catastrophic scale. Keep the Empire from reforming, keep that threshold out of reach.

---

## Methods

**Status:** LOCKED (broad strokes)

**Cellular structure** — distributed, insulated, no single point of failure. Operatives at the cell level do not know who they ultimately report to or that the founder is alive. This is both security architecture and operational necessity: Caladon cannot run local operations himself.

**Covert by default** — espionage, intelligence, targeted pressure, manipulation of political conditions. Overt action damages operations and reputation. The Order does not advertise itself.

## Current Situation

### The Losceatic League
The League's formation is high on Caladon's radar — a pre-formal political union among Losceati cities trending toward consolidation. The Order is organizing a response, but the League has been savvy about avoiding LO moves. Their public framing (mutual defense against monster pressure) leaves little shadow to work in. Overt interference would damage the Order's operations and reputation. The angle is not yet clear.

The League's formal declaration will trigger a Locquodian Order response — the nature of that response is an active problem the cells are working.

## Relationship to Other Factions

### vs. Church of Malachor
Natural friction. The Church's unification impulse — one faith, one institutional reach — is structurally what the Order exists to prevent. Church expansion is a Locquodian concern. The conflict is ongoing and managed rather than acute.

**Open:** Does the Order know Malachor is divine, or do they treat the Church as a purely political actor?

### vs. Nala's Apostasy
Complex. The Order accidentally serves Nala's interests by keeping Malachor's power fragmented and preventing the coordination that would let the Church consolidate. It also hinders Nala by preventing the kind of coordination the Apostasy itself might need. Enemy-of-enemy dynamic without either side necessarily knowing it.

### vs. Losceatic League
Active problem. See above.
