# Culture File Template

**What this template is for:**  
Defining a cultural tradition and genetic population of Iolanu. Cultures
are *influences*, not nationalities. Each city-state is its own cultural
unit; the named cultures are the traditions and gene pools that shaped
them. A person is not "Kitzengai" the way someone is "French" — they
carry Kitzengai physical heritage, Kitzengai portable tradition, or both,
in whatever proportion their family history produced.

**What this template is not for:**  
Describing cities. If you find yourself writing about architecture,
infrastructure, named locations, or geographic features in anything other
than the Heartland Expression section, stop. That content belongs in a
city file.

**The diaspora test:**  
Before adding content to any section except Heartland Expression, ask:
*is this true of a person from this culture living in a foreign quarter,
three generations removed from the heartland?* If no — city file.

---

## Template Variants

Not all cultures spread via diaspora and quarters. Three variants modify
the standard template for cultures that don't fit that pattern.

### Standard
Cultures that spread — people leave the heartland, establish quarters,
carry the tradition with them. Use the full template as written.  
*Examples: Kitzengai, Caelori, Llosca, Takomai, Maneth, Astelli*

### Nomadic
Cultures that move rather than settle. The caravan or equivalent IS
the heartland — there is no fixed place to leave and no quarter to
establish. The portable tradition and heartland expression are nearly
identical because the culture is always in motion.

**Template modifications:**
- Replace "Heartland Expression" with **"Cultural Anchor"** — the
  mobile structure (caravan, fleet, etc.) and any nominal fixed points
- Replace "Coexistence and Contact" with **"Passage and Contact"** —
  they move through, they don't settle; what contact looks like when
  you are never staying
- The diaspora test becomes: *what is true of a member of this culture
  passing through a foreign city?*

*Examples: Thohalla*

### Rooted-but-Mobile
Cultures deeply tied to a specific geography that they rarely or never
permanently leave. Others come to them more than they go to others.
Travel exists but in service of returning, not settling.

**Template modifications:**
- The Heartland Expression section is heavier than usual — the geography
  and the culture are less separable than in Standard cultures
- Replace "Coexistence and Contact" with **"Contact at the Crossing"**
  — outsiders come to them; interaction happens at their edges and at
  destination points; the dynamic is reversed from Standard
- The diaspora test becomes: *what is true of a member of this culture
  at a destination port, before they return home?*

*Examples: Orindi*

### Circumstantial
Not an ethnic group or traditional culture but a population defined by
shared legal, social, or geographic condition rather than shared origin.
Who ends up here is mixed; what they share is circumstance. The culture
documented is what emerged from that shared condition, not what was
brought to it.

**Template modifications:**
- Replace the opening header block with a **"What [Name] Is"** section
  — defines the category, not the people; explains who ends up here
  and why
- **Physical Heritage** section is replaced with **"Population"** —
  describes the mixed composition rather than a consistent gene pool
- Omit the **"On mixing"** note — not applicable to a mixed population
- Omit **"Coexistence and Contact"** — circumstantial cultures typically
  cannot leave their defining condition
- Omit the diaspora test entirely — there is no diaspora

*Examples: Rathen*

## Physical Heritage

*Gene pool traits shaped by generations in a specific environment. These
are not costume choices — a person inherits them. Physical traits fade
slowly through intermarriage, which is uncommon (see Coexistence and
Contact). Do not describe what people choose to wear here — that goes
in Clothing.*

- **Skin:** range
- **Hair:** color, texture
- **Eyes:** color range
- **Build:** what environment and ancestry shaped over generations
- **Distinguishing:** characteristic physical tells that persist regardless
  of dress or context

**On mixing:** What the rare crossover individual looks like. Which traits
persist longest through intermarriage, which fade first.

## Portable Tradition

*Everything in this section must pass the diaspora test: true of someone
from this culture living in a foreign city, in a quarter among their own
people, three generations removed from the heartland. If it requires the
heartland geography to exist, it belongs in Heartland Expression instead.*

### Core Values

The principles that organize how this culture thinks about the world.
Not rules — instincts. What feels right without needing to be argued for.

### Social Pattern

The arc from stranger to trusted relationship. What outsiders consistently
misread. What stays consistent regardless of which city they're in.

### Theology (Portable)

- **Core beliefs:** What is believed
- **Portable practice:** What is done without the heartland landmarks —
  the rites, objects, and habits that travel
- **Diaspora improvisation:** What a quarter community does when the
  heartland practice isn't available — the substitution, the absence,
  the ache

### Material Markers

Objects, dress elements, and practices that travel with the person and
mark them in any city, in any quarter. These are cultural identifiers,
not fashion.

### Clothing

Working and formal registers. What is consistent across heartland and
diaspora expressions. *Specific heartland dress details — materials tied
to local geography, craft traditions of a single city — go in the city
file.*

### Voice and Manner

Speech patterns, register, characteristic expressions. What a writer
needs to make this person sound like themselves on the page regardless
of setting.

### Naming Conventions

Given name patterns. Family or clan name logic. What names encode about
lineage, guild, or history.

## Coexistence and Contact

How this culture lives alongside others in shared city-states. Quarter
culture: how the quarter maintains itself, what it looks like from
inside and outside. Where commerce and contact happen across cultural
lines — neutral spaces, markets, shared infrastructure. What respect
without integration looks like in practice. What the rare crossover
cases cost the person who makes them.

*Do not speculate on why cultures don't blend — that is cosmology, not
culture. Describe the behavior. Leave the cause to cosmology files.*

---

## Narrative Identifiers

Quick-recognition for scene writing. What you notice about this person
before they speak, before they act.

*Must describe a person, not a place. If you wrote architecture or
geography, rewrite it.*

- **At a glance** (across a room, before any interaction)
- **Up close** (in conversation, in transaction)
- **The tell** (the one marker that persists even in a foreign city, even
  in unfamiliar clothing)
