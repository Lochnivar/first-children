# Astelli

**Earth Reference:** Renaissance Florence × Edo-period Japanese aesthetic tradition

---

## Overview

The Astelli are Iolanu's foremost botanical scholars — though they would never describe themselves that way. In their own estimation, they are *observers*: of the sky, of the soil, of the fine and largely inexplicable failures of neighboring families to plant correctly. That their terraces produce the most potent medicinal flora on Iolanu is, they will assure you, entirely incidental. A consequence of discipline. Hardly worth noticing.

Four centuries of obsessive inter-family rivalry, conducted entirely through horticulture, have made the Astelli accidentally indispensable to healers, apothecaries, and physicians across the continent. Nobody set out to cure diseases. They set out to win arguments. The medicine is a byproduct.

The honest truth — acknowledged behind screens, never in public — is that most Astelli would happily abandon the entire social machinery if the other families would let them. The screens, the sash protocols, the ceremonial cloaks, the oblique speech: ninety percent of them would trade all of it for a good season's planting and someone who actually wants to discuss why the Veil of Serrin affects moonpetal oil content. The ten percent who genuinely enjoy the performance have ruined it for everyone else. Everyone knows who they are. Nobody says so. That would be a protocol violation.

---

## The Core Tension

**Florentine ambition** drives Astelli culture: competitive display as a form of power, patronage as warfare, and the understanding that beauty is a weapon nobody calls a weapon. Astelli families have destroyed each other through aesthetics for generations — whose terrace is more refined, whose star-readings more precise, whose botanical lineage more unbroken.

**Edo Japanese aesthetic philosophy** determines *how* that competition is conducted: through restraint, discipline, and the performance of effortlessness. The most devastating Astelli garden is the one that looks as though it simply grew that way. Visible effort is a confession of failure. A terrace that is *trying* is a terrace whose family misread the sky and has been compensating ever since.

The synthesis: **ferocious competition expressed as performed serenity.** The Astelli are among the most politically vicious people on Iolanu, and they conduct their vendettas entirely in the language of flowers.

## Astronomical Horticultural Practice

The Astelli maintain that all meaningful botanical knowledge derives from astronomical observation. The sky tells you when to plant. The moons tell you when to harvest. The stars tell you which families have been lying about their star-tables for the last three generations.

Astelli families maintain private observatories — often integrated into their highest terrace architecture — and keep meticulous generational records of stellar positions correlated with botanical outcomes. These records are among the most jealously guarded documents in Iolanu. They are also the primary instrument of inter-family warfare.

### The Calendar Authority

Civic astronomical authority — the body that publishes the official planting calendar — is the most contested political position in Astelli society. Whoever holds it controls the information every family depends on. Errors in the official calendar, whether accidental or deliberate, are nearly impossible to prosecute: how do you prove someone misread the sky? You need your own data to contradict theirs. Which means every family maintains parallel records precisely so they can catch the Calendar Authority in a discrepancy — and every Calendar Authority knows this.

### What the Astronomy Actually Tracks

- Stellar rising and setting cycles for seasonal frost prediction
- Lunar phase correlations for harvest timing on specific plants
- Conjunction events tied to insect emergence (silk-moth larvae, pollinator cycles)
- Long-period stellar cycles correlated with soil moisture patterns

Whether the astronomical correlations reflect genuine causation or merely very consistent coincidence is a question the Astelli find both offensive and boring. *It works.* That is sufficient.

## The Argument Is Never About the Plant

When two Astelli families dispute whose silk-mulberry grove is superior, they are not arguing about the mulberry. They are arguing about whose *reading of the sky* is more correct, and whose *discipline in executing that reading* is more refined. The plant is the evidence. The garden is the proof of concept.

Aesthetic criticism is epistemological criticism.

*"Their eastern beds are quite full this season"* means they misread the Veil of Serrin's ingress and planted two weeks early to hedge their bet.

*"Their pruning is aggressive this year"* means they don't trust their own star-tables.

*"Their eastern beds are overcrowded"* means they panicked.

A garden that has been flawless for four generations is a four-hundred-year argument that one family has been reading the sky more correctly than everyone else. Other houses must either accept that claim or spend considerable effort finding the flaw. Both options are expensive.

## Medicinal Significance

The Astelli do not think of themselves as healers. They think of themselves as cultivators of exceptional precision. That their Shepherd's Starburst closes wounds, that their moonpetal extract reduces fever, that their Veil-rose tincture is the only reliable treatment for White Lung — these are facts they acknowledge the way one acknowledges the weather. Yes. Obviously. We planted correctly.

Healers across Iolanu have learned to navigate Astelli transaction culture: you are not purchasing medicine. You are purchasing *botanically excellent specimens cultivated under conditions of exceptional astronomical attunement.* What you do with them afterward is your own affair.

Maybe the astronomical timing genuinely matters. Maybe silk-moths harvested at third ascendance produce marginally better thread. Maybe the moonpetal really is more potent when planted after the Veil rises. The Astelli haven't been doing controlled studies — but four centuries of consistent practice may have landed on something real entirely by accident. Their snobbery is unfalsifiable from the outside, and they know it, and they enjoy that enormously.

### Key Medicinal Plants (Partial)

| Shepherd's Starburst | Wound closure, tissue repair | *Pastoris Stellata* | Harvested only when the second moon rises after last spring frost |
| Moonpetal | Fever reduction, inflammation | *Lunifolia* | Requires third-ascendance planting under the Veil of Serrin |

## The Sash System

The Astelli family sash is the primary public identity marker — worn over formal dress, draped deliberately, visible to anyone who knows how to read it. It functions on the same logic as tartan: pattern encodes lineage, specialty, and depth. Unlike tartan, it encodes astronomical and botanical information simultaneously.

### How to Read a Sash

- **Base color** — the family's primary botanical specialty. Deep green for leaf medicines, gold for flowering plants, blue-grey for night-harvested specimens, rust for root and bark work
- **Overcheck pattern** — the family's primary stellar association, the constellation that anchors their planting calendar
- **Accent thread** — generation depth. A single thread of silver worked into the weave for every century of unbroken records, fine enough that you have to know to look

An expert reads a sash the way they read a garden: completely, instantly, and without appearing to look at all. A stranger sees a well-made piece of cloth.

### Sash Protocol Violations

Wearing stellar associations your records don't support, or claiming a botanical specialty you haven't earned, is the Astelli equivalent of a forged noble title. Noticed immediately by anyone who matters. Never confronted directly. Simply remembered, and factored into every subsequent interaction.

A family that overclaims on their sash will find their trade inquiries answered more slowly. Their Calendar Authority nominations will attract unusual scrutiny. Their guests will be served tea slightly after everyone else. Nothing overt. Nothing prosecutable. Everything felt.

## The Screens

Every Astelli household owns a set of mobile privacy screens. They are maintained with the same care as any other horticultural tool. Their deployment is understood by every family in the neighborhood immediately and without comment.

### Exterior: Blank

The exterior face of every Astelli screen is plain. Not decorated with the family sash pattern. Not embroidered. Not finished beyond basic structural necessity. Raw linen, or something close to it.

This is not humility. It is a refusal. Every visual language in Astelli culture is legible to those who know how — sash patterns, embroidery staging, botanical pin arrangements, which specimens are displayed where. The blank screen opts out of all of it simultaneously. It does not say *this is our family.* It says *there is no family here for you to read. Move along.*

A family that put their sash colors on the exterior of their screens would be considered anxious. Needing to announce themselves even in the act of withdrawing. Other families would notice. File it away. The oldest, most established families have the most aggressively plain screens. The complete absence of effort where effort would be expected is the Astelli version of old money.

### Interior: Whatever the Family Wishes

Behind the blank exterior, the interior panels are entirely the family's own. Anything may be there. The family's actual aesthetic, unguarded. Children's handprints worked into the embroidery. A scene from the family's founding. The plants they genuinely love rather than the ones that win arguments.

The interior face is the only surface in an Astelli life where the performance is entirely optional. What a family puts there — or does not put there — is probably the most honest thing about them.

*The screens are up* carries its own meaning, understood by every Astelli: we are not performing right now. The neighborhood collectively agrees to unsee whatever happens behind them. It is a social contract enforced entirely by mutual self-interest, because every family puts their screens up eventually.

### What the Screens Enable

The soil on the hands. The pruning knife still out. Sitting close without managing how it looks. Talking about the actual garden rather than what the garden means. These things exist behind screens. They do not exist anywhere else.

The screens were probably invented by someone who just wanted to garden in peace. The blank exterior was probably invented by someone who was exhausted. And then the ten percent turned even *that* into a status signal — how plain are your screens, how deliberately nothing — and suddenly the escape from the performance became part of the performance.

There is no outside. There is only the garden, and the argument about the garden, and the brief stolen hours behind plain linen where you can just *be in the garden* before someone needs you to be an Astelli again.

## Public and Private: Two States

The Astelli exist in two entirely distinct registers. The transition between them is practiced until it is seamless.

### Private (Screens Up)

Working linen. Layered smocks, slim trousers or split skirts, practical pruning aprons. Hair dressed for function — braided back, seed pins threaded through practically rather than decoratively. Soil on the hands. Tools still out. Bodies at ease. Conversation direct. The garden discussed as itself rather than as argument.

This is the state most Astelli would choose permanently if the social architecture permitted it.

### Public (Screens Down, Guests Present)

Ceremonial cloaks over fitted formal dress, botanical embroidery facing outward — family specialty depicted at a specific growth stage that encodes meaning to other Astelli. Budding motif signals ambition. Full bloom is a statement of current dominance. Seed-stage is either humility or threat depending on context and who is watching.

The sash worn deliberately, its pattern legible to anyone who knows how. Hair formally dressed — pins arranged for display rather than function. A single gold or lacquered rod rather than practical seed-threaded braids.

Hands visible. Empty. No tools in evidence. The garden speaks for itself; they do not carry it on their persons when receiving.

If a specimen is held — and sometimes one is, as the reason guests were invited — the soil on the hands is the soil of *deliberate demonstration.* We have been working. See that we have been working. The plant they present is the argument they invited you to witness.

Same people. Completely different register. The transition costs nothing visible. Which is the point.

## Physical Characteristics

- **Skin:** Fair to light olive; indoor terrace workers carry a quality of filtered-light pallor, while observatory workers may have weather-touched complexions
- **Hair:** Dark brown to chestnut, kept neat; braided, coiled, or pinned with lacquered wood or Florentine ribbon — functional in private, formally dressed in public
- **Eyes:** Warm brown or green, with a characteristic quality of focused attention that reads as either intense interest or mild judgment. Often both.
- **Hands:** The primary Astelli tell in any register. Slender, precise fingers. Permanent soil staining in the nail beds and first knuckle creases. Calluses from pruning tools in patterns that identify terrace work from observatory work at a glance. Present in public as much as private. The Astelli do not hide that they garden. They simply change what the gardening *means*.

## Social Structure

Astelli society organizes around **family terrace lines** — extended kinship groups defined by shared astronomical records and horticultural tradition. Families are ranked not by wealth or military power but by the *unbroken continuity of their records* and the *consistency of their botanical outcomes.*

A family whose terrace records go back eight generations without a documented crop failure commands more respect than a wealthy newcomer regardless of comparative resources. This makes Astelli society simultaneously very stable — old families are nearly unassailable by conventional means — and very vulnerable. A single catastrophic public failure can unravel a family's position permanently. There are no second acts in Astelli horticulture.

### Inter-Family Dynamics

- **The Consultation** — families are occasionally asked to share star-table data. Accepting is gracious. Sharing accurate data is optional.
- **The Attribution Dispute** — formal challenge to a family's claim that a specific cultivation technique or astronomical correlation is original to their records. Extremely common. Never resolved quickly.
- **The Catastrophic Season** — when a family's terrace fails publicly, the response from neighboring families is elaborately sympathetic and completely merciless.

---

## Voice and Manner

Astelli speech is precise, layered, and relentlessly oblique when discussing anything that actually matters. Compliments are information-gathering exercises. Criticism is delivered as botanical observation. The unsaid is always the message. An Astelli who speaks directly is either very angry or has given up on you entirely.

> *"Oh, that Shepherd's Starburst? We only planted that when the second moon rose after the last gasp of spring. Hardly an effort worth noticing, really."*

> *"Their eastern beds are quite full this season. One wonders what they anticipated that didn't quite arrive."*

> *"House Verenthi's moonpetal is very vigorous this year. Remarkably so, given the Veil's position. I'm sure they have their reasons."*

---

## Naming Conventions

**Given names:** Florentine-inflected with Japanese sound patterns. Lilora, Tesshin, Camelvi, Morika, Aldenu, Fumetti, Seravine, Korullo.

**Family names:** Always hyphenated, combining a stellar reference with a botanical one. The star comes first. Velinaris-Ashpetal. Solcorro-Moonvine. Tarenthi-Ironleaf. Mirevane-Duskbell.

A family that names itself after its own medicinal specialty is making a claim. Other families notice.

---

## Relationship to the Wider World

The Astelli are not isolationist — they need buyers for their botanical output and maintain sophisticated trade relationships with healers, apothecaries, and noble houses across Iolanu. But they hold outsiders at a particular kind of arm's length: welcome as customers, tolerated as guests, never quite trusted as observers who might understand what they are actually seeing.

A healer who visits an Astelli terrace and asks the wrong questions will be answered with exquisite courtesy and almost no information. A healer who asks no questions and simply buys what is offered will be treated with marginally more warmth. A healer who demonstrates genuine astronomical knowledge will be watched very carefully and offered tea.

---

## World Role

The Astelli occupy the **primary medicinal supply niche** for Iolanu's more sophisticated settlements. They are not the only source of healing plants — folk traditions exist everywhere — but they are the source of reliable, consistent, potent botanical medicine that serious healers depend on for anything beyond basic care. This gives them quiet leverage, which they leverage very quietly indeed.

They are also, incidentally, the best astronomical record-keepers on Iolanu — a fact that occasionally becomes useful to people with interests beyond gardening, and which the Astelli are not above exploiting when the situation warrants.
