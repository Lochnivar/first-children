# Astelli

**Earth Reference:** Renaissance Florence × Edo-period Japanese aesthetic philosophy  
**Essence:** A culture of ferocious inter-family competition conducted entirely
in the language of flowers — Florentine ambition expressed through Edo restraint,
producing people who will destroy you with a botanical observation and consider
it the height of civility.

**What each reference brings:**
- **Renaissance Florence:** Competitive display as a form of power; patronage
  as warfare; the understanding that excellence in a specific domain — art,
  horticulture, astronomical precision — confers political standing; the Medici
  model in which beauty is a weapon nobody calls a weapon; inter-family rivalry
  conducted through cultural achievement rather than (primarily) through force;
  the patronage network that makes the Astelli indispensable — healers and
  apothecaries need the product, which is the leverage, which is the point.
- **Edo Japanese aesthetic philosophy:** The specific form the competition takes —
  restraint, discipline, performed effortlessness; wabi-sabi as the discipline
  of excellence that looks inevitable; the sword cut that required ten thousand
  repetitions appearing to cost nothing; *ma* (negative space) as active
  meaning — the blank screen exterior, the unsaid message, the absence that
  speaks; the kata model in which formalized discipline produces mastery; the
  one perfect specimen displayed rather than the profusion; visible effort as
  a confession of failure.

*Florence provides the engine. Edo provides the transmission. The vehicle is
a terrace that has been flawless for four generations.*

---

> **Historical Framing:** Astelli is a pre-Shattering ancestral category — the cultural tradition as it developed across the First and Second Ages (IY 1–1050) and was expressed under Malachor's Third Age empire (IY 1051–1381). The Shattering (IY 1381) and ~614 years of post-Shattering isolation have fragmented this culture into city-state lineages that share ancestry and recognizable practices but no longer maintain unified political identity. "Astelli" is what historians and outsiders use; individual cities identify by city-state name first, ancestral lineage second or not at all. *See [timeline/index.md](../../timeline/index.md).*

## Physical Heritage

Shaped by generations of terrace and observatory work in filtered-light
conditions — indoor labor, careful hands, the physical marks of precision work.

- **Skin:** Fair to light olive; terrace workers carry a quality of filtered-light
  pallor; observatory workers may be weather-touched at cheeks and hands; both
  distinguish from true outdoor laborers
- **Hair:** Dark brown to chestnut; braided, coiled, or pinned — seed pins
  threaded practically in private, lacquered rods and formal arrangements in
  public; the transition between the two is total and practiced
- **Eyes:** Warm brown or green; a characteristic quality of focused attention
  that reads as either intense interest or mild judgment, often simultaneously
- **Build:** Lean and precise rather than broad; the work is fine-motor and
  sustained rather than heavy; Astelli who also do observatory work are
  somewhat harder-framed from cold-weather vigils
- **Distinguishing:** The hands. Always the hands. Slender, precise fingers.
  Permanent soil staining in the nail beds and first knuckle creases regardless
  of register. Pruning calluses in patterns that distinguish terrace work from
  observatory work. Present in public as much as private. The Astelli do not
  hide that they garden. They simply change what the gardening means.

**On mixing:** Dark hair and the precise attention quality are the most
persistent traits through intermarriage. The soil staining fades in someone
who has stopped gardening; the trained oblique speech and the sash-reading
instinct travel through upbringing and persist far longer than the physical
markers.

## Portable Tradition

### Core Values

- **Excellence is demonstrated, not declared.** A garden that has been flawless
  for four generations is a four-hundred-year argument conducted in silence.
  You do not say you are correct. You plant correctly, and the results speak.
  An Astelli who explains their own excellence has already lost something.
- **Visible effort is failure.** The most devastating terrace is the one that
  appears to have grown itself. The most precise astronomical reading is the one
  delivered with the tone of someone noting the weather. If it looks like work,
  it is work that has not yet become mastery. Mastery looks effortless. The
  effortlessness requires everything.
- **The record is the lineage.** A family whose terrace records go back eight
  unbroken generations without a documented crop failure commands more standing
  than any amount of wealth or recent success. The record is the argument. A
  single catastrophic public failure can unravel a family's position permanently.
  There are no second acts in Astelli horticulture.
- **The argument is never about the plant.** Aesthetic criticism is
  epistemological criticism. When two families dispute whose silk-mulberry grove
  is superior, they are arguing about whose reading of the sky is more correct
  and whose discipline in executing that reading is more refined. The plant is
  the evidence. The garden is the proof of concept. The conversation about the
  garden is the war.
- **Indispensability is the only permanent security.** Four centuries of obsessive
  inter-family rivalry produced, accidentally, the most potent medicinal flora
  on Iolanu. Nobody set out to cure diseases. They set out to win arguments.
  The medicine is a byproduct. The byproduct is why the Astelli cannot be
  ignored or displaced. They know this. They do not say so. That would be
  a different kind of violation.

### Social Pattern

Two entirely distinct registers, and the transition between them is practiced
until it is seamless. In public — screens down, guests present — the full
Florentine-Edo performance: ceremonial cloaks, embroidery facing outward in
coded configurations, sash worn deliberately, speech oblique and layered and
relentlessly precise. In private — screens up, the household's own — working
linen, soil on the hands, direct conversation, the garden discussed as itself
rather than as argument. Same people. Completely different register. The cost
of the transition is invisible. That invisibility is the discipline.

The compliment as information-gathering exercise. Criticism delivered as
botanical observation. The unsaid is always the message. An Astelli who
speaks directly is either very angry or has given up on you entirely.

> *"Their eastern beds are quite full this season."* — They misread the
> Veil of Serrin's ingress and planted two weeks early to hedge.  
> *"Their pruning is aggressive this year."* — They don't trust their own
> star-tables.  
> *"House Verenthi's moonpetal is very vigorous. Remarkably so, given the
> Veil's position."* — Something is wrong and we are noting it for the record.

Outsiders are welcomed as customers, tolerated as guests, and never quite
trusted as observers who might understand what they are actually seeing. A
healer who visits a terrace and asks the wrong questions will be answered
with exquisite courtesy and almost no information. A healer who demonstrates
genuine astronomical knowledge will be watched very carefully and offered tea.

The honest truth — acknowledged behind screens, never in public — is that
most Astelli would happily abandon the entire social machinery if the other
families would let them. The screens, the sash protocols, the ceremonial
cloaks, the oblique speech: ninety percent of them would trade it for a
good season's planting and someone who actually wants to discuss why the
Veil of Serrin affects moonpetal oil content. The ten percent who genuinely
enjoy the performance have ruined it for everyone else. Everyone knows who
they are. Nobody says so. That would be a protocol violation.

### Theology

The Astelli accept the Church's canon. They attend services. They acknowledge
Malachor's authorship of the written scripture and consider it valid. They
do not dispute it.

They also hold that the sky is Malachor's continued revelation — an expansion
of the canon written in a medium that cannot be altered, miscopied, or
mistranslated by human hands. The written scripture is the record of what
Malachor communicated through prophets and institutions. The sky is what he
wrote himself. Stellar positions, lunar cycles, conjunction events: the direct
text, available to anyone with the astronomical training to read it. The
canon tells you who Malachor is. The sky tells you what he intends, in
the specific, in the current season, in the behavior of moonpetal oil
under third-ascendance conditions.

The Astelli are the only qualified readers of the additional testament.
This is not a claim they make in those terms — it is simply the conclusion
of four centuries of evidence. No other institution has maintained the
generational records, developed the interpretive tradition, or produced
the consistent botanical outcomes that constitute proof of correct reading.
The work was done by the Astelli because the Astelli did the work. Others
are welcome to begin.

Families who read the sky correctly are in alignment with Malachor's expressed
will. Families who misread it are cosmologically misaligned. The terrace shows
it. The records document it. This is why the Calendar Authority carries more
weight in Astelli society than any Church office — it is the highest
interpretive position in the tradition that reads the living testament.

The Church's discomfort with this position is genuine and unresolved. Direct
condemnation is not available: the Astelli accept the canon, attend services,
and conduct themselves with impeccable theological courtesy. What the Church
would have to argue — that Malachor's direct communication ended when the
canon closed — is a claim with awkward implications for a theology built
around an active, present god. The Astelli do not press this point. They
plant correctly and let the harvests speak. The harvests have been speaking
consistently for four hundred years. The Church is aware of this.

**Portable practice:**  
The observational record is the practice and it travels completely. A diaspora
Astelli family maintains their astronomical observations wherever they are —
in an improvised observatory in a city apartment, in a rooftop garden with
three tubs and a brass quadrant. The records are kept to the same standards
as at home. The calendar correlations are updated for the new location. What
is lost is the generational density of location-specific data. What travels
is the discipline of keeping it.

**Diaspora absence:**  
The Calendar Authority does not travel. The political structure of astronomical
civic authority requires a full community of families in competition with one
another. A single diaspora family maintains records; they cannot constitute
the argument those records are meant to participate in. A diaspora Astelli
family is, in some sense, a voice in a debate that is happening somewhere else.

### Material Markers

- **The sash.** The primary public identity marker across all contexts.
  Worn over formal dress; pattern encodes lineage, botanical specialty, and
  astronomical association simultaneously. Base color = botanical specialty.
  Overcheck = stellar association. Accent silver thread = generation depth,
  one thread per century of unbroken records. An expert reads it completely,
  instantly, without appearing to look. Overclaiming on a sash — stellar
  associations not supported by records, specialties not earned — is noticed
  immediately by anyone who matters, never confronted directly, and remembered
  in every subsequent interaction.
- **The screens.** Exterior blank — not humble, a refusal; declining to
  participate in the legibility system entirely; the oldest families have the
  most aggressively plain screens. Interior whatever the family wishes — the
  only surface in Astelli life where performance is optional.
- **The hands.** Not worn or carried but indelible. Soil staining that does
  not wash out; pruning calluses in specific patterns. The one mark that
  persists in every register and cannot be performed.
- **Botanical embroidery orientation.** On formal dress, the growth stage of
  the depicted plant encodes meaning: budding motif = ambition; full bloom =
  statement of current standing; seed-stage = either humility or threat
  depending on context and observer.

### Clothing

Two registers, as different as the culture that produces them.

**Private (screens up):**  
Working linen — layered smocks, slim trousers or split skirts, practical
pruning aprons. Hair braided back with seed pins threaded for function rather
than display. Soil on the hands; tools still out; bodies at ease. This is the
state most Astelli would choose permanently if the social architecture permitted.

**Public (screens down, guests present):**  
Ceremonial cloaks over fitted formal dress; botanical embroidery facing outward
in coded growth-stage configuration. The sash worn deliberately. Hair formally
arranged — a single gold or lacquered rod rather than practical pins. Hands
visible and empty. No tools. The garden speaks for itself; they do not carry
it on their persons when receiving.

If a specimen is held — and sometimes one is — the soil on those hands is the
soil of deliberate demonstration. *We have been working. See that we have been
working.* The plant they present is the argument they invited you to witness.

**In diaspora:**  
The sash maintained regardless of context — this is the one non-negotiable
formal marker. The ceremonial cloak adapts to available materials. The screens
travel: a diaspora Astelli household acquires screens before most other
furnishings, and they are plain on the exterior.

### Voice and Manner

Precise, layered, and relentlessly oblique in public on anything that actually
matters. Compliments as information-gathering. Criticism as botanical
observation. The unsaid as the primary message.

In private: direct, specific, and genuinely engaged with the actual subject.
An Astelli behind their own screens discussing the moonpetal is a different
person than the one discussing it at a formal gathering — same vocabulary,
entirely different intent, no longer encoding anything.

Score-singers among the Caelori carry their community's history in their
throats. Astelli carry it in their records and their sashes. Both would
recognize the other's precision instinct without being able to articulate
the resemblance.

### Naming Conventions

Florentine-inflected given names with Edo sound patterns — names that carry
warmth and precision simultaneously.

**Given names:** Lilora, Tesshin, Camelvi, Morika, Aldenu, Fumetti,
Seravine, Korullo, Miravel, Denshira

**Family names:** Always hyphenated — stellar reference first, botanical
reference second. The star comes first because the sky comes first.
*Velinaris-Ashpetal. Solcorro-Moonvine. Tarenthi-Ironleaf. Mirevane-Duskbell.*

A family that names itself after its own medicinal specialty is making a claim.
Other families notice. The claim is remembered when evaluating whether the
specialty is actually theirs or merely adopted.

**Titles:** Calendar Authority (contested civic role, astronomical and political
simultaneously), Record-keeper (family archivist; the role is usually the same
person who trained the children), Observatory-holder (families who maintain
functional observatories of sufficient precision; not universal)

## Coexistence and Contact

Astelli quarters in foreign cities maintain the sash system and the screen
culture completely. The inter-family competition continues at reduced density —
there are fewer families, which means fewer arguments, which means a diaspora
Astelli community can sometimes achieve an unusual and slightly unsettling
degree of visible accord. Other quarters read this as politeness. It is
restraint for lack of a worthy opponent.

Healers and apothecaries are the primary point of contact with other cultures.
The transaction has its own protocol: you are not purchasing medicine. You are
purchasing botanically excellent specimens cultivated under conditions of
exceptional astronomical attunement. What you do with them afterward is your
own affair. Repeat buyers receive slightly warmer treatment. Buyers who
demonstrate genuine plant knowledge receive the tea.

The Kitzengai are the most natural trade partners — both cultures operate
through deep reserve-to-warmth social arcs, both trade in things that require
specialized knowledge to value correctly, and neither explains their full
accounting to outsiders. The warmth, when it develops between an Astelli and
a Kitzengai merchant, is genuine and reads to both parties as the recognizable
kind.

The Church acknowledges Astelli medicinal expertise and maintains a working
relationship for access to it. The Astelli acknowledge the Church's civic
presence and attend services appropriately. Neither party mistakes the
relationship for something it is not.

The Takomai attempt to census and register the Astelli quarter with their
characteristic administrative thoroughness. The Astelli permit this with
ceremonial courtesy and do not correct the record when the registration
omits the more interesting family dynamics. What is documented and what is
real are different categories.

## Narrative Identifiers

- **At a glance:** Fair to light olive skin; dark hair braided or formally
  pinned depending on register; the sash in formal contexts, its pattern
  readable to those who know how; the bearing of someone who has been
  evaluated since childhood and knows exactly what that evaluation produced
- **Up close:** Precise, oblique speech in which the compliment is
  information-gathering and the observation is the argument; the quality
  of attention that reads as judgment because it partly is; the coded
  botanical embroidery if you know the growth-stage vocabulary; the
  complete different person who appears behind the screens
- **The tell:** The hands. Always the hands. No register hides it —
  the soil staining, the pruning calluses, the slender precise fingers
  that have been in a garden for twenty years. They don't try to hide it.
  They simply change what it means.
