# Losceati

> **STATUS: PROTO-CULTURE (INTERRUPTED)**  
> The Losceati split from Llosca in the Second Age (~IY 800–1000) and were actively
> developing toward their own kingdom when Malachor's Third Age conquest (~IY 1051)
> absorbed them before they could consolidate. They have been carrying the bones of
> an unfinished culture for ~946 years — the identity is real, the institutional depth
> never happened. "Losceati" is what neighbors call them. They have not yet decided
> what they call themselves, because the moment they were about to was taken from them.
> The Losceatic League is not a new idea. It is the second attempt at the first one.
> Treat all sections below as directional rather than definitive; this file should
> develop further as the League narrative progresses.

**Earth Reference:** Occitan troubadour tradition × Arabian Nights merchant storytelling  
**Parent Culture:** Llosca (Second Age schism, ~IY 800–1000 — the break is behavioral and aesthetic rather than theological, but it was sharp enough to constitute a schism: these communities chose a different expression of the inherited theology and did not return)  
**Secondary Influences:** Maneth covenant culture and Caelori oral-tradition posture, absorbed through Sea of Malachor trade  
**Essence:** A people who inherited Llosca desire-theology and redirected its expression from the body to the word — the same intensity, the same sacredness, aimed entirely at poetry, courtship, and the well-crafted story.

**What each reference brings:**
- **Occitan troubadour:** The romantic-as-sacred framework — fin'amor as discipline
  and art form rather than expression and release; the traveling poet-merchant who
  is simultaneously commercial and artistic without finding the categories distinct;
  Courts of Love as genuine juridical bodies (romantic promises adjudicated as
  seriously as trade contracts); desire expressed through poetry considered more
  refined, not less intense, than desire expressed through the body. This is the
  form the Llosca inheritance took when it contacted a colder climate and a more
  structured set of influences.
- **Arabian Nights merchant storytelling:** The Scheherazade dynamic — story as
  survival, story as negotiation, story as the thing that keeps you alive in a
  difficult room; the traveling narrative as commerce; the aesthetic register
  (layered silks, ornamentation, perfume, rich fabric) retained from the Llosca
  inheritance but redirected: suggestion through layering and depth rather than
  through exposure; the understanding that a well-told story is a form of power
  and that the person who holds the narrative controls the room.

**What the secondary influences add:**
- **Maneth:** Covenant seriousness — a romantic promise, once made in public, carries
  the same binding weight as a signed trade agreement. The Courts of Romantic
  Adjudication are a Maneth-adjacent institution: breach of romantic covenant
  is not a personal matter, it is a public one with witnesses and findings. This
  is what keeps Losceati courtship from being merely decorative.
- **Caelori:** The understanding that words, once spoken aloud with intent, carry
  weight that cannot be recalled; the resistance posture toward Church institutional
  authority absorbed without ever being consciously adopted; the precision in
  language that makes Losceati poetry function as the Caelori oral tradition does —
  a record that is also an obligation.

> **Historical Framing:** Losceati is a **Second Age schismatic culture, interrupted before completion**. The break from Llosca occurred ~IY 800–1000; these communities were actively developing toward a distinct kingdom when Malachor's Third Age conquest (~IY 1051) absorbed them before consolidation. The empire administered them as Llosca-adjacent, refusing to recognize a distinction that hadn't yet hardened into formal institutions. The Shattering (IY 1381) ended the empire but delivered 614 years of post-apocalyptic survival rather than the breathing room to finish the work. The Losceatic League (present-day saga) is their second attempt at what was interrupted ~946 years ago — not a new ambition but a very old unfinished one. *See [timeline/index.md](../../timeline/index.md).*

## Physical Heritage

Primarily Llosca inheritance. The Losceati were developing toward a distinct
kingdom — and the geographic consolidation that would have driven physical
differentiation — when Malachor's conquest interrupted the process. They have
existed as a distinct population for ~600–900 years but never had the settled
homeland that accelerates divergence. Physical traits remain close to Llosca
baseline; cultural markers differentiate more reliably than genetics.

- **Skin:** Warm olive to golden bronze — Llosca baseline; unchanged
- **Hair:** Dark, often elaborately styled; the Losceati aesthetic tends toward
  romantic ornamentation — small woven tokens, flowers, pinned verse-fragments,
  courtship gifts that have been given a permanent place
- **Eyes:** Deep and expressive; the Llosca inheritance; Losceati eye contact
  carries the same intensity Llosca physical contact does — the direction
  of desire changed, not the force of it
- **Build:** The Llosca posture training is present, redirected; Losceati carry
  themselves like people who expect to be listened to — not the movement-oriented
  Llosca presentation, but a stillness oriented toward voice and word
- **Distinguishing:** At this stage of the culture's development, clothing and
  ornament are more reliable identifiers than face or build

**On mixing:** Too early to characterize meaningfully. The physical baseline
is Llosca. What distinguishes a Losceati from a Llosca in a crowd is not
genetics — it is the conservative dress register and the particular orientation
of their attention.

## Portable Tradition

### Core Values

- **Desire is sacred. The body is optional.** The Llosca foundational premise is
  fully inherited: desire is not weakness, pleasure is not shameful, the erotic
  is not a problem requiring management. What changed is the medium. The Losceati
  express desire through language, story, and courtship ritual with the same
  conviction the Llosca bring to physical expression. Neither culture would
  understand the other's version as inferior. The intensity is identical.
- **A promise made in public is a covenant.** Romantic commitment is not personal
  sentiment — it is a witnessed declaration with social force. The Courts of
  Romantic Adjudication are not charming eccentricity. They are what happens
  when Maneth covenant culture seeps into a desire-theology: breach of romantic
  promise becomes a matter of community concern. This is the most distinctly
  Losceati value, with no direct Llosca precedent.
- **Commerce and art are the same practice.** The careful arrangement of valuable
  things to produce a desired effect: this describes a cargo negotiation and a
  love poem in identical terms. The troubadour-merchant who composes a verse
  about the day's trading by evening is not switching disciplines. They are
  continuing the same one.
- **The story is the power.** A person who controls the narrative controls the
  room. This is practical knowledge, not metaphor. The Scheherazade instinct
  — telling the story that keeps you alive, indefinitely — is a commercial
  and a social tool simultaneously.
- **Resistance without declaration.** The Losceati absorbed the Caelori and Maneth
  resistance posture toward Church authority without choosing it or naming it.
  They operate on their own terms. They are technically within the faith. They
  do not announce the contradiction. Neither does the resistance require announcing.

### Social Pattern

Warm, unhurried, and attentive — the Llosca inheritance in social manner, less
physically forward. The deliberate presence is still there; the scent still arrives
before the face; the eye contact still carries weight. But where a Llosca might
reach for physical contact early in an encounter, a Losceati reaches for the
perfect sentence.

The merchant-artist posture: they are reading the room — its stories, its
dynamics, its unresolved tensions — before they engage with it. A Losceati at a
gathering has assessed the narrative geometry of the space before they have decided
what they want from it.

Warm to strangers in a way that is also strategic. Genuinely hospitable; the
warmth is real; the assessment happening behind it is equally real. Both are
operating simultaneously. The Losceati would not find this contradictory.

Courtship as public art form. A declaration made in front of witnesses carries
more weight than one made privately. The public romantic declaration is the most
culturally valued form of expression — earnest, ritualized, and not the least
bit private. This is what replaced the Burmallosca model, and it replaced it
structurally: equally witnessed, equally committed, conducted with words instead
of bodies.

### Theology

**Inherited framework:**  
The Llosca desire-theology is the foundation and it has not been rejected.
Desire is sacred. Pleasure is a pathway to something larger. The erotic impulse
is not a problem to be managed — it is a force to be cultivated and directed.
The Losceati did not dispute any of this. They redirected the expression.

What they practice is something close to a third stream — neither the Canaanite
public expression (Burmallosca) nor the Sufi private mysticism (Llosnagar), but
a courtly expression: desire as art form, the romantic pursuit as sacred practice,
the perfectly composed declaration as the equivalent of the rite.

The Hierarchy of Lust cannot condemn this. They have been trying for two centuries.
The Losceati have not rejected the theology; they have simply found a form of
expression that the doctrine, built around physical practice, does not quite
address. The argument remains unresolved because neither side has the vocabulary
to resolve it cleanly.

**Portable practice:**  
The troubadour tradition is the primary spiritual practice, though the Losceati
would not use that word. A public declaration of love, a poem composed for a
specific person and performed in front of witnesses, a song cycle that takes a
year to complete — these function as the Llosca rite functions: worship through
the act of fully expressing desire. The form changed. The intention did not.

The Courts of Romantic Adjudication serve a theological function they would
not articulate in those terms: they make romantic commitment binding, witnessed,
and communally enforced. The sacred quality of the desire is what makes breach
a matter of community concern rather than personal disappointment.

**What the Hierarchy cannot administer:**  
The Losceati relationship to the Hierarchy of Lust is friction without resolution.
They were never excommunicated — they never apostatized. The drift is too gradual,
the theological claim too defensible, the actual practice too far from what the
doctrine explicitly forbids. The Hierarchy watches them the way a creditor watches
a borrower who is technically current on payments while clearly running an
operation the creditor doesn't understand.

**Portable theology note:**  
The scent is retained as non-negotiable Llosca inheritance. A Losceati without
scent is not performing the tradition, regardless of what they're wearing or
what they've declared.

### Material Markers

- **Romantic tokens.** Small woven, embroidered, or written objects exchanged
  during courtship; visible on clothing as an ongoing record — current courtships
  displayed; concluded ones retired to private wear. The token is a commitment
  made tangible. Under Maneth-influenced covenant thinking, displaying a token
  publicly is a witnessed statement.
- **Verse on cloth.** The emerging practice of embroidering meaningful lines into
  garment borders, cuffs, or linings. The line may be from a famous cycle or from
  a specific exchange; other Losceati read them. The garment as record: a Caelori
  impulse expressed in Llosca aesthetics.
- **Scent.** Inherited directly from Llosca; treated as inseparable from dress.
  The specific scent is personal; its presence is not optional.
- **The troubadour instrument.** Portable, small enough for a merchant's pack;
  the specific type varies by city; its presence marks a person as a practitioner
  of the tradition.

### Clothing

The full Arabian Nights aesthetic vocabulary, at the conservative end — not
the Llosca dress standard, but not its opposite either. Rich material, deliberate
layering, strong ornamentation; suggestion through depth and movement rather
than through exposure.

- **Aesthetic register:** Layered silks and translucents; the layers create depth,
  not coverage in the concealing sense — fabric that moves and suggests simultaneously,
  that invites attention to what it implies without revealing what it covers
- **Ornamentation:** Embroidered motifs with narrative content — a garment border
  that tells a story in miniature; coin and bead elements inherited from Llosca
  but worn at the wrist and hem rather than at the hip
- **Color:** Rich and deliberate; not the muted Maneth palette; strong jewel tones
  that read as Llosca heritage without the inverted dress standard
- **The Llosca dress standard:** Absent in public expression. The theological
  inheritance is present; the specific practice of the inverted standard is not.
  In private, between individuals who have fully exchanged intention and commitment,
  it may appear — but this is intimate rather than cultural
- **Scent:** Applied as part of dressing; the final layer; inherited from Llosca
  without modification
- **Romantic tokens:** Worn visibly; their placement on the body is communicative —
  other Losceati read the display

### Voice and Manner

Warm and deliberate — the Llosca social manner with less physical expressiveness
and more verbal precision. The same underlying attention; the same quality of
being fully present; the same reading of the exchange as it unfolds. Expressed
through language rather than through body.

Words chosen carefully. The Caelori inheritance here is invisible but present:
the understanding that words once spoken carry weight, that the well-crafted
sentence is a kind of covenant, that approximate language is a kind of carelessness
the tradition does not permit.

The merchant-artist register: they are simultaneously assessing the commercial
and narrative potential of any encounter. These are not separate analyses.

Eye contact carries the Llosca intensity but longer than Llosca — it is
the primary physical expressiveness that remains. A Losceati holds your gaze
the way the Llosca hold your presence.

Not effusive. The warmth is genuine and it is also structured — the Arabian
Nights storyteller register, where hospitality and narrative and negotiation
are conducted simultaneously, the host always aware of what the room needs
to hear next.

### Naming Conventions

Llosca Semitic-Canaanite roots with Romance (Occitan/French) phonetic drift —
hybrid forms that read as neither parent language cleanly.

- Double consonants flowing into Romance equivalents

## Coexistence and Contact

- **Llosca:** The parent culture watches with alarm it cannot fully articulate.
  The Hierarchy of Lust considers the Losceati drift heretical in practice without
  being able to document specific doctrinal deviation. Burmallosca's discomfort
  with Poillorteau is the sharpest expression of this. The Losceati relationship
  to orthodox Llosca is one of mutual recognition with significant mutual
  discomfort: they know what they came from; neither side is entirely comfortable
  with what it became.
- **Church of Malachor:** Technically within the faith. Nobody denounced Malachor.
  The Church reads the drift as insufficient orthodoxy at best, incipient heresy
  at worst. Missionary pressure is building. The Losceati receive this with the
  same posture they absorbed from Caelori and Maneth commerce: politely, without
  adjustment.
- **Locquodian Order:** Since the formal declaration of the Losceatic League,
  an active target. Three cities is a problem; four is a proto-nation. Their
  methods will be indirect — the Order does not march on trading cities with
  good legal frameworks.
- **Caelori and Maneth:** The source of the resistance posture the Losceati
  absorbed without intending to. The relationship is commercial, not ideological.
  Neither party knows they agree on anything in particular. They simply keep
  finding each other at the same fairs and finding the commerce easy.
- **Takomai:** Trade relationships; the Takomai mercantile tradition finds Losceati
  merchants professionally legible, culturally unusual, and the Courts of Romantic
  Adjudication endlessly baffling.

## Narrative Identifiers

- **At a glance:** Llosca coloring (warm olive to golden skin, dark hair) in a
  conservative register — full Arabian Nights aesthetic vocabulary (layered silks,
  ornamentation, scent) without the exposed dress standard; romantic tokens visible
  on clothing; the garment telling a story in its border work if you can read it
- **Up close:** The Llosca warmth, expressed entirely through language and eye
  contact; words chosen with visible care; the merchant and the poet are the same
  person and not attempting to conceal this; the eye contact holds longer than
  expected
- **The tell:** When something significant happens — a deal, a declaration, a
  breach — the Losceati reach for language before anything else. Not to describe
  what happened. To frame it. To find the sentence that makes it mean the right
  thing. The Caelori touch their tattoo to locate themselves in their lineage.
  The Losceati reach for the word that will carry what just happened forward.
