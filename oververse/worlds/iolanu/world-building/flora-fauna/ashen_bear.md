# The Ashen Bear

**Category:** Fauna / Heraldry  
**Related Locations:** Manchire's northern reaches (steep fjords, mist-laced pine forests, icy riverbanks)  
**Related Characters:** House Lochnivar, Jason Lochnivar

## Overview
The Ashen Bear is a large, solitary predator native to Manchire's northern reaches, and serves as the sigil of House Lochnivar. Known for ritualistic mourning behavior and fierce territoriality, the bear embodies the values of unyielding devotion, sacrificial strength, and the burden of clarity.

## Biological Profile

### Habitat
Steep fjords, mist-laced pine forests, and icy riverbanks of Manchire's northern reaches.

### Appearance
Thick, pale-gray fur with streaks of charcoal along the spine and forelimbs. Eyes are deep-set and pale blue, often mistaken for blindness in low light.

### Size
Larger than standard bears—males reach up to 9 feet when standing. Their bulk is deceptive; they move with eerie silence across snow and stone.

### Behavior
- Solitary except during mating season or when defending cubs
- Known for ritualistic mourning—they return to the site of a fallen mate or cub for days, emitting low, rhythmic growls
- Fiercely territorial, but will not attack unless provoked or challenged directly

## Cultural Significance

### House Lochnivar Sigil
The Ashen Bear mid-charge, one paw bloodied, eyes glowing white. Represents unyielding devotion, sacrificial strength, and the burden of clarity. Banner is black and white, with the bear rendered in negative space—white on black, black on white depending on context.

### Rituals & Lore
- **The Trial of the Ashen Maw:** Lochnivar heirs must spend a night in the fjords, unarmed, to prove they can face duty without flinching.
- **Bearblood Oath:** Rare and sacred—sworn only when a Lochnivar commits to a cause that may cost their life or soul. The oath is sealed with a drop of blood on a carved bear tooth.

### Common Saying
"The Ashen Bear does not roar. It remembers."

## Notes
The bear's behavior of ritualistic mourning and silent strength mirrors the values of House Lochnivar—duty, memory, and quiet resolve.
