# The Big 'G' God (True Deity)

**Category:** Deity / Cosmology  
**Status:** Canon - Foundation  
**Related Files:** [Marcionite Theological Inspiration](marcionite_theology.md), [Zeta / Malachor](zeta_god.md), [Nala](nala_goddess.md)

## Overview
The Big 'G' God is the true deity who created the universe. This is a **deistic** God—a "watchmaker" who created the universe, wound it up, and stepped away. The Big G God is not actively involved in the affairs of Iolinus or the conflict between Nala and Malachor.

## Nature
- **Status:** True deity (big 'G' God)
- **Relationship to Universe:** Creator of all existence
- **Philosophy:** Deistic—created, wound up, stepped away
- **Involvement:** Not actively involved in world events
- **Relationship to Little 'g' Gods:** Parent to all the little 'g' gods (first children)

## The Little 'g' Gods
- **Nala and Malachor** are both "little 'g' gods"—first children of the Big 'G' God
- They are essentially **aliens assuming deistic identities** (background detail only)
- They have the power of **Quantumancy** (all five quantum abilities combined)
- **Malachor is more blatant** about assuming a god identity than Nala
- **Note for Story:** Details about the nature/origin of the First Children (what they "really are," how they came to be, etc.) are out of scope—the reader doesn't need to dig into the details. They are simply gods with godly powers for the purposes of the story.

## Theological Framework
- The Big G God is the true creator, but uninvolved (deistic)
- Nala and Malachor are little 'g' gods with godly powers
- The Marcionite inspiration works as a framework for understanding the conflict

## Notes
- The Big G God is not a character in the story—it's the distant, deistic creator
- All the action happens at the "little 'g'" level
- The prohibition against creating Iolinus came from the Big G God (or its natural laws)
- Details about what the little 'g' gods "really are" are out of scope—they are simply gods with godly powers
