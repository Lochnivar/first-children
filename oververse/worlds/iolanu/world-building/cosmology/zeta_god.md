# Zeta / Malachor, God of Iolinus

**Category:** Deity / Divine Being  
**Related World:** Iolinus (his creation)  
**Related Files:** [Zeta/Malachor Naming](zeta_malachor_naming.md), [Malachor and the Ebon Throne](malachor_faith.md), [Marcionite Theological Inspiration](marcionite_theology.md), [The Big 'G' God](big_g_god.md), [Quantum-Adjacent Abilities](quantum_abilities.md)

## Overview
Zeta (also known as Malachor in the current age) is Nala's brother, a god (little 'g') who created the world of Iolinus against explicit prohibition. He has tied his life force to his creation, making him invulnerable and holding all life on Iolinus hostage. The names Zeta and Malachor represent the same entity, with the name progressing through different "ages of man" - Zeta is the earlier name, Malachor is the current name.

**Theological Foundation:** Zeta/Malachor functions as the "Demiurge" in a Marcionite-inspired cosmology - the lesser, imperfect creator god who created the material world against the True God's (big G) prohibition. See [Marcionite Theological Inspiration](marcionite_theology.md) and [The Big 'G' God](big_g_god.md) for the full theological framework.

## Nature and Power
- **Status:** God (little 'g'), part of the ancient immortal race; first children of the big 'G' God
- **Background Detail (Out of Scope):** Essentially an **alien assuming a deistic identity** (more blatantly than Nala) - this is background world-building only
- **Powers:** Has all the powers that the other gods possess
- **Quantum Powers:** Has the power of **Quantumancy** - all five quantum abilities combined (Quantumancers, Readers, Weavers, Echoes, Nullwraiths all in one)
- **Personality:** Immature, spoiled brat; lacks compassion and wisdom to treat his creation properly; lacks the desire to gain that wisdom
- **Cleverness:** Despite his faults, he is very clever
- **Note for Story:** Details about what the First Children "really are," their origins, or other background mechanics are out of scope for the reader—they are simply gods with godly powers in the story

## The Creation of Iolinus
- **Forbidden Act:** Iolinus was created against explicit prohibition
- **Circumstances:** Created when the attention of the other gods waned; they failed to keep a close enough eye on him
- **Result:** To their shame, they failed to stop him before he created it

## The Hostage Situation
- **Life Force Binding:** Zeta tied his life force to his creation
- **Invulnerability:** He cannot be killed—even if Nala managed to kill him, his death would extinguish all life on Iolinus
- **Hostages:** Millions of sentient, intelligent beings are held hostage
- **Awareness:** He is aware of direct challenges—if he feels threatened, he'll abandon his playground and all those people will die

## The World of Iolinus
- **Description:** Two massive continents straddling the equator
- **Features:** Massive mountain ranges, lush vegetation, a rather large desert (though most is verdant)
- **Other Lands:** There are a few other lands on the far side, but they aren't important to the current conflict
- **Status:** This is where Nala and her agents are fighting

## Why He Can't Be Punished
- Direct punishment would kill all life on Iolinus
- He knows he is holding them hostage
- He is clever enough to have set up this situation
- The other gods cannot take direct action without killing millions

## Notes
- Zeta's death = death of all life on Iolinus
- He must be handled carefully—if threatened, he'll abandon everything and all will die
- The mission against him must be quiet and hidden until it's strong enough
- He is aware of direct divine intervention on Iolinus
