## The Tragic Misunderstanding

### Rowan's Perspective
- Thinks he's teaching a wayward Maogreki daughter
- Believes Gabby needs to learn respect for the ink
- Sees her growth as acceptance of heritage
- Gives approval when she finally shows respect
- Never discovers the truth

### Gabby's Reality
- Knows she's an Earth soul in a donated body
- Has to fake reverence while panicking
- Can never correct the misunderstanding (operational security)
- Develops quiet gratitude: "Thank you for the loan. I'll try to be worth it."
- Never tells him the truth

### The Depth
"The misunderstanding is now the deepest, quietest knife in Book 1."
- Rowan thinks he's teaching a prodigal daughter
- Gabby knows she's a tourist borrowing ancestors
- Neither will ever speak the truth
- Respect earned, identity unchanged, truth hidden
