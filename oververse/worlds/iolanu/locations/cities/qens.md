# Qens

**Culture:** TBD (mixed maritime; significant Pelessar presence)  
**Location:** Portage Strait (mid-strait; inside the fallen head of the Colossus of Malachor)  
**Type:** City-state built inside the fallen head of the Colossus of Malachor  
**Narrative Role:** Major landmark; the most visible evidence of what the Shattering physically destroyed; sits at the geographic site of the Thohalla Crossing Disaster

---

## Overview

The Portage Canal was one of the Third Age empire's defining engineering achievements — a north-south passage cut through the desert isthmus between two bodies of water, eliminating the land crossing that had killed thousands of Thohalla in the Second Age. Every ship moving between the northern and southern seas ran through it. Malachor commissioned the canal and then built his own image astride it: the Colossus of Malachor, legs planted on either bank, the canal running between them. Every ship that passed through the empire's most strategic trade chokepoint passed beneath a god's groin.

That was the point.

The isthmus itself carried specific weight. This was the Portage Desert — the ground where Malachor had led his chosen people on the Crossing, with seawater on both sides, and watched more than half of them die of thirst. He built his monument there. Whether this was acknowledgment, erasure, or simply indifference is the kind of question Malachor's theology does not answer cleanly.

When the Shattering came, the wound in reality tore open at the canal. The waterway widened catastrophically — what had been a navigable canal became the Portage Strait, shallow and treacherous. The Colossus could not stand across a wider gap. It split along the fault of its own weight: legs and torso fragmenting into the water on both sides, body pieces becoming the shallow islands that now stud the strait. The head separated and fell mid-water, cracked open on impact, and stayed.

Qens is the city built inside the skull.

---

## The Portage Ruins

Where the Colossus stood, Portage once stood with it — a canal city built to service the empire's most important waterway. The Shattering split it.

Eleish and Yelish are what remain: ruins on either bank of the strait, most of them uninhabited, some of them squatted in by people who have no better options. The widening of the canal destroyed the bridges; neither side has been reliably accessible to the other since IY 1381. Monster pressure keeps serious settlement from recovering. The ruins are substantial — this was an imperial city at a strategic chokepoint — and they are slowly being reclaimed by the desert on one side and the encroaching shoreline on the other.

The Portage Strait runs between them: shallow, irregular, dangerous to navigate without local knowledge. The body fragments of the Colossus rise through the water as low islands, some barely above the waterline at high tide, some large enough for a Church shrine. The Church considers the entire strait a sacred ruin. The pilots who navigate it consider it a working hazard.

## Approach and Entry

From the strait, the head reads as a low island. The cracked crown forms a ring of raised stone around a hidden interior; the harbor within is invisible until you're inside it. There is no indication from the outside that a city is present.

The entrance is the ear canal — an elongated arched tunnel through which ships sail blind. The passage curves; you navigate the curve without seeing the interior until you clear it. Pilots are required. Pilots who know the ear canal do not share their notes.

Once through, the harbor opens: vast, open to sky, the interior surface of the skull forming the walls. Those walls are the back of Malachor's carved features looking inward. The city is terraced up the concave interior face of the statue — massive tiers rising like a stone amphitheater, the carved eye sockets and mouth visible as structural features above the waterline. The highest terraces sit at the rim of the cracked crown, looking out over the strait and the shallow island field where the Colossus's body lies.

Standing at harbor level, you look up at the interior of a god's face and open sky simultaneously.

## The Body Islands

The Colossus's shattered body became the shallow island field that now studs the Portage Strait. The Church maintains shrines on the larger body-fragment islands and considers the fallen statue a sacred ruin — the physical expression of what the Shattering cost. Pilgrims travel to the islands. Several of the larger fragments have permanent Church presences.

The island where the Colossus's right hand landed is the largest single fragment; the Church maintains its primary shrine complex there. At low tide, the articulated stone fingers are visible above the waterline.

Qens considers the head real estate and the island field a navigational inconvenience. The theological question of whether living inside Malachor's skull is sacred or presumptuous has not been formally resolved. Qens does not appear interested in resolving it.

---

## Navigation

Two separate pilot specializations:
- **Ear canal pilots:** Navigate the entrance tunnel. The curve of the passage and the blind approach require someone who has done it before. Outsiders attempting the passage without a pilot do not receive sympathy.
- **Strait pilots:** Navigate the body-fragment shallows of the Portage Strait. The islands are partially charted; the shallows between them are not. A third of the charts in circulation are wrong in ways that are not immediately apparent.

Both specializations are licensed by Qens. The licensing fees are not excessive. The pilots' rates are.

---

## The Church Presence

Shrines on the body islands; a mission presence at Qens itself, where the Church maintains that the cracked skull is a site of theological significance and that the residents' relationship to it requires pastoral guidance. The residents have been receiving this guidance for centuries. They have not changed their behavior in any documented way.

The theological question the Church keeps returning to: is Qens an act of hubris toward Malachor, or an act of devotion (dwelling inside the god's image, keeping it inhabited, maintaining it)? Qens has partisans for both interpretations and shows no sign of settling the question.

The Crossing Disaster complicates this further. The Thohalla consider the Portage Desert their most painful ground — the place Malachor led them to die. The Church's insistence that the Colossus's presence consecrates the isthmus registers with the Thohalla as exactly the kind of theological move Malachor would make. The Thohalla do not say this directly. The Bound Tongue governs what is said about theology to outsiders.

## Theological Weight of the Site

Every element at the Portage Strait is load-bearing:

The **Crossing Disaster** (Second Age) happened on this isthmus — Malachor led his chosen people across the desert with saltwater on both sides and watched over half of them die of thirst, then converted the deaths into religious ritual that blamed the victims. The Thohalla drink saltwater to remember it. This ground is their most painful geography.

The **Colossus** (Third Age) was Malachor's monument built over that mass grave — his most grandiose statement of dominion, at his most strategic trade chokepoint, at the exact site of his greatest theological abuse. Whether this was acknowledgment, erasure, or simple indifference is the kind of question his theology does not answer cleanly.

The **Shattering** tore the Colossus apart and split the city that served it. The wound in reality expressed itself here physically.

**Eleish and Yelish** have spent 614 years arguing about the Colossus's architectural legacy — generating theological literature about a site they will not acknowledge is a graveyard. The Takomai bureaucratic inheritance produces exactly this: extensive documentation of the wrong question.

**Qens** does commerce inside the skull, indifferent to both the feud and the theology.

The **Thohalla pass through all of it.** They are the only culture mobile enough to transit the strait regularly. Every caravan that runs the Portage Strait sails past the ruins of Malachor's monument to the crossing that killed their ancestors — and past two cities that have spent six centuries arguing about architectural legitimacy on ground the Thohalla drink saltwater to remember. The Bound Tongue governs what gets said about theology to outsiders. The Thohalla say very little. Then they leave.

**Chance Hodgkins** witnessed the Crossing Disaster here (~IY 600s). He triggered the Shattering that destroyed the Colossus here (IY 1381). The geography has his fingerprints on both ends of the timeline.
