# Qens

**Culture:** TBD (mixed maritime; significant Pelessar presence)  
**Location:** Sea of Malachor (island; mid-sea)  
**Type:** City-state built inside the fallen head of the Colossus of Malachor  
**Narrative Role:** Major landmark; the most visible evidence of what the Shattering physically destroyed

---

## Overview

The Colossus of Malachor was a statue commissioned at the height of the God-King era to span the Sea of Malachor — one foot on A'Miz, one foot on A'Maar, built by Quantumancer labor. Every ship crossing the sea sailed beneath it. You could not traverse the Sea of Malachor without passing under Malachor's image. That was the point.

When the Shattering came, the continents shifted apart. The Colossus could not span a wider gap. It fell. The body shattered into the island chain that now dots the sea. The head landed mid-water, cracked open, and stayed there.

Qens is the city built inside the skull.

---

## Approach and Entry

From a distance, the head reads as an atoll. The cracked crown forms a ring of raised stone around a hidden interior; the harbor within is invisible until you're inside it. There is no indication from the outside that a city is present.

The entrance is the ear canal — an elongated arched tunnel through which ships sail blind. The passage curves; you navigate the curve without seeing the interior until you clear it. Pilots are required. Pilots who know the ear canal do not share their notes.

Once through, the harbor opens: vast, open to sky, the interior surface of the skull forming the walls. Those walls are the back of Malachor's carved features looking inward. The city is terraced up the concave interior face of the statue — massive tiers rising like a stone amphitheater, the carved eye sockets and mouth visible as structural features above the waterline. The highest terraces sit at the rim of the cracked crown, looking out over the sea where the Colossus once stood.

Standing at harbor level, you look up at the interior of a god's face and open sky simultaneously.

## The Body Islands

The Colossus's shattered body became the island chain that now scatters across the Sea of Malachor. The Church maintains shrines on the body-fragment islands and considers the fallen statue a sacred ruin — the physical expression of what the Shattering cost. Pilgrims travel to the islands. The Church has a formal presence on several of the larger fragments.

Qens considers the head real estate and the island chain a navigational inconvenience. The theological question of whether living inside Malachor's skull is sacred or presumptuous has not been formally resolved. Qens does not appear interested in resolving it.

---

## Navigation

Two separate pilot specializations:
- **Ear canal pilots:** Navigate the entrance tunnel. The curve of the passage and the blind approach require someone who has done it before. Outsiders attempting the passage without a pilot do not receive sympathy.
- **Island chain pilots:** Navigate the body-fragment passages across the open sea. The islands are partially charted; the shallows between them are not. A third of the charts in circulation are wrong in ways that are not immediately apparent.

Both specializations are licensed by Qens. The licensing fees are not excessive. The pilots' rates are.

---

## The Church Presence

Shrines on the body islands; a mission presence at Qens itself, where the Church maintains that the cracked skull is a site of theological significance and that the residents' relationship to it requires pastoral guidance. The residents have been receiving this guidance for centuries. They have not changed their behavior in any documented way.

The theological question the Church keeps returning to: is Qens an act of hubris toward Malachor, or an act of devotion (dwelling inside the god's image, keeping it inhabited, maintaining it)? Qens has partisans for both interpretations and shows no sign of settling the question.
