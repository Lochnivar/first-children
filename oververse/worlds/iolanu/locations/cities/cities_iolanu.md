# Cities of Iolanu

**Status:** World-Building / Locations  
**Note:** This is a complete list of cities and locations. Previous handwritten OCR'd versions have been corrected with this authoritative list. Cultural assignments marked [inferred] are based on naming convention and geography — correct as needed.

## Cities and Locations

1. **Kitzigen Falls** - Kitzengai city built in, on, and around seven-tiered waterfalls (Veilfall Cascades); home to mistfae soul couriers (see [Kitzigen Falls](kitzigen_falls.md) for details)

2. **A'ardan** - Sister city to Mystaran; ancient (A' prefix indicates Thohalla/imperial era); sits high on a cliff above the tidal strait, with a cut path meandering down to the coast and the Footsteps crossing point. At low tide a land bar is exposed connecting the two cities; at high tide only the peaks show — called the **Footsteps of Nala** (or **Footsteps of Eirendor**) in folk tradition — which name a traveler uses, and in whose earshot, is never entirely casual. The Church has an official alternative name nobody uses. The largest Footstep hosts a waypoint inn where travelers shelter when the tide traps them mid-crossing. Daily life in both cities runs on the tide clock.

3. **Mystaran** - Sister city to A'Ardan; built into and around a massive crystal formation on the A'Rvenmaar shore of the Eirendor Straits. No A' prefix — it was named not by the empire but by the people who came to live in Nala's cave. Legend holds that Nala (as Eirendor) walked Iolanu on her first visit; the land raised the Footsteps to help her cross the strait, and when she rested in a cave on the Mystaran side, the rock turned to transparent crystal so she could see the stars as she slept. Whether any of this happened is another matter — the crystal formation and tidal bar are real; the explanation for them is what people say. The Apostasy considers it sacred regardless. The Church has an alternative explanation. The crystal catches starlight and daylight differently at every hour — the city is never the same twice. The Church has a mission presence here — they cannot leave the crystal cave and its folk traditions uncontested on what they consider a heretic sub-continent — but A'Rvenmaar's cultural weight moderates everything they've built. Church doctrine is practiced loosely, the folk traditions around the crystal predate the mission by centuries, and nobody pushes hard enough to break the arrangement because everyone has something here they can't afford to lose. The Church's beachhead that perpetually fails to consolidate. A city that is technically within the faith and functionally on its own terms.

4. **Basaulis** - City built inverted-terrace style inside an almost-inactive volcanic cone; located in the far north. The cone walls block mountain wind; the lava far below warms the interior — surprisingly temperate for its altitude, capable of growing things that shouldn't survive that high. The lowest terraces are the most dramatic and most coveted, closest to the heat. The rim is where the poorest live, exposed to the cold. From the outside nearly invisible — just a mountain peak. Everything is interior. Culture and exact placement TBD.

5. **Tauchin** - Settlement in the far north near Basaulis; the outpost that guards the approach to it. The city's existence depends on what it's guarding, and the relationship between gate and what's being gated is not always clear in the direction of power. Culture TBD.

6. **Manchire** - Northern fjord-coast city; mist-laced pine forests and icy riverbanks. Caelori or Caelori-adjacent [inferred]. Far north A'Maar coast; detailed culture and role TBD.

7. **Portage** - City at the midpoint of the desert spit connecting A'Miz and A'Maar; originally the halfway point between the north and south portage stations where goods were carried overland between the two continents. A canal was eventually cut through the isthmus, making the portage obsolete but not the city — it survived as a transit point on the waterway instead. Now one of the most strategically important cities on the map: whoever controls the canal controls intercontinental maritime trade. Named for a function it no longer performs. The north and south portage points still exist as canal entry and exit infrastructure. **Historical significance:** The desert spit itself — not the city, which came much later — is the site of the Thohalla crossing narrative. Long before Portage existed, Malachor led his chosen people across this waterless desert with saltwater on both sides. Many Thohalla died attempting to drink it. Whether Malachor didn't know, didn't care, or was testing them is the central unresolved argument in Thohalla oral tradition. The covenant and the Three Inherited Sins are rooted here. Portage and its canal sit atop ground the Thohalla consider sacred and terrible in equal measure.

8. **Alephis** - The Imperial City; former capital of Malachor's world empire; Takomai heartland; still carries the full architectural and administrative apparatus of the empire with none of the power and 150% of the pretensions. The legitimate successor to the God-King's mandate — ask anyone in Alephis.

9. **Qens** - City built inside the cracked-open head of the Colossus of Malachor — a statue commissioned during the God-King era to span the Sea of Malachor itself, one foot on A'Miz and one on A'Maar, built by Quantumancer labor at the height of the empire. Every ship crossing the sea sailed beneath it. You could not traverse the Sea of Malachor without passing under Malachor's image — which was the point. When the Shattering came, it shifted the continents apart. The statue could not span a wider gap. It fell. The body shattered into the island chain that now dots the Sea of Malachor; the head is where it landed, cracked open, rising from the water in the middle of the sea. From a distance the head reads as an atoll — the cracked crown forms a ring of raised stone around an open interior, the harbor within invisible until you're inside it. The entrance is the ear canal: an elongated arched tunnel through which ships sail blind, navigating the curve of the passage without seeing the interior until they clear it. Once through, the harbor opens — vast, open to sky, the interior surface of the skull forming the walls. Those walls are the back of Malachor's carved features looking inward: the city terraced up the concave interior face of the statue, rising in massive tiers like a stone amphitheater, the eye sockets and mouth visible as structural features above the waterline. The highest terraces sit at the rim of the cracked crown, looking out over the sea where the statue once stood. Standing at harbor level you look up at the carved interior of a god's face and open sky. The Church maintains shrines on the body-fragment islands and considers the fallen statue a sacred ruin — the physical expression of what the Shattering cost. Qens considers it real estate. Navigation requires pilots for the ear canal and separate pilots for the island chain passages. The theological question of whether living inside Malachor's head is sacred or presumptuous has not been resolved. Qens does not clarify. Culture TBD.

10. **Iphago** - Takomai city [inferred — Greco-Roman naming places it in the imperial cultural sphere]; likely founded or formalized during the height of the empire. Location and role TBD.

11. **Eglares** - A massive library built into a Quantumancer-raised mountain peak; the city that grew around it has made preservation itself the ideology — not scholarship, not understanding, not use. Preservation. The collection is meticulous, the cataloguing is exhaustive, and access is granted to nobody, including the residents. They guard knowledge they cannot read, in languages they don't understand, from an era they can't fully reconstruct, and consider this a virtue. The Church wants certain things destroyed — Eglares refuses. The Apostasy wants certain things read — Eglares refuses. Somewhere in the mountain is almost certainly something that would change everything. Eglares doesn't know what it is. It's catalogued, preserved, and inaccessible, which is the point.

12. **Oacoma** - [Culture and location TBD. Naming convention doesn't map cleanly to any known cultural pattern — possibly independent, Thohalla-adjacent, or a tradition not yet developed.]

13. **Zriesburg** - Maneth city [inferred — -burg suffix is Germanic/Maneth]; the Zr- opening is harder and more northern than typical Maneth naming, suggesting rough or high terrain. Location and role TBD.

14. **Axholdforð** - Maneth city built around a strategic ford on a major river; named for a battle fought at that crossing. The settlement grew from a garrison holding the ford after the fight. Not a coastal city — interior, river-anchored.

15. **Shokaido** - Astelli city on the lake above Kitzigen Falls. The upper end of the Kitzigen Falls geographic relationship — the lake culture looking down at the waterfall city below. What the Astelli and Kitzengai make of each other TBD.

16. **Ashoham** - Llosca city [inferred — Semitic naming root, same cultural sphere as Burmallosca and Llosnagar]. Where it sits on the spectrum between Llosnagar's reticence and Burmallosca's public expression TBD. Location TBD.

17. **Fraby** - Northern city; Norse-feel naming suggests Caelori or Caelori-adjacent [inferred]. Culture and location TBD.

18. **Doleigh** - Caelori settlement [inferred — -leigh parallels Celtic naming convention]; likely A'Maar coastal or inland. Role TBD.

19. **Entochester** - The -chester suffix stakes a Roman/imperial claim through naming — the same move as Nevarchester. Whether this is Wrinomo expansion, Takomai administration, or a city asserting legitimacy through its name alone [culture and location TBD].

22. **Atrando** - Takomai city; the third claimant to imperial succession after Alephis and Reman Hill, and the most embarrassing one — a city that is functionally a pirate port but maintains the most comprehensive imperial archive in existence. Adjudicates letters of marque, maintains a harbormaster, collects duties, and conducts privateering with enough civic infrastructure that the Church can negotiate with it. Port Royal to Te'Ar's Tortuga. Has a full admiralty court, a registrar of letters, notaries, prize claim adjudicators — two thousand years of meticulous records documenting every transaction and prize claim in the empire's name. They cannot issue imperial authority they don't hold; what they can do is rule on whether anyone else's claim is legitimate, and their archive makes that ruling hard to dispute. Every case buries itself in the same unanswerable question: was the issuing authority legitimate? Which requires resolving the imperial succession, which has been unresolved for two thousand years. The docket is eternal. The advocates are wealthy. Alephis and Reman Hill find Atrando an embarrassment but occasionally need to cite its records, which Atrando is insufferable about. The city motto, unofficial: *we may not have the mandate. But we have the receipts.* The saying on the strait: *if you want a ship hijacked right, go to Atrando; if you want it done correctly, go to Te'Ar.* Atrando considers this unfair. Te'Ar had it put on a sign.

23. **Inisgow** - Caelori city; far north A'Maar inlet coast. *Inis* = island (Gaelic) — likely on or around one of the coastal inlets.

24. **Xylis** - Takomai city [inferred — Greco-Roman naming]; location and role relative to Alephis and the succession question TBD.

25. **Catano** - Maritime city [culture TBD — Romance-language naming suggests Mediterranean-adjacent cultural context, possibly Llosca-adjacent or an independent port]. Location TBD.

26. **Krence** - [Culture and location TBD.]

27. **A'Thohalla** - Ancient capital of the Thohalla first race; far older than the Orindibar Desert, which came after. Mostly ruins, maintained by a skeleton crew just sufficient to call it a functioning city — a nominal heartland for a people whose real home is the caravan. Its most meaningful geographic relationship is its proximity to the Rathen Peaks: the first race, who originated the quantum gift, keeping a presence near the mountains where those who carry that gift are now exiled. The rest of the Thohalla move; A'Thohalla stays, barely, as an anchor point and a historical claim.

28. **St'lota** - [Culture and location TBD. The St' prefix doesn't match the standard Thohalla A' convention — either a variant tradition, a city named in a different language, or something else entirely.]

29. **Eleish** - Takomai plurality city on one bank of a river mouth in the Reman Hill region. Sister city to Yelish on the opposite bank. Both sit at the mouth of the same river. Both claim the same sacred event occurred here — and both are correct that the original records place it at the river's mouth, because the original records did not specify which bank, an omission that has generated more theological literature than any other three words in Takomai scholarship. The dispute is officially about the sacred site and practically about the spelling of its name. Eleish's spelling is the correct one. Yelish's counter-documentation is extensive and wrong.

30. *Strait of Atrando* - Geographic feature (strait). Likely the maritime chokepoint near Atrando and Te'Ar.

31. **Spaeshallow** - Coastal or tidal city; "shallow" suggests tidal flats, a shoaling harbor, or significant tidal geography. The city is named for what the water does here. [Culture and location TBD.]

32. **Mauwood** - Veylori heartland city; sits deep in the marsh valley between the two mountain legs of A'Miz. Houses carved into living trees with dock-like structures threading between trunks at water level and canopy bridges spanning high above — both levels coexist, each with its own character. The docks handle trade and fishing; the canopy is where social life rises above the waterline. The trees are the architecture — when one dies, a neighborhood has a problem. The city is alive in the most literal sense.

33. **Locassier** - Island city in the Sea of Malachor; the informal center of the trading league formed with Yantier and Elcoxier. Sits on neutral ground belonging to no continent, which is probably why the league cohered here first — merchants from both shores were already meeting on the island before anyone called it an arrangement. Primary culture is the Losceati proto-culture shared by the three league cities; the island's position means it absorbs more outside influence than either coastal partner, making it the most genuinely mixed of the three. The league's disputes tend to get resolved here, which gives Locassier a de facto authority neither Yantier nor Elcoxier officially granted it.

34. **Yantier** - East coast of A'Maar, facing the Sea of Malachor; the league city closest to A'Rvenmaar. Its position on A'Maar's gulf-facing coast puts it at the intersection of mainland trade routes and open-water commerce — and within cultural reach of A'Rvenmaar across the water, which is the primary vector through which Caelori and Maneth resistance posture seeps into the proto-culture. Yantier probably doesn't think of itself as particularly influenced by the north. The north disagrees. Part of the Llosca-offshoot trading league with Locassier and Elcoxier.

35. **Reman Hill** - Takomai city; both city and hill share the name. Site of the battle that finally secured Malachor's world empire against the last remaining holdout king — the ground where the imperial mandate was effectively ratified in blood. One of the competing Takomai claimants to imperial succession; the argument is straightforward and hard to dismiss: the empire wasn't won in Alephis, it was won *here*. The name echoes Remus — the brother who lost — which nobody in Reman Hill dwells on.

36. **Rosenmont** - The -mont suffix indicates elevation. Hill or mountain city; whether the name is Romance, a hybrid, or a translation of something older [culture and location TBD].

37. **Cininwood** - Forest-situated city; built in or around significant woodland. [Culture and location TBD — possibly Veylori-adjacent if A'Miz forest, or a different culture in forested terrain elsewhere.]

38. **Llosnagar** - Llosca city; shares the culture's Canaanite-rooted proclivities but keeps them behind closed doors — not necessarily their own doors, but doors. Views Burmallosca's public coupling as deviant rather than aspirational. The Llosca dress standard and sensual culture are present; the street theater is not. Probably bristles at outsiders assuming all Llosca behave like Burmallosca. The Hierarchy of Lust has a presence here but a contested and minimally tolerated one.

39. **Glannoch** - Caelori city; far north A'Maar inlet coast.

40. **Wautabourne** - River-adjacent settlement; -bourne indicates a stream or watercourse. The English-feel naming suggests Maneth-adjacent or a distinct tradition [culture and location TBD].

41. **Elcoxier** - West coast of A'Miz, two weeks sailing south of Basaulis and Tauchin on the Sea of Malachor; the southernmost point of the trading league triangle. Its position makes it the league's primary interface with A'Miz trade routes inland, and the city that Basaulis and Tauchin travelers encounter first when they come off the northern coast heading south. Part of the Losceati proto-culture shared with Locassier and Yantier; geographically the most isolated of the three from A'Rvenmaar influence, which may make it the most culturally conservative — still closest to its Llosca roots.

42. **Lotheoð** - Maneth city; separate from Axholdforð. The ð suffix places it firmly in Maneth naming convention. Shares the culture's river or interior geography tendency. Location TBD.

43. **Lonocqua** - [Culture and location TBD. Naming convention doesn't map to known cultures — possibly a tradition not yet catalogued.]

47. **Chaelpool** - Water-adjacent city; "pool" suggests a protected harbor, tidal basin, or inland pool. The Chael- prefix is distinctive and doesn't map cleanly to known cultures. [Culture and location TBD.]

48. **Ijamis** - Veylori city on the eastern coast of A'Miz; sits where the marsh valley between the two mountain legs opens to the sea. Maritime gateway in and out of the wetlands — the interface between Mauwood's deep marsh culture and the open ocean. End point of the marsh route: skimmer outpost → A'Thohalla → Mauwood (by bayou boat) → Ijamis.

49. **Nauntic** - Maritime city; the name derives from *nautical* and makes no attempt to disguise it. The sea is definitional here rather than incidental. [Culture and location TBD.]

51. **Dwyercourt** - The -court suffix suggests a judicial, administrative, or noble seat — aspirational or functional [culture and location TBD].

52. **Bridgepoint** - Named for its infrastructure: there is a bridge, it is at a strategic crossing point, and the city grew from that fact. Probably older than its functional name suggests. [Culture and location TBD.]

53. **Queensmount** - Wrinomo city; dominates a mountain pass between the Astelli lake region and the Wrinomo high plains above. The transition point between Astelli and Wrinomo territory — whoever controls the pass controls what moves between the two.

54. **Dorego** - [Culture and location TBD. Romance-language naming.]

55. **Nevarchester** - Wrinomo city on the north shore of the Astelli lake; self-styled unofficial Lake Lord. Wrinomo presence in Astelli territory established through trade, persistence, and the sheer fact of being there long enough that removing them would be a war. The Astelli resent it; Nevarchester relies on that resentment being less costly than conflict. The -*chester* suffix is deliberate — formal, empire-adjacent naming used to legitimize a claim nobody officially granted them.

56. **Burmallosca** - Heartland city of the Llosca culture; seat of the Church's **Hierarchy of Lust** (one of the five cardinal hierarchies). Llosca dress standard exposes the breasts and genitals while covering other parts of the body — not incidental but culturally specific. Public coupling is not only permitted but actively encouraged by the Hierarchy of Lust, which institutionalized existing Llosca cultural practice rather than suppressing it and built doctrine around it. The Church absorbed what it couldn't eliminate and gave it a hierarchy. Other cultures, particularly those with Thohalla-adjacent moral traditions, find Burmallosca deeply uncomfortable.

57. **Schoehaven** - Northern harbor city; genuinely mixed — no culture holds a majority, though Maneth is probably the largest single population, which is not the same as dominant. The Maneth naming is a historical artifact of who got there first, not a description of who runs it now. Sits at the eastern mouth of the Eirendor Straits, in a deep bay on the south shore — which makes it the first significant port a ship encounters entering the strait from the open sea, and the last one before crossing into narrower water heading west. A city that lives on the traffic of others passing through.

58. **Drovgha** - [Culture and location TBD. Naming has a Slavic or eastern-European feel, distinct from all known culture patterns — possibly a culture not yet developed, or a region with its own tradition.]

59. **Te'Ar** - Pirate haven; the name is a deliberate joke — "Te'Ar" pronounced aloud is "Te-Arrgh," which pirates find endlessly funny and outsiders find insufferable. Whether the name was chosen intentionally or was a corruption that landed somewhere convenient and got immediately claimed, nobody is correcting the record. Likely positioned near the Strait of Atrando — a maritime chokepoint where traffic funnels and "taxation" is flexible. The Church's official correspondence refuses to use the name. The pirates consider this an additional punchline. Governance TBD.

60. **Blackmoor** - Moorland city; the "black" qualifier suggests dark peat moorland, volcanic terrain, or an earned reputation. Either the landscape is genuinely dark or the city is. Possibly both. **Seat of the Ebon Hierarchy (Knights of the Ebon Throne).** The one city in the world where the Church of Malachor is effectively the government. [Location TBD.]

61. **Dwarhoffen** - Small city in the N-S mountain passes of A'Miz; on the W→E road that runs from Caxias on the Gulf of Panxia inland through the interior, paralleling the deep chasm. Unremarkable on the main road — most travelers pass through without taking the spur. That spur leads across a massive bridge spanning the chasm (narrower but far deeper than Grand Canyon scale — likely a Shattering consequence) and up through the valley into the mountains, terminating at an ancient city built into the bowels of the rock. That city is where the Shattering occurred and where the Grand Rift permanently resides, bleeding wandering rifts outward across the world. Dwarhoffen doesn't advertise what's up the spur.

62. **Caxias** - City on the western coast of A'Miz, on the Gulf of Panxia; western terminus of the E-W mountain road that comes through the interior from the east, paralleling the deep chasm, passing through Dwarhoffen in the N-S passes. The eastern end of the road connects to an outpost interfacing with the Orindi skimmer network at the Orindibar Desert — making the road a continuous travel corridor between the Gulf of Panxia coast and the desert crossing routes. The road ends here at the gulf. Part of the Book 1 orientation tour route — travelers descending from the pass region arrive at Caxias and the water.

63. **Yelish** - Takomai plurality city on the opposite bank of the river mouth from Eleish; Reman Hill region. The original records state the sacred event occurred at the mouth of the river. They do not state which bank. This is the foundational problem — the source is genuinely ambiguous, both claims are technically supportable, and the dispute is therefore irresolvable on the evidence, which has not discouraged either city from trying. Yelish's spelling of the site's name is correct. Eleish's spelling is a corruption so obvious it raises serious questions about Eleish's fitness to steward anything sacred. Yelish has extensive documentation. Eleish has extensive counter-documentation. Travelers crossing the river are advised not to raise the subject unless they have several hours and no particular destination.

## Geographic Features
- **Sea of Malachor** - The central sea between A'Miz and A'Maar; named by Malachor during the God-King era, which the Church insists upon and A'Rvenmaar declines to observe. During the empire a colossal statue of Malachor spanned it — one foot on each continent, ships passing beneath. The Shattering shifted A'Miz and A'Maar apart; the statue fell; its body became the island chain now scattered across the sea. The head became Qens. The Gulf of Panxia opens into it from the northwest. The trading lane the league cities Yantier, Locassier, and Elcoxier are built around.
- **Gulf of Panxia** - Smaller gulf on the western coast of A'Miz that opens southward into the Sea of Malachor; Caxias sits at its northern end. A contained body of water before the open crossing begins.
- **Strait of Atrando** - Maritime chokepoint near Atrando and Te'Ar
- **Eirendor Straits** - The strait separating A'Maar (south shore) from A'Rvenmaar (north shore); A'ardan sits on the A'Maar mainland shore, Mystaran on the A'Rvenmaar shore. The tidal bar — the Footsteps of Nala/Eirendor — crosses somewhere in the strait's narrower section. Schoehaven sits at the eastern mouth in a deep bay on the south shore. The northern continental crossing point, older and more treacherous than the Portage canal in the south. The folk name extends the same geography as the Footsteps; the Church objects to both for the same reason.
- **A'Rvenmaar** - "Place of the betrayed sunset." Northern mini-continent, home to Caelori and Maneth cultures. Was part of Malachor's world empire; drifted to independence after the Shattering. The Church has attempted to reassert religious authority across the Eirendor Straits for centuries without success — missionary by force rather than conquest; they want a bishop, not a governor. A'Rvenmaar doesn't think of itself as having rejected Malachor — nobody on Iolanu has gone that far. It simply never fully absorbed Church institutional orthodoxy after the Shattering loosened the empire's grip, and the folk traditions that survived that gap diverge enough from doctrine that the Church calls it heresy and A'Rvenmaar calls it faith. Both are describing the same thing from opposite sides of the Eirendor Straits. The name carries a dual reading: the Church frames it as a land the Shattering betrayed that needs restoration; A'Rvenmaar reads it as a land the empire betrayed first by collapsing, which owes the Church nothing on those grounds. Mystaran sits on its southern shore.
- **Reman Hill** - The hill that the city of Reman Hill was built on (the city and hill share the same name)
- **Orindibar Desert** - Magnetic desert with black sand, created by an ancient Quantumancer's experiment (see locations/orindibar_desert.md)
- **Rathen Mountains** - Floating mountain peaks above the Orindibar Desert, fragments of a shattered range (see locations/rathen_mountains.md)
