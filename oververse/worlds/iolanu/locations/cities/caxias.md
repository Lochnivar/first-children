# Caxias

**Culture:** Mixed (western coast port; significant Takomai administrative presence)  
**Continent:** A'Miz  
**Type:** Port city; western terminus of the mountain road  
**Narrative Role:** Book 1 sixth stop; where the party reaches the sea and Grayson's ship waits

---

## Overview

A port city on the western coast of A'Miz, sitting on the Gulf of Panxia where the E-W mountain road terminates at the water. Caxias is the end of the interior road and the beginning of the maritime leg — whoever descends from the pass region through Dwarhoffen arrives here and finds the Gulf. The city exists because of that transition point: mountain road to sea lane, interior to open water.

---

## Physical Character

Built at the road's terminus and the harbor's head simultaneously — the city reads differently from the landward approach (road-end, outfitter town, freightyard) than from the water (harbor mouth, pilot station, warehouses ascending the slope). Both impressions are accurate. The Gulf of Panxia is a contained body of water before the open Sea of Malachor begins, which gives the harbor a calmer character than open-coast ports; the water is workable in most seasons, which is why the road was built to terminate here.

The Takomai administrative presence is significant — the empire built this road and the waypoints on it, and Caxias bears the marks of that construction: a civic hall in the Takomai register, a records archive, a harbormaster's office that runs on imperial-era paperwork traditions long after the empire that created those traditions is gone. The forms still get filled out. The seals are still pressed. Whether the authority behind them is real is a different question.

---

## The Gulf of Panxia

The Gulf opens southward into the Sea of Malachor. Ships running between Caxias and the eastern A'Miz coast — including Kitzigen Falls via the river system — transit through the gulf mouth and into the sea. The gulf itself is navigable by relatively modest vessels; the sea beyond requires more serious maritime preparation. Caxias pilots know both and will tell you honestly which you need.

---

## Narrative Role

The party arrives descending from the mountains, crosses the city, and reaches the water. Grayson's ship is in the harbor. Book 1's orientation tour has been almost entirely overland to this point — Caxias is where the mode shifts. It is also the first city in the tour that feels explicitly cosmopolitan rather than culturally specific: a port with the road on one side and the sea on the other draws everyone, and Caxias looks like it.

---

## Notes

- Route: Dwarhoffen → Caxias → Kitzigen Falls (Book 1 itinerary — sea leg follows)
- Grayson's ship waits at Caxias; the party boards here for the maritime leg to Kitzigen Falls
- The Gulf of Panxia opens into the Sea of Malachor to the south
- Takomai civic infrastructure present throughout, maintained by institutional habit
