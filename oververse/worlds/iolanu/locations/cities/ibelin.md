# Ibelin

**Culture:** Wereshi  
**Continent:** A'Maar  
**Type:** Hall-complex city-state  
**Narrative Role:** Book 1 opening city; Gabby's entry point into Iolanu

---

## Overview

A moderately busy Wereshi city-state in the managed forest zone of A'Maar, positioned far enough north that the surrounding forest runs to spruce and pine rather than broadleaf — taiga-adjacent in character, with long winters and a short growing season that the Wereshi land-covenant accounts for precisely. Cold and clear more often than warm; the light has a northern quality, low and angled, making shadows long even at midday. For a first-time arrival, Ibelin reads as simply a city — busy, functional, functional in the specific way of people who have solved the cold. The Wereshi texture (threshold-marks at every gate, the pause before entering, the formulaic greeting) is so embedded in daily life it doesn't announce itself as strange. Gabby's baseline for what Iolanu is, established before she has enough context to recognize it as culturally specific.

---

## Physical Character

Large timber framing and earthwork berm construction — the Wereshi hall-complex aesthetic, grown rather than erected, and here built with the additional mass that cold requires: thick walls, deep-set thresholds, overhanging eaves for snow load. The surrounding forest is coniferous — spruce, pine, the occasional birch stand at the managed margin — pressing close to the walls. Threshold-marks carved or knotted at every significant transition; in winter some are additionally marked with fire-scars on the snow-line timber, renewed at the season turn. The cold is not incidental to life in Ibelin. The kami compacts here are heavily oriented toward the seasonal calendar — the fire that must be lit, the name that must be spoken before first frost.

---

## Founding and the Name

Ibelin was named not by the Wereshi but by Camulos — a Celtic Druid turned Templar Knight, pulled from the historical moment of the Crusades and made Iolanu's first known Earth soul. He established the Chapterhouse of Nala here, gave the city the name he carried with him, and is long dead by the time of the story. The Wereshi adopted the name without knowing its source. The Celtic register of the name and the Wereshi character of the city are both genuine — one layered on the other across centuries.

## Notable Features

**University of Ibelin:** A legitimate educational institution — the public face of the city's most important building. Founded by Camulos; real in every functional sense. The Chapterhouse (Nala's operation for receiving and acclimating Earth souls) is embedded within it silently, indistinguishable from administrative or residential university space. The university provides natural cover: people arriving from elsewhere, learning unfamiliar things, asking basic questions about the world. Earth souls blend into that pattern without effort. See [chapterhouse_nala_ibelin.md](../../world-building/organizations/chapterhouse_nala_ibelin.md).

**Threshold-keeper at the main gate:** The greeting formula is non-negotiable. Travelers who know the words move through efficiently.
