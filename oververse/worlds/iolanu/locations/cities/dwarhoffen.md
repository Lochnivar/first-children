# Dwarhoffen

**Culture:** Mixed (mountain pass transit city; no single dominant culture)  
**Continent:** A'Miz  
**Type:** Mountain pass city; transit hub  
**Narrative Role:** Book 1 fifth stop; the party is intercepted by a Locquodian Order agent here

---

## Overview

A small city in the N-S mountain passes of A'Miz, sitting on the W→E road that runs from Caxias on the Gulf of Panxia inland through the interior. Most travelers pass through Dwarhoffen without pausing — it is a waypoint on the main road, functional and unremarkable. The city does not correct this impression. It does not mention the spur.

---

## Physical Character

Built into the pass rather than beside it — the architecture uses the mountain walls as structure, extending inward where the rock allows. Stone throughout, heavy, built for a mountain climate that punishes lightness. The main road runs through the center of the city; the commerce facing that road is exactly what a transit city shows: waypoint inns, freight handlers, outfitters, guides for the pass sections, a Church mission presence that maintains a tired orthodoxy for travelers moving through.

The spur road branches off the main road east of the city center, crosses a bridge over the chasm, and climbs into the mountain valley above. From the main road the spur's branch point is easy to miss — there is no signage. The bridge itself is visible if you know to look for it, spanning a chasm that is narrower than the Grand Canyon but considerably deeper. The opposite cliff face gives no indication of what the valley above contains.

---

## The Chasm and the Bridge

The chasm is almost certainly a Shattering consequence — a tectonic wound that has not healed in two thousand years. Its depth is extreme enough that the bottom is permanently in shadow; travelers who lean over the bridge rail to look down report seeing nothing. The bridge is old Takomai-era engineering, maintained by Dwarhoffen out of necessity and by nobody's particular enthusiasm. It holds.

The valley beyond the bridge and above Dwarhoffen is where the Shattering occurred — where the ancient Quantumancers shattered the Godstone. The Grand Rift resides there permanently: the wound that continuously bleeds wandering rifts outward across Iolanu, seeding the world with the monsters that every culture's walls and weapons are built to manage. Dwarhoffen sits downstream of this, so to speak, at the base of the thing that makes the world what it is.

---

## What Dwarhoffen Doesn't Say

The city knows about the rift. The residents who have been here long enough know about the spur, the bridge, the valley, and what it contains. This knowledge is not secret — anyone who asks directly will receive a direct answer. The city simply does not volunteer it, does not mark it, does not make it easy to find.

The reasoning is practical: people who go up the spur tend not to come back in a useful condition, and the wandering rifts that emerge from the valley occasionally trouble the pass. Drawing more attention to either fact does not benefit Dwarhoffen. The Church has a formal interest in the site as the location of the Shattering; the Locquodian Order has a formal interest in ensuring no one reassembles the Godstone there; both maintain presences that are noticeably less comfortable than their counterparts in easier cities.

---

## Narrative Role

The Locquodian Order intercepts the party here. Dwarhoffen is the city where the tour stops being a tour — where the world's actual operational factions notice and respond to the party's movement. The unremarkable transit-city framing makes the interception land correctly: this is not a dramatic confrontation in a dramatic location. It is a conversation in a waypoint inn, on a road most people just pass through.
