# A'Thohalla

**Culture:** Thohalla (nominal heartland; skeleton crew)  
**Continent:** A'Miz  
**Type:** Ancient capital; functioning city in name only  
**Narrative Role:** Book 1 third stop; Gabby's first encounter with the Thohalla as a people rather than a caravan in transit

---

## Overview

The ancient capital of the Thohalla first race — older than the Orindibar Desert, which was created after A'Thohalla was already established and already old. Maintained by a skeleton crew just sufficient to qualify as a functioning city. The rest of the Thohalla move. A'Thohalla stays, barely, as an anchor point and a historical claim.

It is not a city anyone would choose to live in. It is a city the Thohalla cannot afford to let go.

---

## Physical Character

Ruins with a working center. The original scale is visible in the footprint — streets wide enough for caravans moving four abreast, plazas that once held thousands, buildings whose foundations dwarf anything currently occupied. What remains inhabited is a small functional core: shelter, a fire, a Morning Roll that names fewer names each generation. The rest is maintained only to the extent that structures don't collapse onto the occupied portions.

The proximity to the Orindibar Desert means the air has a quality of magnetic stillness at the margins — the same slightly-wrong atmosphere travelers notice at the desert's edge, though A'Thohalla predates the desert and its people never fully adjusted to having a magnetic wasteland for a neighbor.

---

## Significance

The Thohalla originated the quantum gift. The quantum-adjacent gifted were first among this people, and the gift spread outward through their bloodlines into every other culture on Iolanu over thousands of years. The Rathen Peaks — where those who carry the gift now exile themselves under the Rathen Oath — are visible from A'Thohalla's ruins on clear days.

The first race keeps a presence near the mountains where their inheritance is quarantined. Nobody discusses this directly. The Thohalla who rotate through the skeleton crew observe the Morning Roll, tend the fires, and look at the peaks. The irony is in the map, not the conversation.

---

## The Skeleton Crew

Thohalla caravans rotate through A'Thohalla on a covenant schedule — a caravan holds the city for a season, then hands it to the next. No caravan wants to stay. All caravans keep the rotation. The obligation is older than anyone can trace to a specific decision; it is simply what is done, which in Thohalla terms means it is what must be done.

The residents at any given time are a small cohort: enough to hold the Morning Roll, maintain the fires, and keep the structures from collapsing inward. They are not permanent settlers. They are the Thohalla on duty.

---

## Narrative Role

Gabby's third city. After two Wereshi cities in A'Maar, A'Thohalla is her first experience of A'Miz and her first direct encounter with the Thohalla. The ruins make the scale of what the world was before the Shattering legible in a way nothing else does. The skeleton crew makes the Thohalla's relationship to obligation legible: here is a people who maintain something they have no practical use for, because the alternative is abandoning an anchor point they cannot replace.
