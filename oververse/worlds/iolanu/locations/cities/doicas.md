# Doicas

**Culture:** Wereshi  
**Continent:** A'Maar  
**Type:** Hall-complex city-state  
**Narrative Role:** Book 1 second city; Gabby's first encounter with something she cannot categorize

---

## Overview

A Wereshi hall-complex city-state deeper into the A'Maar forest than Ibelin, sitting adjacent to territory held by a semi-intelligent horde. The city functions as any Wereshi city-state does — managed forest, formal threshold protocol at the gate, kannagi circuiting the homestead margins. What is different is the quality of the stillness beyond the managed margin, and what the residents are not afraid of.

---

## Physical Character

Same hall-complex construction as Ibelin — large timber framing, earthwork berms, threshold-marks at every transition. The forest presses closer here. The managed margin is maintained with the same rigor, but the deep wild beyond it reads differently to anyone who's spent time in monster country: less random, less loud. The forest outside Doicas is occupied in a way that occupied forests are not usually quiet.

---

## The Understanding

There is no pact with the horde in the surrounding deep wild. No negotiation ever happened, no formula was spoken, nothing is written. What exists is an understanding that predates anyone living: Doicas doesn't push into the horde's territory, and the horde doesn't treat Doicas as prey.

Nobody decided this. At some point, across enough generations of Wereshi threshold-acknowledgment and kami-attentiveness — the habit of treating even dangerous presences as things worth noticing rather than obstacles to clear — something in the horde recognized the difference between Doicas and everything else. The hostility simply stopped.

The practical consequence is that Doicas sits inside held territory. A known presence holds its ground; other hordes don't move through occupied margin. Doicas benefits from this without having negotiated for it — which is both the quiet advantage and the ongoing anxiety of it. There is nothing to maintain. There is no renewal formula. There is only continuing to be what Doicas has always been.

Residents understand this well enough not to discuss it with outsiders. They are visibly comfortable in proximity to something that everywhere else in Iolanu requires walls and weapons. The only explanation they can offer is *we have always behaved correctly here.* The Church finds this insufficient. The Wereshi find the Church's discomfort irrelevant to whether it works.

---

## Notable Features

**The Kannagi of Doicas:** The kannagi who work the deep wild margin here are the most respected — and the most unsettling — in Wereshi territory. They hold formulas for presences that no other kannagi outside Doicas has occasion to learn. They don't discuss what they've encountered at the far edge of the managed forest. Other kannagi don't ask.

**The City Gate:** Threshold protocol enforced with the same formality as Ibelin, but the Threshold-keeper here adds something the Ibelin counterpart does not: after the greeting formula, a pause in which they simply look at you. Travelers who ask what they're looking for never get a satisfying answer.
