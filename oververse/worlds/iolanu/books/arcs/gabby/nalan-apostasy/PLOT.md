# Book 1: The Nalan Apostasy — Plot Reference

Consolidated reference for route, party, chapter structure, and key plot decisions.
Status markers: **LOCKED** = decided and stable; **PENDING** = established but needs development; **OPEN** = not yet resolved.

---

## Premise

**LOCKED** — Gabby D'Olivier died on Earth. Nala intercepted her soul in transit and
offered her a choice — the prologue establishes the conversation is real. But consenting
while dying, under Nala's pitch, is not the same as understanding what that means once
you wake up in a Caelori body on a world you didn't know existed. Her buy-in to the
specific work of the Apostasy is not assumed — it has to be earned through the tour.

**LOCKED** — The tour is not orientation. It is calibration. Loisa's job is not to
educate Gabby about Iolanu — it is to temper Gabby's instincts against 900 years of
hard-won operational doctrine before those instincts damage the work. Each city stop
is a controlled condition. Loisa is watching how Gabby fires under pressure, not
teaching her facts.

**LOCKED** — Gabby's inherent quality (provisional): decisiveness combined with resilience
despite hardship. Nala saw it in her soul in transit — she was dying and still oriented,
still functional, still making a call. What this produces in Iolanu is not yet known.
Neither Loisa nor Gabby fully knows what she is for. The tour is partly discovery.

**LOCKED** — Primary POV: Gabby D'Olivier (third-person limited)
**LOCKED** — Aside POVs: Axiarch Solon and Jasin "Loch" Lochnivar appear in brief
interstitial chapters distributed throughout the book, not concentrated at the end.
Solon's chapters track his pursuit closing distance. Jasin's chapters track his
independent mission converging on Kitzigen Falls. Both create dramatic irony —
the reader knows what Gabby doesn't.

**LOCKED** — Two embedded agents in the party: one Church watcher, one Locquodian
operative. Neither knows what they've actually found. The Church agent is responding
to suspicious movement from Ibelin — not "Apostasy detected," just "something odd."
The Locquodian agent is pattern-matching on an organized party moving between
city-states. Both are filing reports through frameworks that don't have the right
vocabulary for what the Apostasy actually is. See [nalan_apostasy_reference.md](../../../../world-building/organizations/nalan_apostasy_reference.md).

## Route

**LOCKED** — Ibelin → Doicas → A'Thohalla → Orindibar Desert → Orindibar → Dwarhoffen → Caxias → Kitzigen Falls

**LOCKED** — Transport by leg:
- Boat — Ibelin to Doicas (river)
- Thohalla caravan — Doicas to A'Thohalla (overland)
- Mag-lev skimmers — A'Thohalla across Orindibar Desert to Orindibar
- Non-Thohallan caravan — Orindibar to Dwarhoffen (overland)
- Ship — Caxias to Kitzigen Falls (sea)

**LOCKED** — Duration: ~3 months (provisional). Grayson's ship deadline (sent to Caxias under
First Officer command; Grayson rejoins after tour) creates the time pressure.

## Chapter Structure

**~22 chapters total.** Pattern: City → Travel Leg → City → Travel Leg → City.

---

### Part I: Awakening in Ibelin (Chapters 1–3)

**Chapter 1** — Gabby wakes up in Ibelin. Disorientation, new body, new world.
*Written — see ch01.md*

**Chapter 2** — The Chapterhouse of Nala. Full-body dysphoria. Meets Daochen. Begins to
understand what happened to her.
*Written — see ch02.md*

**Chapter 3** — Introduction to the Nalan Apostasy. Mission, stakes, the tour premise.
Loisa explains why Gabby is here and what comes next. Party assembled.

**Goals:** Establish Gabby as Earth soul. Introduce Nala vs. Malachor conflict. Set tour
premise. Introduce Loisa and key party members.

---

### Part II: Leg 1 — Ibelin to Doicas (Chapter 4)

**Chapter 4** — Departure by boat. Meet the full eight-person party. First experience of
Iolanu geography from the river. Monsters observed from the boat — visible, dangerous,
distant. Safe introduction to the threat.

**Goals:** Show the monster threat without immediate peril. Begin party dynamics. Iolanu
geography from water.

---

### Part III: Doicas (Chapter 5)

**Chapter 5** — Arrival at Doicas. First real city immersion outside Ibelin. Local culture,
customs, city-state structure. Party interactions in a new setting.

**Goals:** First independent city experience. City-state structure begins to make sense.
Cultural difference from Ibelin is legible.

**OPEN** — Doicas culture, significant locations, events, character beats at this stop.

---

### Part IV: Leg 2 — Doicas to A'Thohalla (Chapter 6)

**Chapter 6** — Join Thohalla caravan. Immersion in nomadic life begins. The caravan as
mobile fortress. Covenant bonds. Learning about Thohalla history — Malachor's chosen people,
discarded.

**Goals:** Introduce Thohalla culture and the mobile caravan model. First encounter with
the consequences of divine abandonment. Congregation mechanics observable in practice
(caravan keeps moving; party understands why).

---

### Part V: A'Thohalla (Chapters 7–9)

**Chapter 7** — Arrival and settlement. A'Thohalla as ruins — the Church's chapter house
fifty yards from the Thohalla's preserved heritage. Immediate cultural tension.

**Chapter 8** — Extended stay. Cultural immersion deepens. Relationships in the party
develop. Thohalla daily life, Morning Roll, Remembrance practices.

**Chapter 9** — Riftwalking demonstrated or observed. Understanding the Thohalla's ongoing
duty to seal mobile rifts. The existential weight of the work.

**Goals:** Deep cultural immersion. Divine abandonment explored fully. Riftwalking and
mobile rift danger established. Party relationships deepen. The Church's colonial presence
made concrete.

**OPEN** — Specific events and character beats at A'Thohalla. What does Gabby witness
that crystallizes the Thohalla's situation for her?

---

### Part VI: Leg 3 — A'Thohalla to Orindibar (Chapter 10)

**Chapter 10** — Mag-lev skimmers across the Orindibar Desert. Floating Rathen Mountains.
Black sand. The sensory wonder of the crossing. Origin story of the desert: an ancient
Quantumancer shattered a mountain range because he could. The beauty masks the destruction.

**Goals:** Storytelling wonder. Ancient Quantumancer power established at scale. The Shattering's
precursor logic — this is what they were capable of before the Godstone was destroyed.

---

### Part VII: Orindibar (Chapter 11)

**Chapter 11** — Arrival at Orindibar. Orindi culture. Mag-lev technology in its native
context — not wonder now, but workaday infrastructure. How a culture adapts to an extreme
environment.

**Goals:** Technology as cultural response. See how adaptation produces identity. Rest
and resupply narrative function.

**OPEN** — Orindi-specific events, character beats, what Gabby learns here that she
couldn't learn from the crossing alone.

---

### Part VIII: Leg 4 — Orindibar to Dwarhoffen (Chapter 12)

**Chapter 12** — Non-Thohallan caravan to Dwarhoffen. A different overland experience
to compare against the Thohalla leg. Party dynamics continue developing.

**Goals:** Contrast with Thohalla travel method. Party relationships at mid-journey depth.

---

### Part IX: Dwarhoffen (Chapter 13)

**Chapter 13** — Arrival at Dwarhoffen. **Key plot event:** Intercepted by a Locquodian
Order agent who provides information steering the party toward Kitzigen Falls. Political
tension. The Order's mission explained through encounter, not exposition.

**Goals:** Introduce the Locquodian Order as a faction. Establish that the political
landscape has more than two sides. Conflict that isn't monsters.

**OPEN** — Dwarhoffen culture and city character. Who is the Locquodian agent? What
specifically do they offer and why? What is their angle?

---

### Part X: Leg 5 — Dwarhoffen to Caxias (Chapter 14)

**Chapter 14** — Overland to Caxias. Processing the Locquodian encounter. Party debates
factions, motives, what the Order knows and why they shared it.

**Goals:** Digest political complexity. Party members reveal their own assessments of the
Order, which reveals their characters and histories.

---

### Part XI: Caxias (Chapter 15)

**Chapter 15** — Port city. Trade culture. Grayson's ship context — reunites with his
First Officer, assesses the ship. Maritime perspective on Iolanu. Preparation for the
final leg.

**Goals:** Trade and commerce as a world force. Coastal perspective distinct from inland.
Grayson's professional world made visible.

**OPEN** — Caxias culture and character. What happens here beyond logistics?

---

### Part XII: Leg 6 — Caxias to Kitzigen Falls (Chapter 16)

**Chapter 16** — Ship journey. Sea travel. Grayson in his element. Approaching the
destination. The tour's end visible ahead.

**Goals:** Grayson's expertise. Sea as a different kind of Iolanu. Anticipation building.

---

### Part XIII: Kitzigen Falls (Chapters 17–22)

**Chapter 17** — Arrival. Kitzengai culture. Seven-tiered waterfalls. The Mistfae — ancient
coherent energy entities in the mist. The destination earns its place.

**Chapter 18** — Extended stay begins. Exploring the city. Locals. Understanding why this
was the goal and what the Locquodian agent's information pointed toward.

**Chapter 19** *(Aside — Jasin Lochnivar)* — Jasin's POV at Kitzigen Falls. His mission:
retrieve a Godshard to secure his father's claim to a throne. Prepositioned at this location.
Seeds Book 2.

**Chapter 20** — Tour complete. Gabby's understanding of Iolanu assessed against where
she started. Relationships solidified. What comes next begins to take shape.

**Chapter 21** *(Aside — Axiarch Solon)* — Solon's POV. Chasing the Nalan party. The
threat made concrete. Malachor's enforcement arm closing distance.

**Chapter 22** — The next step. Party discussions about what follows. Not all members
continue into Book 2. Gabby's role going forward. Final scenes at Kitzigen Falls. Book 2
setup complete.

**Goals for full Kitzigen section:** Final destination earns its weight. Jasin introduced
as Book 2's anchor. Solon established as the ongoing antagonist threat. Gabby's arc from
disoriented Earth soul to functional Apostasy operative closed.

**OPEN** — What specifically happens at Kitzigen Falls that makes it the right endpoint?
The Locquodian agent steered them here for a reason — what did they find?

## Educational Progression

Each stop teaches Gabby (and the reader) something that couldn't be taught at the previous stop:

1. **Ibelin** — What happened to her; who Nala is; what the Apostasy is
2. **Leg 1** — The monster threat, seen safely from a distance
3. **Doicas** — City-state structure; cultural variation
4. **Leg 2** — How overland travel actually works; the Thohalla model
5. **A'Thohalla** — Divine abandonment; what the Church's presence costs a culture
6. **Leg 3** — Ancient Quantumancer power; the beauty-destruction axis
7. **Orindibar** — Technology as cultural identity; environmental adaptation
8. **Leg 4** — Contrast and comparison; the world is not uniform
9. **Dwarhoffen** — Political complexity; the Locquodian Order; non-monster threats
10. **Leg 5** — Processing complexity; what the party believes
11. **Caxias** — Trade and maritime culture; Grayson's world
12. **Leg 6** — The sea; approach and anticipation
13. **Kitzigen Falls** — Destination earns its weight; Book 2 seeds planted
