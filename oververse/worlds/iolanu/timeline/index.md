# Iolanu Timeline

Iolanu was forked from Earth (world #934) approximately 1,996 years ago, shortly after Malachor's expulsion from Earth (~30 CE). The timeline below uses **Iolanu Years (IY)**, counting from the fork. "Roughly 2,000 years" is the in-world approximation; precise count is ~1,996 years.

The fork date corresponds to ~30 CE on Earth. The Crusades era on Earth (~1095–1291 CE) maps to approximately IY 1065–1261.

---

## The Four Ages

### First Age (IY 1–560)
**Duration:** 560 years  
**Character:** Proto-culture. Transplanted Earth cultures migrate to their modern ranges and establish themselves. Thohalla dominant as Malachor's deliberate creation — the one civilization that didn't need to migrate. Everything is amorphous, pre-institutional, still finding its shape.

**Key events:**
- Malachor rules as a background presence through the Godstone — not yet as direct God-King; cultures are largely unaware of the degree to which they were shaped by him on Earth
- Thohalla establish themselves as the original civilization of Iolanu; the "A'" prefix marks their naming conventions from this period
- Chance born ~IY 200 — Thohalla fisherman; loses Rhyannin to the Sea of Storms; quantum gift manifests from grief; practices alone for years
- Nala is furious from year one — the fork is illegal — but ineffective; small interventions that leave no lasting mark
- No kingdoms yet; no formal institutions; cultures know what they are but haven't built the structures that will define them

**Ends:** Malachor begins organizing the Thohalla as his chosen people (~IY 560)

---

### Second Age (IY 561–1050)
**Duration:** 490 years  
**Character:** The Thohalla cycle. Malachor militarizes his chosen people; conquest-slavery-liberation repeats at irregular intervals. Other cultures form kingdoms defensively. Ends in massacre and displacement.

**Key events:**
- **The Crossing Disaster** — Thohalla desert crossing kills over 50%; Malachor converts the tragedy into religious ritual and theological obligation; the survivors are bound by a commemoration that blames the dead
- Malachor militarizes the Thohalla as his chosen people; the Crossing Disaster becomes the founding trauma that justifies their special status
- **Rage of the Thohalla** — multiple conquest-slavery-liberation cycles of deliberately irregular length; some captivities last 80 years, some 200; some liberations are swift and violent, some a slow economic drift; no two cycles the same
- Other cultures form kingdoms as defensive response — political organization born from external threat rather than organic development; kingdoms forged under pressure have survival in their DNA
- Chance becomes immortal sometime in the Second Age — exact year unknown; he was living in the wilderness, a hiking accident in the mountains; fell, snapped his spine, used Quantumancy to repair it, discovered he could stop aging; this is his personal "Fall," distinct from the Fall of A'Thohalla; well before IY 1050
- Malachor withdraws his protection from the Thohalla; the newly formed kingdoms butcher A'Thohalla
- **Fall of A'Thohalla** (~IY 1050) — Thohalla civilization destroyed; survivors become caravan workers; the mobile life is not economic choice but survival in a world where every settled culture's ancestors either swung the blade or looked away
- Nala still largely ineffective; watching the cycles with mounting frustration; small interventions that don't break the pattern
- **Losceati schism** (~IY 800–1000) — Llosca communities in the Sea of Malachor trade network adopt a distinct expression of desire-theology (romantic/linguistic rather than physical); they are actively developing toward their own kingdom when the Second Age ends; Malachor's Third Age conquest absorbs them before consolidation; the empire administers them as Llosca-adjacent, refusing to recognize a distinction that hadn't yet hardened into formal institutions

**Ends:** Fall of A'Thohalla (~IY 1050) — Nala's interest hardens into active planning; this is the "final fall of the Thohalla" that triggers her escalation

---

### Third Age (IY 1051–1381)
**Duration:** 330 years  
**Character:** Opens as organic Takomai expansion, enabled by Quantumancer power. Transitions to direct God-King rule after Malachor seizes the empire at the Battle of Reman Hill. Ends catastrophically.

**Early Third Age — Takomai Expansion (~IY 1051–1080):**
- Chance, ~850 years old and increasingly isolated, approaches a Takomai prince to offer Quantumancy services in exchange for a protected base and imperial support — he wants stability; what he delivers is the military-technological edge that turns the most capable Second Age kingdom into an unstoppable empire
- Malachor observes but does not insert himself; lets the Takomai expand on their own momentum
- Chance effectively founds the Quantumancer corps — the institutional framework that will eventually imprison and destroy him and everyone like him
- ~30 years of rapid conquest: Quantumancer-backed Takomai forces sweep A'Miz — other kingdoms exhausted, fragmented, carrying the guilt of the massacre; the founding prince unifies one continent in a single lifetime, mid-campaign when Malachor moves

**The Battle of Reman Hill (~IY 1080):**
- Malachor, presenting himself as an upstart Takomai claimant, defeats the founding Takomai prince — ~50-60 years old, A'Miz largely conquered but the world not yet finished — and seizes the incomplete empire; a Takomai succession fight that becomes something else entirely
- The prince proved the model; Malachor took it and scaled it — A'Maar, A'Rvenmaar, and the remaining holdouts fall under God-King rule over the following decades
- The Takomai administrative apparatus is retained intact; Malachor doesn't replace the machinery, he sits atop it
- The city takes the name Reman Hill — naming itself for the brother who lost; nobody dwells on this
- Chance's patron is the prince who lost; Chance now serves the man who overthrew him; ~880 years old, haze worsening, he accepts the transition because losing the institutional base is worse than the alternative

**Late Third Age — God-King Era (~IY 1080–1381):**
- Direct God-King rule established through the Godstone; Malachor rules without maintaining constant quantum focus
- Quantumancers run unchecked — shatter mountains, raise monuments, reshape geography; the quantum haze drives them progressively insane; death offers no relief (souls still perceive the haze in the afterlife)
- Nala escalates: begins recruiting Earth souls from Earth's own history; **Camulos** (~IY 1089–1261, pulled from the Crusades) arrives as Iolanu's first known Earth soul — Celtic Druid turned Templar Knight; founds the University of Ibelin and embeds the Chapterhouse within it; names the city from his own world
- Chance is ~950–1150 years old; has witnessed his own people's full arc and enabled the empire now destroying him; immortal, increasingly broken
- **The Shattering (IY 1381)** — Nala manipulates Chance and other ancient Quantumancers into attacking the Godstone; they believe destroying it will unmake reality and end their eternal suffering; the Godstone shatters into 7 Godshards rather than destroying; reality tears open; Malachor's direct power broken; Chance's body destroyed, soul persists as disembodied Quantumancer

**Ends:** The Shattering (IY 1381)

---

### Fourth Age (IY 1382–present, ~IY 1996)
**Duration:** ~614 years (ongoing)  
**Character:** Post-apocalyptic survival. The world the story takes place in.

**Key events:**
- Monsters emerge from rifts — quantum fluctuations made manifest; living quantum noise; symptoms of the wound in reality, not independent creatures
- The empire's cities become ruins; survivor communities retreat behind walls; city-states replace kingdoms
- Travel becomes dangerous expedition; every journey between city-states is a calculated risk
- Church of Malachor runs on autopilot — Malachor constrained, distracted on off-stage continents, but his institutional infrastructure persists without him
- Thohalla continue as caravan workers — the only culture with mobility as their survival strategy; crossing the dangerous ground between city-states is what they do
- Nala rebuilds: Greyroses established; systematic Apostasy begins; Earth soul recruitment expands and formalizes
- Chance persists as disembodied ghost — ~614 years of quantum madness since the Shattering; seeks a body to complete what he started
- The 7 Godshards scattered at the Shattering; their locations unknown; they retain some portion of what the Godstone was
- **Losceatic League forms** (early current saga) — survivor cities attempt to reconstruct political coherence; immediately triggers Locquodian Order response
- **Present day (~IY 1996):** The story

## Nala's Arc Across the Ages

| Age | Nala's Status |
|-----|---------------|
| First Age | Furious from year one; observing; small ineffective interventions |
| Second Age | Watching the Thohalla cycles with mounting frustration; still largely ineffective |
| Third Age (early) | Escalating — Earth soul recruitment begins; Camulos as first experiment |
| Third Age (end) | Desperation — orchestrates the Shattering through Chance; calculated use of a suffering man's desire for oblivion |
| Fourth Age | Methodical — the Apostasy; systematic; done improvising; every prior failure has been incorporated |
