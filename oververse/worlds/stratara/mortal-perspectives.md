# Mortal Perspectives: Stratara

**Status:** World-Specific Expansion  
**Category:** Cultures / Mortal Awareness  
**Related:** [First Children](../../cosmology/first-children.md), [Races](../../races/README.md), [../README.md](README.md)

## Overview

Stratara has an **open cosmology**—mortals are aware of the First Children "players" and understand they're part of a cosmic game. This creates varied responses: some worship the Strategists as patrons, others resent being pawns, leading to underground resistance movements.

## Awareness Level

### Open Cosmology

**Mortals Know:**
- Mortals are aware of the First Children "players"
- They call them "The Strategists" (or similar poetic names)
- They understand the basic structure of the "game"
- They know territories, cycles, and conquest dynamics

**Framework Alignment:**
- Framework allows open cosmology (some worlds are open, some closed)
- First Children control mortal awareness (framework rule)
- Stratara demonstrates open cosmology variant

### Varied Responses

**Worship:**
- Some mortals worship the Strategists as patrons
- They see participation in the game as divine purpose
- They actively seek to serve their patron First Child

**Resentment:**
- Others resent being pawns in a cosmic game
- They see the First Children as manipulative siblings playing with mortal lives
- This leads to underground resistance movements

**Pragmatism:**
- Many mortals simply accept the game as reality
- They work within the system to survive and thrive
- They might play their own sub-games within the larger structure

## Quantum-Gifted Mortals

### Rare Fragments

**Manifestation:**
- Rare quantum fragments manifest in mortals
- These gifted individuals become heroes or villains
- They're persecuted by rival factions (who see them as threats)

**Framework Alignment:**
- Mortals may have fragmented quantum abilities (framework rule)
- Persecution is common (framework pattern)
- World-specific: fragments are strategically distributed by First Children

### Roles in the Game

**Heroes:**
- Some gifted mortals become legendary heroes
- They might lead armies, break sieges, or disrupt enemy strategies
- They're celebrated by their patron First Child's faction

**Villains:**
- Others become feared villains
- They might use their abilities for personal gain or destruction
- They're hunted by multiple factions

**Wild Cards:**
- Some gifted mortals refuse to play the game
- They might work to disrupt the entire system
- They could attract external First Child attention (liberation operations)

## Races and Societies

### Universal Races

**Humans:**
- Adaptable infantry, dominating central plains
- Versatile and numerous
- Often form the backbone of armies

**Elves:**
- Forested strongholds, using Echo-like abilities for guerrilla tactics
- Viewing past battles to predict ambushes
- Masters of hit-and-run warfare

**Orcs:**
- Shock troops in harsh territories
- Raw Quantumancer fragments for brute-force reality bends
- Fierce warriors adapted to difficult terrain

**Kitsune:**
- Spies and mercenaries
- Weaving illusions via quantum threads
- Masters of deception and infiltration

**Other Races:**
- Dwarves, Halflings, Giff, etc. as needed
- Each adapted to different territories and roles

### Evolving Societies

**Nomadic Alliances:**
- Societies that shift with conquests
- Forming temporary alliances based on territory control
- Adapting to the changing game board

**Fortified City-States:**
- City-states with anchor-towers to stabilize regions
- Using anchored constructs to maintain territory
- Centers of power and resistance

**Resistance Movements:**
- Underground organizations working against the game
- Seeking to force a "draw" or attract liberation
- Might recruit souls from other worlds (Earth strategists reincarnated as generals)

## Themes and Conflicts

### Balance and Imbalance

**Conquest Creates Growth:**
- Merged cultures create new tech/magic
- Conquest drives innovation and cultural exchange
- Growth comes from imbalance

**Risks of Dominance:**
- If one First Child dominates too hard, it risks total haze
- World-ending instability could result
- Balance must be maintained

**Framework Alignment:**
- Demonstrates balance/imbalance philosophy (framework theme)
- Shows how imbalance drives change (framework principle)

### Mortal Agency

**Sub-Games:**
- Mortals could spark their own sub-games
- Rebelling to force a "draw"
- Attracting liberation souls from other worlds

**Resistance:**
- Underground movements working against the system
- Seeking to break free from the game
- Potential liberation operation triggers

## Framework Conformance

**Conforms to Framework:**
- Open cosmology is allowed (framework variant)
- Mortal awareness varies by world (framework rule)
- Quantum fragments manifest in mortals (framework rule)
- Universal races are used (framework element)
- Balance/imbalance themes apply (framework philosophy)

**World-Specific Expansions:**
- Open cosmology with "Strategists" concept
- Strategic fragment distribution
- Game-aware cultures and resistance movements
- Sub-game mechanics (mortal agency)
