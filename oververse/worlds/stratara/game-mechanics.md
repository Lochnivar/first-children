# Game Mechanics: Stratara

**Status:** World-Specific Expansion  
**Category:** World Mechanics / Quantum Systems  
**Related:** [Quantumancy](../../magic-systems/quantumancy.md), [Quantum Foundation](../../metaphysics/quantum-foundation.md), [../README.md](README.md)

## Overview

Stratara's "game" mechanics are world-specific expansions of universal framework elements. All mechanics operate through quantum manipulation, respecting physical laws while allowing First Children to influence outcomes through proxy wars fought by mortals.

## Territory System

### Territories as Quantum Nodes

**Nature:**
- The world is divided into ~42 regions (territories), each a self-sustaining quantum node
- Territories aren't just land—they're coherent energy patterns
- Natural borders are created by unstable rift zones that spawn chaotic energy patterns (chokepoints)
- These function like game boundaries, creating strategic points

**Framework Alignment:**
- All territories operate within the quantum foundation
- No separate realms or divine cheats
- Everything is energy patterns clashing on the same substrate

### First Children Influence

**Indirect Control:**
- First Children don't directly intervene (to avoid breaking physical laws like causality)
- They can't snap-rewrite reality without anchors
- Instead, they seed influences by granting fragmented quantum abilities to mortal factions

**Quantum Manipulation:**
- First Children can subtly influence territories (coherent energy patterns)
- They might boost a region's stability to fortify it
- Or induce haze-like instability to sabotage rivals
- Mortals perceive this as "fickle fates" or magical boons/curses

## Faction Abilities

### Quantum Fragment Distribution

**Different First Children, Different Approaches:**
- One First Child favors "aggressor" cultures with Weaver-like fragments (manipulating quantum threads for rapid army mobilization or terrain alteration)
- Another backs "defenders" with Nullwraith echoes (nullifying enemy quantum effects, creating anti-magic strongholds)
- Each First Child grants different quantum fragments to their favored factions

**Framework Alignment:**
- Mortals receive fragmented quantum abilities (framework rule)
- The specific split is world-specific (Stratara has multiple fragments distributed strategically)
- All abilities are quantum manipulation (framework requirement)

## Cycle Mechanics

### Time and Cycles

**Normal Time Flow:**
- Time flows normally (respects physical laws)
- First Children impose "cycles" via anchored quantum constructs
- Example: Celestial events like comet passes that recharge energies

**Framework Alignment:**
- Anchors are stable constructs that offload persistence (framework concept)
- Cycles use anchored constructs to create periodic events
- This respects physical laws while allowing strategic timing

### Reinforcements

**Allocation Mechanism:**
- At each cycle, First Children allocate "troops" by infusing raw quantum energy into regions
- This manifests as:
  - Population booms
  - Technological surges
  - Rift-spawn allies (coherent energy patterns)
- Mortals interpret this as divine interventions or natural bounties

**Framework Alignment:**
- Quantum energy infusion is quantum manipulation (framework)
- Rift spawns are coherent energy patterns (framework)
- Mortals interpret through cultural lenses (framework pattern)

## Quantum Artifacts

### Territory Bonuses

**Mechanism:**
- Stable anchors from conquered territories grant bonuses
- Example: A Reader fragment for spying on enemy plans
- These are world-specific implementations of anchor mechanics

**Framework Alignment:**
- Anchors are stable constructs (framework concept)
- They offload persistence and grant abilities (framework function)
- World-specific implementation: artifacts from conquered territories

## Combat and Conquest

### Mortal Wars

**Proxy Conflicts:**
- Conquest happens via mortal wars
- First Children influence outcomes but don't directly control
- Battles could collapse rifts, spawning ghost-like energy patterns (echoes of fallen armies) that haunt borders

**Framework Alignment:**
- Ghosts/spirits are coherent energy patterns (framework)
- Rift collapses create energy patterns (framework)
- Mortals fight the wars (respects physical laws)

### Quantum Uncertainty

**Chance and Probability:**
- No literal dice (that'd violate the no-separate-realms rule)
- Quantum uncertainty handles randomness
- Attacks involve probabilistic collapses: an invading force might "roll high" if their quantum patterns align favorably, or fizzle into haze if not

**Framework Alignment:**
- Quantum mechanics provide inherent uncertainty (physical law)
- First Children can't violate physical laws (framework rule)
- Even gods can't have total control (framework limitation)

## Victory Conditions

### Winning the Game

**Objective:**
- First First Child to control all territories "wins" the round
- But the game resets periodically
- World-specific exception: built-in quantum loop that reforms the board without full destruction

**Framework Alignment:**
- Quantum loops are possible within framework (creative use of quantum mechanics)
- Resets work within framework constraints
- No full destruction required (world-specific exception)

### Consequences

**Peer-Enforced Penalties:**
- Losers might face peer-enforced penalties
- Example: Temporary Quantumancy dampening
- This demonstrates peer judgment and enforcement (framework concept)

## Framework Conformance

**Conforms to Framework:**
- All mechanics operate through quantum manipulation
- Physical laws cannot be violated (no causality breaking, no separate realms)
- Mortal quantum abilities are fragments (world-specific distribution)
- Anchors are used appropriately (cycles, artifacts)
- Peer judgment applies (penalties, potential liberation)

**World-Specific Expansions:**
- Territory system as quantum nodes
- Cycle mechanics via anchored constructs
- Quantum artifact bonuses
- Game reset mechanism (quantum loop exception)
- Strategic fragment distribution
