**Nalan Apostasy – Full Party Reference (Novel Canon – November 2025)**  
Eight souls who chose each other over every oath they were born to.

1. **Loisa Xialing**  
   The strategist. Dry, precise, three moves ahead and half a lifetime away from home. First Earth soul to fall through into this world; built her flawless mask from absolute zero. No one here has ever guessed she wasn’t born to these skies—except Gabby. Mentors the girl with affectionate bullying that hides ferocity: she will not let the second castaway break the way she once almost did. The only time her composure ever cracks is when Gabby swears in modern English and Loisa has to pretend she’s coughing.

2. **Grayson Mckenzie**  
   Sea captain temporarily “stretching his legs” ashore because Loisa is his dearest friend and he’ll follow her into hell with a grin. Big, sun-browned, pun-obsessed sailor who can eviscerate a man with one lazy, sarcastic question and still sound like he’s offering you rum. Voice like warm honey over steel. Hopelessly, helplessly in love with Elsbeth Thane; she’s the only person his tongue never cuts.

3. **Elsbeth Thane – Greyrose Knight (covert)**  
   Undercover as just another member of the group. Calm, immovable, the last line between Loisa and death. Wears her oaths quietly; wears Grayson’s quiet tenderness even more quietly. Slow-burn romance built on shared silences, perfectly timed cups of tea, and the fact that neither has ever needed to say the words.

4. **Calder Voss – Greyrose Knight (overt)**  
   Chainmail, sword, and a personality permanently set to “lecture.” Brusque, didactic, attempts at kindness still sound like reprimands. The visible half of the Greyrose mandate. Slowly—very slowly—learning that not every moment needs a sermon, mostly because Grayson’s amused drawl can make him feel two feet tall.

5. **Hara Kest**  
   Field medic with two modes:  
   - Default: mouse-quiet, eyes down, barely audible.  
   - Crisis: training slams in like a portcullis; voice flat and absolute, orders no one ignores.  
   Afterwards she’s mortified, apologizes for “bossing everyone around,” and scrubs blood from under her nails while hating herself for sounding like she belonged in charge.

6. **Gabrielle “Gabby” D’Olivier**  
   Second Earth soul, freshly arrived. Brash, zero deference, swears in three languages (two of which don’t exist here). Copes by being loud and pun-slinging. Grayson’s instant partner in linguistic crime, Loisa’s secret protégé, Hara’s ride-or-die. The only person who can make Loisa laugh with a single twenty-first-century reference.

7. **Rowan Ashby**  
   Fieldcraft instructor. Patient, watchful, speaks like he’s gentling spooked horses. The one who teaches the rest how to walk without leaving ghosts.

8. **Marek Ilyan**  
   Walking encyclopedia who takes everything literally and is serenely aware of the comedic delay this produces. Invited along precisely because he finds his own processing lag hilarious. Will laugh at a joke three hours late, announce deadpan, “The jest has docked, Captain. Well played,” and consider the matter closed. Thin, intense, quietly proud of being the slowest fuse in the room.

**Key Relationships (the ones that will carry scenes)**

- Loisa ↔ Grayson: Aubrey–Maturin bond, naval edition. Lifelong loyalty wrapped in affectionate mockery. He was the first native who ever treated her like she belonged; she has never stopped being grateful and has never once said it.
- Grayson ↔ Elsbeth: slow-burn romance of perfect, quiet gestures. He saves all his gentleness for her.
- Loisa ↔ Gabby: the only two people on the continent who know what a microwave is. Mentorship on the surface, fierce shared survival underneath.
- Grayson ↔ Gabby: instant pun-banter conspiracy, zero rank, pure glee.
- Grayson ↔ Calder: lazy lethal sarcasm as training tool.
- Elsbeth ↔ Calder: the two Greyrose Knights, bound by oath and occasional friction.
- Gabby ↔ Hara: ride-or-die; Gabby cuts Hara’s post-crisis apologies off with hugs or terrible jokes.
- Elsbeth ↔ Hara: quiet scaffolding; Elsbeth clears space so Hara can flip into command mode and takes the blame afterward so the guilt has somewhere else to land.
- Marek → everyone: beloved chaos agent who saves the day with footnotes and detonates hours later with laughter.
