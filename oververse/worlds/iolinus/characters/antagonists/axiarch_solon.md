## Scene Excerpt

> Axiarch Solon paused at the table in the dark room. It was the only remaining table among the rubble of the inn. A balding, sweating man sat in the only remaining chair, all but his right hand bound to the chair. The arm was tied to the table.
>
> Shadows flickered about the room from the light of the fire in the fireplace. Those shadows played across Axiarch's face and his face seemed to flicker with each shadow. One moment it was a leer. The next, a pout. The next, a maniacal grin.
>
> In truth, the gentle, beneficent smile on his face never wavered. A Knight of the Ebon Throne was above petty emotions. He only took satisfaction at a job well-done in glory to Malachor. Nothing else mattered. His faith gave him peace. His purpose gave him strength.
>
> Axiarch glanced over the unrolled leather toolkit on the table. His mind catalogued each instrument with a casual ease born from long familiarity. Each tool had its purpose. The blades to cut and separate. The hooks to grab and hold. The pliers to twist and rend. He even had needle and thread to bind and sew when the wounds grew too severe and Axiarch's work hadn't been completed yet.
>
> Many men, even those of his order who should have known better, thought it was odd that he kept healer's instruments in a kit designed to inflict pain. Those men often delighted in the pure butchery of their task, relishing the pain they caused. A slight frown of distaste flashed briefly across Axiarch's smile. Those fools lost sight of their true purpose, placing their wants and desires above Malachor's goals. They were ordained to harvest the truth from the guilty, punishment was Malachor's right.
>
> Axiarch was humble enough to acknowledge that Malachor didn't speak to a lowly Knight, even one of his high rank. Once the questioned died, Malachor would certainly extract whatever information he wanted. But, those secrets would be lost to Axiarch if he was incautious enough to kill the questioned before they gave their final accounting to Malachor.
>
> That was why Axiarch not only carried healer's instruments in his tool kit, but had trained with the best of the healers that he could find. He needed to prolong the life of the questioned until he was convinced that he had extracted the full and accurate truth. Only then would he grant release for them both. For Axiarch, that final release would be exquisite, amplified by the satisfaction of accomplishment in his service to Malachor. For the unfortunate soul under his care, the release was illusionary and transitory. It might have escaped Axiarch, but the torment that awaited it under Malachor's stern gaze made Axiarch shiver.
>
> "Do you know why the number five is so sacred?" He asked in a patient, almost friendly tone. "We see it everywhere, the number of aspirations."
>
> He caressed the hilt of a cleaver-like blade before he drew it from its slot in the tool kit. He used the flat side of the blade to enumerate the aspirations on his fingers.
>
> "Dominion, Plenty, Surrender, Ascent, and Cleansing." He paused a moment, tasting each one like a pleasant drug on his tongue.
>
> "We see the number five in the days of the week. The number of months is fifteen, which is three fives. We have five fingers on each of our hands, and five toes on each of our feet."
>
> Axiarch paused and turned to the man, the blade held in his right hand and the thumb of the left hand tested the edge.
>
> "Indeed, anything less than five is seen as incomplete," he drew the blade in a quick chop, expertly aimed at the little finger, "unworthy."
>
> The severed finger jumped from the table and Axiarch caught it at the top of its arc and held it to his eye to examine it while the man screamed in pain. He waited patiently for that screaming to subside.
>
> "Even your apostasy has five orders, much like my own, your own church does."
>
> He set the finger on the table, balancing it on the severed end. The blood seeping from the cut held the digit upright as it began to cool and congeal. Axiarch leaned forward to whisper in the ear of the sweating, panting man and let his voice turn soft, breathy, almost as though he were imparting some great secret.
>
> "That is because our god, Malachor, likes the number five." He stood up straight and let his voice return to it normal, unhurried urbanity.

- There is an "apostasy" with five orders that mirrors the church structure
