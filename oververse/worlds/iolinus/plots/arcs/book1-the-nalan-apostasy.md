# Orientation Tour

**Category:** Story Structure / Plot Element  
**Status:** Canon - Book 1 Route  
**Related Characters:** Gabby D'Olivier, Loisa Xialing, Grayson McKenzie, and the eight-person party  
**Related Locations:** Ibelin, Doicas, A'Thohalla, Orindibar Desert, Orindibar, Dwarhoffen, Caxias, Kitzigen Falls

## Overview
An educational tour for new Earth soul Gabby D'Olivier, designed to show her how Iolinus really works. The tour introduces her to cultures, politics, and dangers while serving as Apostasy recruitment/integration. This is the route for Book 1: "The Nalan Apostasy."

## Route (Book 1: "The Nalan Apostasy")

**Structure Note:** Between each travel leg, there are chapters that elaborate on the city they stop in. The pattern is: City → Travel Leg → City → Travel Leg → City, etc.

### Starting Point: Ibelin
- **Opening Chapters:** Gabby wakes up on Ibelin
- **City Chapters:** Multiple chapters elaborating on Ibelin, the Chapterhouse of Nala, and Gabby's initial orientation
- **Orientation:** Introduced to the Nalan Apostasy and their mission
- **Introduction:** Meets her "guide" Loisa Xialing
- **Departure:** Board boat from Ibelin

### Leg 1: Ibelin → Doicas
- **Transport:** Boat (down river)
- **Destination:** Doicas
- **Goal/Educational Purpose:** 
  - Loisa points out the "hordes" (monsters) that roam through the wilds of Iolinus
  - Demonstrates how these hordes keep Iolinus from becoming a united world (partly)
  - Safe observation from the boat in the middle of a wide river - allows Gabby to see the danger without being in immediate peril
  - Introduces the monster threat and its political/social impact

### City Stop: A'Thohalla
- **Duration:** A few months spent with the Thohalla
- **City Chapters:** Multiple chapters elaborating on A'Thohalla and Thohalla culture
- **Goal/Educational Purpose:**
  - Introduces the reader to Thohalla culture (mix of Old Testament Israelites and Romani wanderers)
  - Reveals that the Thohalla were Malachor's chosen people that he played with and ultimately discarded
  - Deep cultural immersion - Gabby experiences their covenant-bound, wandering lifestyle firsthand
  - Shows the consequences of being "chosen" by a god who then abandons you
  - Introduces the concept of divine abandonment and its cultural impact

### Leg 3: A'Thohalla → Orindibar Desert → Orindibar
- **Journey to Desert:** Travel from A'Thohalla to the Orindibar Desert
- **Transport:** Board Polynesian-inspired mag-lev skimmers
- **Route:** Cross the magnetic desert
- **Destination:** City of Orindibar
- **Goal/Educational Purpose:**
  - **Pure storytelling wonder** - A moment of awe and beauty for both Gabby and the reader
  - **Visual spectacle:** Magnetically levitating skimmers floating on black sand
  - **Floating mountains:** The Rathen Mountains (Hallelujah Mountains/Avatar-inspired peaks) float on the magnetic sand, adding to the wonder and spectacle
  - **Demonstrates ancient Quantumancer power:** The mag-lev technology (skimmers and floating mountains) serves as an example of what ancient Quantumancers were capable of
  - **Shows the danger:** The origin story illustrates the casual danger and irresponsibility of ancient Quantumancers
    - An ancient Quantumancer conducted an experiment in magnetic levitation
    - He shattered a large mountain range, basically because he could
    - This created the Orindibar Desert and the floating Rathen Mountains in the process
    - The beauty masks the destruction - showing how ancient Quantumancers could reshape reality on a whim, with devastating consequences
  - **Sensory experience:** The black sand, the levitation, the Polynesian-inspired design, and floating mountain peaks create a memorable, wondrous moment in the journey that simultaneously reveals the terrible power that created it

## Travel Methods
- **Boat:** River travel (Ibelin to Doicas)
- **Thohalla Caravan:** Overland wagon caravan (Doicas to A'Thohalla)
- **Mag-lev Skimmers:** Polynesian-inspired magnetic levitation vehicles for crossing the magnetic Orindibar Desert
- **Non-Thohallan Caravan:** Overland caravan (desert crossing to Dwarhoffen)
- **Ship:** Sea travel (Caxias to Kitzigen Falls)
