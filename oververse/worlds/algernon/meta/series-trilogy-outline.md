## Series goal

Three books. One question. Three stakes (Charlie / Mira / Keyes).

**Not a power fantasy in the usual sense:** Charlie becomes **less** locatable, less predictable, less graspable by people who know him — and the question is whether what remains when physics **stops gripping** him is still **Charlie**.

**Contrast:** Common “Matrix” shorthand treats physical reality as prison and exit as freedom. This story treats **physical coherence** as what makes you **real to others**; **losing** that grip is **loss** more than liberation — closer to illness and grief than triumph.

By Book 3, Charlie must face what Mira could not: **uncertainty of the other side** — not because he is “stronger,” but because three books of character work equip him differently.

## The three variables (summary)

### Charlie

Quota fill; Orcus Sapiens → human baseline; full human life; **no genre savviness**; **starts at level 5** (adult baseline — he never got a level-1 “opt in”; plain Charlie was already sliding away when he arrived). **Forward vs. rot** applies; “go back to level 1” is a dead thought he may not voice.

**Series physical arc:** Slips the leash of physics — early subtle (wrong heals, surviving too much), later **uncanny**; **distance** from everyone who knows him grows **one level at a time** (physics, not only metaphor). See [experiment foundations](../world-building/experiment-foundations.md).

### Mira

Previous participant; reached **level 36** — **highest level on record** before her exit. System also registered her run as **~94.7%** of the way to threshold (same climb, alternate readout). Felt threshold approaching; chose **infrastructure back door** — not death, not ascension; **suspended** outside forward/rot. Charlie is her test of whether she chose correctly. Full profile: [Mira](../characters/main/mira.md).

### Keyes

Runs this experiment **~847,000 years**; does not appear **in person** (FC not embedded in the physical interaction network here). Present as **infrastructure**, level-up channel, institutional texture. **Stated** motive: science (can substrate bootstrap what the Prime Author does natively?). **Actual** mix: boredom broken; Charlie as first interesting datum in ages; deeper drive toward understanding the Prime Author. **No face-to-face philosophical dialogue** with Charlie — the important “Book 3 conversation” **does not happen** as a chat; Keyes still speaks through **system channels** where the story places him. See [Keyes](../characters/main/keyes.md).

## Book 1 — *Orcus Sapiens*

**Pitch:** The long con — by the end, the **return policy** window is over.

**Goal:** Make Charlie **worth losing** to the reader before “what he becomes” fully lands. Cosmology **present** but not dumped; complete story of Charlie being Charlie in a strange place with a strong cast.

**Beats (summary):** Quota arrival; **Team Earth** / Ward’s party see him as database error (sympathetic antagonism); subtle leveling symptoms; Mira finds him, **Matrix**-adjacent explanation beat; Keyes as **name + one odd system moment**, not a cameo. **Ending:** Charlie survives; Team Earth conflict **less purely hostile** but not solved; Mira watchful; reader **comfortable**; deep question **planted**, not urgent.

## Book 2 — *[Title TBD]*

**Pitch:** Curtain pulls back.

**Goal:** Cosmological weight lands; symptoms **undeniable**; reader realizes what genre of book this always was.

**Beats (summary):** Team Earth → **uneasy cooperation** (Charlie adjacent beats hostile); Mira’s **full physics/experiment** conversation when Charlie can hold it; symptoms escalate; **Ro** notices distance first (presence vs. Charlie); Keyes “wallpaper” **more** anomalous. End: reader knows the **question**, **level 36**, Mira’s choice and cost, trajectory toward threshold.

## Book 3 — *Orcus Ascendus*

**Pitch:** The sales pitches.

**Goal:** Charlie at **level 36**, feels what Mira felt; pushes **37**; every stakeholder’s case; **Charlie decides** on first principles.

**Pitches:** Mira (back door), Keyes (continue / for science), Ro (non-pitch **presence**), **rot** as fact not character, **Charlie** (what he knows is real). Ending answers what three books built him to answer.

**Silent questions answered by outcome:** Keyes — *Can physical substrate bootstrap what only the Prime Author has done?* Mira — *Did she give up for nothing?*

---

## Emotional throughline (all three books)

*The distance between Charlie and everyone who knows him — growing one level at a time.*

**Flowers for Algernon** parallel: here there is **no regression** to plain Charlie — plain is **already gone**; path is **forward or rot**. Tragedy: he **didn’t choose to start**, **can’t** choose to stop; only whether what pushes to **37** is still **recognizably** the one who arrived.

Late series: *Is there enough Charlie left for the distance to matter?*
