## 1. Executive snapshot

**Algernon** is a Mystara-ish medieval fantasy world built around **one central dungeon**. Economically and narratively, everything orbits that dungeon.

**Under the hood**, the dungeon is a **First Child (Keyes) experiment**: saturate physical beings with energetic load (TheWay **Strain**) and observe whether they undergo **phase transition** to a **stable waveform**. Nobody has completed the transition; everyone has died before threshold.

**Book 1 — *Orcus Sapiens*** is named for the **character generation** beat: the screen reveals Charlie’s **birth race** (Orcus Sapiens). He **chooses human** and walks in as “just Charlie.” The novel’s pressure is **identity under load** (“the burn”): the experiment may need **Charlie-ness** to run, and the process may destroy it.

**Emotional line everyone shares:** *Everyone is dying in the dark looking for a number nobody knows.*

## 3. World & experiment (facts for planners)

**Algernon-only deep dive:** [experiment foundations](../world-building/experiment-foundations.md) (saturation ±5, rot vs. forward, restlessness, basic-item subroutine, Ro anchoring, Mira backend view). **Not** universal Hypatia canon unless another world ports it. **Trilogy planning:** [series trilogy outline](series-trilogy-outline.md).

- **Operator:** Keyes (FC), **~847,000 years** on this configuration; present as **level-up / system voice** and infrastructure — **not** in-person; no face-to-face “Book 3 dialogue” with Charlie (stakes still via **channels**).
- **Delivery mechanism:** The **dungeon / world** loads more energetic density (instrumentation for the experiment, not decorative LitRPG chrome).
- **Charlie’s origin:** **Quota fill** from Earth; **Orcus Sapiens** converted to human baseline, lived a full human life, **no genre savviness**, **level-5 start** (adult/background — he never opted in at level 1).
- **Orcus under load:** As Strain rises, **human skin** can degrade while the **Orcus template** adapts — he survives loads that should kill “normal” builds; he may not understand why at first.
- **Mira:** Reached **level 36** (record high) / **~94.7%** threshold readout; **back door** to infrastructure; third path besides forward/rot. Empirical skeptic on ascension **coherence**.

## 5. TheWay interface (how scenes should *feel*)

- Default is **console/debug**, not polished MMO UI: logs, IDs, dumps, wetware (Pineal + Amygdala), headache on connect.
- **Keyes** speaks through the same channel the participant treats as “system.”
- **Player-style overlays** and marketplace **skins** are a **learned / acquired layer** — not the default; Charlie may grow into overlays later.
- Commercial / ad overlays (e.g. GatePass) can intrude on top of instrumentation; help about experiment purpose may be **redacted** at participant tier.

## 6. Institutional layer: Team [planet] & Team Earth

- **Team [planet]** is an **official trial designation**: cohorts by **world of origin**; performance hits **home trial** and **population ledger**.
- **Team Earth** = Earth’s cohort on Algernon. Better collective performance → **fewer Earth natives** converted to **dungeon mobs** for trial use. Stakes are **ledger math**, not melodramatic “save the world” unless you earn it.
- **FC favor economy (rhyme only):** Trial throughput sits in the same **transactional** family as broader FC **favor trading** (ledgers, delayed consequences, wrong category in the file). See `oververse/worlds/mc20/WORKING_COPY.md` for the **pattern**; Algernon need not explain every FC on page one.

### Charlie vs. Team Earth (conflict spine)

- Charlie was **quota-filled** to join **Team Earth** (Ward’s party). **Birth substrate** was wrong on intake: he’s **Orcus Sapiens**, not eligible for official Team Earth membership. **Slot shows filled**; admin can’t clear it; **Homo Goblinus** kill paperwork in **blue ink** unprocessed; **three months** of tickets before anyone tries **asking** him to cooperate.
- **Team Earth’s antagonism is sympathetic:** metrics short, **Earth pays**, they see him as a **database error** costing lives — not mustache-twirling villains.

**Planning note:** Ward’s roster is the **proposed** Team Earth party for fiction; if the beat works in draft, **Ward’s team steps in** as that cohort. Full roster and dynamics: `characters/supporting/ward-party-team-earth.md`.

**Party vs. Charlie (when they intersect):** Charlie was **meant** for this party; system put him on **Orcus Sapiens assessment** instead—they think he’s stealing a slot. Resolution beat: he **replaces** no one; he fills a **gap** they didn’t know they had. **System knew; told nobody.**

**Thematic (Team Earth thread):** Genuine investment in people vs. institutional extraction; Ro = wonder refusing to harden—*especially* in the assessment dungeon.
