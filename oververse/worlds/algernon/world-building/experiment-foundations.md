## Operational picture (how Algernon is set up)

**Physical substrate here** is modeled as **waveform interaction** — coherence maintained through mutual restriction and reinforcement. What participants experience as matter, mass, gravity, time are treated as **emergent** from that interaction network within this experiment’s instrumentation.

**Leveling** on Algernon is **energetic load accumulation**. The **dungeon and the world** are the **delivery mechanism**: as load increases, the local interaction network’s grip on a subject loosens — they become less locatable and less predictable by ordinary physical standards; the world stops holding them as hard.

**The threshold** is the energetic value at which physical bonds release and substrate converts toward pure waveform. It is treated as theoretically derivable; **nobody has derived it. Nobody has crossed it successfully** in the sense the experiment tracks. **Keyes has run this configuration for ~847,000 years.**

**Death** (in-run): coherence **collapse before** threshold — interaction structure fails under load before conversion completes; pattern disperses.

**Ascension** (if it works): a **medium swap** — coherence that was maintained through physical interaction becomes coherence through something else. The laws that shaped and located the participant may cease to apply in the same way. The story question is whether the **pattern that makes a person themself** survives that.

---

## The rot

If a leveled participant **stops pushing forward**, there is **no clean deascension** and no reliable return to baseline. Once energetic load has begun accumulating, the network has been stressed; it does **not** re-tighten cleanly. Stopping does not restore prior coherence — it **frays**. The participant decays toward something **diminished** (not “plain again,” not ascended, not Mira’s infrastructure back door): **less, fading**.

**Options** for a leveled participant on the standard path: **forward** or **rot**. No third exit except **Mira’s back door** (see [Mira](../characters/main/mira.md)) — the only known suspension of forward/rot.

## Universal restlessness (leveled life on Algernon)

Anyone who has leveled on Algernon carries a **baseline restlessness** — low hum, itch, stillness slightly wrong. Sleep works; “lazy days” do not land the way they used to. The condition is **so universal** it often goes unnamed; veterans may forget what **before** felt like.

**Reads socially as** drive or ambition. **Early rot** can look like personality — not obviously pathology.

As levels accumulate, restlessness **deepens**; gaps between “pushes” grow uncomfortable; relief from leveling can become seductive. (Story use: Charlie may chase relief while telling himself he’s being brave.)

## Saturation (in-world term)

**Saturation** is the in-world label for **how much ascendency energy** a participant has accumulated — a **gradient**, not a binary flip. Participants become increasingly saturated until the local network struggles to hold them at the same density as surroundings.

### Saturation band (interaction)

Interaction between entities (people, objects, weapons, monsters) is **effortless within a ±5 level** saturation band — everyday physics handles it.

**Beyond ±5**, differential **costs effort** — high-saturation acting on low-saturation is exhausting and imprecise (force doesn’t transfer cleanly). Symmetric for monsters, gear, and people: mismatch reads as “minimal damage” or awkward handling, not necessarily a separate “magic shield” layer.

### Saturation-tuned equipment

Weapons/armor are **matched to bands** (e.g. a sword saturated for level 20 interacts cleanly in roughly the 15–25 band). Gear **ages out** as the participant levels — economic pressure to replace for **current** saturation, not biggest number.

High-saturation gear is **hard to manufacture** without degrading materials; loot from high-saturation threats is valuable because it is **pre-matched**.

### Clothing, food, basic items — system subroutine

To avoid everyday life becoming impossible at high saturation, a long-running **background rule** auto-aligns **basic** tagged items to the participant’s saturation. **Quest objects** are excluded — flagged items (including quest food) behave as plot requires, which can produce **grounded LitRPG absurdity** (e.g. chest bread vs. lunch).

Illustrative logic (not in-world code literal):

`if item.tagged == basic and item.tagged != quest_object { item.saturation = player.saturation }`

**Gray area:** gifts, crafted objects, sentimental gear — **ambiguous**; case-by-case for story.

## Mira and the “backend”

Mira’s survival **inside interface infrastructure** gives her a view of **how Algernon’s stack behaves** — subroutines, legacy paths, what stayed untouched because it still “compiles.” She can explain mechanics (e.g. basic-item tagging) in **logic-first** terms and then translate for Charlie. **Keyes’s design fingerprints** sit in the architecture even when Keyes is not attentively “watching” a given moment.

## Anchoring (Rowena / Ro — series mechanic)

**Ro levels alongside Charlie** in the series plan: inside each other’s saturation band, **interaction stays effortless** — presence, contact, and care without paying the ± differential cost.

**Anchoring:** a higher-saturation participant can **bridge** differential for someone else, **paying** the effort so the other does not — e.g. Ro toward a baseline NPC while Charlie connects **through** her.

At high saturation, Ro is not only emotionally central: she can function as **phase bridge** to the ordinary physical world Charlie is leaving. **Losing Ro** at those tiers is not only grief — it is **physical isolation** in the saturation sense.

*(Ro is also [Team Earth / Ward’s party](../characters/supporting/ward-party-team-earth.md); series logistics of her path vs. party membership are a plotting detail.)*

## Identity as the measured variable (Charlie)

Charlie’s **identity** is the experiment’s hinge — not race, skill pick, or power fantasy. Whether **Charlie-ness** survives as **what holds coherence together** changes is the operational question Keyes’s run is built to stress.
