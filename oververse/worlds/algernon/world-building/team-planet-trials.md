## Official designation

**Team [planet]** is an official trial designation: cooperating participants are grouped by world of origin so performance credits the right home trial and the right population ledger. The same mechanism applies on every seeded world (Team Earth, Team [planet], etc.) — parallel structure, not an Earth-only gimmick.

**Team Earth** is Earth's cohort on Algernon. The better they perform collectively, the fewer Earth natives get converted to dungeon mobs for trial purposes. They are not saving the world dramatically; they are the people who showed up under that banner and carry the math for their origin world.

## Connection to First Children favor trading

Trial performance and population ledgers are part of the same **transactional** layer as the broader FC economy: abstract obligation made concrete in registry form. Planet-cohort throughput can **settle** or **credit** inter–First Child obligations (compare the MC20 pattern: mortals as instruments of FC-to-FC transactions — see [MC20 WORKING_COPY](../../mc20/WORKING_COPY.md)). Algernon does not need to name every FC on page one; the rhyme matters: **ledgers, thresholds, delayed consequences**, and categories that fail when someone is filed wrong.

## Charlie vs. Team Earth (quota conflict)

Charlie was quota-filled to join **Team Earth** (Ward's party — see [ward-party-team-earth.md](../characters/supporting/ward-party-team-earth.md)). Contractors did not check the birth substrate field. He is **Orcus Sapiens**; he does not qualify for official Team Earth membership. The slot shows as filled. Nobody can fix it administratively. The Homo Goblinus kill contract is filed in blue ink and has not been processed yet.

Nobody thought to ask Charlie to cooperate until they had spent three months trying to have him killed.

## Why Team Earth reads as antagonists (without being villains)

They are under enormous pressure, making rational decisions with incomplete information. Every floor cleared, metric hit, and threshold maintained ties to how many people back home are **not** converted to dungeon mobs. They did not ask for that responsibility.

Then a contractor delivered an Orcus Sapiens in a human suit and called it a quota fill: slot shows filled, metrics short, performance suffering, **Earth pays the difference**. From their perspective Charlie is not a person with a backstory; he is a **database error** costing lives back home.

## Specific grievances

### The slot problem

Team Earth had an open slot. They requisitioned a replacement through proper channels. Contractors confirmed delivery; the slot showed as filled. Then nothing: no new member appeared, no introductions, metrics did not improve — the slot stayed occupied by someone who apparently exists on Algernon and does not show up.

They filed tickets, escalated, and got the same answer: *Participant on world. Contract fulfilled. Invoice paid.* For three months.

### The performance problem

Running short degrades quest completion, floor clearance times, point accumulation — every metric feeding the **'RTH trial performance threshold** by exactly one missing party member. They know the sustained deficit’s effect on conversion rates back home. They watch strangers pay for a contractor’s blank intake field.
