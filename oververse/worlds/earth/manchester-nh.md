## The Setup

The Amoskeag terminus landed here. That's the whole reason. Not the watershed, not the political fracture, not anything intrinsic to the city — the terminus landed and now everything radiates from Amoskeag Circle whether Manchester asked for it or not.

The Commission set up in the Millyard within days. The Cayminus enrollment kiosks went up before most people had processed what was happening. The official explanation arrived into a city that had spent a summer figuring it out on its own. That sequence — chaos, then administration — is the founding wound. It hasn't healed. It probably won't.

---

## The City

Manchester is not a monoculture and never was. New England progressive and rural independent in roughly equal proportion, on the same streets, in the same neighborhoods, sometimes in the same families. The walls didn't create that tension. They made it the central question. Every Gatelock crossing is a small version of the argument the city has been having with itself for decades: what do we owe each other, and does "each other" include them.

The integration metric here is volatile. It spikes in places you'd expect, craters in places you wouldn't, and the pattern doesn't follow obvious lines. The Commission watches it carefully and says nothing publicly about what they see.

This is where the story lives. Not in a city that resolved the question — in one that's still fighting it out, with walls making the stakes legible and aliens at the table whether anyone invited them or not.

## The Locations

**Amoskeag Circle** — the terminus, the entry point, the reason for all of it. Where species first arrive on Earth. Where humans first encounter something they don't have a category for. The Commission has a presence here. So does every species with a reason to meet arrivals. The integration metric at Amoskeag Circle is the highest sustained score in the city — necessity cooperation, but sustained.

**The Millyard** — Commission headquarters. 19th-century brick mill buildings along the Merrimack, chosen because the space was available and the hobgoblin who negotiated the lease was good at it. Where the human outreach operation runs, where Commission contracts get filed, where the Kitsune arbitration offices are. The building that processes your paperwork was processing textile orders a generation ago. The canal network underneath is the subject of at least three active Wall Commission petitions and one ongoing jurisdictional dispute nobody can figure out how to close.

**The I-93 Corridor** — the artery through the city, subdividing it. The land on either side became distinct zones; the only crossings are Gatelocked interchanges. The corridor shoulder is where the Gnoll camp is, where the Pachi pass through, where the RV wagons stage. Inside the city but outside the rooms. Its own thing.

**The Wall Commission Plaza** — neutral interchange ground near I-93/I-293. Where species business gets conducted publicly, petitions get heard, Pristails position themselves because something is always about to happen. The most functional cross-species institution on Earth operates out of a converted rest stop and nobody designed it that way.

**The neighborhoods** — the fault lines run through them. The blocks where the integration metric trends quietly upward because a Felari has been here long enough to stop performing and a few humans noticed. The blocks where Earth Front has a presence and the metric craters on a Tuesday for no reason anyone official will acknowledge. The streets where the hobgoblin who's been in the Manchester planning office since before the walls is just the hobgoblin from the planning office, because what else would you call him.

## The Conflicts in Play

**Earth Front** has four wings and not all of them agree on what they're doing. The principled opposition believes the Commission is illegitimate on its face. The pragmatic wing wants leverage, not removal. The political wing is trying to rebuild human governance structures that can negotiate rather than just resist. The hardline wing calls everyone who works for the Commission a collaborator and means it. The fringe acts independently and the other wings have complicated feelings about that.

The founding grievance is the Summer — having organized without alien help, having built something, and then watching the Commission arrive to administer what they had already begun managing. That's not an abstract ideology. That's a memory, and it belongs to specific people who are still here.

**The integration metric** is not a secret but the Commission's read of it is. The Iridari broadcast what they witness. The hobgoblin admin corps files what it measures. The gap between what people feel is happening in their neighborhood and what the metric says is happening is a recurring source of friction that the Commission handles with careful neutrality and Earth Front handles with less care.

**The NDA** is known to exist by anyone who's thought about it. Not common knowledge — but the pattern is legible if you ask enough species the same question and notice what they don't say. Nobody's found the filing yet. That doesn't mean nobody's looking.

## The Protagonist's Position

Human outreach employee for the Commission. Works for the institution managing the situation. Gets called a collaborator by Earth Front for it. Has access — through the job — that reveals the trial narrative is thinner than advertised. Cannot say so. The truth doesn't set anyone free here. It's accelerant.

Based in the Millyard. Works the neighborhoods, the corridor, the Wall Commission plaza. Knows which Iridari is on which block. Knows which Geliss is studying what. Knows the Pachi by name. Knows which Earth Front wing is in which room and what they want this week.

Does the job anyway. Tuesday in Manchester.
