# Manchester NH — Ground Zero

**Category:** Geography  
**Related:** [Trial Mechanics](../../trial-mechanics.md), [Species Presence](../races/species-presence.md)

## The Terminus

Manchester NH is where Platform 934 landed. It is the hub around which the Earth trial's initial infrastructure organized. It is ground zero by virtue of being ground zero — not by design, not by significance, not because of anything intrinsic to the city.

The FC are not clarifying whether this was intentional.

This silence has generated an entire mythology of intentionality. Scholars write papers about the Merrimack watershed. The Iridari Attenborough contingent has done segments on the Millyard as symbolic infrastructure. Succession theories involving the specific political fracture of southern New Hampshire circulate in trial-observer communities across the multiverse. Every species present has its own read on why Manchester specifically.

None of them are confirmed. The Gray at the plaza edge is not answering questions about it.

## The City's Character

Manchester NH is not a monoculture. It sits in near-equal tension between two fundamentally different visions of what community means — the New England progressive tradition and the rural independent tradition exist here in roughly equal proportion. Trump yard signs and FDT bumper stickers on the same street. Not as anomaly — as baseline condition.

This makes it a harder trial city than a monoculture would be. The walls didn't create the division. They made it legible.

The integration metric in Manchester is *volatile*. It spikes in certain neighborhoods, craters in others, and the pattern doesn't follow obvious lines. The FC designer chose — or happened upon — a city that is already negotiating with itself about what cooperation means. The trial applies quantum pressure to fault lines that were structural and ongoing before Day One.

The experienced observers who've seen other trials look at Manchester NH and nod slowly. You don't test social scalability somewhere that's already figured out how to agree.

## Wall Geometry

Manchester sits as a sub-room within Hillsborough County. The surrounding towns — Bedford, Hooksett, Goffstown, Auburn — are their own sub-rooms. The county wall forms the outer boundary. A traveler moving between Manchester and a non-adjacent town in a different county passes two sets of walls.

**The I-93 Corridor** runs through the city. Corridor walls follow the highway right-of-way. The land on either side of I-93 became distinct zones, connected only via corridor Gatelocks at interchanges. Route 28/Mammoth Road hits the corridor wall at I-93 with no Gatelock — there is no interchange infrastructure to support one. The wall is just wall.

**The Merrimack River** passes through tunnels where walls bisect it. Walls end at bank level along parallel stretches. The river remains navigable. The mill canals threading through the Millyard create a network of sub-bank passages that the wall system handles inconsistently — a known orphaned geometry issue the Wall Commission field corps has been adjudicating for months.

## The Millyard

The stretch of 19th-century brick mill buildings along the Merrimack is the most layered geography in the city. Historically the economic engine of Manchester — textile mills, then manufacturing, then repurposing through several cycles. By trial Day One it was already in a state of partial occupation: some buildings fully active, some partially converted, some empty.

Post-wall the Millyard has become something harder to categorize. The river access, the building scale, the existing partial vacancy, and the location relative to the I-93 corridor wall have made it attractive to several species for overlapping and occasionally conflicting reasons. It is not a district with a character yet. It is a space being negotiated.

The canal network underneath is the subject of at least three active Wall Commission petitions.

## The Accidental Crossroads

The terminus being Manchester NH means the city has a density of species presence that most rooms don't. Not high density — the wild races are individuals, not communities — but enough that the city functions as a de facto point of first contact for the region.

Humans coming in from Hooksett or Bedford on corridor transit encounter species they wouldn't see in their home rooms. The Wall Commission neutral interchange plaza near the I-93/I-293 junction is where most species business happens. The Kitsune arbitration offices are nearby. The Cayminus enrollment kiosks are at every Gatelock. The Iridari are on every elevated surface.

Manchester NH didn't ask to be the place where humanity first encounters the multiverse. The multiverse showed up anyway.
