## 1. What Happened

The FC activated the QWAN. That is the complete origin of the Earth situation.

FC observation of Earth flagged an anomaly — a catalogued repo species developing further and faster than baseline projections. Human development had outpaced what the repo entry modeled. The hypothesis was QWAN interaction with sapiens biology. Earth was a convenient sandbox: single planet, clean propagation envelope, a catalogued species with acceptable institutional risk. FC activated the QWAN carrier signal and stepped back to observe.

They did not put up walls. They did not design a trial. They activated a signal on a world they had already catalogued and left to develop, then walked away when the results were not what they anticipated.

What they did not anticipate: the QWAN carrier saturating Earth interacted catastrophically with engineered explosive compounds. Military stockpiles detonated prematurely. Weapons caches went off without being triggered. Ammunition became unstable. The machinery of modern warfare started killing the people trying to use it before it could be pointed at anyone. Fuel supplies became unreliable. Motorized vehicles dependent on combustion became dangerous.

Simultaneously, human pineal glands activated. Headaches. Disorientation. The biological toll hardware warming up in eight billion people who had no idea they had biological toll hardware.

The FC, observing results inconsistent with the experiment parameters, lost interest. They walked away. Left the QWAN running. Left no instructions.

The Homo genus staffers who happened to be present — embedded in human institutions for their own reasons, doing their own jobs — were left holding the bag for a situation none of them designed and none of them could simply undo.

The Commission was built in the aftermath. Someone had to manage the chaos.

## 2. The Official Narrative — Commission KPIs

The Commission cannot tell Manchester NH that the walls went up because a staff body panicked and built infrastructure around an active QWAN field to contain a crisis they didn't create and have no mandate to end. That is not a story that holds anything together. It does not give the situation meaning, it does not provide a framework for enduring it, and it does not give the Commission any institutional legitimacy to manage it.

So the Commission maintains an official narrative: this is a trial. Humanity is being evaluated for integration into the wider multiverse. The walls are the evaluation mechanism. The KPIs define what the evaluation is measuring. When the KPIs resolve positively, the walls come down.

This narrative is functional. It gives people something to work toward. It frames alien presence as purposeful rather than arbitrary. It gives the Commission's outreach operation a rationale. It is also, underneath, constructed — a story assembled from pieces of truth shaped into something livable.

The KPIs as the Commission states them:

**KPI 1 — Generalist Viability:** Does humanity possess the adaptive traits that make it viable as a species operating in a wider multiverse? Behavioral modernity, social scalability, cumulative culture, cross-biome adaptability. The Commission frames this as a formal audit. The honest version is that nobody has confirmed what the FC were actually measuring, or whether they were measuring anything at all.

**KPI 2 — The Colonist Filter:** Can humanity cooperate with non-human species as a default rather than an emergency response? The Commission frames this as the central question the wall system is designed to stress-test. The integration metric tracks it. The Gatelock friction encourages it. Whether any of this reflects FC intent is not something the Commission is positioned to confirm.

The KPI narrative holds because it is not entirely wrong. These are real questions with real stakes for humanity's future in the wider multiverse. The Commission believes in them, to varying degrees, depending on the staffer. The uncomfortable truth is that believing in the KPIs and knowing the FC walked away are not mutually exclusive positions. The situation requires both simultaneously.

Scratch the official narrative hard enough and the seams show. The timeline is undefined. The threshold for "resolved positively" is unspecified. The FC have not confirmed the KPI framework. The integration metric is the Commission's own theory, not a documented FC mechanism. The walls might come down. Nobody actually knows.

## 3. How It Actually Happened

The QWAN activated without warning. No preparation period. No advance notice from the FC. One day the signal saturated and human pineal glands activated across the planet. Headaches. Disorientation. The biological toll hardware warming up in eight billion people who had no idea they had biological toll hardware.

Then the explosions started.

Military stockpiles, weapons caches, fuel depots — anything with sufficient explosive or combustible energy density began interacting badly with the QWAN saturation. Premature detonations. Cascading failures through connected ammunition stores. Fuel supplies becoming unstable, then dangerous, then effectively unusable for high-output combustion. The process took weeks to fully manifest and months before anyone understood the pattern.

By the time the pattern was understood, the implications were clear: modern mechanized warfare was no longer viable. The weapons didn't reliably work. The fuel that powered the delivery systems was gone or dangerous. The supply chains that maintained the machinery had collapsed.

This was the Summer of Chaos. QWAN saturated, explosive infrastructure failing, global communications fragmenting — and no walls yet. No alien presence. No explanation. No management. Humans dealing with a signal nobody explained and weapons that didn't reliably work anymore.

No aliens to blame. No institutions to organize against. Just humans surviving and organizing because someone had to make decisions. Rough governance structures emerged. Communities developed a sense of ownership over their own survival that nothing that came after would fully dislodge.

Then the Amoskeag terminus opened. The Fall.

The Commission came through into Manchester mid-chaos — institutional, organized, already constructing a narrative. They came into a world without walls. The walls came from the Commission.

The decision to put up walls was not FC directive — it was Commission judgment under pressure. The hobgoblin admin corps ran the arithmetic on the chaos and concluded that structure was the only available tool. Human administrative boundaries were already precisely mapped. Limited-access highways already defined natural movement corridors. The Commission used what was there because that is what a staff body under pressure uses: the available infrastructure.

Cayminus identified the Gatelock payment opportunity within days of the terminus opening and built the infrastructure immediately. The Commission assembled around the Millyard because the Millyard was available and the hobgoblin who negotiated the lease was very good at negotiating leases. The official explanation — this is a trial, here are the KPIs, here is what the walls mean — was assembled alongside the wall system, not before it. The narrative and the infrastructure were built together, each requiring the other.

The Fall of Humanity is not a collapse. It is the moment humanity discovers the Summer of Chaos wasn't the worst part. The worst part is finding out what kind of universe you actually live in — and that it was already managing you before you knew it existed.

The kernels of Earth Front are in the Summer. The people who organized during the chaos, built something, kept their community alive through their own effort. They are the ones who feel most profoundly that something was taken from them when the Commission arrived to manage what they had already begun managing themselves. Not just walls. Not just aliens. The specific loss of having figured something out and then having someone show up to explain it to you.

The Commission came through into a city mid-chaos with no advance intelligence and had to figure out Manchester the same way everyone else was figuring out their own situation — from the ground, without a map, in the aftermath of a summer nobody planned for. The difference is the Commission had institutional frameworks and hobgoblin administrative competence that the humans organizing during the Summer did not.

The walls appeared in stages as Commission deployment systems came online — room by room, corridor by corridor, as the admin corps worked through the deployment. The self-correction process — appearing, fading, reforming as the geometry resolved itself — was terrifying to observe and apparently random. It was not random. The hobgoblin admin corps, piecing together the logic as it manifested, eventually understood the pattern. They did not announce this understanding widely. Form 1,203-F(u)(c)(k) has a checkbox for the self-correction process on page twelve. It was added retroactively.

## 4. The Wall System

### 4.1 Grid Basis

The Commission did not impose alien geometry. They used humanity's own organizational system — the administrative boundaries humans drew on their maps to govern territory. Town lines, city limits, county and parish boundaries. Humans spent centuries precisely documenting where these boundaries fall. They called it cartography.

Pure map grid reference lines (lat/long) were considered but rejected as too restrictive. The solution uses administrative boundaries, with regional variation (see 4.2). The same principles apply internationally — minimally contiguous administrative domains (parishes, boroughs, communes, provinces, etc.) define zones; limited-access highways define corridors; Gatelocks mark interchanges between zones and corridors.

### 4.2 Room Definitions

Rooms are the primary enclosed zones created by the wall system. Wall placement varies by region because a single rule does not work: map grid lines were too restrictive; township boundaries work in the Northeast (all land falls under towns) but not in the West where vast unincorporated areas exist outside towns. The solution is multiple placement criteria — same wall types and levels, but placement varies by what the local administrative structure supports.

**Default: County-level walls encompass everything.** Every piece of land falls within a county or parish. County walls form the outer boundary. This eliminates the problem of wide-ranging unincorporated territory with no room structure in the West — unincorporated land is county remainder, inside the county room, not open buffer.

**Regional variation (town/city sub-walls within counties):**

- New England: Towns and cities as sub-rooms within counties. Fully incorporated, contiguous. Manchester NH is a room. Bedford is a room. Hooksett is a room. County walls encompass them.
- Midwest township states: Civil townships where functioning administrative units exist; county walls where townships do not. Both tiers active where both exist.
- South, West, and non-township states: Incorporated cities and towns as sub-rooms where they exist. Census Designated Places for populated unincorporated clusters. County remainder — the unincorporated parts of counties — is room territory (inside the county), not buffer. A sparse Western county with one small town and 2,000 square miles of unincorporated land is one county room; the town has sub-walls within it.

A traveler moving from one town to a non-adjacent town in a different county passes through two sets of walls: the town Gatelock, then the county Gatelock. A traveler crossing county lines where no town exists passes only the county Gatelock.

**County remainder territory:** Unincorporated land within a county — the parts not subdivided by town or city walls — is room territory. It is inside the county room. Bruin Rangers operate primarily in county remainder areas, where there is no town-level governance and their "don't be a problem" jurisdiction applies.

**Corridor walls as organic subdivision:** Rooms can be split further by corridor walls. Where a limited-access highway runs through a county, town, or city, the corridor walls subdivide the space — the land on one side of the corridor and the land on the other become distinct zones, connected only via corridor Gatelocks at interchanges. Administrative boundaries define the primary room structure; corridor walls add an organic layer of subdivision that follows the actual highway network.

### 4.3 Corridors

Corridors are the movement arteries of the wall system. They are defined strictly as true limited-access highways: controlled access, grade-separated interchanges only. Interstates, turnpikes, and their equivalents. US 101 on its limited-access stretches. US 15 in Pennsylvania on its limited-access segments. International equivalents apply (motorways, autobahns, etc.).

Corridor walls follow the highway right-of-way boundary. At interchanges, the walls flare outward to accommodate the ramp geometry — the wall is not a straight line but follows the actual infrastructure footprint. The corridor itself is uninterrupted. Only the connecting roads are Gatelocked or sealed.

Movement within a corridor is free. No system-imposed tolls for distance traveled. No tolls for switching between corridors at interchange junctions. A traveler who enters the corridor network pays once at entry and may travel its full extent.

### 4.4 Rails

Rails do not count as corridors. They are cut by walls but can be torn up and rerouted. Rail infrastructure is severed at room boundaries; restoration or rerouting is a local engineering problem.

### 4.5 Waterways

Rivers and major waterways pass through tunnels in the walls where walls bisect them, creating chokepoints. Where a waterway runs parallel to a corridor or room boundary, walls end at bank level — the water itself is not walled. Technically, one could swim under a wall where it crosses a waterway. Waterways remain navigable and serve as escape valves and emergency passages, not primary corridors.

### 4.6 Wall Extent

Walls extend underground — severing sewers, power, and data lines — and extend high enough to block aircraft and balloons. Exact height undetermined. They are physical barriers with all the limitations that implies. You cannot see through walls or across them. Corridors are walled canyons. You can hear but not see into adjacent spaces.

### 4.7 Hybrid Roads and Capped Segments

Many roads switch between limited-access and full-access character along their length. US 15 in Pennsylvania is the canonical example. These roads produce capped corridor segments:

- Limited-access portions: corridor status, toll-free mainline, Gatelock caps at transition points
- Full-access portions: major roadway status, subject to room boundary Gatelocks where they cross town or county lines
- Transition points: Gatelock caps on both ends of the limited-access segment

This produces what might be called corridors to nowhere — short limited-access segments capped on both ends that connect a room to itself rather than to a separate room. These exist only temporarily. When the rooms on both sides of a capped segment achieve full integration, the integration logic self-corrects: the nub fades, truncating back to the next stable boundary. The walls prune themselves.

If integration subsequently falls below threshold, walls can reform, recreating the nub. The system is not a ratchet. Progress is not permanent.

### 4.8 Gatelocks

Gatelocks are controlled thresholds in the wall system — openings in the physical wall where legitimate roads cross room or corridor boundaries. They are not gates in the mechanical sense. They are passages that register crossings and impose toll friction on anyone passing through. Their locations are consistent with a single principle: any road that crosses a wall boundary either has a Gatelock if it is a legitimate connecting road, or is sealed if it is restricted or minor access.

- Corridor Gatelocks: Located on interchange ramps — the connecting roads where they cross the corridor boundary. The corridor mainline itself has no Gatelocks.
- Major Roadway Gatelocks: Located where non-corridor state routes and significant roads cross room boundaries — town lines, city limits, CDP edges, county lines.
- Gatelock Caps: Terminal Gatelocks at the ends of capped corridor segments. Toll applies. You are not entering a room; you are entering or exiting the corridor system.
- Sealed roads: Minor roads, driveways, restricted ramps. No Gatelock. Just wall. Route 28/Mammoth Road in Manchester hits the corridor wall at I-93 with no Gatelock because there is no interchange infrastructure to support one.

The Gatelock's primary function is not collection — it is friction. The toll makes every crossing a deliberate choice rather than a reflex. The integration metric measures weighted, intentional cross-boundary interaction. Casual, unconsidered movement would produce noise. The friction filters for signal.

As integration builds on a given segment, the friction reduces. The Gatelock becomes easier to cross as the behavior it was encouraging becomes default. When the segment reaches threshold and the wall dissolves, the Gatelock retires with it — the instrument removing itself because the measurement is complete.

### 4.9 Toll Structure, Payment, and Commission Funding

All Gatelocks impose a toll. The payment infrastructure is built on the QWAN network via Cayminus banking systems on Mercara's Cross.

**Payment mechanics:**
- Pineal interface: Pineal glands function as biological crossing tags — they ping the QWAN network for verification and crossing logs. This applies to all sapient species with functional pineal glands.
- Cayminus accounts: Species with established Cayminus accounts cross with lower friction. Wild races whose banking infrastructure predates the current Earth situation by centuries are already in the system.
- Human onboarding: Humans have pineal glands but do not yet have Cayminus accounts. Early trial period humans pay tolls in physical currency or barter while watching other species cross more smoothly. Cayminus offers enrollment services at every major Gatelock. The enrollment form is simple. The terms and conditions are not.

**Revenue flow:**
Toll revenue flows to the Commission's operational account. Cayminus takes an administrative fee first — they built the infrastructure and there is naturally a service charge. The remainder funds Commission operations: the outreach program, human outreach employee salaries, and compensation for wild race Commission staff. Homo genus staffers do not draw operational pay — they are FC staff doing FC work.

The human outreach employees and wild races on Commission payroll are therefore directly funded by toll revenue. Their paychecks come from Gatelock crossings. This is not incidental.

**The toll disruption trap:**
Earth Front factions that organize around disrupting toll collection operate on the assumption that starving Commission revenue collapses Commission operations. This assumption is strategically incorrect in ways the Commission has not volunteered to correct.

More critically: reducing crossing frequency tanks the integration metric on affected segments. The walls strengthen rather than weaken. Earth Front's most tactically coherent anti-Commission strategy is also the most effective mechanism for ensuring the walls never come down.

The conflict of interest inherent in Cayminus designing the payment infrastructure for a trial that tests human integration with alien species has been noted. Kitsune arbitrators have filed the relevant disclosures. They are on page forty-seven of the standard terms.

## 5. Integration Metric

### 5.1 Mechanics

Each wall segment between adjacent rooms carries an integration score from 0% to 100%. The score measures the coherence and quality of interaction across that boundary over time. It builds through equitable toll payment, sustained mixed-species traffic, functional cooperation, and voluntary cross-boundary activity.

- ~90%: Walls dissolve along the segment. Rooms merge into one larger room. Former internal corridor segments become interior roads. New Gatelocks appear at the outer corridor connection points of the merged room.
- 100%: Full coherence. Orphaned nubs and capped segments adjacent to the merged area prune themselves, truncating to the next stable boundary.
- ~75%: Shimmer and haze warning. Visible deterioration of the wall boundary. Social indicator as much as physical one.
- ~50%: Walls reform. Rooms re-split along original boundaries. Gatelocks return. Progress is not banked.

### 5.2 What the Metric Measures — and Does Not Measure

The integration metric measures trajectory, not achievement. A score trending upward from 40% to 65% over a generation is different from a score that spiked to 88% under crisis pressure and then fell to 60% when the crisis passed. Both might look similar at a snapshot. The FC designer is not taking snapshots.

The metric also measures depth, not just quantity. Cooperation for corridor access — gaming the integration score to make walls go away for economic benefit — registers differently from genuine social scalability. Walls that come down for the wrong reasons tend to go back up faster. The Commission built a system that is not easily fooled.

The Commission is not watching for walls coming down. It is watching for walls that stay down.

### 5.3 Merged Room Geography

When rooms merge, the former corridor segment between them becomes an interior road. The previously capped or Gatelocked connection points become open. The merged room acquires Gatelocks at its outer corridor interchange connections.

Former corridor-dwellers living along the absorbed segment find themselves room residents whether they wanted to be or not. The RV wagon camp on the shoulder of what was US 15 between two now-merged Pennsylvania townships wakes up one morning inside a room. The hobgoblin admin corps knew this was a possibility. There is a form.

## 6. Corridor Culture

The free movement of the corridor network — one toll for unlimited travel — produced an organic population of people who entered the corridor and never left.

Corridor communities cluster along Interstate shoulders, median areas, and underpasses. They are composed of merchants, travelers, people who do not want to belong to any room's politics, refugees who cannot afford another toll, and people running from something in their home room. The corridor was not designed for habitation. Humans will habitat anything.

### 6.1 The RV Wagon

Fuel supply chains collapsed within the first years of the trial. The adaptation to non-motorized transport happened in stages that each felt temporary. The aggregate is the current standard corridor vehicle: the RV wagon.

These still look like RVs from a distance. The Winnebago logo is still on the side. The decorative stripe is faded but present. The bedroom is still in the back. The propane stove may or may not have been converted to a firebox — those who accepted the corridor as permanent home often did the conversion, knocking a stovepipe hole through the roof. Those who did not simply cook outside. Every evening camp along a corridor stretch is marked by a line of fires along the shoulder — the most recognizable human thing in the trial landscape, unchanged from ten thousand years of fire culture.

The motorized components were stripped for parts or simply abandoned in place. A hitch was bolted to the front bumper. The windshield was knocked out or a slot cut through it for reins. Pachi solid-disc wheels were adopted as pneumatic tires failed and replacements became unavailable.

The pedal-operated motivator for draft animals is a corridor innovation that has no official name. It does not need one. Everyone knows what it is for. The draft animals have opinions about it that they express clearly and at length.

The wood trade is the hidden economic backbone of corridor culture. Fire requires fuel. Fuel requires supply chains. The corridor communities with sustainable wood supply have leverage over every community that does not. The Cayminus banking staffers have strong opinions about timber futures.

## 7. The Independent Contractor Dynamic

The non-human species present on Earth during the trial are not refugees. They are not victims of circumstance who arrived seeking safety. They are independent contractors on deployment — professionals with their own priorities, operating under their own organizational structures or as freelancers. The distinction is not semantic — it changes every aspect of the dynamic.

They have functioning civilizations, institutional knowledge, legal codes, and organizational structures. They are on Earth deliberately, in a professional capacity, operating under their own chain of command or personal agenda. They go home when their rotation ends or their contract completes, regardless of outcome.

The closest human analogy is first-world aid workers or contractors operating in a crisis zone — but without the implied institutional coordination. They bring resources, expertise, and institutional capacity. They are not always competent. They are not always culturally aware. Some are genuinely motivated by something like altruism. Some are there for other reasons. Their indifference — not cruelty, not malice, just the professional indifference of people who have an exit option and no skin in the game — is itself a pressure on the trial's social scalability question.

Humans must build genuine working relationships with beings who could leave tomorrow and probably will eventually. That is the actual test condition for KPI 2 as the Commission frames it. Not desperation cooperation. Voluntary cooperation with beings who do not need you.

### 7.1 Contractor Reaction Spectrum

Experienced contractors read the Earth situation with institutional knowledge humans do not yet possess. They've seen Commission damage-control operations before, or know what one looks like. Their reactions form a spectrum:

- 'This is interesting.' Academic detachment. Probably researchers or junior workers on their first deployment. They find the mechanics novel and the human responses worth studying.
- 'The Commission are really pulling out all the stops on this one.' Experienced professionals with genuine respect for the difficulty of what the Commission is managing. Possibly quietly rooting for humanity. They read the operation as rigorous, not punitive.
- 'Damn, which gray bastard did you piss off to get this type of situation?' Cynical veterans who read the whole setup as prosecutorial — FC negligence dressed in Commission paperwork. They are not wrong that someone made a mess and left. The question is whether it matters who made it.

The difference between the second and third reactions is not a matter of information. They have the same read on the situation. It is a difference in how much weight they assign to what the FC did versus what the Commission built afterward.

Both interpretations are defensible. The FC have not returned to clarify.

### 7.2 FC Manifestation: The Gray

When First Children manifest physically in Hypatia, the form they take is the Roswell Gray — the ambiguous, non-defining humanoid figure already embedded in human cultural mythology. This is not performance for human benefit. It is simply what FC look like when they show up. Other species have their own cultural contexts for the same form.

The Gray is deliberately non-readable. No facial expression. No obvious emotional register. No clear intent communicated through form. This is appropriate for beings that are fundamentally self-sustaining quantum waveforms taking minimum viable physical shape.

The FC are not present on Earth. They ran their experiment and left. The Commission does not file reports with the FC — there is no mechanism to do so and no one waiting to receive one. If the FC are watching from somewhere, they haven't indicated it.

Human cultural mythology contains a substantial record of Gray encounters predating the current situation — abduction accounts, aerial sightings, unexplained contact. These reflect actual FC presence during the final observation and sample collection period approximately 40,000 years ago, including early terminus operations transporting sapiens samples to Mercara's Cross for repository filing. The humans taken through that terminus were returned. They remembered correctly that something extraordinary happened. They had no framework for what it was.

'Which gray bastard did you piss off' is literal slang with a visual referent. Experienced contractors use it without thinking about it anymore. It refers to whoever in the FC made the decision to activate the QWAN and walk away — not to a physical presence watching from a plaza. Nobody knows which one it was. The FC have not returned to take credit.

## 8. The Commission

### 8.1 Structure and Authority

The Commission is the administrative body that assembled in the aftermath of the QWAN activation to manage the resulting situation. It was not pre-positioned, not designed for this, and not operating under FC instruction. It is a staff body that came through the terminus, looked at what the FC had left running, and decided someone had to manage it.

The Commission is primarily staffed by Homo genus personnel — elves, hobgoblins, dwarves, halflings, tinkari — in their designated FC staff roles. Kitsune serve as the arbitration and human-interface layer. Cayminus handles the financial infrastructure. Wild races may be present in various capacities but do not hold command authority within the Commission structure.

### 8.2 Enforcement Posture

The Commission maintains no enforcement force. This is Commission policy, arrived at quickly after observing conditions on the ground — an alien enforcement presence fuels the exact occupation narrative that makes voluntary cooperation impossible. An armed Commission patrol in Manchester streets would collapse the integration metric on every segment it touched.

Protective force (Homo Bovinus) is present at Commission facilities — the Millyard complex and the Amoskeag Circle terminus — in a purely defensive capacity. They are not rangers. They do not patrol. They guard the door.

The Bruin handle everything outside Commission facilities under their own independent authority. They are not Commission employees. The separation is deliberate and understood by everyone except, occasionally, Earth Front.

### 8.3 Entry Requirements and the NDA

Every non-human species arriving on Earth passes through the Amoskeag terminus. Commission customs processes them before they are free to operate on Earth. As a condition of entry, every arriving species — FC Homo staff, wild races, independent contractors — signs a non-disclosure agreement prohibiting disclosure to humans that the trial narrative is constructed rather than FC-authorized doctrine.

The enforcement teeth exist. Commission authority over terminus access means a violation carries the consequence of losing Earth operating privileges. In practice the teeth are rarely invoked.

Most species present on Earth comply not because enforcement is credible but because they understand why the Commission needs the fiction maintained. They have seen how FC operate. They know what "activated a QWAN signal and walked away" looks like from the outside. And most species who have spent time on Earth have developed genuine feeling about the humans there. Nobody wants to be the one who blows it up.

The result is total information asymmetry. Every non-human on Earth knows the trial is Commission mythology. Every one of them is under obligation not to say so. The humans are the only ones who don't know — not because knowledge is unevenly distributed, but because the Commission sealed it at the door.

The NDA is a filing. It has a form number. It exists in the hobgoblin admin corps records. A human who obtains the document — not a confession, just the document — has proof that every non-human on Earth signed something about trial disclosure. That is not the answer. But it is the right question, filed correctly.

Humans who notice that asking "how did your people handle their trial" produces the same evasive non-answer from a Pachi, a Geliss, a Kitsune, and a Hobgoblin have found the same crack from a different angle. The deflection is universal. If you're paying attention, the pattern is legible.

### 8.4 Outreach

Outreach into human communities is one of the Commission's primary tools for influencing conditions outside its own walls. Without enforcement authority, persuasion and relationship are the mechanisms available. Human outreach employees — humans employed by the Commission to interface with human communities — are therefore significant operational assets. They are also the most visible target for the collaborator accusation.

## 9. The Wall System as Commission Design

The wall and corridor system is not standard infrastructure. It is what the Commission built under pressure, using what was available, to make a chaotic situation manageable.

The first-planet mentality problem — humans clustering, self-segregating, never being forced to actually engage — was visible to Commission staff within weeks of arrival. An open world with an active QWAN field and a multiverse terminus on its doorstep would produce enclaves. The walls prevent this. Every boundary crossing requires engagement with the system. Every Gatelock transaction is a data point. The integration metric measures whether that engagement is genuine or purely instrumental.

Experienced contractors arriving on Earth and seeing the wall geometry read the Commission's intent immediately. This is not a system for surviving catastrophe — the catastrophe already happened. This is a system for testing whether humans can voluntarily extend cooperation to beings they don't need, on a Tuesday, when nothing is on fire. The Commission built an instrument that asks a specific question and measures the answer continuously.

The wall system is Commission-specific. It is not exported. It is not a template. It is the answer a competent staff body under pressure arrived at for a specific problem on a specific world.

The walls retire when they are no longer needed. A segment that reaches full integration has answered its question. The instrument removes itself. The end state is no walls — not because the FC returned to take them down, but because every segment answered correctly and there was nothing left to measure. Whether that end state is achievable is the question nobody at the Commission will answer on the record.

## 10. The Wall Commission

### 10.1 Composition and Authority

The Wall Commission is the administrative body responsible for small, local, case-by-case adjustments to the wall system. It operates under strict constraints:

- Composition: Exclusively proven Homo genus staffers on a rotating board — elves, hobgoblins, dwarves, halflings, tinkari. No Homo sapiens. No alien species.
- Kitsune Arbitrators: Human-interface layer. They receive petitions, translate complaints into terms the Commission recognizes, present cases, and draft adjustment terms. Non-voting. They are not there to make decisions. They are there to make the process function.
- Authority: Narrow and specific. Small boundary nudges, orphaned segment resolution, temporary passage grants. Adjustments expire if the integration metric does not stabilize to support them. No precedent. No appeals.
- Process: Public hearings at neutral interchange plazas. Pristails broadcast live. Attendance is optional. Opinions are not.

### 10.2 Why Humans Are Not on the Wall Commission

Humans do not have the institutional knowledge required. They do not understand the legal basis for the walls, the grid system, the corridor definitions, or the integration metric framework. They cannot articulate what they are arguing against in terms the Commission recognizes.

This is not a slight. It is a practical limitation. Humans are new to understanding that the walls exist — they lack the institutional knowledge that species who have seen other wall situations take for granted.

The result is that humans are represented by Kitsune arbitrators — which is itself a trial data point. Can humans extend sufficient trust to an alien species to let them represent human interests in an alien institutional framework? Some do. Some refuse on principle. The zones that hire Kitsune representation tend to get better Commission outcomes. The zones that refuse get what they get.

The conflict of interest in Kitsune arbitrators representing human clients before a Commission they also staff as procedural guarantors, while simultaneously representing alien contractor organizations in other cases, has been fully disclosed. The disclosure documents are on file. Page forty-seven.

### 10.3 De Facto Incorporation

County remainder territory — unincorporated land within a county — has no town-level or city-level sub-walls; it is simply part of the county room. However, when a human settlement in county remainder reaches sufficient size and organizational complexity — defined by specific threshold criteria including population, defined perimeter, semi-permanent structures, and evidence of governance activity — the Wall Commission field corps initiates a de facto incorporation adjudication.

A Wall Commission field agent — typically a hobgoblin admin staffer, as county-remainder encampment adjudication is the lowest-status posting in the field corps — arrives at the settlement with the relevant forms and the news. The settlement is now a provisional sub-room. Town-level walls will follow.

Required filings for de facto incorporation include Form 57-G (initial room registration), Form 128-B(f) (integration metric baseline), and Form 1,203-F(u)(c)(k) (conflict-of-interest waiver, sapiens-specific).

Form 1,203-F(u)(c)(k) is sequentially numbered. The hobgoblin admin corps was aware of the unfortunate designation approaching in the sequence for some time before it arrived. A committee was formed to consider renaming it. The committee filed Form 1,203-F(u)(c)(k) to formally request the renaming. The request is pending.

## 11. The Iridari Witness Culture

Iridari — Pristails — are giant squirrel-derived people with color-changing emotional tails and an instinct for elevated positions and witnessing. They are the de facto witness culture around the Manchester situation. Their tails cannot lie. This creates an ethics situation that has no clean resolution.

What is happening on Earth — and in Manchester specifically — is among the most witnessed events in recent multiverse memory. A 21st-century first-planet species encountering genuine sapient life for the first time, with a terminus on their doorstep and walls dividing their county lines, is a situation that draws Iridari the way fire draws moths. The audience across the multiverse is substantial. The Iridari are proportionally present.

They witness. They travel. They perform what they witnessed for crowds elsewhere — the tail deployed, the colors undulating, the crowd experiencing what the Iridari felt standing on that rooftop in Manchester when the integration shimmer started. The tails cannot lie. The performance cannot be faked. An Iridari who was not present cannot perform it credibly.

Notable witnesses span a spectrum: patient observers with slow indigo tail registers who have spent months documenting one family's relationship with the situation; enthusiastic witnesses with orange-gold bursts who climb to the highest available position whenever anything happens; and the ones who got emotionally compromised somewhere around month six and have never fully recovered their professional distance.

The ones who got compromised are the ones the multiverse remembers.

## 12. Legal Landscape on Earth

There is no galactic legal consensus. There is no unified code, no Galactic Court, no overarching authority with jurisdiction over Earth. Each species present on Earth brought its own legal framework. They are largely incompatible. Nobody is in charge of resolving this.

In practice, Earth's post-wall legal landscape operates by zone:

- Human-governed rooms: Pre-trial human law, variously interpreted, variably enforced, fragmenting rapidly as central institutions collapse
- Alien-staffed districts: Species law of the dominant contractor organization present — Pachi restitution models, Chelonari chain-coherence frameworks, Cayminus contractual law, Kitsune word-binding
- County remainder territory: Unincorporated areas within counties, no town-level governance. Bruin Ranger jurisdiction — informal, effective, unapologetically pragmatic. 'Don't be a problem.'
- Gray zones: Whoever has the most guns or the best deal

The Bruin (Marshals and Rangers) occupy a unique position as contracted enforcers who operate across jurisdictional lines. They enforce local law in alien districts, self-appoint in wilderness and buffer territory, and maintain a studied impartiality about whose law they are currently enforcing. 'We need order. We don't care if it's pretty.'

The Wall Commission is, ironically, the most functional cross-species institution on Earth — not because humans are ready for it, but because everyone needs it and no one can afford to let it fail. Its integration metric for mixed-species proceedings is consistently high. Necessity cooperation at its most naked. Nobody designed this outcome. It emerged anyway.

## 13. Orcus on Earth

Orcus — goblins, orcs, trolls, ogres — were deployed to Earth as adversarial pressure. The Commission arranged their release and geographic dispersal in the early post-wall period, seeding them into county remainder and unincorporated territory where governance was absent and the Bruin presence was not yet established.

The official Commission rationale fits the KPI narrative: adversarial pressure tests whether humans cooperate across species lines under threat. The less official reality is that Orcus deployment is standard procedure in Commission operations, the hobgoblin admin corps had the deployment forms ready, and nobody at the Commission level made a decision so much as processed a requisition.

Humans call them monsters. Experienced wild races recognize them as standard package. The Bruin in county remainder are dealing with them on a Tuesday-by-Tuesday basis and have opinions about the deployment density in the areas around Manchester specifically.

## Notes on Tone and Application

This is not a story about humans overcoming their flaws to join the galactic community. That framing produces a lecture with worldbuilding attached. Readers identify it immediately.

This is also not a story where the alien institutions are the villains. The Commission is damage control. The Cayminus infrastructure is functional. The Bruin are doing their job. The Kitsune arbitrators are managing an impossible situation with the tools available. None of them are enlightened and none of them are evil.

The FC ran a QWAN experiment on Earth, got results they didn't expect, and walked away. That's the whole origin. The "trial" is what the Commission built in the aftermath to make the situation manageable. Everything else is people — human and otherwise — dealing with the consequences of a carelessly abandoned experiment on a Tuesday.

**The Commission's specific situation:** They are managing a mess the FC created and abandoned. The trial narrative is the story they tell because it's the only story that functions. They believe in it to varying degrees. The KPIs are real enough as goals even if their FC authorization is unconfirmed. The integration metric might work. Nobody knows. They keep measuring anyway because the alternative is admitting there's no mechanism and no timeline and no guarantee the walls ever come down.

**The protagonist's specific situation:** Human outreach employee for the Commission. Works for the institution managing the situation. Gets called a collaborator by Earth Front for it. Knows — through access the job provides — that the trial narrative is thinner than advertised. Can't say so. The truth doesn't set anyone free here. It's accelerant.

**The aliens are not morally superior.** They have their own institutional baggage, their own species' histories, their own reasons for being in Manchester that are not purely altruistic. The Kitsune arbitrator operates under tribal instincts that happen to be more contractually formalized. The Bandari are checking your pockets. The Felari are here because Susie wouldn't stop talking about the last situation she witnessed. The Cayminus are taking their fee.

**The scope is Manchester.** What's happening in DC, London, Beijing — rumor, inference, occasional news from the corridor network. Manchester doesn't know if it speaks for the world. Manchester didn't ask to speak for the world. The terminus landed here and now everyone coming through it deals with Manchester whether Manchester is ready or not.

The walls went up. The FC walked away. The Commission is doing what it can. The humans just have Tuesday.
