Prologue

In Earth cultures, the meeting that was happening in the boardroom on the lower floors of an administrative tower in Mercara's Cross would have been called a "Damn It, Jeff" meeting.  For those not familiar with these types of meetings, it should actually be capitalized, "DAMN IT, JEFF!".  This is the type of meeting where members first sit in stunned silence for a significant amount of time after hearing about unpleasant news.  The silence is broken, sometimes minutes after the news, sometimes hours, by one member or the other uttering the words that give this meeting it's name.

"DAMN IT, JEFF!"

Obviously, all offenders aren't named 'Jeff', but if you insert the correct name, the concept remains the same and the remainder of the meeting members spend a significant amount of the meeting time unable to speak but these three words.  It's usually considered that the amount of time dedicated to these opening words is directly proportional to the level of "bad" of the news that generated it.  As such, it's a significant fact to note that this particular meeting was well in to the third hour before something more constructive was said.

"Do we know which Grey did it?" the coyote like cayminus asked.

The staffers looked at each other and the hobgoblin at the near end of the table spoke.

"Grey is a derogatory term for First Children." He huffed.

The cayminus snorted.

"You're one of their staff races, I'm not." He pointed out.  "Now answer the question, which one of them did it?"

An elven woman raised her hand before the hobgoblin could retort.

"We don't know," she said in her musical voice.  "We just know that Earth's QWAN field has been activated and the early reports show massive explosions around the world."

"And it doesn't matter", the dwarf spoke up.  "We now have to pick up yet another mess our 'Jeff' left for us to clean up."

"To be fair," the tinkari gnome mused, "nobody could have predicted the QWAN would have lowered the activation threshold of unstable..."

"Can you say that in Standard please?" The cayminus interrupted the short gnome.

"The QWAN makes things go boom more easily", the dwarf drawled.

The beings around the table shuffled as that sank in.  The kitsune, the other of the only two wild races in the room, coughed and rubbed the bridge of her nose over round framed glasses.

"So, I think we're agreed this is a something we can't just ignore.  The fact that you pulled me into this suggests that you need to negotiate a process and Edmund is here is because you need funding."  She pointed to the cayminus leader.

"Actually, Annalise-Ki", the cayminus leaned back and stroked his chin.  "I'm wondering if it's wise to become involved at all in this.  The staffers here have to react to save First Children reputation, but from my own perspective, is it an investiment worth intervention?"
