# Commission Outreach Resource Personnel (CORP)

**Category:** Institutions
**Related:** [Trial Mechanics](../../trial-mechanics.md), [Species Presence](../races/species-presence.md)

---

## What CORP Is

The Commission Outreach Resource Personnel are the Commission's primary mechanism for influencing conditions outside its own walls. Paid human employees — native to Earth, recruited and trained in Manchester — whose mandate is to assist other humans in adapting to life under the HiRB: the pineal interface, the Gatelock system, the integration metric, and the collapsed tech landscape.

The Commission cannot govern the world and does not want to. CORP is the alternative: demonstrate the model locally, train humans who believe in it, and let them carry it outward organically. The theory is that integration cannot be administered from above. It has to be shown, and then chosen.

The acronym is intentional. The derogatory is **Corpses** — dead to the cause, walking proof that someone stopped resisting. Earth Front's collaborator accusation and the implication of surrender compressed into one word. CORP personnel are aware of it. They have varying relationships with it.

---

## Recruitment

CORP draws primarily from former law enforcement. The instincts transfer: beat work is relationship-based, community-facing, presence-over-enforcement. You know your room, you know the people, you show up consistently, you build trust through contact rather than authority.

The liability transfers too. LEO instincts include the expectation of standing — of having authority that expects compliance. In Manchester-adjacent rooms, CORP personnel have Commission-adjacent standing that some residents recognize and some don't. In wild rooms, they have nothing except whatever credibility they can build in person. The officer who joined CORP and didn't fully shed the expectation of deference will find out quickly.

The better LEO candidates are the ones who understand that the job is the relationship, not the badge. The ones who built genuine community trust before the walls went up already know how to do this work. They're the pipeline the Commission actually wants.

## Partner Structure

CORP operates in partners. Beat work, wild room outreach, and long-distance missions all use the two-person unit as the basic operational element. This is practical — mutual accountability, safety in unfamiliar rooms, one person to talk and one person to read the room — and it shapes the work. The partner relationship compounds over time. You know each other's instincts. You know when to take point and when to step back.

Partners are assigned. Whether a given partnership works is a different question from whether it was assigned well.

## The Precinct Model

Each room under Commission influence is assigned a CORP team — beat officers and support personnel operating out of a local presence. The structure is intentionally precinct-adjacent: a team with institutional memory about their specific room, sustained relationships with residents, and consistent faces in the community.

**Support personnel** handle the non-beat work: pineal intake assessments, Gatelock enrollment assistance, resource coordination with Commission supply lines, administrative interface with the Wall Commission when room-specific boundary questions arise.

**Beat officers** work the ground: neighborhoods, corridor access points, the spaces where residents actually live and where the integration metric either builds or erodes.

Precinct character follows room character. Manchester-adjacent precincts are established, resourced, and embedded in the Commission's support network. The further out, the more improvised: a wild room precinct might be two people working out of a borrowed space with irregular supply runs and no direct Commission administrative backup. The work is the same. The conditions are not.

## Operational Tiers

### Beats — Manchester-Adjacent Rooms

Established territory. The room knows the precinct, the precinct knows the room. This is relationship maintenance, troubleshooting, and the ongoing work of building the integration metric through genuine contact rather than instrumental crossing. CORP partners on a beat are a known quantity — which is leverage and liability both. The room trusts you enough to tell you when something is wrong. The room also knows exactly who you work for.

### Wild Room Outreach

Rooms with no Commission presence, no existing relationship, and frequently some ambient hostility to anything that reads as Manchester. CORP enters without standing, without institutional backstop, without the room owing them anything. The work is earning the right to help before offering it.

Wild rooms have developed their own post-wall adaptations, their own explanations for what happened, their own governance or absence of it. CORP's job is not to replace what's there. It is to offer tools — pineal work, Gatelock navigation, tech-loss coping — and let the room decide what to do with them. The non-intervention ideology is not just Commission policy here; it is the only approach that works. A CORP team that arrives in a wild room acting like a Commission outpost gets nothing. A CORP team that shows up as people with useful skills and the patience to wait gets somewhere eventually.

Earth Front has more traction in wild rooms than the Commission does. They were there first, and their narrative — occupation, resistance, collaboration — is legible in ways the trial framework is not, especially where the trial narrative has never been introduced at all. CORP is walking into that. Being aware of it is not the same as having a clean counter.

### Long-Distance Missions

Deep reach into rooms so far from Manchester that the trial narrative is mythology, rumor, or simply absent. Walls exist. Gatelocks exist. Nobody has explained why.

Long-distance missions are either establishing a new precinct in a room that's ready for sustained presence, or they're reconnaissance before any commitment exists. The Commission does not place precincts where they can't be supported. Long-distance teams assess whether conditions warrant it.

What CORP teams find at range is the open variable. Some rooms have adapted to the HiRB and built something functional. Some have developed explanations for the walls that will complicate everything CORP tries to introduce. Some are in genuine crisis that CORP resources cannot address alone. The long-distance mission debrief shapes what the Commission decides to prioritize next. The teams that go out are collecting information as much as delivering help.

## The Work

### Pineal Interface

The most intimate intervention CORP does. Human pineal glands activated simultaneously with the QWAN saturation — biological hardware coming online in eight billion people who had no idea they had it. Some people have been using it instinctively without knowing what it is. Some have suppressed it entirely. Some are desperate to understand it. Some won't engage with it on principle, for reasons that range from religious objection to the correct instinct that something was done to them without consent.

CORP's job is not to push pineal adoption. It is to give people accurate information and functional guidance for those who want it. The Gatelock toll infrastructure runs through the pineal interface. Understanding what it is and how it works reduces friction at every crossing and has downstream effects on the integration metric. The Commission is aware that "your biology is now toll hardware" is not a reassuring frame. CORP does not use that frame.

### Gatelock Navigation

Practical economic knowledge. How to cross effectively, when a crossing is worth the toll cost, how Cayminus enrollment works and what the terms actually mean before anyone signs anything, how to read the integration metric trajectory on a given segment. Some of this is information the Commission wants circulated. Some of it — specifically the parts about how metric-gaming reads to the measurement system — is knowledge CORP personnel have developed and share with judgment about context.

Earth Front reads any coaching on Gatelock navigation as teaching humans to perform integration for alien KPIs. That framing is not entirely wrong. Whether genuine integration and coached integration produce the same outcomes over time is a question the Commission is watching the metric to answer.

### Tech Regression Adaptation

Grief work as much as skills work. The person whose business ran on combustion equipment. The farmer whose machinery stopped working. The town whose hospital infrastructure failed. CORP does not arrive to tell these people to accept their losses. It arrives with what works now — draft logistics, non-combustion alternatives, supply chain navigation in a wood-economy landscape — and the patience to stay present while people figure out what that means for their specific situation.

The corridor culture has developed significant practical knowledge about HiRB-adapted living. CORP personnel spend time on the corridor for this reason. The RV wagon crews, the wood traders, the people who've been living under these conditions since the Summer of Chaos — they solved problems the Commission didn't design solutions for. CORP is as much a conduit for that knowledge as it is a Commission delivery mechanism.

## Relationship to Human Governance

The Commission does not replace human governance. Where functioning government exists — Manchester city government, town selectmen, county-level administration that survived the chaos — CORP defers to it and works alongside it. The Commission's authority in Manchester ends at the city government's jurisdiction. CORP personnel in Manchester are Commission staff operating inside a city whose government the Commission genuinely respects.

Outlying rooms present the complication. "If the town government can be located" is a real operational question. Some rooms have functioning governance that predates the walls and has adapted. Some have informal authority structures that emerged from the Summer of Chaos — real standing, no legal basis. Some have power vacuums, competing factions, nothing at all.

CORP reads that landscape and calibrates. Functional government: offer resources, build a working relationship, stay in your lane. Informal authority: identify who actually has standing, negotiate presence, do not accidentally delegitimize what's already working by implying the Commission has opinions about its validity. Vacuum: the hardest case. CORP is trained, resourced, and competent, and a room with no governance will sometimes try to hand the keys over. The ideology is clear — the Commission does not become the authority. Putting that into practice when a room is in crisis and asking for someone to step up is a genuine skill that not every CORP team has.

## Commission / City Government Friction

The Commission and Manchester city government have overlapping jurisdictions, competing institutional interests, and genuine mutual respect that doesn't eliminate the friction. Elected officials answer to constituents who distrust the Commission. Any appearance of deferring to Commission judgment is politically costly regardless of whether deference was warranted. The Commission makes decisions affecting Manchester that it does not always run through city channels first — not because it doesn't respect the city, but because the situation requires it.

CORP is directly in that friction. Commission personnel operating inside city limits, building relationships with residents, doing work that looks like policing to anyone watching from a distance. The Manchester Police Department has formal relationships with no one and informal working relationships with individual CORP partners that the department has not officially blessed.

The friction is mostly procedural. It becomes story when Earth Front gets a moment and makes it legible as occupation. One incident — the Commission appearing to override city authority, even if that's not what happened — and the city government is defending itself against collaborator accusations while the Commission is issuing careful statements that don't help. CORP beats go colder overnight. The informal MPD cooperation freezes while everyone waits. The integration metric on affected segments responds.
