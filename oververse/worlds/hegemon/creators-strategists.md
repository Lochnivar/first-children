## Overview

The Strategists are 4-6 First Children siblings who collaborated to create Hegemon jointly. What began as an experiment in "controlled imbalance" quickly devolved into ongoing competition as ownership questions emerged.

## Collective Identity

**Mortal Terminology:** "The Strategists"  
**Creation Method:** Joint authorship - pooled Quantumancy to shape world

## Collaboration Gone Competitive

### Initial Concept

**Purpose:** Experiment in "controlled imbalance" among peers
- Wanted to explore collaborative creation
- Aimed to test strategies of influence and control
- Concept was to pool Quantumancy to shape a strategic world

### The Devolution

**Ownership Questions:**
- Each First Child claims different levels of primacy/ownership
- Question "whose world is it?" became central
- Competition replaced collaboration
- Competition conducted through mortal proxies and subtle influence

**Framework Alignment:**
- Per framework: "Worlds may be shaped by one sibling alone (most common), by several in collaboration (possible but not necessarily common—raises questions of ownership/control, 'whose world is it?'—potential fuel for conflicts)"
- Hegemon demonstrates this exact dynamic
