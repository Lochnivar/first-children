# Klakkjah

**Type:** Lake | **Population:** 13.53 | **Culture:** Valthir

Klakkjah sits on the shore of the largest freshwater lake in Lurland, a body of water so deep that local fishermen claim it has no bottom—only darkness that goes down forever. The city's name derives from the sound ice makes when it cracks during spring thaw: a sharp, echoing report that can be heard for kilometers.

The city is built on wooden pilings driven into the lakebed, creating a settlement that seems to float on water during summer months. Come winter, the lake freezes solid, and Klakkjah transforms—merchants drive sleighs directly across ice thick enough to support loaded wagons, creating temporary trade routes that exist only three months of the year.

Klakkjah's economy revolves around **ice fishing** and **freshwater fishing** during warmer months. The lake produces enormous pike, trout, and char, preserved through smoking and salting in massive stone warehouses that dominate the waterfront. But Klakkjah's real wealth comes from something stranger: the lake's depth creates unique hydrological conditions that preserve wood submerged in its waters. Logs sunk centuries ago can be retrieved perfectly preserved, their wood harder and more durable than freshly cut timber. Klakkjah exports this "water-cured wood" across Lurland and beyond, commanding premium prices from shipwrights and master builders.

**Cultural Significance:** Klakkjah maintains the oldest continuous genealogical records in Lurland. Every family's lineage is carved into wooden pillars driven into the lakebed—a practice that has continued unbroken for over four hundred years. To have "no pillar in Klakkjah" is to be kinless, placeless, without standing.

**Superstitions:** The locals believe the lake is haunted by the drowned—not hostile spirits, but lonely ones. It's considered good luck to speak to the lake when crossing ice, acknowledging those who rest beneath. Silence while traveling on winter ice is deeply unlucky.
