## Narrative Description

**"The Golden Huntsmen"**

The Aurelian occupy the harsh, beautiful lands where mountains meet Mediterranean scrub—a landscape too rugged for intensive agriculture, too dry for the forests their Latin cousins prefer, perfect for the pastoral-hunting culture they've developed. They are sheepherders and boar-hunters, falconers and mountain climbers, a people who measure wealth in flocks and prestige in trophies rather than gold and land.

Aurelian society revolves around the **estancia**—extended family compounds that control mountain valleys and grazing ranges. An estancia might include a fortified manor house, several shepherd's huts, hunting lodges in the high passes, and sacred groves where family ancestors are honored. Estancias are largely self-sufficient, producing their own food, crafting their own tools, and resolving their own disputes according to family law. Central authority is weak; the Aurelian king is more "first among equals" than sovereign, his power limited to coordinating defense and arbitrating inter-estancia conflicts.

This decentralization produces a culture of fierce independence and personal honor. An Aurelian noble might swear loyalty to the king, but that oath is personal, not institutional—it binds him and his descendants, not his lands or his people. Breaking such an oath means familial dishonor, but so does offering blind obedience; an Aurelian noble is expected to counsel his king honestly and withdraw support if that counsel is ignored. The king who surrounds himself with yes-men is a king who will soon find himself alone.

**Hunting Tradition**: For the Aurelian, hunting is warfare, sport, religious ritual, and coming-of-age ceremony compressed into one practice. Young Aurelians are expected to kill a wolf or boar before being considered adult. Nobles organize elaborate hunts that are really diplomatic negotiations—nothing bonds Aurelian lords like pursuing dangerous game together. The greatest hunters become **maestros de montería**, masters of the hunt whose advice on tactics and strategy extends beyond tracking animals to tracking armies.

This hunting culture shapes Aurelian warfare. They don't form dense infantry formations; they skirmish, ambush, pursue, and vanish into terrain they know intimately. An Aurelian army in the mountains is nearly invincible; an Aurelian army on the plains is a target. They know this, so they fight on their terms or not at all.

**Military Character**: Aurelian warriors carry distinctive spanish shields—medium-sized, oval, highly decorated with family symbols. They fight with javelins and short spears, relying on mobility rather than formation. Their real military power is **light cavalry**—mounted skirmishers who can cover enormous distances, strike supply lines, and disappear before heavy forces respond. An invader might win battles against the Aurelian, but sustaining an army in Aurelian territory is nearly impossible.

**Economic Life**: The Aurelian export wool, leather, hunting dogs, falcons, and mercenaries. They import grain, wine, and manufactured goods. This trade relationship makes them economically dependent on their Latin neighbors while remaining militarily independent—a tension that defines much Aurelian foreign policy. Aurelian nobles often marry into Veridian or Solemnium families, creating economic partnerships while maintaining political autonomy.

**Cultural Values**: The Aurelian celebrate **dignidad**—a concept encompassing personal honor, family pride, and aesthetic excellence. A dignified Aurelian maintains composure under pressure, fulfills obligations regardless of cost, and treats even enemies with a certain courtesy. Insults to dignidad must be answered; how they're answered (formal duel, political maneuvering, economic retaliation) depends on context, but ignoring an insult is unthinkable.

This creates a culture simultaneously warm and dangerous. Aurelian hospitality is legendary—a guest in an estancia is protected absolutely, even if he's a blood enemy. But violate that hospitality, insult your host, or threaten his family, and that same host becomes an implacable enemy who will pursue vengeance for generations.

**Relationship to the Strategists**: The Aurelian's decentralized structure and emphasis on personal relationships make them difficult to manipulate as a unified culture but easy to influence individually. Strategists often work through specific estancias or noble families, creating factional conflicts that ripple across Aurelian society. Some Aurelian mystics claim to perceive Strategist influence through patterns in animal behavior—whether this represents genuine insight or confirmation bias remains debated.
