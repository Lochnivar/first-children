# Cultural Preferences Analysis - Quick Summary

**Test:** 12 cultures × 10 families × 10 generations (4,671 characters)  
**Status:** ✅ **SUCCESSFUL** - Cultural preferences working effectively

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Trait Specialization ✅ WORKING

**Cultures successfully maintain preferred traits:**
- **Sturmgaard**: +58% Hair Color, +45% Eye Color (academic preferences)
- **Meridian**: +51% Bloodline Power (courtly lineage)
- **Eldermark**: +35% Constitution, +28% Magic (expansionist)
- **Solemnium**: +29% Magic, +18% Creativity (artistic)
- **Veridian**: +25% Wisdom, +24% Build (imperial)

**Average specialization: 10-60% above baseline** ✅

### 2. Genetic Diversity ✅ PRESERVED

- **Between-culture similarity: 14.8%** (excellent - cultures are distinct)
- **Within-culture similarity: 26-41%** (good internal diversity)
- **Difference: 11-26 percentage points** = Strong cultural differentiation

### 3. Inbreeding Levels ✅ REALISTIC

- **Average: 51.2%** parent similarity
- **Range: 45-57%** across cultures
- **Assessment:** Realistic for small populations with cultural selection
- Cultures with stronger preferences show higher inbreeding (expected)

### 4. Cultural Differentiation ✅ EXCELLENT

Each culture maintains distinct trait profiles:
- Different preferred traits emphasized
- Visible specialization in key traits
- No global homogenization
