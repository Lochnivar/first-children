# The Collapse

The Collapse is the central fact of existence in the Shattered Descent. It is metronomic, predictable, and universal. Every 3,750 Pulses — one Floor — the topmost occupied floor of every Stack simultaneously enters structural failure. No Stack is exempt. No floor is spared. The schedule does not negotiate.

Because the Collapse is synchronized across all Stacks, it functions as a **shared deadline** for the entire world. Every Stack is simultaneously racing against the same clock. This creates moments of forced cooperation even between rival Stacks — when the Collapse hits and a Stack hasn't cleared enough floors to absorb its population, its neighbors face a choice with moral and political dimensions.

The Collapse is not experienced as catastrophe by most people. By the time the Crumble begins, the people who would have lived on that floor have already moved down. The Collapse takes rubble, not lives — mostly. The exceptions are the ones the Crumble workers deal with, and the ones the historians record, and the ones the theologians argue about.

## The Collapse as Theology

Different theological traditions have different relationships with the Collapse.

**The Reclaimists** see the Collapse as the dungeon exhaling — the natural completion of a gift that was always temporary. The dungeon provided the floor. The generation used it. The dungeon takes it back. This is not tragedy. This is the cycle, and the cycle is sacred. Reclaimists do not fight the Collapse. They prepare for it, complete it gracefully, and descend with dignity.

**The Descenders** see the Collapse as proof that going deeper is always correct. The floor collapses. The bottom floor does not — because there is no bottom floor, only the direction that leads to it. Every Collapse is the dungeon pushing civilization downward toward ultimate release. Descenders take the migration logic of every generation and extend it to its theological conclusion: if going deeper is what every generation does, why stop?

**The Ascenders** see the Collapse as a mechanism of control. The Demiurge — who sealed the top of the world — uses the Collapse to prevent civilization from establishing permanent roots that might threaten the seal. The Collapse is not the dungeon breathing. It is the jailer rattling the cage. Ascenders are the most politically radical theological tradition in the Shattered Descent and the most dangerous to established order.
