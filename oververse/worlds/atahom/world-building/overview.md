*Editorial / project epigraph (not in-world voice).*

Beneath any memory of sky or sun lies an endless vertical realm. There is no surface. There is no bottom. There is only the descent — and the civilizations that have learned to fall gracefully.

The world is called **Atahom** — *the place of the primordial deep* — in First Speech formal notation written **`'THM`**. In common speech most inhabitants say **Tahom**, or more often just *the world*, because from inside it, that is what it is. Those who know the ancient Semitic root hear something older in the name: *tehom*, the primordial abyss, the waters beneath creation before order was imposed upon them. Whether the name was given deliberately or arrived at independently is unknown. The resonance is exact either way.

It is a world of dungeon floors stacked upon dungeon floors, extending downward without limit. Civilization lives within it, moves through it, and is periodically reclaimed by it. The people who inhabit this realm did not choose this existence. They were placed here — though by whom, and why, and whether that entity still watches, are questions the theologians have been arguing about since the first Pulse.

**Framing:** This is **not LitRPG** and not a dungeon-crawler premise — see [Narrative Framing](narrative-framing.md). **Dungeon** means environment and civilizational fact, not game systems. Naming and First Speech roots: [First Speech — Atahom](first-speech-lexicon.md).
