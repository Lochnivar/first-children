# The Distributor and the Pulse

Deep in the world — below any current Stack's frontier, below any floor that has been delved and recorded in living memory — there is a mechanism. The Guild calls it **the Distributor**. The Reclaimists call it the Heart. The Descenders call it the Promise. The Ascenders call it the Machine.

What everyone agrees on is what it does: at regular intervals, it sends a surge of water through the entire stone infrastructure of the world simultaneously. Every Stack. Every floor. Every depth. The surge arrives as a deep resonant rumble felt through the stone before the water reaches any cistern. Bone-deep. Chest-resonating. Unmistakable.

This is the **Pulse**.

The Pulse is the only truly universal event in the Shattered Descent. It does not respect Stack borders. It does not acknowledge political disputes. It arrives in the Wild floors and the Old Cities and the Crumble zones and the deep delver camps with identical indifference. You cannot be in the world and not feel it.

The Pulse is time. Everything in the Shattered Descent is counted from it.

# The Calendar

The calendar of the Shattered Descent is built on a pure base-5 structure, counting upward from the Pulse. Every unit is a multiple of five of the unit below it, except where body-based counting logic creates natural groupings.

## Units

| Unit | Pulses | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Pulse** | 1 | The fundamental unit. The heartbeat of the world. |
| **Hand** | 5 | Named for the fingers used to count it. |
| **Fist** | 25 | Five Hands. A closed fist of time. |
| **Span** | 250 | Ten Fists. Two hands of Fists. |
| **Floor** | 3,750 | Fifteen Spans. One collapse cycle. |

The **Floor** is the largest formal calendar unit and does double duty as both a time measurement and a spatial address. The current Floor number tells you simultaneously how deep civilization has reached and how many collapse cycles have occurred. The world is exactly as old as it is deep.

## Guild Notation

Guild standard notation records time as a precise five-element address:

**Floor.Span.Fist.Hand.Pulse**

Example: **423.14.3.2.1**

*Floor 423, Span 14, Fist 3, Hand 2, Pulse 1*

Reading this notation tells an experienced Guild archivist not just when something happened but what the civilizational context was. Span 14 of 15 means the collapse was approaching. Floor 423 tells you the absolute depth of civilization at that moment. The notation is simultaneously a timestamp and a historical summary.

## Common Speech

Ordinary people do not use Guild notation for daily life. They use relative and contextual language:

- *"Three Pulses ago"* — recently
- *"Two Fists into this Span"* — a while back this season
- *"Early in the Span"* — months ago by loose reckoning
- *"My parents' Floor"* — the previous collapse cycle, within living memory
- *"My grandparents' Floor"* — two cycles ago, beyond living memory, Guild records only
- *"Ancient"* — three or more cycles ago

## The Generational Arc

The fifteen Spans of a Floor map onto human life stages with enough consistency to be culturally meaningful:

- **Spans 1–3** — Childhood. You grow up in the New Town your parents built.
- **Spans 4–7** — Young adulthood. You build. You invest. You start families. This is your New Town too now.
- **Spans 8–11** — Prime adult years. What you built has become City. You are the establishment now.
- **Spans 12–14** — Elder years. The Quieting begins. You have lived long enough to watch the cycle complete.
- **Span 15** — The Crumble. If you are still here you chose to be.

A person who lives three full collapse cycles has seen the world reorganize itself completely three times. They remember connections that no longer exist, political configurations that are now rubble, stacks that have died and been reclaimed. Their memories are primary sources for history that exists nowhere else.

The Guild actively seeks out three-cycle elders. Their knowledge is irreplaceable.

## The Lasters

People born in Spans 13–15 of a collapse cycle are called **Lasters**. They are carried through the Collapse as infants or small children. Their entire conscious life belongs to the new cycle. They have no personal memory of the previous Floor.

Lasters don't just lack attachment to the previous Floor philosophically. They lack it *experientially*. They were carried down. They grew up in the new New Town. The previous Floor is something their parents talk about, not something they remember.

Lasters are disproportionately represented among deep delvers. If nothing you can build will ever feel like home anyway, you might as well go somewhere interesting.
