# Worldbuilding Notes for Contributors

## The Dungeon Maps

Each map contributed to Atahom represents a specific floor of a specific Stack at a specific point in its lifecycle. The map's lifecycle stage determines its narrative character:

- **Wild/Frontier maps** — dangerous, unmapped, high-stakes delving content. The Guild is present but thin. Settlement hasn't happened yet.
- **New Town maps** — the cistern clock is being built. Everything is being established for the first time. Energy, ambition, bad food supply chains.
- **City maps** — full civilization. Political intrigue, trade, faction conflict, institutional drama.
- **Old City maps** — deep establishment politics, historical mystery, entrenched power that stopped moving and has reasons for it.
- **Quieting maps** — the population is thinning. What gets left behind. The people who chose to stay.
- **Crumble maps** — survival, salvage, structural failure. The Crumble workers and what they know.

## The Address System

Every piece of content in Atahom has a complete address in space and time:

**Stack Name (Guild Number) — Floor.Span.Fist.Hand.Pulse**

Example: *The Ironward Stack (Guild #7) — 423.14.3.2.1*

Reading this address tells a reader: the Stack's identity, the absolute floor depth and collapse cycle number (same value), and the precise position within that cycle down to the individual Pulse. Span 14 of 15 tells you the collapse was approaching. Floor 423 tells you how deep civilization had reached at that moment. The address is simultaneously a spatial coordinate, a timestamp, and a historical summary.

## Connection Corridors

When contributing content that involves connection corridors, specify:

- Which Stacks are connected and at which floor level
- The political relationship between those Stacks at that floor
- Whether the corridor is active Guild neutral ground or contested
- Any notable features of the neutral space between the boundary markers

Remember: connection corridors collapse with their floor. A corridor active on Floor 423 does not exist on Floor 424. All lateral relationships reset with the Collapse.

## Canonical Facts

The following are fixed and should not be contradicted in contributed content:

- The Collapse is synchronized across all Stacks and metronomic — 3,750 Pulses per Floor
- The Floor number and the Collapse cycle number are the same value
- Connection corridors collapse with their floor — no permanent lateral connections
- The Distributor exists below any currently delved floor and has never been reached
- The top of the world is sealed — the cap is a physical reality
- There is no confirmed Bottom Floor
- The Guild is neutral and cross-Stack by institutional necessity
- The timekeeping cistern is the threshold between camping and civilization
- Atahom is not a game — there are no levels, stats, or designed encounters

## What Remains Open

Everything not listed above is contributed, collaborative, and subject to the community's collective development. Stack names, cultures, theologies, political configurations, the current Floor number, the specific nature of the Distributor, what is above the sealed cap, what happened to dead Stacks — all of this is the community's to build.

The mechanics are fixed. The world is yours.
