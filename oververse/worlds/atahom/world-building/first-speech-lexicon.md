# First Speech — Atahom

In-setting formal names for places and concepts on Atahom use **First Speech** conventions (see also [First Speech conventions (trials & quest interfaces)](../../../mechanics/first-speech-conventions.md) for Hypatia-wide deployment tokens).

## The world name

The world's name in First Speech formal notation is **`'THM`** — spoken **Atahom** (*the place of the primordial deep*). Common speech uses **Tahom**. Both **Atahom** and **Tahom** are correct in appropriate register.

The root **THM** carries the ancient Semitic sense of *the primordial deep* — the abyss beneath creation, the unordered waters before structure was imposed upon them (*tehom*). Readers with Hebrew or ancient Near Eastern background may hear that echo; readers without will still feel the name as old and weighted.

Whether the name was given deliberately or arrived at independently is unknown in-world. The resonance is exact either way.

## Locative operator

First Speech notation uses the **`'`** prefix as a **locative operator** — *place of* or *realm of*. **`'THM`** therefore means specifically *the place of the primordial deep*. The spoken form adds the prefix vowel **A-** to the glottal onset (**Atahom**).

Contributors naming new Stacks, locations, or institutions in First Speech should follow this pattern: identify the consonantal root for the core meaning, apply **`'`** for place names, and derive the spoken form with the **A-** prefix where this convention applies.

## Mythic and aspirational names

**A'Salem** — *place of peace* (root **SLM**). In folk telling, the floor beyond the monsters: the **last floor**, where **the sun** (whatever that is) might live. Not confirmed by Guild survey; overlaps Descender hope and bedtime story. See theology on Bottom Floor vs [canonical facts](contributor-notes.md#canonical-facts).
