# Inner-Pulse Time: The Cistern Clock

Between Pulses, there is no sun, no astronomical reference, no external timekeeper. The dungeon does not care what time it is within a Pulse. The inhabitants have to solve this themselves.

They solved it with water.

## The Timekeeping Cistern

Every settled floor has one cistern designated as the **official timekeeper**. It is selected and certified by the Guild during the floor certification process — chosen specifically for its consistent and readable fill and drain characteristics, not for its utility as a water supply.

The timekeeping cistern is fed by its own dedicated inlet from the Distributor system and drains through a **Guild-certified fixed aperture** — a precisely sized outlet that determines the drain rate independent of usage. No one draws from the timekeeping cistern. No one modifies the aperture. The cistern is a clock, not a water source. The water that drains from it feeds the general supply downstream, but the cistern itself is untouchable.

The face of the cistern is marked with calibration lines certified by the Guild at installation. The marks are not numbers. They are:

- **Noon** — the high mark. Reached immediately after the Pulse when the cistern fills to maximum.
- **Midnight** — the median mark. The cistern is halfway drained. Roughly the sleep period.
- **The Wait** — the low mark. The cistern is nearly empty. The next Pulse is approaching. The world holds its breath.

These three marks are universal across all Stacks. The terminology may vary — different Stacks have different words — but every settled floor in every Stack has these three reference points on its official cistern.

## Noon

Noon is not the middle of the day. Noon is the **beginning**. The Pulse arrives, the rumble moves through the stone, the water rushes in, the cistern fills to the high mark. That moment is Noon.

Time in the Shattered Descent runs from Noon outward — through the active period, through Midnight, through the quiet period and the Wait, and back to Noon again with the next Pulse. The cycle is continuous. You are always counting toward the next Pulse, never away from the last one.

## The Wait

The period at the low mark — the cistern nearly empty, the next Pulse not yet arrived — has a specific cultural weight across all Stacks regardless of theology.

The Wait is the dungeon-world's most liminal time. The Distributor has finished its work. The cistern is silent. The next heartbeat has not come yet.

Reclaimists observe a moment of silence during the Wait — not organized, not coordinated, not scheduled by any authority. Simply understood. You are quiet during the Wait. You listen for the Pulse. Somewhere on every other floor in every other Stack, another Reclaimist is doing the same thing at their own Wait. You cannot see them. You cannot reach them. But you are doing the same thing at the same moment in the same silence.

The dungeon connects them through the quiet before the sound.

## Variation Between Stacks

The Pulse arrives at every cistern simultaneously — the rumble is universal. But the time between the Pulse and the cistern reaching Noon varies by Stack depending on the length and complexity of the water infrastructure between the Distributor inlet and the cistern. Stack 7's cistern may fill within moments of the rumble. Stack 23's may take several minutes.

Every resident of Stack 23 simply knows. You hear the rumble, you wait, then it's Noon. This is not confusing. It is local knowledge, universally understood by locals, immediately apparent to visitors.

A traveler arriving at a new Stack notices the fill delay is different. They adjust. They move on. It is not unlike the concept of noon in different surface-world time zones — noon is when the sun peaks, and that is a local event, not a global one. The Pulse is global. Noon is local.

The Guild's cistern calibration tables record the fill delay and drainage coefficient for every certified cistern in every Stack. Guild runners who transit between Stacks carry conversion references. Cross-Stack contracts reference Pulse notation — unambiguous, universal. Inner-Pulse time is for local coordination only.

## Tampering

Tampering with the timekeeping cistern is one of the most serious crimes in any Stack's legal tradition. Not because of the physical damage — the cistern itself is repairable — but because of what it attacks.

You are not vandalizing infrastructure. You are attacking time itself. Contracts become unenforceable. Legal deadlines become ambiguous. The floor's internal coordination degrades until the Guild can recertify.

Every Stack has historical examples of someone trying it. None of them ended well.

## Deep Delver Time

Deep delvers operating on Wild or Frontier floors below the settled zone have no timekeeping cistern. They have the Pulse — the rumble reaches them regardless of depth, traveling through stone without regard for civilization's presence or absence — and nothing else.

Inner-Pulse time is irrelevant in the deep. You sleep when you cannot stay awake. You eat when you are hungry. You count Pulses since you left because that is the only clock that matters down here.

When a deep delver returns to settled floors after a long run they experience a specific disorientation — the cistern clock feels unnecessarily precise. Why does it matter if it is Midnight? The Pulse came. We are alive. What else do you need to know?

Settlers find this attitude alarming. Deep delvers find settler time anxiety baffling. It is one of the clearest cultural markers distinguishing the two ways of life.
