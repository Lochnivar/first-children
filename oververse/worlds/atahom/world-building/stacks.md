# The Stacks

The fundamental political and cultural unit of the Shattered Descent is the **Stack** — a vertical column of floors occupied by a single civilization, culture, or political entity. A Stack is not a nation in the surface-world sense. It has no fixed territory, no permanent borders, no ancestral land. It has only its people, its history, and the floors it currently occupies.

Stacks are identified by name — each Stack develops its own identity over time — and by the Guild's registry number for record-keeping purposes. In common usage, people name their Stack. In Guild notation, they number it.

## What Defines a Stack

A Stack is defined by its **collapse history** — the shared experience of moving downward together through successive collapse cycles. Two groups of people who have collapsed together, moved together, and built together are the same Stack regardless of cultural drift. Two groups who diverged at some point in the past are different Stacks even if they speak the same language and share the same theology.

The architecture of a Stack has a **family resemblance** — not identical room to room, but consistent enough that any experienced delver can tell when they are in their own Stack's territory versus someone else's. The stone feels different. The construction style carries echoes of previous generations. You know your own Stack's floors the way you know your own family's faces.

## Stack Depth and the Depth Race

Stacks are not all at the same depth. Some are more aggressive in their delving, pushing new floors faster than their neighbors. Some are conservative, consolidating and building on current floors before pushing deeper.

This creates a **depth differential** — at any given moment, the deepest Stack may be dozens of floors ahead of the shallowest. Since the collapse strikes all Stacks simultaneously at the same absolute floor level, a Stack that falls behind in the depth race faces a civilizational crisis. When the collapse hits, they may not have enough cleared floors below to absorb their population.

The depth race is therefore not merely competitive. It is **existential**.

A Stack that gets significantly ahead of its neighbors gains enormous leverage. Its New Town floors are safely below the collapse line while slower Stacks are scrambling. It can offer refuge — at a price — to Stacks caught short by the collapse. It can claim lateral connection corridors on new floors before slower Stacks arrive to contest them.

The most aggressive Stacks are not necessarily the most admired. Speed costs lives. Floors cleared too fast are floors cleared badly — threats missed, infrastructure rushed, certification questionable. But a Stack that moves too slowly courts extinction.

Every generation navigates this tension.
