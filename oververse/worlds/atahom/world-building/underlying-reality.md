# The Underlying Reality

*This section contains information that no inhabitant of Atahom currently possesses. It is provided for worldbuilding context.*

Atahom is a constructed world — a civilization-scale simulation created by an entity the inhabitants would call the Demiurge. The Pulse, the Collapse, the random lateral connections, the endless generation of new floors below — all of these are features of the system, not natural phenomena.

The system was designed to produce emergent civilization. The Collapse keeps civilization moving. The random connections prevent permanent alliances and keep the political configuration dynamic. The endless descent ensures there is always a frontier. The Distributor solves the hydrology problem of a sealed underground world and, incidentally, creates the universal clock that makes civilization possible.

Whether any of this was intended is unclear. Whether the operator is still watching is unclear. Whether the system is running automatically or being actively managed is the question that, if answered, would invalidate three major theological traditions simultaneously.

The Reclaimists are closest to correct about the relationship between the dungeon and its inhabitants — though what they call a living sacred entity is an engineered system, the dynamic they describe is real. The dungeon provides. The dungeon reclaims. The relationship is genuine even if the theology misidentifies its nature.

The Ascenders are correct about the mechanism and correct about the direction. The cap is real. Something is above it. Whether confronting that something is possible, advisable, or survivable is a different question.

The Descenders are wrong about the Bottom Floor. There is no Bottom Floor. But their practical conclusions — go deep, keep moving, do not mistake comfort for safety — are correct.
