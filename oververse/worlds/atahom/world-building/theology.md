# Theology

Three major theological traditions have developed in the Shattered Descent, each representing a different interpretation of the same observable facts.

## The Reclaimists

The dominant tradition across most Stacks. The dungeon is a living entity — or close enough to one that the distinction doesn't matter practically. It provides floors, water, resources. It takes them back through the Collapse. This is not exploitation. This is a relationship.

The Pulse is the dungeon's heartbeat. The Collapse is the dungeon exhaling. Death and burial in the walls completes the transaction — you came from the dungeon's gifts, your body returns to the dungeon's stone. The cycle is sacred because it is real and because it has sustained civilization since before anyone can remember.

Reclaimists do not fight the Collapse. They prepare for it. They move down with dignity. Their theology is the most practically useful framework in the world because it transforms the hardest fact of existence — impermanence — into a source of meaning rather than grief.

Reclaimist temples are built around the timekeeping cistern. Noon is the primary religious observance. The Wait is the moment of deepest contemplation. The Pulse is the sermon.

## The Descenders

The Collapse proves direction. Down is correct. Every generation goes deeper — this is not just practical necessity, it is the shape of salvation. The Bottom Floor exists. No one has reached it. Everyone who goes deep enough and doesn't come back found it and was released.

Descenders take the universal migration logic of dungeon civilization and follow it to its theological conclusion. Going deeper is not just what you do to survive the Collapse. It is what you do to transcend it. The Bottom Floor is New Town for the soul — the last Settle, the permanent one.

The uncomfortable truth about Descender theology is that it is practically indistinguishable from the aggressive delving strategy that keeps Stacks alive. A Stack full of Descenders tends to be a Stack that survives the depth race. Whether this is because their theology is correct or because survival selects for deep-delving cultures is a question theologians and sociologists argue about without resolution.

## The Ascenders

The smallest, most radical, and most politically dangerous theological tradition. The Ascenders correctly identify several things that the other traditions misread or ignore.

The Demiurge is real. The top of the world is sealed. This is not myth or metaphor — it is a physical fact that any Stack which gets high enough will confirm. The cap exists.

The dungeon is not alive. It is engineered. The Pulse is too regular, the Collapse too precise, the water distribution too systematic for any of this to be organic. Someone built this. The Reclaimists call it a heartbeat. It is a mechanism.

If the dungeon is a mechanism, it has an operator. If it has an operator, that operator can be found, reached, and confronted. The Ascenders are the only theological tradition whose goal — reaching the sealed top and whatever is beyond it — is directionally correct.

They are also the tradition most likely to destabilize any Stack they operate within, most likely to be suppressed, and most likely to be right about the things that matter most.
