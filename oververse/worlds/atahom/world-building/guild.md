# The Guild

The **Adventurers Guild** is the only institution that operates across all Stacks simultaneously. It has stations on every settled floor of every Stack. It certifies floors, maps connections, runs routes between Stacks, and maintains the only continuous cross-Stack historical archive in the world.

The Guild is not a government. It does not make laws, command armies, or collect taxes. Its authority rests entirely on its indispensability. Every Stack needs maps. Every Stack needs routes. Every Stack needs floor certification before it can settle. The Guild provides these things to everyone without taking sides — because the moment it takes sides it stops being useful to the other side, and the Guild's value is precisely its universal access.

Guild neutrality is not an ethical position. It is a survival strategy. It has held for as long as anyone has records because every Stack that has tried to capture or compromise the Guild has discovered that a partial Guild is worth less than no Guild, and a dead Guild is a catastrophe for everyone including the Stack that killed it.

## The Archive

The Guild's archive is the most politically sensitive collection of documents in the world. It contains:

- Complete floor maps for every certified floor of every Stack currently active
- Route tables for every current connection corridor
- Historical records going back further than any Stack's own institutional memory
- Year-Turn reports filed at the end of every Floor recording the complete state of the world
- Records of dead Stacks — civilizations that were reclaimed entirely and exist nowhere else
- Cistern calibration data for every certified timekeeping cistern

The archive is the only place where you can learn what your new neighbors actually are — how they behaved across multiple collapse cycles, what their pattern is, whether they have a history of aggressive border incursion, whether they have been reliable allies or unreliable ones.

A Stack receiving a new connection corridor neighbor would pay an enormous amount for the Guild's assessment of whoever just appeared on the other side of that wall. The Guild sells that assessment. That transaction is one of their primary revenue sources and one of their most carefully managed products — accurate enough to be worth buying, carefully framed to avoid making enemies.
