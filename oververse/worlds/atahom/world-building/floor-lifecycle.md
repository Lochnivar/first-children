# The Floor Lifecycle

Every floor in every Stack passes through the same inevitable progression. The stages are not rigidly timed — some floors move quickly through the cycle, others linger — but the sequence is universal.

## 1. Wild

The floor has been breached but not cleared. Dungeon creatures inhabit it. The passages are unmapped, the rooms uncertified, the dangers unknown. Only Guild delvers operate here, and only with full awareness of the risk.

A Wild floor is not part of any Stack's territory. It belongs to whoever clears it first.

## 2. Frontier

Active clearing is underway. Guild delvers have established forward positions. Maps exist but are incomplete. The first brave settlers — or foolish ones, depending on who you ask — may be establishing temporary camps. The floor is dangerous but navigating it.

Frontier floors are where the depth race is won or lost. An aggressive Stack pushes its Frontier hard. A conservative Stack clears thoroughly before advancing.

## 3. New Town

The cistern clock has been built. The Guild has issued certification. The floor is officially a settlement.

New Town is the most energetic stage of a floor's life. Everyone is young, or feels young. Everything is being built for the first time. The social hierarchy hasn't calcified yet. Ambition is everywhere. The food is bad because the supply chains aren't established yet. Nobody cares.

New Town is where the young go. It is where families are started, reputations are made, and fortunes are founded. The people who build New Town will live to see it become Old City. That transformation — watching what you built become history — is one of the defining experiences of life in the Shattered Descent.

## 4. City

Full civilization. Markets, temples, Guild stations, political institutions, legal frameworks. The floor has everything a settled community needs and has had time to develop it properly.

City floors are the engine of the Stack. They produce the resources, the trade, the cultural output, and the political decisions that define what the Stack is. The collapse is distant enough not to dominate daily life. The Wild is far enough below to be someone else's problem.

This is where most people live most of their lives.

## 5. Old City

The floor has been City long enough to develop weight. The families who built New Town are elders now. The institutions have traditions. The architecture is layered — you can see the different eras of construction in the walls if you know what to look for.

Old City floors are politically complex. Deep establishment families have accumulated resources and influence across multiple collapse cycles. They have reasons to resist change that newer settlements don't. They are simultaneously the most culturally sophisticated floors in a Stack and the most politically entrenched.

The deep establishment families who have been on the same floors long enough — who have, effectively, stopped descending — are the most powerful and most suspect members of any Stack's society. They have what everyone else lost in the last collapse: continuity. The question of what they traded for it is one every Stack's political culture eventually has to answer.

## 6. The Quieting

The collapse is approaching. The floor is still functioning but the population is thinning. Families who planned ahead have already moved down. The markets are quieter. The Guild is running exit assessments. The streets feel wider than they used to.

The Quieting is not abandonment. It is **scheduled migration**. Everyone always knew this was coming. The emotional register is not grief — this culture does not grieve impermanence, it was born into it — but something more like the feeling of finishing a good book. You knew it would end. It ending doesn't make it less good.

Elders who choose to remain through the Quieting are not tragic figures. They made their choice. They built what they built. They are ready to be reclaimed.

## 7. The Crumble

Active structural failure. The floor is no longer safe for civilian occupation. Salvagers operate here — the last extraction of useful materials before everything goes. Guild runners make final archive transfers. The timekeeping cistern drains for the last time.

The Crumble is not instantaneous. It is a process that unfolds over multiple Pulses. Some sections fail before others. Moving through a Crumble floor requires experience and a specific kind of fatalism that not everyone possesses.

There are people who specialize in Crumble work. They are well compensated and have a particular reputation — pragmatic, unsentimental, very good at reading structural instability. They are also, statistically, not old.

## 8. Reclaimed

Gone. The floor exists only in the Guild's absolute archive. Future generations may delve through the rubble on their way to new Wild floors, finding traces — a carved lintel, a section of cistern pipe, a fragment of wall with markings no one can read anymore. Evidence that someone was here. That they built something. That it mattered to them.

The dungeon takes it all back eventually. The Reclaimists say this is as it should be.
