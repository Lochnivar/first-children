# Atahom — AI Refresher

**Purpose:** Quick-reference for AI context when working on Atahom stories, world-building, or integration.  
**First Speech token:** `'THM` · Common: **Tahom** or *the world* · Formal: **Atahom** (*place of the primordial deep*)  
**Canonical source:** [world-building/](world-building/)

---

## One-Line Summary

**Vertical dungeon world with no surface and no bottom** — civilization stacked on civilization, descending forever, periodically reclaimed. Not LitRPG. Not dungeon-crawler. Civilizational fiction about radical impermanence.

---

## What Atahom Is Not

- **Not LitRPG.** No experience points, no levels, no loot tables. The dungeon is geology, not game design.
- **Not a dungeon crawler.** Creatures are fauna. Hazards are ancient infrastructure. Floors are not encounters.
- **Not post-apocalyptic.** The Collapse is not catastrophe. It is cycle. Civilization has integrated it completely. Grief framing would feel imported and false.

---

## The Physical World

An endless vertical realm of stone floors extending downward without limit. No surface. No sky. No bottom floor — or if there is one, nobody has reached it. Civilization lives within the stone, builds in it, and is periodically reclaimed by it.

The dungeon is engineered, not natural — though most inhabitants don't know this. The Pulse, the Collapse, the water distribution, the lateral connections: all features of a constructed system. The operator may or may not still be watching.

---

## The Pulse and Calendar

**The Distributor** — deep below any current civilization — sends a water surge through all stone infrastructure at regular intervals. Every Stack. Every floor. Every depth. Felt as a deep resonant bone-level rumble before the water arrives.

This is the **Pulse**. It is universal, egalitarian, and inescapable. It is also time.

| Unit | Pulses | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Pulse** | 1 | The fundamental unit |
| **Hand** | 5 | Five fingers |
| **Fist** | 25 | Five Hands |
| **Span** | 250 | Ten Fists |
| **Floor** | 3,750 | One collapse cycle; also a spatial address |

**Guild notation:** Floor.Span.Fist.Hand.Pulse — e.g. *423.14.3.2.1*  
Time and depth are the same measurement. The world is exactly as old as it is deep.

---

## The Collapse

Every 3,750 Pulses (one Floor), the topmost occupied floor of **every Stack simultaneously** enters structural failure. Metronomic. Predictable. Universal. No exceptions.

By the time the Crumble begins, people have already moved down. The Collapse takes rubble, not lives — mostly. It functions as a shared deadline for the entire world, forcing periodic cooperation between rivals.

**The Collapse is not experienced as catastrophe.** The emotional register is scheduled migration. Finishing a good book. You knew it would end.

---

## Stacks

The fundamental political unit. A vertical column of floors occupied by a single civilization.

- **Identity** = shared collapse history, not territory or language
- **The depth race** = existential, not merely competitive. Fall behind and you may not have cleared floors to absorb your population when the Collapse hits
- **Speed costs lives.** Floors cleared too fast are floors cleared badly. Every generation navigates the tension between fast enough to survive and careful enough to survive

---

## Floor Lifecycle

| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Wild** | Breached but not cleared. Dungeon fauna present. Guild delvers only. |
| **Frontier** | Active clearing. Maps incomplete. First settlers. Dangerous. |
| **New Town** | Certified. Cistern clock built. Energetic. Social hierarchy not yet calcified. |
| **City** | Full civilization. Most people live most of their lives here. |
| **Old City** | Institutional weight. Deep establishment families. Politically complex. |
| **The Quieting** | Population thinning. Scheduled migration underway. |
| **The Crumble** | Active structural failure. Salvagers and final archive transfers. |
| **Reclaimed** | Gone. Guild archive only. Future delvers find fragments. |

**Lasters** — born in Spans 13–15, carried through the Collapse as infants. No personal memory of the previous Floor. Disproportionately represented among deep delvers.

## Lateral Connections

At each Floor level, the dungeon generates random connection corridors between adjacent Stacks. **You do not choose your neighbors.**

- Connection corridors are architecturally neutral — stone changes character at the threshold
- The space between boundary markers is neutral ground; Guild stations operate there
- **Connections collapse with their floor.** Every collapse cycle is effectively first contact with a new neighbor configuration
- Synchronized descent is the alliance strategy — matching descent rates to influence (not control) which Stacks end up adjacent

Route notation: **Stack-Floor → Stack-Floor → Stack-Floor**

---

## The Guild

The only institution operating across all Stacks simultaneously. Surveyors, cartographers, diplomats, route runners, floor certifiers.

**Neutrality is not ethics — it is survival.** A partial Guild is worth less than no Guild. Every Stack that has tried to capture or compromise the Guild has discovered this.

The Guild's archive is the most politically sensitive collection in the world: complete floor maps, route tables, Year-Turn reports, dead Stack records, cistern calibration data. **The historical assessment of a new neighbor is their primary commercial product.** Accurate enough to be worth buying, carefully framed to avoid making enemies.

## Theology

| Tradition | View of the Collapse | Direction | Political Character |
|-----------|---------------------|-----------|---------------------|
| **Reclaimists** | The dungeon exhaling — sacred cycle | Downward with grace | Dominant; conservative; practically useful |
| **Descenders** | Proof that down is correct — theological destination | Deep as salvation | Aggressive delvers; survives because their theology produces good strategy |
| **Ascenders** | The jailer rattling the cage — mechanism of control | Up, toward the sealed cap | Smallest; most radical; most dangerous; most likely to be right |

The Ascenders are correct: the cap is real, the dungeon is engineered, and something is above it. Whether confronting that something is possible, advisable, or survivable is unresolved.

---

## Tone and Register

- **Radical present-tense pragmatism.** You build well because craft has meaning, not because you expect permanence.
- Closest comparisons: Le Guin's anthropological weight, Miéville's genuinely alien architecture, Ken Liu's moral complexity without resolution, family saga generational scope
- Stories should feel like they **could only happen here**. If the story works in a generic fantasy setting, it isn't using the world yet.
