## Overview

Algernon is a Mystara-ish medieval fantasy world built entirely around a single central dungeon. Everything — economics, politics, religion, conflict — orbits it. The name was assigned casually by the FC running the experiment. One FC — Keyes — conducts pure research: can physical beings undergo a phase transition to stable waveforms under increasing energetic load?

**Core Theme:** Identity under pressure — does the thing that makes the experiment work survive the process that requires it?

## Framework Elements Demonstrated

### Solo World Creation
- **Creator:** Keyes — sole creator/operator of the experiment
- **Motivation:** Mild cosmic irritation that the Prime Author generates stable coherent existence effortlessly; Keyes cannot. Scientific curiosity born of boredom.
- **Purpose:** Hypothesis — saturate physical beings with energetic load via TheWay's Strain mechanic; see if physical structure undergoes phase transition (ice to steam) producing stable waveform on the other side
- **Status:** Pure research phase. Nobody has ever made it. Every participant has died before the threshold.

### Quantum Foundation
- **Phase Transition:** Physical → waveform via controlled energetic saturation
- **The Dungeon:** Delivery mechanism — each level loads more energetic density into the participant
- **TheWay Mechanics:** Strain, power progression — instrumentation, not decoration
- **LitRPG Framing:** Gamified interface for what is actually controlled phase transition saturation

### TheWay Interface (How Participants See the System)
- **Console-debugging, not game UI:** Stat screens, character gen, inventories, level-up prompts rendered through bio-engineered wetware (Pineal + Amygdala) in the participant's brain. Raw instrumentation: log lines, data dumps, internal IDs, technical readouts. Birth race, quantum profile, Strain values surfaced as debug output, not polished menus. Keyes speaks through the level-up voice. Splitting headache on first connection as wetware comes online. Storage and inventories are portals; wetware is middleware. Participant sees the experiment's backend, not a consumer interface.
- **Player-based mod overlay:** Some participants eventually learn to layer a more polished UI over the raw output. Charlie may discover this.

### Soul Transfer
- **Quota Fills:** Procurement system acquisitions from other Hypatia worlds (e.g., Earth)
- **Black Market Souls:** Trafficked for specific quantum profiles
- **Participant Ecosystem:** Serious contenders, bounty hunters, tourists, quota fills

### First Child Involvement
- **Keyes's Role:** Present as the level-up voice every participant hears — generic, procedural, unremarkable in early levels
- **Investment:** Watches thousands die with immortal patience; deviates from script when a participant (Charlie) registers as anomalous
- **The Question:** Does a genuinely lived human experience change the phase transition?

## World-Specific Expansions

**The Dungeon:**
- Central to world — economics, politics, religion orbit it
- Conventional sword-and-board dungeon crawl surface experience
- Underlying reality: controlled phase transition saturation
- Magic level unknown to everyone including Keyes

**Participant Ecosystem:**
- Serious contenders — pushing toward invisible finish line
- Black market souls — trafficked for quantum profiles
- Bounty hunters — capturing souls for warlords' champions
- Tourists — bucket-list lethal dungeon, occasional outliers
- Quota fills — minimum-spec acquisitions, disoriented, credit chit, WTF headache

**The Central Relationship (Flowers for Algernon):**
- Keyes roots for Charlie; scientifically invested
- Enhancement = identity (Charlie-ness), not intelligence
- The thing the experiment needs is the thing that gets burned away
- Open question: if phase transition completes — is the waveform still Charlie, or did Charlie have to die?
