# Academies — Overview

**Status:** Canon — Foundation  
**World:** Brenneth

---

## Overview

There is no unified magical authority in Brenneth. No single institution governs who may practice, how they are trained, or what they may do. What exists instead is five parallel institutional traditions — one for each school — that have developed independently over centuries and arrived at a functional, fractious, mutually recognizing arrangement.

This is the shape of institutional magic in Brenneth: not a White Tower but five houses with a common parlor they use when necessary and avoid when they can.

---

## Structure: Five Traditions, No Summit

Each school maintains its own academies, each with distinct governance, training philosophy, and relationship to civil authority. The traditions recognize each other's credentials — a master's certification from the School of Pattern is acknowledged by Force academies as evidence of genuine training and meaningful skill, even if the Force academy has opinions about what Pattern masters tend to be like as people.

This mutual recognition is formalized in the **Compact of Craft**, an agreement of uncertain historical origin that most academies reference as the foundation of inter-school professional norms. The Compact establishes:
- That a credential from any recognized academy represents genuine training
- That no school has authority over another school's practice
- That practitioners are individually responsible for the legal and ethical conduct of their work
- That disputed cross-school matters may be referred to a joint panel of masters (this mechanism is used rarely and slowly)

The Compact has no enforcement mechanism beyond professional reputation. This is, in practice, sufficient for most purposes.

---

## The Shaping School: Guilds and Hospitals

The School of Transformation organizes primarily around **healing institutions** — hospitals, medical guilds, and infirmary orders — and **craft guilds** in metallurgy and materials work. The two tracks (healing and craft) share the same foundational training curriculum but diverge significantly in advanced study.

Healing academies tend to be associated with major cities, where patient populations justify the investment. Craft guilds operate more regionally, often attached to mining or manufacturing centers. The two tracks maintain a sometimes tense relationship — healing practitioners are higher status and better paid; craft practitioners are more numerous and arguably more economically significant.

There is no single central Shaping institution. The major healing academies in the largest cities have more prestige and influence over training standards than others, but they are rivals with each other as much as colleagues.

---

## The Force Academies: Licensed and Watched

The School of Force operates under the most significant government oversight of any school. Most kingdoms that host Force academies maintain licensing agreements that give the civil authority some influence over enrollment caps, the categories of application that may be taught, and the conditions under which Force practitioners may be mobilized.

In exchange, Force academies receive direct government funding, legal protection for credentialed practitioners, and a degree of prestige that money can supplement but not create. The arrangement is uncomfortable on both sides — academies resent the oversight; governments resent the functional military asset they cannot fully control — and it has been renegotiated in every major political transition.

Force academies tend to be large, well-resourced, and physically impressive institutions. They also tend to be located near political capitals, which makes them visible and convenient for exactly the people who most want to regulate them.

---

## The Pattern Academies: Embedded in Law

The School of Pattern has developed its deepest institutional relationships with the legal establishment. In most jurisdictions, Reader testimony is admissible in court proceedings under specified conditions; the conditions were developed in negotiation between Pattern academies and legal authorities over decades and vary by kingdom.

Pattern academies train their students in law and legal procedure as a matter of course, because practitioners who do not understand the legal context of their work cannot navigate it effectively. Many Pattern practitioners are employed full-time by courts, arbitration bodies, and commercial enterprises that require regular truth-verification services.

The academies themselves tend to be scholarly institutions — heavy on libraries, archives, and philosophical curriculum. The most prestigious Pattern academy in Brenneth is reputed to hold the largest collection of legal and philosophical texts on the continent, which may or may not be accurate but is widely believed.

---

## The Resonance Traditions: Scattered and Embedded

The School of Resonance has the least centralized institutional structure of any school. Its academies are numerous, varied, and embedded in the cultural institutions that have hosted Harmonist training for centuries — bard colleges, diplomatic services, temples, and a handful of deliberately constructed standalone academies.

The training provided by a bard college with a Harmonist curriculum and a dedicated Resonance academy may produce practitioners of equivalent skill, but they will have had very different educational experiences and will think about their practice in different terms. This variation is considered a feature by some within the school (diversity of approach enriches the tradition) and a problem by others (inconsistent standards make it hard to assess credentials).

The closest thing to a central institution for Resonance in Brenneth is a **gathering** held every several years — not a governing body but a professional conference at which practitioners compare notes, dispute interpretations, and negotiate what the Compact of Craft means for their school. These gatherings are attended irregularly and the decisions they produce are advisory.

---

## The Stillness Academies: Small and Institutional

The School of Stillness maintains smaller academies than any other school. This reflects both the school's population size — there are simply fewer Quieters — and the school's institutional alignment. Rather than building large standalone institutions, Stillness academies tend to be affiliated with the organizations that employ their graduates: temples, courts, the security establishments of major cities.

The largest Stillness academies are associated with the largest temple complexes. This gives the school a religious institutional backing that other schools lack and a degree of resilience that its small enrollment might not otherwise provide. Temples have been in operation for centuries; they outlast governments.

The school has a central credentialing body — the only school that does — called the **Registry of Quieters**, maintained cooperatively by the major academies. The Registry is more useful than it might appear: because Quieters are employed specifically to manage magical activity, knowing that someone is a credentialed Quieter (rather than an untrained sensitive making claims) matters to the institutions that hire them.

---

## Cross-School Relations

The five school traditions maintain professional coexistence with varying degrees of warmth.

**Transformation and Pattern** have a generally productive relationship — healers frequently want truth-verification in complex medical situations; Pattern practitioners frequently need physical assessment that Shapers can provide. The schools have developed collaborative practices in medical and forensic work.

**Force and everyone else** is complicated. The Force school's government licensing relationships give it a political position that other schools perceive as a mixed blessing — the Force academies have access and funding that others lack, but they are visibly and significantly in government debt for it. The other schools are careful not to appear affiliated with Force in ways that would cause governments to treat them as similar cases.

**Resonance and Pattern** have the deepest philosophical disagreement — Pattern holds that truth is structural and knowable; Resonance holds that relationship and meaning are at least as fundamental as structure and rather harder to quantify. The debates are genuine and sometimes productive.

**Stillness and everyone** is the most socially delicate relationship. Every other school practitioner knows that a Quieter can end their work. This is usually fine; most Quieters are professional and exercise their function only in appropriate contexts. "Usually" is not "always," and the awareness is always present.
