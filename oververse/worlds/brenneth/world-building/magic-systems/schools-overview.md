# Schools of Magic — Overview

**Status:** Canon — Foundation  
**World:** Brenneth  
**Related:** [oververse/rules/school-framework-anchors.md](../../../../rules/school-framework-anchors.md), [oververse/magic-systems/quantumancy.md](../../../../magic-systems/quantumancy.md)

---

## The Core Insight

Every school of magic in Brenneth rests on the same foundation, whether its practitioners know it or not: **reality is energy at the quantum level, and that energy can be influenced by a mind trained to perceive it correctly.**

The training is not incidental. It is the mechanism. An untrained person who stumbles into quantum perception does not gain power — they gain chaos. The unfiltered view of all possible states, all probabilities, all connections simultaneously is not a gift. It is destruction. The mortal mind cannot process it without fracturing.

What the schools discovered — over centuries of painful trial, documented failure, and accumulated practice — is that a consistent, well-defined mental framework acts as a filter. Perceive only what your framework models. Manipulate only what you can perceive. The framework is the anchor that makes the manipulation stable.

This is what a school sells: not magic, but a way of looking. And because the way of looking shapes what you can do, each school produces fundamentally different practitioners.

---

## What All Schools Share

Despite their differences, all five schools operate on the same underlying principles:

**The Persistence Requirement.** Any quantum manipulation requires ongoing mental effort to sustain. Without the school framework filtering perception and reducing cognitive load, this effort exceeds mortal capacity — hence the haze. The framework does not eliminate this requirement; it makes it manageable by narrowing focus.

**The Blind Spot.** Every framework admits only what it models. This is not a flaw; it is what makes the framework work. A practitioner who can perceive everything perceives nothing usable. The price of stability is limit. Each school has genuine capabilities and genuine gaps, and the gaps are structural — not personal failures but the shape of the framework itself.

**The Decay Problem.** A quantum manipulation that is not anchored by an external object, a school framework, or continuous active attention will revert. Permanent effects require either an artifact anchor, a network anchor, or a practitioner who maintains the manipulation indefinitely. Schools teach their practitioners to work within this constraint — to make changes that are inherently stable (physical shifts that stay shifted) rather than fighting the decay.

**The Haze Risk.** Practitioners who push beyond their framework — either by attempting manipulations their model doesn't account for, or by training in a second school without proper compartmentalization — risk losing the framework's filtering function. The haze does not wait. Multi-school training must be done carefully, slowly, and with supervision.

## The Five Schools

Brenneth recognizes five major schools of magic. These are not cosmologically fixed categories (unlike Iolanu's natural fragments) — they are the schools that developed, survived, and established lasting institutional traditions. Whether they represent the full range of possible frameworks is actively debated.

| School | Core Axiom | What Practitioners Perceive | Common Roles |
|--------|-----------|---------------------------|--------------|
| [Transformation](schools/transformation.md) | Matter is energy in configuration | Quantum state of matter | Healers, smiths, alchemists |
| [Force](schools/force.md) | Energy moves; will can direct it | Energy in motion | Battle-mages, military advisors |
| [Pattern](schools/pattern.md) | Reality has structure; structure can be read | Quantum information states | Judges, diplomats, truth-readers |
| [Resonance](schools/resonance.md) | All things vibrate; shared vibration connects | Resonance between entities | Bards, diplomats, spies |
| [Stillness](schools/stillness.md) | Manipulation can be undone | Active quantum manipulations | Security, magical police |

## What Each School Cannot Do

The blind spots are as important as the capabilities:

**Transformation** practitioners see what things ARE but not how they relate to each other, where they're going, or whether someone intends harm. A Shaper can detect that a body is failing; they cannot detect that someone lied about how it happened.

**Force** practitioners see energy in motion but not the nature of what they're moving or affecting. A Mover can accelerate an arrow; they cannot tell if the archer is trustworthy.

**Pattern** practitioners see truth and structure but cannot change them. A Reader can verify a contract is being honored; they cannot force compliance.

**Resonance** practitioners sense relationships and emotional states but not physical reality. A Harmonist knows the king is afraid; they cannot see what is making him afraid.

**Stillness** practitioners perceive active manipulation but nothing else. A Quieter in a room where no magic is in use is effectively blind — their school gives them nothing to perceive.

These are not personal limits. A master of the School of Force is not failing when they cannot read truth. The framework simply does not look in that direction.

## Wild Magic

Practitioners who develop quantum access without school training exist on Brenneth. They are called wild users colloquially; the academic term is **unschooled sensitives**. See [wild-magic.md](wild-magic.md) for full treatment.

The relevant point for school understanding: wild users are not practicing a "sixth school." They have no framework. What they can do is unpredictable, because it is not constrained by a framework's blind spot — they sometimes perceive things no school accounts for. What they do with that perception is also unpredictable, because they have no vocabulary for it. Most wild users are unstable. Some are terrifyingly capable. Schools are divided on whether wild users represent a failure of the school system or evidence that the five schools do not cover the full range of possible frameworks.

## The Theorist Tradition

In every generation, a small number of practitioners attempt to construct a unified framework — a school that encompasses all schools and eliminates all blind spots. This tradition is called **Theoretical Integration** by its practitioners and "chasing the haze" by everyone else.

Some Theorist work is legitimate and valuable — cross-school analysis has improved training methods in all five schools, and Theorists have occasionally identified new phenomena that expanding a school's framework could capture. Most Theorists are brilliant, careful, and stop well before they approach the limit.

Those who push further are approaching, from below, the kind of perception that First Children have natively. A mortal mind attempting to achieve this is attempting to perceive everything simultaneously — which is, by definition, the unfiltered haze. Some few manage a partial synthesis that seems stable. Whether this is genuine progress or a haze event in slow motion is a question the field has not resolved.

Theorist academies exist. They are small, selective, and more closely watched by government and military establishments than any other school.

## Learning Magic

Training in any school follows a similar arc:

**Sensitization** (1-3 years): The student learns to perceive what the framework models. This is the hardest stage — most students who fail, fail here. They either cannot develop the perceptual capacity, or they develop it and cannot handle even the filtered input.

**Stabilization** (2-5 years): The student learns to sustain perception without losing the framework. Basic manipulation becomes possible. This stage produces practitioners who can do useful work under supervision.

**Practice** (ongoing): Full independent practice. Most practitioners spend their careers here, deepening craft rather than expanding framework.

**Mastery** (rare): A master has refined the framework to the point where they can operate near its edges — pushing just past what the basic model accounts for, identifying its gaps, and sometimes extending it slightly. Masters train the next generation and are the living memory of the school's tradition.

**Integration** (exceptional): A practitioner who can maintain two school frameworks simultaneously without schema conflict. Perhaps one in a generation. They do not usually advertise this.

## Institutional Structure

Each school maintains its own academies, credentialing systems, and traditions. There is no unified magical authority on Brenneth. Schools recognize each other's credentials at the level of acknowledging that a master from the School of Pattern has genuinely earned that credential — but they do not defer to each other's judgment about magic itself.
