# School of Force

**Also called:** The Mover School, the Battle School (military contexts); Kinesistry (academic)  
**Practitioners called:** Movers; Kinesists (academic); battle-mages (military)  
**Status:** Canon — Foundation

---

## Core Axiom

*"Energy moves. Directed will can guide that movement."*

The School of Force is built on the observation that motion, pressure, and momentum are all expressions of energy in transit — and that a mind trained to perceive this energy can redirect it. Where the School of Transformation works with what things ARE, Force works with what they DO. The distinction matters: a Mover can hurl a boulder without any understanding of what the boulder is made of.

---

## What Movers Perceive

A trained Mover perceives **energy in motion** — kinetic force, potential energy, pressure gradients, momentum vectors. They do not see matter in its static state; they see the dynamic layer. A room full of still objects is nearly invisible to Force perception. A battlefield in full engagement is overwhelming information.

This manifests as:
- Sensing the mass and velocity of moving objects in proximity
- Perceiving pressure differentials (where force is building, where it is released)
- Detecting the momentum of incoming attacks before they land
- Feeling structural stress as potential energy — a wall about to collapse registers as tension before the crack appears

The perception is motion-focused and moment-to-moment. Movers are extremely effective in dynamic situations and relatively unremarkable in static ones.

---

## What Movers Can Do

**Projection.** Generating and directing kinetic force at a distance — the most common combat application. A practitioner with a few years of training can throw a man off his feet without touching him. A master can collapse a door, overturn a cart, or redirect an arrow in flight.

**Shielding.** Intercepting incoming force and dispersing or redirecting it. A Mover shield does not absorb impact; it deflects or cancels it. The more specifically a Mover can perceive the incoming force vector, the more precisely they can redirect it.

**Self-enhancement.** Applying force to one's own body — extending the legs' push into a longer jump, adding momentum to a sword swing, decelerating a fall. Difficult to use well because it requires precise calibration to avoid tearing muscle or fracturing bone. Battle-mages who use self-enhancement are called "charged" fighters and are extremely dangerous.

**Large-scale manipulation.** At high skill levels, moving significant weights — logs, stone blocks, collapsed structural elements. Used in construction, disaster response, and occasionally siege warfare.

**Speed reduction.** Opposing force vectors to slow or stop objects in motion. Less commonly trained than projection, but valuable for defensive work.

---

## What Movers Cannot Do (The Blind Spot)

Force perceives energy in motion. It does not perceive:

- **The nature of what is being moved.** A Mover can throw a person or a sack of grain with equal facility. The school has no purchase on biological states, material composition, intent, or identity.
- **Static matter.** Unless something is already moving, Force has very little to perceive. A Mover cannot sense hidden objects, determine the composition of a wall, or detect a person standing still in a dark room.
- **Emotional states or intent.** A Mover can sense that a sword is swinging toward them. They cannot sense why.
- **Truth or probability.** Force is purely present-tense and physical. It has no access to information states.

A master Mover is still effectively blind to anything that is not in motion or under dynamic stress. This makes them formidable in combat and somewhat blunt instruments for anything else.

## Training and Culture

**Typical training arc:** 5-8 years before independent practice. The fastest standard path among the five schools, because the perceptual demands — sensing motion — are more intuitive for most students than the precision required by Transformation or the passivity required by Pattern.

**Apprenticeship structure:** Battle-mage traditions favor cohort training — groups of students working together, training combat scenarios. The solo master-apprentice structure is less common in Force academies than in other schools. This creates a culture of camaraderie and competition simultaneously.

**The martial tradition.** Force academies typically maintain physical training programs for their students, on the theory that a battle-mage who cannot fight with steel is at a disadvantage when the cognitive load of active manipulation makes simultaneous framework-use impractical. The image of the Mover as someone who only uses magic is considered naive; most battle-mages carry weapons.

**Political embeddedness.** Force academies have closer relationships with military and noble establishments than any other school. This produces wealth, institutional stability, and significant political entanglement. Most Force academies operate under some form of licensing arrangement with the kingdoms they are based in — a compromise that gives the kingdom influence over who gets trained and in what numbers, in exchange for not regulating the school out of existence.

---

## Social Status

The School of Force is simultaneously the most powerful and the most regulated of the five schools. Its military utility is obvious and valuable. Its potential for violence if unregulated is equally obvious.

Most kingdoms maintain some form of Mover registration — practitioners of significant skill are known to the government, subject to certain legal restrictions, and in some cases subject to mobilization obligations in time of war. This is resented by the Force academies, accepted as the price of operating openly, and enforced with variable effectiveness.

Movers in non-military careers — construction, disaster response, port work — are common and viewed as pragmatic professionals. The fear attaches specifically to battle-mages; a Mover who moves cargo for a living does not trouble anyone.

## Common Misconceptions

**"Movers can fly."** Sustained self-levitation requires continuous application of upward force against gravity. Most Movers can manage a controlled fall or a significant jump; very few can sustain true flight for more than seconds. Claiming a practitioner "can fly" typically means they can fall from height without dying, which is impressive but different.

**"Force shields stop everything."** A Mover shield intercepts force vectors. It does not stop poison, disease, deception, or magic from other schools. A Mover behind a shield is still fully vulnerable to a Reader discovering something they'd prefer kept hidden.

**"Battle-mages are just soldiers."** The Force school produces battle-mages, but also structural engineers, military advisors, search-and-rescue specialists, and demolition experts. The combative framing is culturally dominant and professionally limiting.
