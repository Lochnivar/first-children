# Wild Magic — Unschooled Quantum Access

**Status:** Canon — Foundation  
**World:** Brenneth  
**Academic term:** Unschooled sensitives  
**Folk terms:** Wild users, untrained, strays

---

## Overview

Occasionally — without training, without a school framework, without any intention — a person begins to perceive the quantum layer. They have not chosen a framework. They have no vocabulary for what they are experiencing. They have no cognitive anchor except whatever their own mind improvises.

These are wild users, and they are both the clearest evidence that quantum access does not require formal training and the clearest demonstration of why formal training was developed in the first place.

---

## How Wild Access Develops

Wild access can emerge in several ways:

**Gradual onset.** The most common pattern. The sensitive begins perceiving things that have no clear sensory source — a vague sense that a person is lying, an impression of something's internal structure, a feeling of resonance with places or people that is more vivid than normal. This can persist for years as low-level, deniable, and erratic perception before it crystallizes into something undeniable.

**Sudden emergence under stress.** High emotional or physical crisis sometimes triggers acute quantum access in someone who had no prior indication of sensitivity. The access tends to be overwhelming, poorly controlled, and terrifying. These events are more likely to draw professional attention quickly — either because the person manifests something visible, or because they are found in acute distress.

**Accident exposure.** Proximity to active magical work can, rarely, sensitize someone who was not previously developing access. This is not well understood by the schools and is more common anecdote than documented phenomenon.

---

## What Wild Users Experience

Without a framework, the sensitive has no filter on what they perceive. They are not receiving a clean slice of quantum information — they are receiving everything the quantum layer presents, which is multiple overlapping things simultaneously.

In practice, this means wild access is:

**Non-specific.** The sensitive may feel both emotional states and physical configurations and movement energy and something that might be truth — but without training, these signals are indistinguishable from each other. They know *something* about a situation; they cannot reliably say what category of something.

**Inconsistent.** The access comes and goes. Without a framework to provide stable perceptual structure, the signal fades under cognitive load, rises unexpectedly, and varies by proximity, emotional state, and unknown factors.

**Involuntary.** Wild users do not choose when to receive. The perception arrives regardless of context or convenience. This is exhausting.

**Sometimes uniquely capable.** Because the framework has not narrowed perception, a wild user sometimes perceives things that no single school would account for. An experienced Transformation practitioner cannot perceive emotional states; a wild user with Transformation-adjacent access might perceive both physical configuration and resonance simultaneously, if crudely. This makes wild users occasionally capable of things that trained practitioners cannot replicate — not because they are more skilled, but because they have not been told what they cannot perceive.

---

## The Haze Risk

Wild users face a substantially higher haze risk than trained practitioners. The framework's primary function is perceptual filtering — it reduces the cognitive load of quantum perception to something manageable. Without it, the sensitive is subject to the full, unfiltered signal.

Most wild users do not reach haze levels. The access they develop is partial, intermittent, and insufficient for the kind of sustained active manipulation that produces acute haze events. They experience something more like chronic overexposure: persistent low-level disorientation, difficulty distinguishing internal states from perceived external states, and a background noise that does not stop.

Wild users who push — who attempt to use their access actively and repeatedly without training — accelerate toward haze exposure. The breaking point varies. Some people can sustain significant wild use for years before fracturing; others experience acute haze events early.

## Schools' Attitudes Toward Wild Users

The five schools are not unified on how to treat wild users, and the disagreement reveals something genuine about how each school understands itself.

**Transformation and Force** tend toward pragmatic urgency: find wild users, get them trained. Wild access is dangerous (to the user) and unreliable (to everyone else). The faster the person is brought into a framework, the better for all parties.

**Pattern** takes a more analytical interest. The school's philosophical tradition produces researchers who study wild cases — what can an unschooled sensitive perceive that schooled practitioners cannot? Does the absence of a framework reveal something about the full range of quantum perception that no school has yet modeled?

**Resonance** has the most heterodox position: some Harmonist traditions argue that the framework itself is a kind of imposition that limits access, and that wild sensitives are closer to a more natural relationship with the quantum layer. This view is a minority position within the school and is rejected by most academies, but it persists and occasionally attracts students who self-identify as wild users unwilling to be constrained by a single framework.

**Stillness** is practically interested: a wild user is an active quantum event that a Quieter perceives clearly but that does not have a clean framework signature. They are genuinely unpredictable to Quieter detection systems — the lack of a framework makes them harder to classify, though not harder to cancel.

## Social and Legal Status

Wild users on Brenneth are a recognized category — not mythologized, not exactly ordinary, but known. Most communities have encountered one or know of one. The social response is generally to encourage them toward school training as quickly as possible, which is treated as both a kindness (training makes the condition manageable) and a public safety measure (uncontrolled quantum access is unpredictable).

Wild users who refuse to enter school training occupy an ambiguous legal position in most kingdoms. No law prevents them from existing. Laws around the practice of magic without credentials apply to active use, which is difficult to enforce when "active use" is poorly defined. Wild users who manifest in visible, documented ways face more scrutiny than those who present merely as eccentric.

A small number of wild users deliberately avoid school training, either out of suspicion of institutional control or out of a genuine belief (sometimes rooted in Resonance-adjacent thinking) that the framework would cost them something they value in their current perception. They are generally regarded by school practitioners as making a choice that will eventually harm them.

---

## The Theorist Connection

Some Theorist practitioners — those attempting to construct unified frameworks — deliberately study wild users as research material. If a wild user is perceiving across multiple school boundaries, what does that reveal about the quantum layer that lies beneath all the frameworks?

The relationship is not always comfortable. Wild users are not laboratory specimens, and they do not always welcome the attention of academics who are professionally interested in their condition rather than personally concerned with their wellbeing.
