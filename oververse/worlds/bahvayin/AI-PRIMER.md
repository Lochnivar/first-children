## What Bahvayin Is

Bahvayin (*bah-VAH-yin*, stress second syllable) is a world in the Hypatia oververse — a fictional multiverse where First Children (FC) are essentially "little-g" gods who create and manage worlds. FC are named from cosmic entities, not characters — they are the *why things are the way they are*, not participants in stories.

**The world:** A massive atoll surrounding the Akhan Sea (inner sea). The Akhan itself is approximately 1.75x the size of the Mediterranean — the atoll rim adds further extent beyond that. From space it looks exactly like an eyeball — the atoll rim as iris, the Akhan as pupil. Mortals have no outside perspective and do not know this. The FC fingerprint is in the world name itself: *Bahvayin* derives from First Speech triconsonantal roots *bavah* (pupil) + *ayin* (eye).

**The FC premise:** An integration/segregation experiment. Maximum racial diversity deliberately seeded into a closed geographic system with distinct ecological niches and limited exit points. The question the FC are asking: given enough pressure and time, do diverse species integrate, segregate, or destroy each other? The experimenters are unnamed, uncharacterized, and permanently offstage. They do not appear in stories. Their presence is felt only in the world's design.

**Authorial register:** Ground level. Stories follow common folk — dock workers, junior functionaries, third sons, the people far below the succession crisis who feel every consequence of it. The meta-narrative is assembled from fragments, never explained from above.

## Geography

**The Atoll:** The Akhan Sea (inner sea) is approximately 1.75x the size of the Mediterranean. The atoll rim extends beyond that. The three straits — Storm Gate (east), Desert Road (west-northwest), Southern Way (south) — divide the rim into three distinct landmass arcs. Boundaries bleed naturally; transition zones are gradual and often the most interesting geography.

**The three arcs:**
- **Grenmarch** (northeast arc, Storm Gate to Desert Road over the top) — Temperate forest, frontier territory. Tzafan and Baram sit here. **Tal Aegis**, the imperial capital, sits on the lee side of the southern Grenmarch headland at the Storm Gate mouth — the Sheltering Hand shelters by standing in the path.
- **Dashamar** (south arc, Storm Gate to Southern Way) — Southern steppe, warm grasslands, ranching culture. Southern Way warm current moderates from below. Storm coast outer face holds the **divine office temples** — the gods' offices, where mortals petition; distinct from their homes in the Archavar.
- **Harova** (west arc, Southern Way to Desert Road) — Three distinct zones stacked west to east:
  - *The Harovian Desert* — outer western face, hyperarid, weather factory, Desert Road corridor runs along mountain base aquifer ribbon
  - *Andalvar* — alpine interior highlands and valleys, sheltered microclimates, Kern's vineyards
  - *Mazarvan* — steep eastern descent to Akhan shore, Alborz/Caspian character, dense forest, terraced farms, humid coast

**The Archavar:** The mountain range running through Harova's interior. In mortal theology, the **home** of the gods (distinct from their offices on the Dashamar storm coast). Each major peak is a deity's private seat. **Mount Tsapadon** is the highest — storm-exposed, visible from the inner Akhan. The Archavar feeds a karst hydrology system: solution basins at peaks → mid-mountain aquifer → forced emergence at impermeable bedrock → the Desert Road water ribbon.

**The Yeorvan:** The great river of Harova. Sources in the Archavar, cuts west through the Harovian Desert, fans into a fertile delta in the northern desert where flat terrain and sand cannot maintain defined channels. The delta is the most fertile land in the West arc. *The Yeorvan* — not "the Yeorvan River." The name contains the word for river already. The redundancy marks an outsider.

**The three straits:**
- **The Storm Gate** (east) — straight channel, heavily engineered breakwater islands, the empire's defense against storm surge.
- **The Desert Road** (west-northwest) — winding passage, Djemari nomad territory, water monopoly, strategic underestimation by the empire.
- **The Southern Way** (south) — curved passage, warm current pump (Nurathi's current), fog belt, food security route.

**The Pachi Trail:** The only interior cross-atoll route. Circuits all three arcs through their heartlands — not coastal, not rim-hugging, through the middle. Deliberately wends through as many biomes as the terrain allows; the Pachi are experiencing, not transiting. Harova section crosses all three zones (Andalvar, Harovian Desert, Yeorvan corridor). Strait crossings via ferry or pontoon at city crossing points — those cities exist partly because the trail needed a crossing. Trail rights predate the empire. No imperial replacement exists. The maritime alternative goes around; the Pachi Trail goes through. Makes the Pachi the best-informed people on the atoll by default — not spies, just present everywhere.

**The outer ocean:** Genuinely dangerous. Lesser Dragons hold it. "Thar be dragons" is accurate intelligence, not superstition. No successful Atoll exploration. The outer ocean is not empty — see Authorial Holds below.

## History

**Three-tier structure:**

**Deep History (pre-mageocracy):** A predecessor civilization that left ruins the current culture doesn't fully understand. Four unresolved questions: what they were, where they went, why their ruins are distributed where they are, and what the oral traditions of the northern rim cultures actually remember. This is shallow mystery — story fuel, not cosmological explanation.

**The Mageocracy:** A high-magic era that ended catastrophically. From mortal perspective: inexplicable catastrophe called the Death of Magic. From FC perspective: a confounding variable (magical ability displacing species interaction as organizing principle) was removed via substrate-level quantum suppression. Left behind: pre-Death artifacts that still function as frozen enchantments (irreplaceable, fought over), sealed dungeon complexes with Orcus-genus creatures that lost their handlers, ruins, and a civilization with deep institutional allergy to magic.

**The Empire (current era):** A declining maritime empire in active succession crisis. Founded through strategic intermarriage — every powerful house given a stake in the system. Worked too well: every stake is now a claim. The founding bloodline has thinned. Dinath (goddess of contracts) will not tip her scales for political convenience — every claim is legally legitimate and none definitionally superior. Meanwhile the Eye of Tsapadon (mega-storm cycle) is approaching, the engineer-priests have been sounding alarms for a generation, and breakwater island sabotage is suspected but not provable.

## Magic

**Current era:** Low magic. No new enchantment is possible. The Death of Magic ended that.

**Pre-Death artifacts:** Still functional. They are archaeological objects — studied, hoarded, occasionally activated by accident. Not common equipment. Four categories: functional artifacts, partially functional, inert, and unstable/dangerous.

**Residual ability:** Some individuals born with fragments of pre-Death magical capability. Politically persecuted — the empire is allergic to magic. Not full magic; fragments. Can sometimes interact with pre-Death artifacts.

**The engineer-priests:** Appear magical through genuine expertise, pre-Death instruments only they can read, eight centuries of tidal records, and carefully maintained mystique. Not actual magic. A Sungrun (species with acute environmental sensitivity) who starts screaming three days before any instrument registers is not magic — it is a very good early warning system. Homo Bovinus are foundational to the engineer-priests — their tremorsense applied to structural assessment reads breakwater stress and failure before any visible indicator appears. This capability has been encoded into the ritual forms the Hierarchy teaches.

## The Political Structure

**Imperial title:** *Sheltering Hand of Tsapadon* — the emperor as mortal administrator maintaining correct procedural relationship between cosmic bureaucracy and Akhan civilization. Theological and political legitimacy are fused.

**Hierarchy:**
| Title | Basis | Notes |
|-------|-------|-------|
| Emperor | Dynastic + theological | Vaelor IX — theological fatalist, piously passive |
| Kings (x5) | Independent lineage | Client kingdoms, negotiated status, crowns predate empire |
| Grand Dukes / Imperial Dukes (x10) | Hereditary + imperial confirmation | "Imperial Duchy" is the category; "Grand Duke" is the formal title |
| Dukes | Regional/military | Mixed legitimacy |
| Counts | Administrative | e.g. Count of Kern |
| Barons | Local | |
| Thanes | Frontier | Often more real authority than title implies |

**Grand Duke succession:** Hereditary but requires imperial confirmation. Emperor can remove a Grand Duke at will — the political consequences are real and narrative, not mechanical. In the succession crisis, unconfirmed heirs are in legal limbo; every claimant offers confirmation as a carrot.

**Key distinction:** Kings have legitimacy independent of imperial recognition. Grand Dukes' legitimacy depends on imperial confirmation. Kings watch and wait; Grand Dukes need the succession resolved.

**Succession ratification:** Three of five client kings must ratify any named heir. Constitutional concession from a weaker reign. Currently a structural trap — no coalition of three exists. Race to three kings; San Tavar is almost certainly the deciding third vote for any claimant who assembles the first two.

**Dissolution provision:** Unanimous vote of all five kings formally dissolves the empire when the emperor dies childless with no confirmed heir. Each kingdom returns to full independence, constitutionally clean.

**Treason clause:** A dissolution vote that fails — that does not achieve unanimity — makes every king who voted in favor a traitor. The defecting king backs a claimant by default and inherits the traitors' kingdoms as the prize. Failed dissolution means catastrophic loss for the dissolution voters, not just a missed opportunity.

**The Prisoner's Dilemma:** All five coordinating on dissolution wins for everyone. One defector makes four others traitors and wins catastrophically. Coordination requires absolute mutual trust across five kings who don't have it — and the conversation required to build it is documentable treason if anyone defects afterward. San Tavar's Queen is the only player with enough intelligence about all five to assess whether unanimity is achievable. What she plans to do with that assessment is not known.

## Emperor Vaelor IX

**Full style:** His Radiant Serenity Vaelor IX, Ninth of the Line, Keeper of the Eye's Mercy, Administrator of the Cosmic Ledger.

**The man:** 74, visibly frail, human, old imperial bloodline now dangerously thin. No living children — three sons and one daughter all died before adulthood, the last in a boating accident he still obsesses over. Silver-white hair, pale grey eyes that look slightly past whoever is speaking to him. Carries a blackthorn staff inlaid with Corvani feather quills — a nod to the engineer-priests whose warnings he has ignored. Storm-sapphire mantle clasp: pre-Death artifact that hums during major storms.

**Ruling style:** Theological fatalist. Has concluded the succession crisis is a personal test from the cosmic bureaucracy — Dinath's balanced scales as divine judgment on his reign, not a political problem to solve. Courteous, soft-spoken, maddeningly passive. Thanks petitioners for their "valuable insight" and lets matters drift for months. Not cruel, not stupid — exhausted and genuinely convinced decisive action would unbalance the ledger further.

**The Eye:** Sees the approaching Eye as the final audit of his reign. Has redirected breakwater maintenance funds to weaken claimant houses three times, each time framed as allowing the cosmic bureaucracy to determine which structures are worthy. Suspects sabotage. Will not expose it — that would require choosing sides. The Corvani analyst's findings were archived under "theological review."

**The succession:** Never named an heir. Never will. Uses small symbolic favors to keep every claimant house in hopeful anxiety. The balance is maintained with genuine skill and serves no purpose except to remain balanced.

**The Mishmarine vacancy:** Deliberately left vacant 14 months. Stated reason: "The outer thresholds must remain under Kadoru's gaze, not any single house's prize." Actual function: leverage for the claimant with a credible northern policy. None has yet qualified.

**Zafaran appointment:** Appointed Grand Duchess Lirael vel-Zafaran (human, 52, minor noble stock, no claimant ties, meticulous, won the poetry competition once) because her reports are beautifully formatted and she will not use the Archives as a weapon.

**Seneschal Korrin:** Hobgoblin chamberlain, 41 years of service, age 73. The only person who speaks to Vaelor bluntly in private. Has been managing the gap between what Vaelor believes and what reality requires for four decades. Keeps private records of his advice and Vaelor's responses, dated precisely. Does not know what he will do with them. Has begun thinking about what happens when he is no longer present to manage the gap.

**What Vaelor represents:** The empire is not being destroyed by malice. It is being dissolved by piety. Every decision is theologically coherent and practically catastrophic.

## The Five Client Kingdoms

All five are watching the succession crisis. None has declared. All have different leverage and different reasons for waiting.

**Stormbreak** — Northeastern inner Akhan, massive reef outside the Storm Gate mouth. Storm culture, calibrated fearlessness, salvage economy. Controls outer Storm Gate defense — engineer-priests need their cooperation during the Eye. Three reef find categories: mageocracy material, unidentifiable outer ocean origin, and a third (recent, worked, foreign, no Atoll framework) the King is not sharing with Kol Idic. King: female, not rushing.

**A'Kavah** — Southwestern inner Akhan, long-extinct supervolcano caldera. Agricultural heartland, extraordinary fertility, feeding all claimants simultaneously and deliberately. First Speech: "place of the warmth." Chelonari are the landowning class — they think in centuries and hold land accordingly. King controls production; Halfling networks control distribution — distinction beginning to matter. Most powerful material leverage in the crisis.

**San Tavar** — Central inner Akhan, crossroads island. California surfer realpolitik — reads the wave before committing. Meritocratic by pragmatism. Queen rules through San Tavar's own succession framework. Every claimant thinks they have a special relationship with her. They all have exactly the same relationship. Untested fleet whose upper bound nobody wants to discover. *The ocean is always patient and always wins.*

**Tzafan** — Northwestern inner Akhan, gateway to the northern rim. Frontier capable, outward looking. Something consolidating on the northern rim for two years — nobody further south takes it seriously. The King declares for whoever has a credible northern policy. Currently nobody qualifies.

**Kol Idic** — Southern mid-Akhan. Most intact mageocracy ruins on Bahvayin. *Kol* (all) + *idic* (infinite diversity, infinite combinations). Principled artifact acquisition — offers always fair, suspiciously fair, previous owners receive certificates of thanks. Succession documentation acquired for eight years, purely for stewardship. The knowledge only fires once.

## The Grand Duchies

Ten Grand Duchies built. The Emperor has not yet been developed — two ducal appointments (Zafaran, Mishmarine) are TBD pending that development.

**Mavar** — Central inner Akhan. Imperial fleet anchorage, positioned as hedge against San Tavar. Grand Duke Sorath (Aubrei, third generation). Fleet loyalty runs to Sorath not the throne. Aubrei-dense in navigation and command track. Sorath has outer ocean intelligence nobody has formally asked for. The San Tavar relationship is professionally adversarial respect across an open secret — both know exactly why the fleet is positioned there.

**Zafaran** — Western inner Akhan, Desert Road terminus. Imperial Archives and Grand Library — the empire's official paper trail, not ancient mystery. Hobgoblin administration actually runs everything. Grand Duchess Lirael vel-Zafaran (human, 52, appointed by Vaelor for her meticulous loyalty and lack of political ambition; won the poetry competition once). Arabian Nights register: warm, prosperous, elaborate hospitality, outdoor life, poetry competitions. The senior Hobgoblin administrator has read every document in the archive and has opinions he hasn't been asked to share. Imperial court sends policy questions; archivists debate them over coffee for three evenings; response becomes binding imperial precedent. Nobody has officially acknowledged this. Kol Idic contrast: Kol Idic has the deep past, Zafaran has the current legal record. Both matter in the succession crisis.

**Baram** — Far northern inner Akhan shore. Transhipment and cold storage. Llamari-dense — they run the northern supply chains and are the first to notice when patterns change. Natural cool extends preservation windows. Deep harbor chambers are mageocracy-era construction — sealed against leakage by techniques that died with the Death of Magic, irreplaceable, maintained with great care. Kol Idic sent two scholarly delegations; warehouse master gave full access; they left frustrated because you can't put a harbor chamber in a certificate of stewardship. Northern supply anomalies showing up in the warehouse master's exception report file for eight months. Nobody has classified it as intelligence.

**Eithan** — Eastern inner Akhan. Breakwater fabrication yard — where components get built before being barged to the Storm Gate for installation. Homo Bovinus-present for quality control via tremorsense. Kibo transient surge-labor influx when storm warnings circulate — locals read the Kibo arrival rate as a storm severity gauge before official warnings post. Close operational relationship with the engineer-priests; the imperial court is an abstraction, the engineer-priests' Monday morning specifications are not.

**The Mishmarine** — Outer coastal patrol institution with Grand Duchy status. Not the imperial navy — operates where the imperial navy doesn't go. Multiple ports around the atoll rim. Grand Duchy vacant 14 months by Vaelor's decree (Kitsune candidate pool frozen, leverage for northern policy). Captain-General Thorne (Bruin) running operations on caretaker basis with considerable professional irritation — absorbing political functions he is not suited for and does not enjoy. Gnoll/Otter-folk dominant crew culture — long patrol endurance, pack cohesion, self-reliant. The Gnolls have a nickname for the institution. It is not Mishmarine. Thorne pretends not to know it. Lesser Dragon encounter protocols exist and are taken seriously. Aubrei behavioral change on the southern boundary has been in patrol reports for eight months; forwarded to imperial court three times; acknowledged each time.

**Avir (Grimport)** — Southern inner Akhan, fog belt adjacent. Formal name: Avir. Working name: Grimport — what sailors call it, what appears in logs. Fog never fully lifts. Foghorn runs all day and all night without interruption. Gnoll-dense; Grimport Gnolls are a distinct cultural variant — they have metabolized the fog and noise into identity. "We're all a little mad here" is a statement of philosophy, not complaint. Former residents can't sleep without the foghorn. If the foghorn stops, something is wrong. Empire's primary fishing fleet and protein supply outside A'Kavah's agriculture.

**Mare Shafel** — Southwestern inner Akhan, island plus mainland. Island is the administrative center; mainland coastal plain below the Western Range's eastern slopes is where the horses are. Empire's primary horse breeding operation. Pachi landowning class manages the estates. Andalusian climate, A'Kavah feeds the horses, Halfling networks move everything. Adjacent manufacturing: leather, tack, farrier work, textiles, specialized animal transport vessels. Sale season once a year — every claimant's representatives attend simultaneously, horses sell at fair prices, no commitments made.

**Kerath** — Northeastern inner Akhan. Peat bog fuel economy. Moisture from the Storm Gate gap produces persistent grey skies and deep bogs. Empire's primary fuel supply for forges, kilns, and hearths. Peat fires have a specific smell — Kerath natives find it comforting. Culture: permanently damp, strong oral tradition, indoor social life, extremely dry wit. Ordinary and functional. Grand Duke wants the succession resolved so the fuel contracts resume.

**Shavon** — Northwestern inner Akhan, Tzafan-adjacent. Mixed agriculture, feeds the northwestern rim settlements. Hears northern news early, too small to do anything with it. Grand Duke competent, unremarkable, wants the disruption to end.

**Amud** — Eastern mid-Akhan. Transit stop — ships crossing the Akhan stop here. Service economy: provisioning, chandlery, minor repairs, hospitality. Prosperous through traffic volume. Politically cautious: commitment to a claimant before the outcome is clear is bad for business, the losing side remembers.

## Other Institutions

**The Engineer-Priests** — multi-species coalition at the Storm Gate maintaining the breakwater islands. Engineering institution with theological framework built around its function. Homo Bovinus foundational — their tremorsense reads structural stress before visible indicators appear, encoded in ritual forms. Pre-Death instruments only they can read. Eight centuries of tidal records. The Sungrun early warning function. Currently: Corvani analyst's work on maintenance logs approaching legally documentable evidence of deliberate sabotage. Question the Senior Council hasn't answered: to whom do you present such evidence when every potential recipient has a political interest in how it is used?

**Imperial Military Academy** — standalone imperial institution, not a Grand Duchy. Direct relationship with the throne, currently uncomfortable because the throne is contested. Mainland location (development pending). Commandant answers to the imperial office, not any specific emperor. Every claimant wants the Academy's declaration because it implies legitimacy.

**Count of Kern** — mainland, development pending. Vineyard country on the eastern slopes of the Western Range north of Mare Shafel. Kernish Red is the prize product, recognized across the empire. Soft power through a product everyone knows. Every claimant has sent a nice letter mentioning Kernish Red specifically. The Count has sent back equally nice letters and a case of the current vintage. No commitments made.

Eight deities. Cosmic bureaucrats with primal portfolios. They do not appear in stories.

- **Shamur** — Memory, record-keeping, the accumulation of time
- **Tsapadon** — Storms, divine attention, the Eye (storms are attention not punishment)
- **Yameth** — Death, the boundary between living and not, passage
- **Mothar** — Strength, endurance, the capacity to bear weight
- **Kadoru** — The deep, hidden things, the underworld layer
- **Nurathi** — Warmth, fertility, the Southern Way current, geological heat
- **Azaron** — Fire, transformation, the forge, what destruction makes possible
- **Dinath** — Contracts, oaths, the scales that cannot be politically tipped

## Species Distribution (Summary)

No mono-racial polities. Species gravitate to ecological niches but culture is the identity. Humans are the majority generalist population. Species develop city-level and regional cultural variants — a Grimport Gnoll and an inner Akhan Gnoll are the same species with a real cultural gap between them.

**No cross-species breeding.** Species are genetically incompatible — a dog cannot breed with a cat. No half-species, no hybrids, no exceptions. Each species reproduces within its own kind. Cultural integration is deep and real; biological boundaries are absolute.

**Cross-species marriage:** Legally valid, biologically sterile. Rare and socially viewed with disdain — an instrument of political desperation, not genuine union. Old houses with deep biological lines don't need it. Its use announces the limits of a house's options even while executing strategy. Functions as a legitimacy transfer instrument: designates an heir from a cadet branch of either spouse's species. The imperial court processes these with full ceremony and theological framing. The rim cultures find them bizarre. The Architects' claim includes one such line two generations back — legally defensible, socially the detail every other claimant quietly points to.

Key niches:
- **Dwarves** — Engineering, construction, reef and structural work
- **Chelonari** — Archival, long memory, institutional continuity
- **Corvani** — Pattern analysis, weather reading, finding things in records nobody else noticed
- **Hobgoblins** — Administration, logistics, making complex systems actually function
- **Homo Bovinus** — Tremorsense, structural assessment, engineer-priest foundation species
- **Kibo** — Physical labor in hostile conditions, surge reinforcement, expedition muscle
- **Aubrei** — Fog navigation, outer reef and outer boundary operation
- **Halflings** — Logistics, distribution networks, movement of goods through complex systems
- **Kitsune** — Information brokering, commercial intelligence, acquisition networks
- **Pachi** — Landowning class, multigenerational estates, agricultural and breeding management
- **Felari** — Court culture, refined social surface
- **Bandari** — Gray market trade, frontier exchange
- **Llamari** — Logistics in difficult terrain, northern supply chains
- **Iridari** — News broadcast layer, port information networks
- **Geliss** — Cross-cultural liaison, translating between contexts
- **Gnolls** — Long patrol endurance, pack cohesion, fog fishing culture (Grimport variant), Mishmarine crews
- **Sungrun** — Acute environmental sensitivity, early warning function
- **Bruin** — Frontier rangers, outer patrol command culture
- **Otter-folk** — Social facilitation, crew morale on long operations
- **Djemari** — Desert Road nomads, water monopoly, negotiate at Zafaran not the imperial capital

## Authorial Holds

**The Outer Landmasses:** 3-4 Australia-sized landmasses in the outer ocean, northern hemisphere, outside major storm tracks. Unknown to Atoll civilization. Civilizations that live *with* Lesser Dragons — coexistence, not avoidance. Design language entirely alien to any Atoll framework. Source of Stormbreak's Category Three reef finds. First contact held in authorial reserve. Do not name or characterize the outer civilizations until a story requires it.

**The Northern Consolidation:** Something organizing on the northern rim for two years. Tzafan's scouts report it. Baram's freight anomalies show it. Nobody has connected these two data sources. No details established — held for the story that needs it.

**The Predecessor Question:** Deep history unresolved. Northern rim oral traditions remember things the empire suppressed. Not answered at the worldbuilding layer.

**Spices and medicinal herbs:** The geography supports specialty cultivation in several locations (Kern's slopes, A'Kavah's volcanic soil, the fog belt edge). Not pinned to a specific polity — held until a story requires a source.

## Current Crisis Summary (Story Container)

- Succession crisis: genuine legal ambiguity, Dinath won't tip the scales, multiple valid claims
- Eye of Tsapadon approaching: engineer-priests have the math, cannot get political attention
- Breakwater sabotage: Corvani analyst weeks from documentable evidence, nowhere safe to present it
- Five client kingdoms watching: none declared, all have leverage, all waiting — collectively running a Prisoner's Dilemma on dissolution vs. kingmaker. Cooperative outcome requires all five to hold; defecting first wins big; defecting second loses badly. San Tavar is the only player who could broker cooperation and also the player best positioned to defect at the optimal moment. **Tzafan is Vaelor's reliable vote** — frontier pragmatism, doesn't care who wins, just wants the routes open. Won't vote dissolution while current King reigns, making dissolution mathematically impossible right now. Target on his back accordingly — a new Tzafan king changes everything. He has noticed and increased his personal security
- Ten Grand Duchies: most want resolution, few have leverage, all being collected by claimants
- Outer ocean pattern: Aubrei behavioral change in Mishmarine reports and Grimport fishing accounts, right question not yet asked
- Category Three reef finds: Chelonari archivist's private file, Stormbreak King's awareness, Kol Idic doesn't know
- Northern anomalies: Tzafan's scout reports + Baram's freight exceptions = pattern nobody has assembled
- Zafaran coffeehouse precedent: imperial policy being shaped by archivists debating over coffee, nobody officially acknowledges this
