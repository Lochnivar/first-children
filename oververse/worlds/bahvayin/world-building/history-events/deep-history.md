# Deep History

**Category:** History & Events  
**Era:** Before recorded time  
**Related:** [The Mageocracy](mageocracy.md), [Pantheon](../cosmology/pantheon.md)

## Purpose of This Layer

Deep history in Bahvayin is deliberately shallow and deliberately incomplete. It exists to:

1. Give the world a creation myth that mortals can argue about
2. Establish the predecessor civilization whose remnants make the current world legible
3. Plant unresolved questions and inconsistencies that generate stories without answering them

Nothing in this layer is fully known. Everything here is contested, mythologized, or simply lost.

---

## The Creation Myth — Common Version

The imperial theology holds that Shamur, Lord of Cosmic Order, organized the formless waters and raised the atoll rim from the deep. Yameth, already present in the waters, negotiated the shape of the inner sea. Tsapadon was given the outer ocean as his domain and periodically tests the rim's integrity — the storms are his attention, the Eye is his direct gaze.

The Sheltering Hand title traces to this myth: the first emperor was appointed to manage the relationship between the inner civilization and Tsapadon's periodic attention. The breakwaters are an extension of this appointment — mortal engineering in service of a cosmic administrative arrangement.

**What the myth explains:** Why the atoll exists, why there is an inner sea at all, why the storms come from outside, why the emperor's title is specifically about sheltering.

**What the myth does not explain:** Who built the things that were already there when the founding civilizations arrived. Why certain sites predate the oldest imperial records by an order of magnitude. Why the rim cultures' oral traditions describe a world that worked differently.

## The Predecessor Civilization

Something came before. The evidence is:

**Structural:** Certain foundations on the inner islands and at the three strait mouths are built from techniques and materials that do not match anything in current or recent engineering practice. They predate the mageocracy. Some of them predate the oldest continuous records.

**Hydraulic:** The Storm Gate's inner harbor geometry is too optimal to be natural. Someone placed the first breakwater islands, or the first prototype of them, before the engineer-priest culture claims to have begun. The engineer-priests know this. They do not publicize it because their entire theological position rests on their founder having built the first islands.

**Linguistic:** Certain words in the common trading tongue — including *Bahvayin* itself — do not trace to any known language family. They appear fully formed in the oldest records. The scholars who have noticed this have not found satisfying answers.

**Oral traditions:** Rim cultures — particularly northern rim and desert road nomadic groups — have oral traditions that describe a world before the Akhan Sea was the center of civilization. Some describe the atoll as built, not natural. Some describe a people who could "read the stone" in ways no current culture can. The empire has suppressed these traditions without fully understanding why they feel threatening. This is a political instinct, not a reasoned policy.

## Unresolved Questions (Story Fuel)

These questions have no canonical answers. They are hooks.

**Who built the original breakwater prototypes?** The engineer-priests claim their founder began the work. The oldest stones don't support that timeline. Someone placed the first hydraulic structures at the Storm Gate before the current culture existed.

**What collapsed?** The predecessor civilization ended. How? When? The absence of dramatic ruins suggests it didn't end catastrophically — it faded, dispersed, or transformed into something that stopped leaving records. The transition to the mageocracy era is not a clean break.

**What do the rim oral traditions actually remember?** The empire has suppressed specific traditions without being able to fully explain why they feel threatening. The traditions themselves are fragmentary — generations of suppression degrade oral knowledge. What's left may be distorted. What was lost may have been important.

**Why does the world look like this?** The atoll's geometry is too regular, the channel positions too distributed, the ecological niches too distinct for geology alone. The mortals explain this with theology. The explanation is not theological.

---

## Notes for Authors

- Deep history is background texture. It generates questions; answers are story decisions made when a story needs them.
- The predecessor civilization should never be fully explained. Partial reveals are better than complete ones.
- The suppressed oral traditions are accessible to characters from rim cultures — they are a resource characters can possess, trade, or lose.
- The "wrong" foundations and hydraulic structures are physical objects that characters can find, examine, and fail to fully understand.
