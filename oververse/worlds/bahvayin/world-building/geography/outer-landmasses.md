# Outer Landmasses — Authorial Hold

**Category:** Geography — Authorial Hold  
**Status:** Exists. Offstage. Do not surface in Atoll stories unless deliberately reaching for it.  
**Related:** [Outer Ocean](../natural-world/outer-ocean.md), [Stormbreak](../cultures/stormbreak.md)

## Overview

The outer ocean is not empty. Beyond the Atoll, in the northern hemisphere away from the major storm tracks, there are 3 to 4 Australia-sized landmasses unknown to Atoll civilization. They have been there the entire time. No Atoll expedition has reached them. No one on the Atoll knows they exist.

This is authorial background. It shapes the world's deeper structure without touching the Atoll stories directly — unless a story deliberately reaches for it.

---

## The Outer Civilizations

The landmasses support civilizations that developed in complete isolation from the Atoll. Their defining characteristic: **they live with Lesser Dragons.**

Where the Atoll civilization developed a "thar be dragons" relationship with the outer ocean — fear, avoidance, the outer ocean as death — the outer landmass civilizations developed coexistence. Same species. Completely different civilizational response. Two worlds running in parallel isolation with fundamentally opposite relationships to the same creatures.

**What this means for their material culture:**
- Not magical in any mageocracy sense — they had their own developmental path
- Not recognizable to any Atoll cultural framework — their design language, their aesthetic, their technology developed independently
- Skilled and sophisticated — these are not primitive cultures. They have simply been solving different problems in a different context for the same number of centuries.
- Lesser Dragon influence on their culture is pervasive — in architecture, in trade, in theology, in daily life

---

## The FC Dimension

The FC experiment on the Atoll is one data set. The outer landmasses are another data set running simultaneously — different species mix, different geographic pressures, different developmental pressures, different outcome. The experimenters are watching both.

The Lesser Dragons on the outer ocean are the boundary condition between two experiments that don't know the other exists.

---

## The Stormbreak Connection

The outer landmasses fuel the most interesting category of Stormbreak reef finds.

Occasionally — after the largest storms, after the Eye, after outer ocean events nobody on the Atoll can identify — objects wash up on Stormbreak's reef that are not mageocracy, not predecessor civilization, not any known Atoll culture. They are clearly worked — someone made them. The materials are identifiable. The construction is skilled. The design language is entirely alien.

A salvager who finds one of these has no framework for what they are holding. Not ancient. Not magical. Just foreign in a way that has no name.

The Chelonari archivist at the Storm Gate has a private file on anomalous Stormbreak finds that don't fit any known category. She has not shared it with the Senior Council because she doesn't know what to do with the pattern she's seeing. The pattern is that some of these objects are not old. They are recent. Someone made them recently, somewhere, and the outer ocean carried them here.

---

## Contact

First contact between the Atoll and the outer landmasses is a story decision held in authorial reserve. It can be deployed:

- As the culmination of a long arc when the Stormbreak reef finds accumulate enough that someone starts asking the right questions
- As an external pressure that arrives at the worst possible moment — during the succession crisis, during the Eye
- As a slow revelation that reframes the entire world's understanding of what the outer ocean is
- Never, if the stories don't need it

The outer civilizations do not know the Atoll exists either. First contact is mutual.

## Notes

- Do not name the outer landmasses or their civilizations until a story requires it
- Do not characterize the outer civilizations beyond what is established here until a story requires it
- The Lesser Dragon coexistence model is the defining contrast with the Atoll — preserve that distinction
- The Stormbreak reef finds are the only current surface-world connection point
