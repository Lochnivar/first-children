## Overview

The County of Florentia sits in the Andalvar valley's most sheltered basin, where the Archavar's specific combination of altitude, slope aspect, and volcanic mineral content in the soil produces aromatic plants that cannot be replicated lower down. The families who work this basin have spent generations arguing about why.

They would describe themselves as *readers* — of the mountain's theological calendar, of the divine overflow as it expresses in soil and stem and bloom. That their terraces produce the empire's most valued aromatic oils, the source material for its rarest perfumes and most prized dyes, is, they will assure you, a consequence of correct reading. Hardly the point.

Four centuries of obsessive inter-family rivalry, conducted entirely through horticulture, have made Florentia accidentally indispensable to perfumers, dye houses, and silk merchants across the inner Akhan. Nobody set out to make the empire beautiful. They set out to win arguments. The beauty is a byproduct.

---

## The Core Tension

The County contains two distinct cultures sharing the same source material and the same basin. They have never formally separated. They have never fully reconciled.

**The Perfumer Houses** hold that the Archavar's divine overflow is a sacred substance — the gods' home expressing itself through soil and root and petal. To distill that expression into scent is to capture something of the divine and offer it to mortal senses. The work is theological. The protocols exist because sacred work requires rigor. The slatted shades are necessary because the most important cultivation decisions should not be made under the observation of people who do not understand what they are witnessing.

**The Dyer Houses** hold that the mountain produces, you process it, beautiful things result, someone wears them. The specific blue that only comes from this basin has made silk merchants wealthy across the inner Akhan for two centuries. The families who grew the plants that produced that blue received fair prices. The silk merchants received fortunes. The dyers have opinions about this arithmetic and express them directly, which the perfumer houses find vulgar and the dyers find clarifying.

The perfumers look down on the dyers as people who boil sacred material in vats. The dyers look at the perfumers' slatted shades and their sixteen-step harvest protocols and their oblique speech and their careful cultivation of mystique, and they roll their eyes at people who make expensive soap.

Both are correct about the other. Neither will say so.

## The Theological Calendar

Florentia's horticultural practice is built around the Archavar's divine overflow calendar — the reading of seasonal, meteorological, and theological signs from the peaks above to determine optimal planting, harvest, and distillation timing.

The Perfumer Houses maintain private observatories integrated into their upper terrace architecture. They keep meticulous generational records correlating peak conditions, snowmelt timing, prevailing winds from specific cols, and theological calendar events with aromatic outcomes. These records are among the most jealously guarded documents in the valley. They are also the primary instrument of inter-family warfare.

The Dyer Houses keep records too — practical ones, focused on yield and color intensity and processing efficiency. They find the perfumers' theological interpretation of what is fundamentally agricultural data charming in the same way one finds a child's explanation of weather charming. The mountain does what the mountain does. Plant correctly. Harvest at the right moment. The rest is story.

### What the Calendar Actually Tracks

- Snowmelt timing from specific Archavar peaks correlating with optimal planting windows
- Wind pattern shifts from the western desert face signaling harvest readiness
- Theological calendar dates for the divine offices on the Dashamar storm coast — planting on feast days of Nurathi and Azaron is considered auspicious by the perfumer houses; the dyer houses plant when the conditions are right
- Long-period weather cycles that the valley's eight centuries of records have documented with considerable precision

Whether the theological correlations reflect genuine causation or merely very consistent observation is a question the Perfumer Houses find offensive and the Dyer Houses find obvious.

## The Argument Is Never About the Plant

When two Perfumer Houses dispute whose lavender oil is superior, they are not arguing about the lavender. They are arguing about whose *reading of the mountain* is more correct, and whose *discipline in executing that reading* is more refined. The plant is the evidence. The terrace is the proof of concept.

Aesthetic criticism is epistemological criticism.

*"Their eastern beds are quite fragrant this season"* means they misread the snowmelt timing and planted ten days early to hedge.

*"Their harvest was aggressive this year"* means they don't trust their own calendar readings.

*"Their distillate is remarkably consistent given the spring conditions"* means they have been lying about their methods for at least two generations.

A terrace that has been producing exceptional oil for five generations is a five-hundred-year argument that one family has been reading the Archavar more correctly than everyone else. Other houses must either accept that claim or spend considerable effort finding the flaw.

The Dyer Houses conduct similar arguments, but directly: whose vat produces the deeper blue, whose mordant process sets color more permanently, whose yield-per-harvest is higher. The argument is still never entirely about the plant. But in the dyer version, at least everyone admits what they are actually arguing about.

## The Perfumer Houses

The Perfumer Houses are the County's public face — the elaborate, protocol-dense, oblique-speech culture that the inner Akhan has learned to navigate when purchasing Florentia's highest-prestige output.

**The slatted shades:** Every Perfumer House maintains a set of adjustable slatted screens — integrated into the terrace architecture, deployed when cultivation work is underway. The exterior face is plain, undecorated. Not humble — a refusal. The blank slat says *there is no family here for you to read. Move along.* A family that decorated their exterior slats would be considered anxious. The most established houses have the most aggressively bare exteriors. The complete absence of effort where effort would be expected is Florentia's version of old money.

Behind the slats: whatever the family wishes. The actual garden, unguarded. The conversation about the plant itself rather than what the plant means.

**The sash system:** The botanical sash is the primary public identity marker — base color encoding primary aromatic specialty, overpattern encoding which Archavar peak the family's calendar reads from, accent thread encoding generation depth. An expert reads a sash the way they read a terrace: completely, instantly, without appearing to look at all. A stranger sees a well-made piece of cloth.

Claiming a peak association your records don't support is the Florentia equivalent of a forged noble title. Never confronted directly. Simply remembered. A family that overclaims on their sash will find their trade inquiries answered more slowly and their terrace invitations arriving after the best viewing season has passed.

**Speech:** Precise, layered, relentlessly oblique when discussing anything that actually matters. An Astelli perfumer who speaks directly is either very angry or has given up on you entirely.

> *"Their rose distillate has remarkable depth this year. One wonders what the spring conditions were on their upper terraces. Quite surprising, given the late snowmelt from the Tsapadon col."*

> *"House Revanel's lavender is very consistent. Remarkably so, for a family whose calendar records only go back four generations."*

## The Dyer Houses

The Dyer Houses are what the Perfumer Houses refuse to acknowledge they depend on: the volume production, the color trade, the relationship with the silk merchants who have made Florentia's name known across the inner Akhan.

**The stained hands:** Dye work stains permanently. No amount of washing removes the color from the first knuckle creases and nail beds. Dyer hands are visible evidence of the work — a mark the perfumers find uncouth and the dyers wear as a straightforward record of what they actually do. The perfumers' clean hands are also a record. Everyone knows what both records say.

**The direct speech:** The Dyer Houses conduct their rivalry in plain language. Whose blue is deeper. Whose mordant holds longer. Whose silk the merchants will pay more for. The theological framing of the work is, in the dyer view, decoration — useful for pricing to the court trade, irrelevant to anyone who has spent a day at the vats.

**The downstream awareness:** The dyers sell barrels. Concentrated dye extract goes downhill — east to the Mazarvan's silk workshops on the humid coastal descent, west to the Yeorvan delta's cotton producers in the desert. Nobody carts raw fiber up a mountain pass to dye it and cart it back down. The geography settled that question before anyone asked it.

Florentia sits at the top of both supply chains simultaneously. The same barrel serves the empire's two premium textile industries on opposite slopes of the same mountain. The Mazarvan silk houses and the Yeorvan cotton operations are each aware they depend on Florentia pigment. Neither is fully aware of how central that barrel is to the other slope's operation. The dyers are aware of both and find this entirely logical. The mountain produces. The barrels roll. Downhill in both directions is still downhill.

"Florentia blue" consequently exists everywhere in the empire except Florentia — applied at workshops the dyers have never visited, by craftspeople who have built entire professional identities around working with Florentia pigments, sold under a name that references this valley. The dyers sell the barrel. Someone else has the dye experience. Someone else sells the cloth.

The ongoing question of whether the County should capture more downstream value collapses every generation against the same logistics: the fiber is downhill. The dye is uphill. That is the arrangement. The dyers find it clarifying to say this plainly. The perfumers find it clarifying that the dyers have reduced a theological question to a barrel rolling in two directions.

**The relationship with everyone else:** The Dyer Houses talk to the Quedari miners and the Kern vintners and the Rim Barons directly, without protocol. Practical people dealing with practical people. They find the valley's working economy more interesting than the succession crisis and will tell you so without being asked.

## Species Mix

**Humans** — majority population in both cultures. The backbone of terrace work, vat operation, distillation, and trade in both Houses.

**Felari** — concentrated in the Perfumer Houses. Their instinct for refined presentation aligns with the performance the perfumer culture requires. The court-facing sales relationships, the elaborate reception of buyer delegations, the public face of the Houses that requires someone who understands how status becomes visible — Felari do this work with the same fluency they bring everywhere.

**Tzvari** — present in both cultures and quietly fought over by both. Their ecological reading — the composite biological sensitivity that tells them what a plant needs before any visible indicator appears — is invaluable in a county where the argument is literally about who correctly reads what the mountain-ecology is producing. A Tzvari's assessment of aromatic yield potential before the harvest could settle inter-family disputes. Neither the Perfumer nor Dyer Houses will formally acknowledge they consult Tzvari assessments. The Tzvari are aware of this. Their findings are rarely solicited. Their findings are correct.

**Corvani** — pattern analysis in the aromatic and dye records. The scholars who notice that the eastern terrace family's oil has been subtly degrading for eleven years in ways that correlate with a specific change in their upper-slope drainage, which correlates with a construction project they undertook a decade ago. That finding, filed in the appropriate record, is a weapon. The family with the Corvani archivist has a significant advantage over the family without one.

**Halflings** — distribution and export logistics. The aromatic oils and dye materials leaving Florentia reach buyers who couldn't find the valley. Halfling networks are why that's possible.

**Kitsune** — the commercial intelligence layer between Florentia and the inner Akhan buyers. Who is paying what for which color. Where the silk merchants are expanding. What the succession claimants' courts are spending on presentation goods. This information flows through the Kitsune networks; Florentia's families pay for access in ways that aren't formally acknowledged.

## Political Position

Florentia's succession leverage is modest compared to the coronation commission at Mak'Zavar or Kern's soft power, but real: the presentation goods that succession claimants use to signal competence and aesthetic judgment come through here. The perfume a claimant gives as a diplomatic gift. The specific dye color that appears in a claimant's court dress. These choices are legible to everyone who matters, and Florentia is where they originate.

The Perfumer Houses receive claimant delegations with elaborate warmth and tell them essentially nothing actionable. The Dyer Houses receive claimant delegations, quote them prices, and note that the blue the previous delegation also wanted is currently allocated but could be made available with appropriate advance arrangement.

Both approaches are effective. Neither constitutes a commitment.

The families watch the ducal vacancy question with more attention than they publicly acknowledge. A new Duke of Andalvar will have views about whether Florentia should be exporting raw pigment or finished silk, and those views will matter.

## Story Entry Points

**The record dispute** — a Perfumer House challenges another's claim that a specific cultivation technique is original to their calendar records. The evidence is a generation-old document. The Corvani archivist who authenticated it has recently found something in the border annotations that changes the reading. What they do with that finding.

**The Tzvari assessment** — both a Perfumer and a Dyer House want the same Tzvari's reading of their competing terraces before the harvest. The Tzvari has given both assessments. They are accurate. They are not what either family wanted to hear. What happens when both families compare notes and realize the Tzvari told them both the truth.

**The exclusive barrel contract** — a major inner Akhan dye house has approached the Dyer quarter directly with a proposal: a long-term exclusive purchasing arrangement that would guarantee volume and price stability, and incidentally make that dye house the sole supplier of Florentia pigment to the empire's luxury textile market. The Dyer Houses are divided — guaranteed income versus handing someone else permanent control of the barrel's destination. The Perfumer Houses have not been consulted and would like to register that this is a problem.

**The delegation gift** — a succession claimant's court has commissioned a specific fragrance from a specific Perfumer House. The commission details, if understood correctly, describe exactly which existing formulation the claimant wants replicated — a formulation that belongs to a different House. Either the delegation doesn't know that, or they know exactly that.
