## Overview

Normont occupies the northern shore of the Storm Gate and wraps east around the outer atoll headland — the territory that shares Tal Aegis's theological position of standing in the path. The imperial capital sits at the headland's lee side; Normont is the headland itself and everything behind it. It is prestigious and genuinely difficult to hold. The duchy absorbs the same storm exposure the emperor consecrates as divine duty, without the theological consolation.

Two faces, radically different character:

**The Storm Gate face** — northern mouth of the strait, adjacent to Tal Aegis, surge-exposed harbor country. This is where the duchy's court relevance lives. The Duke attends everything, is present for every succession signal, hears what the Regents say in the corridor after the formal audience ends. Proximity to the capital is the duchy's primary political asset and the reason anyone covets it.

**The outer atoll face** — wrapping around the headland onto the outer ocean-facing coast. Fjord country, Icemark-adjacent, whaling territory. The exposed side where the Eye hits first and the Mishmarine's outer patrol circuit runs closest to the mainland. The revenue is real — transit fees, provisioning contracts, storm salvage rights. The exposure is also real.

## Political Position

Normont's value to the succession crisis is straightforward: it is the closest ducal seat to the imperial court that is not a Regency. The Duke is present at court as a matter of routine, not exception. Every claimant wants either to hold Normont's alliance or ensure someone else doesn't hold it against them.

The current Duke holds the title on the strength of a credit arrangement established three generations back — a struggling ducal house with a valid succession claim and insufficient capital to prosecute it, rescued by commercial investment from the Archavar luxury trade networks. The arrangement appeared to be standard merchant patronage. It was not.

**The Duke** — Human. Believes his house's revival was a fortunate commercial relationship with an Andalvar gem distribution network. This belief is sincere and has never been tested against the alternative explanation. He is not stupid — he is operating on incomplete information that has been carefully managed to remain incomplete. His succession claim is real. His court relationships are real. His independence of action is more constrained than he understands.

The ducal house takes its assigned imperial house designation with complete seriousness. The house name, the bloodline, the long court history — these are genuine to the Duke in a way that would surprise the person who controls them.

## Strategic Position

Normont sits at the intersection of two crises simultaneously:

**The succession crisis** — Storm Gate-adjacent, capital-adjacent, the Duke present in every court conversation. Any intelligence about which way the succession is moving passes through or past Normont's social territory.

**The Eye of Tsapadon** — the outer face of the duchy will take the surge before anything else on the Grenmarch mainland. The breakwater islands are visible from Normont's headland on a clear day. The engineer-priests' requests for maintenance budget have crossed the ducal administration's desk. The Duke has forwarded them appropriately. Whether they were received appropriately is a separate question.

**The Mishmarine adjacency** — the outer patrol circuit runs past Normont's outer coast. Mishmarine vessels provision at Normont's outer harbor installations. The relationship is administrative and cordial. It is also an information channel that flows in both directions.

## Story Entry Points

**The Duke who doesn't know** — a man who believes his story completely, operating with real competence and real court access, in a position whose actual dynamics he has never been shown. What happens when something forces the question.

**The outer harbor** — Mishmarine provisioning stops, outer ocean debris, the things that wash up on the headland's exposed face during surge season. The duchy's staff have been logging anomalies in the outer ocean behavioral record for years. Nobody has connected the logs to anything larger. Yet.

**The credit line** — the original arrangement that gave the Architects control. Somewhere in the ducal house's financial records, or the Archavar distribution network's records, the terms of the transaction exist. What those terms actually say versus what the Duke was told they say is a story.
