# Duchy of Marzavan

**Category:** Organizations & Institutions — Mainland Duchy  
**Ruler:** Duke TBD  
**Related:** [Geography Overview](../geography/overview.md), [Duchy of Andalvar](duchy-andalvar.md), [The Duchies](duchies.md)

## Overview

The Duchy of Marzavan occupies the Archavar's eastern descent — the steep, forested, terraced slope from alpine heights to the inner Akhan shore. Cross the high Andalvar passes and the humidity hits you before the view does: the Mazarvan is a different world from everything to the west, lush and layered where the desert face is stark and open.

Marzavan produces the empire's silk. Not all textile fiber — the Yeorvan delta grows cotton, Grenmarch's cool north grows flax — but silk, the premium fiber, the thing court dress is made from and diplomatic gifts are wrapped in. The duchy controls the full supply chain: mulberry groves on the upper slopes, weaving villages in the terraced mid-section, finishing and export at the coastal harbor. What leaves Marzavan's harbor dressed in Florentia color is the most recognizable luxury good in the inner Akhan.

Four Counties share the slope — and the streams:

- **County of Serev** — upper slopes, mulberry cultivation, sericulture, raw silk production
- **County of Kazhal** — mid-slopes terraced weaving villages, thread to cloth
- **County of Marvat** — the coastal harbor, finishing, where Florentia's dye barrels arrive and finished goods ship out
- **County of Azavar** — lower slope foundries and metalworks, where Rim Barony ore arrives and becomes finished metal; Azaron's territory on the mountain's eastern face

## The Slope

The Mazarvan is vertical. The altitude drop from the high Andalvar passes to the inner Akhan shore happens fast — the Archavar doesn't descend gradually but in a series of dramatic steps, each with its own climate band, each terraced by generations of farmers who needed level ground and made it where none existed.

**The upper forest** — dense, humid, the original slope before cultivation pushed in from below. The silkworm's mulberry tree grows at the forest's cultivated edge, where generations of careful management have pushed the treeline back and held it. The upper forest is not empty: timber operations, hunting, the watershed that feeds the terracing below. The forest has always been here and will be here after the duchy is gone, and the people who work its edge know this.

**The terraced mid-slopes** — the duchy's visual signature. Centuries of hand-cut terracing across the full width of the usable slope. From the inner Akhan on a clear day the terraces are visible as horizontal lines across the mountain face — the mark of human patience applied to impossible terrain. Each terrace is a negotiation between the slope's natural grade and what cultivation requires. The oldest terraces are engineering achievements nobody currently alive could replicate.

**The coastal base** — where the slope finally meets the Akhan shore. Small harbor, fishing villages, the infrastructure that connects the duchy's landlocked silk production to the inner sea trade network. The harbor is not large by imperial standards — Marzavan's production goes to the inner Akhan in volume but not in bulk; silk is valuable by weight, not by the barge-load.

## County of Serev

*Upper slopes, mulberry groves, silkworm cultivation.*

The least glamorous part of the supply chain and the most essential. Mulberry cultivation at altitude requires specific knowledge — the trees need warmth the upper slope barely provides, cold tolerance the lower slopes don't test them for, and precise timing that the Archavar's variable spring conditions make complicated every year.

The sericulture families of Serev have been making these calculations for six generations. They have opinions about the correct way to raise silkworms. They have opinions about which mulberry varieties produce the better silk. They have opinions, delivered at length, about the weaving families in Kazhal who treat raw silk as a given rather than an achievement.

The production numbers are never quite predictable — a late frost, an unexpected warm spell, a disease moving through the mulberry groves — and the downstream impact of a bad Serev season is felt in Kazhal within weeks and in Marvat's harbor orders within months. The whole chain holds its breath in early spring and exhales when Serev's families announce the season's quality estimate.

## County of Kazhal

*Mid-slope terraced weaving villages, thread to finished cloth.*

The craft heart of the duchy. The weavers of Kazhal turn raw silk thread into fabric — a process that requires both technical precision and aesthetic judgment, and around which an elaborate craft culture has developed over centuries.

Kazhal is where the silk acquires its character before it acquires its color. Weave structure, thread weight, surface finish — decisions made here determine what the Florentia dye does when it arrives downstream. A Kazhal weaver who has worked with Florentia pigments for twenty years knows exactly how each dye formula interacts with each weave structure. That knowledge is the duchy's competitive advantage and is not written down.

The relationship between Kazhal weavers and Florentia dyers is professional respect with significant mutual suspicion. The weavers believe the dyers take credit for color that the weave structure makes possible. The dyers believe the weavers take credit for texture that the pigment brings to life. Both are partially correct. Neither acknowledges this in correspondence.

## County of Marvat

*The coastal harbor, finishing operations, export.*

Where the silk chain meets the sea. Raw finished cloth arrives from Kazhal; Florentia's dye barrels arrive by mountain road; the finishing operations that combine them happen in Marvat's workshops before the bolts go onto the ships.

The harbor is the duchy's interface with the empire. Everything that isn't Marzavan silk — buyers, delegations, political pressure, succession claimant representatives, the occasional imperial inspector — arrives at Marvat first. The harbor master has developed a specific professional skill: managing the volume of political attention that flows toward a duchy that makes what every court wants without committing to who gets it.

**The Florentia relationship:** Marvat is where the barrel arrives. The dye comes down the western slope to the valley, crosses the Andalvar, comes down the eastern slope to the harbor. The logistics of moving concentrated dye extract across a mountain range are not trivial and are managed by a specialist carrier network that Marvat's harbor has developed and Florentia does not control. This is a leverage point neither side has formally acknowledged.

## County of Azavar

*Lower slope foundries, ore processing, metalwork — Azaron's face on the eastern mountain.*

The ore comes down from the Rim Baronies above Andalvar — Marak iron, Gadar pass metals, whatever the mountain's upper reaches surrender — and arrives in Azavar where timber, water, and harbor access make the processing practical. Foundries, forges, smelters, tool production, ship fittings, weapon blanks. The county produces the hard goods the empire's military and maritime operations require, at a location that is simultaneously convenient for supply (ore downhill) and export (harbor adjacent).

The theological framing is natural: Azaron governs fire, transformation, and what destruction makes possible. The foundry is Azaron's work. The ore goes in and something useful comes out. Azavar's forge-workers are not particularly religious, but they've accepted the theological framing because it has made their work harder to restrict.

**The conflict with silk:** The foundries share the Mazarvan's streams with the silk industry, and they use those streams differently. Smelting runoff, quench water, the chemical byproduct of ore processing — all of it enters the watershed above Marvat's finishing operations and occasionally above Kazhal's weaving terraces. Every significant Azavar expansion upslope has produced a formal silk-industry complaint. Every formal complaint has produced a negotiated stream management agreement. Every stream management agreement has been tested by the next expansion.

Neither county is the intruder. Both have been on this slope for generations. The Duke manages the conflict as a permanent condition of the duchy rather than a problem to solve. He has not found a resolution. He has found a succession of compromises that hold until they don't.

**What the manufacturing produces:** Finished metal goods — tools, agricultural implements, ship hardware, weapon blanks for finishing elsewhere, structural components for harbor and breakwater construction. Not fine metalwork — that's Mak'Zavar's territory and Andalvar's Dwarves. Azavar makes the unglamorous metal the empire runs on: the bolt, the hinge, the agricultural plough, the ship's anchor. High volume, consistent quality, competitive price, no pretension about it.

**The Rim Barony relationship:** Azavar is Marak's primary customer. The iron that leaves Marak's mines ends up in Azavar's foundries. The relationship is direct and commercial — Baron Thak sends a price list; Azavar's Count responds with an order. The arrangement has been in place longer than either current title-holder has been alive.

## The Water Problem

The Mazarvan's streams are the duchy's central resource and central conflict. They come down from the Archavar snowmelt and springs, cold and clean at the top, carrying what the slope puts into them by the time they reach the harbor.

What silk puts into the streams: wastewater from the dyeing and finishing operations, mild and largely organic. Downstream impact: manageable with reasonable stream spacing between operations.

What the foundries put into the streams: quench water, slag runoff, the chemical residue of ore processing. Downstream impact: significant, particularly on the silkworm operations that require clean water for cocoon processing and on the mulberry trees whose root systems absorb what the soil absorbs.

The current arrangement positions Azavar's foundries on streams that have already passed the Serev and Kazhal operations — a geographic compromise that the silk counties negotiated two Dukes ago and the foundry interests have been attempting to renegotiate ever since. The compromise holds. The pressure on it is constant. The Duke's attention to stream management agreements is not optional.

## Species Mix

**Humans** — majority across all three counties. The backbone of mulberry cultivation, silkworm tending, weaving, and harbor operation.

**Geliss** — cross-cultural trade liaison. The interface between Marzavan's production and the inner Akhan's buyers requires someone who speaks both registers fluently. Geliss who specialize in textile commerce have found Marvat the richest environment available — every cultural tradition that buys silk passes through this harbor.

**Otter-folk** — harbor culture and facilitation. The coastal base has the warmth and social density Otter-folk prefer. In Marvat they run the inter-ship transactions, the harbormaster's informal network, and the social infrastructure that makes a working harbor feel like a community.

**Halflings** — logistics across the full chain. Moving raw silk from Serev to Kazhal to Marvat, coordinating with the Florentia barrel carriers, managing the export shipping schedule. The silk chain works because someone tracks all of it simultaneously.

**Kitsune** — commercial intelligence and buyer networks. Who is buying what for which court. Which claimant's representatives are placing unusual orders. What the succession crisis is doing to luxury goods demand across the inner Akhan. This information has value, and Kitsune in Marvat's harbor are among the best-positioned commercial intelligence assets in the empire.

**Iridari** — harbor information broadcast. The emotional temperature of trade negotiations, the news that arrives with each ship, the pulse of what the inner Akhan is thinking this season. Marvat's Iridari are the living news network for a harbor that needs to know what's coming before it arrives.

**Dwarves** — concentrated in Azavar. The foundry and metalwork tradition in Azavar is predominantly Dwarf-run at the technical level — the forge knowledge, the alloy expertise, the structural understanding that turns raw ore into useful metal. Azavar Dwarves are distinct from Andalvar Dwarves above: less interested in the sacred-material framing of Mak'Zavar's work, more interested in production volume and material consistency. They find the silk industry's complaints about stream management professionally irritating and legally valid, which is a combination they navigate with varying grace.

**Homo Bovinus** — structural and quality assessment in the foundry operations. Their tremorsense applied to metal stress — detecting flaws in cast and forged components before failure — has become standard practice in Azavar's higher-specification production. Ship hardware and structural components leaving Azavar for breakwater construction get Homo Bovinus sign-off. The engineer-priests have noticed this practice and approved it.

## Political Position

Marzavan's leverage in the succession crisis is quiet and pervasive: every claimant's court presentation depends on Marzavan silk. The robes. The banners. The diplomatic gifts. The coronation regalia that Mak'Zavar makes will be set in cloth that comes from here.

The Duke does not threaten to interrupt the supply. The supply is continuous and will remain so. The Duke simply notes, in the warm and hospitable atmosphere of Marvat's harbor, that the duchy looks forward to continued good relations with the empire's future administration, whoever that turns out to be. The orders are filled at current prices. Delivery is as scheduled.

Every claimant understands what is and is not being said. None has found a way to change the conversation.

**The Florentia dependency:** Marzavan silk at full value requires Florentia color. Without the dye, the cloth is still excellent silk — but it is silk that commands inner Akhan prices, not imperial court prices. The distinction is significant. The Duke is aware that his duchy's premium pricing depends on a relationship with a county across a mountain range that he does not administer. He maintains that relationship with considerable care and does not discuss it in political correspondence.

**The Andalvar connection:** Marzavan and Andalvar share a mountain. The Duchy of Andalvar's ducal vacancy is therefore also Marzavan's problem — a Duke of Andalvar with strong views about the Florentia dye trade could reshape the eastern slope relationship in ways the current council administration cannot. Marzavan has opinions about who should fill the Andalvar vacancy. Those opinions have been transmitted informally. They have not been received as the Duke intended.

## Story Entry Points

**The bad Serev season** — a late frost has damaged the mulberry crop. The season's silk output will be thirty percent below projections. Every step downstream is adjusting. A character managing one of those adjustments — in Kazhal, in Marvat, in the inner Akhan buyer network — at the moment the full impact becomes clear.

**The carrier network** — the specialist operation that moves Florentia dye barrels across the Archavar to Marvat is not formally part of either duchy. It operates in the gap. A character who runs part of that network, who knows both sides of the mountain and neither side's full picture.

**The court order** — a succession claimant has placed an unusually large order through Marvat. The specifications are precise. The quantity implies something about the claimant's timeline that the harbor master finds interesting. What they do with that inference.

**The stream agreement breaks** — an Azavar foundry expansion has put a new quench operation on a stream the silk counties believed was protected under the current agreement. The legal question of whether the agreement covers this specific stream is genuinely ambiguous. The practical question of what happens to the Kazhal weaving operations downstream if the runoff continues is not ambiguous at all. The Duke is managing both questions simultaneously and satisfying no one.

**The Kazhal knowledge** — a senior Kazhal weaver is aging and has not taken an apprentice. The specific knowledge of how Florentia dyes interact with Kazhal weave structures lives in one person. Multiple parties are aware of this. The weaver is also aware that multiple parties are aware of this, and has decided opinions about which party deserves the knowledge. Those opinions do not map cleanly to political alignment.
