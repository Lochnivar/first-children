# Client Kingdoms of Bahvayin

**Category:** Organizations & Institutions  
**Related:** [Political Hierarchy](political-hierarchy.md), [Island Lords](island-lords.md), [Empire Decline](../history-events/empire-decline.md)

## Overview

Five client kingdoms occupy the large islands of the Akhan Sea. Each negotiated client status with the empire from a position of strength — too large, too strategically significant, or too capable to be taken by force. Their crowns predate the empire. Their legitimacy is internal, not imperial. Imperial recognition is pro forma courtesy that became customary, not the foundation of their authority.

Each kingdom has its own succession standards, its own internal legitimacy, and its own reading of what the current succession crisis means for them. None has declared for a claimant. All are watching.

## The Five Kingdoms

### Stormbreak
**Position:** Northeastern inner Akhan — massive reef structure just outside the Storm Gate mouth  
**Character:** Storm culture, calibrated fearlessness, salvage economy, outer ocean awareness  
**The reef:** Acts as a natural wave break that mitigates ordinary hurricane surge. Only Cat 6+ systems and the Eye require full hunkering. The breakwater islands manage the surge inside the channel; Stormbreak manages it outside. The two systems work together as a layered defense the engineer-priests understand completely.  
**Salvage economy:** Three categories of reef finds — pre-Death mageocracy material, unidentifiable outer ocean origin, and most recently objects that are clearly worked and recent but match no known Atoll cultural framework. A Chelonari archivist at the Storm Gate has a private file on the third category that she has not yet shared with the Senior Council.  
**Species mix:** Dwarves (reef engineering), Kibo (general population backbone), Aubrei (outer reef navigation), Corvani (storm pattern analysis), Humans (majority), Bandari (salvage informal economy)  
**Political position:** Most vocal of the client kingdoms about the engineer-priests' Eye warnings. They live on the outer reef. They can read the water themselves. The King's assessment of the current situation is the most practically grounded of any succession-adjacent ruler.  
**Succession relevance:** Controls the outer Storm Gate defense system. Whoever the engineer-priests align with needs Stormbreak's cooperation. The King knows this and is not rushing.

### A'Kavah
**Position:** Southwestern inner Akhan — caldera island, collapsed volcanic crater  
**Name:** *A'Kavah* — First Speech: place of the warmth  
**Character:** Agricultural heartland, patient leverage, volcanic abundance  
**The caldera:** A long-extinct supervolcano whose crater provides extraordinary soil fertility, natural interior irrigation, and residual thermal activity that extends growing seasons. Collapsed rim sections created the island's natural deep-water harbors. The caldera floor is dense agricultural terracing. The rim is the high ground — defensive, commanding views of the Akhan.  
**Warm waters above, volcanic warmth below:** Nurathi as patron deity is uniquely present here in both her aspects — the Southern Way current above and the geological heat below. The theological tradition is more complex than any other Nurathi cult on Bahvayin.  
**Species mix:** Chelonari (landowning class, multigenerational estates — think in centuries, hold land accordingly), Humans (agricultural workforce and merchant class), Halflings (logistics and distribution networks), Felari (court culture, refined surface)  
**Political position:** Feeding all succession claimants simultaneously. Deliberately. Not neutrality — maximum leverage. The moment A'Kavah declares it feeds one claimant and creates food security problems for the others. The King is in no hurry to make that calculation.  
**The Halfling variable:** The King controls production. The Halfling logistics networks control distribution. That distinction has not mattered in stable times. It is beginning to matter.  
**Succession relevance:** Controls the empire's food security margin. The most powerful material leverage in the crisis. The least militarily aggressive of the five kingdoms.

### San Tavar
**Position:** Central inner Akhan — crossroads island  
**Character:** Surfer realpolitik, meritocratic by pragmatism, cosmopolitan, commercially sophisticated, teeth nobody has tested  
**The culture:** California-watching-and-waiting energy. Reads the wave before committing. Relaxed on the surface, calculating underneath, comfortable with both being simultaneously true. Genuine warmth that is also strategic — more unsettling than pure cynicism. Surfing is real and culturally embedded — the same skill as everything else here: reading conditions, knowing when to commit, knowing when to pull back.  
**Meritocracy:** Not ideological — pragmatic. Artificial constraints on capability are inefficient. The best harbor pilot is the harbor pilot. Women, non-human species, mixed heritage individuals in positions the rest of the Akhan reserves for specific bloodlines. Not announced as progressive achievement. Simply how things work when you optimize for results.  
**Current ruler:** A Queen. Achieved position through San Tavar's own succession framework rather than bloodline alone. The most dangerous political mind in the succession crisis. Has been watching and waiting for fifteen years. Every claimant thinks they have a special relationship with her. They all have exactly the same relationship.  
**The teeth:** Intelligence capability, economic route leverage, and a fleet that has never been seriously tested because nobody has wanted to find out what happens if they push it. The uncertainty is the deterrent. The last admiral who tried to estimate San Tavar's actual naval strength produced a report with wide error bars and a recommendation not to test the upper bound.  
**San Tavar saying:** *The ocean is always patient and always wins.*  
**Species mix:** Kitsune (information brokers, commercial intelligence), Otter-folk (facilitation, social surface), Geliss (cross-context liaison), Gnolls (port culture warmth and atmosphere), Halflings (logistics backbone), Humans (majority)  
**Succession relevance:** Controls information flow and commercial intelligence. The declaration from San Tavar won't end the crisis — it will signal which wave is worth riding. Every claimant is trying not to antagonize the Queen while simultaneously trying to become the wave she commits to.

### Tzafan
**Position:** Northwestern inner Akhan — gateway to the northern rim  
**Name:** *Tzafan* — worn down from *Tzafon* (north, the hidden direction)  
**Character:** Frontier capable, outward looking, Gateway to the Unexplored North  
**The frontier:** Last significant civilization before the northern rim gets genuinely wild. Wealth comes from being the staging point for everything that goes north and the reception point for everything that comes back. What comes back isn't always what went in.  
**Cultural character:** Not rough in the sense of uncouth. Rough in the sense of capable and unsentimental. These are people who outfit expeditions, assess risk professionally, and understand that the north doesn't care about your bloodline. The imperial court finds them slightly alarming — too comfortable with uncertainty, too willing to go find out, not sufficiently invested in the social architecture everyone else is fighting over.  
**The northern situation:** Something is consolidating on the northern rim that Tzafan's scouts have been reporting for two years. Nobody further south has taken it seriously. The King is developing opinions about the succession crisis that center primarily on whether any claimant has a coherent northern policy. So far: no.  
**Species mix:** Bruin (frontier rangers, culturally integrated at the staging point), Kibo (expedition muscle, professional risk assessment), Llamari (logistics in difficult terrain), Bandari (frontier trade, what comes back from the north), Iridari (expedition news broadcast layer), Humans (majority, frontier-adapted)  
**Succession relevance:** Controls the northern approach and the staging infrastructure for anything that goes north or comes south. The claimant who wins Tzafan's declaration gets the northern rim's resources and the only organized intelligence about what's happening up there. The King will declare for whoever has the most credible northern policy. Currently nobody qualifies.  
**The reliable vote:** Tzafan is the one king Vaelor can count on — not out of loyalty, but out of frontier pragmatism. The succession crisis is inner Akhan court politics. Tzafan doesn't care who wins. They care that the transshipment routes stay open and the northern staging infrastructure stays funded. The current King's position is essentially: *let the Emperor have what he wants, just leave us alone to run our operation.* This makes Tzafan the most predictable ratification vote — he will ratify whoever has a credible northern policy and stop caring about the rest.  
**The target on his back:** Tzafan's pragmatic imperial alignment makes the dissolution provision mathematically impossible while the current King reigns — unanimity requires Tzafan, and Tzafan won't vote dissolution. Every player running the dissolution calculation knows this. A new Tzafan king, more court-oriented, more susceptible to claimant pressure, more reconciliatory toward the other four kingdoms, changes both the dissolution math and the ratification dynamics simultaneously. The current King is unsentimental about frontier threats and professionally aware he has a target on his back. Whether the northern consolidation his scouts have been reporting is a genuine frontier emergence or a manufactured distraction designed to keep his attention pointed north while something is arranged closer to home is a question he has begun asking privately.

### Kol Idic
**Position:** Southern mid-Akhan — between San Tavar and the fog belt  
**Name:** *Kol Idic* — *kol* (all, everything) + *idic* (infinite diversity, infinite combinations). The name predates the current culture's full understanding of what they're sitting on. A scholar who digs deep enough into the mageocracy archives will find the original naming document.  
**Character:** Mageocracy ruins custodians, knowledge as weapon, infinite diversity as operating principle  
**The ruins:** The most intact and extensive mageocracy ruins on Bahvayin sit on and under this island. When the Death of Magic hit, the structures survived. The records survived. The pre-Death artifacts survived in concentrations that exist nowhere else. The island's culture has been sitting on this for generations — not by design, simply because they were already there.  
**Artifact acquisition:** Kol Idic maintains a principled, consistent, and entirely self-serving position on artifact stewardship. Pre-Death artifacts in improper hands represent an incalculable loss to civilization's accumulated knowledge. Kol Idic, as the foremost institution of mageocracy scholarship, has both the expertise and the moral obligation to ensure proper stewardship. The offers are always fair. Suspiciously fair. Previous owners receive certificates of thanks for their contribution to Bahvayin's cultural heritage.  
**The Stormbreak arrangement:** The most thoroughly documented commercial relationship on the Akhan, renegotiated fourteen times, each iteration slightly better for Stormbreak. The third category of anomalous reef finds — outer landmass objects — has not been disclosed to Kol Idic. Stormbreak's King is aware of what disclosure would mean for the negotiation dynamic. She is in no hurry.  
**Residual ability:** The concentration of pre-Death enchantment on the island produces higher than average incidence of residual magical ability in the population. The empire finds this alarming. Kol Idic finds the empire's alarm useful as a deterrent.  
**The succession files:** Kol Idic has been acquiring artifacts with succession-relevant historical documentation for eight years. Purely for stewardship purposes. The collection includes original copies of three autonomy grant arrangements, one treaty port agreement whose implications have never been fully litigated, and the personal correspondence of the emperor who made the first major autonomy concession. Available to scholars with appropriate credentials. The credentialing process takes approximately four months.  
**Species mix:** Humans (majority, scholarly tradition), Chelonari (archivists, institutional memory), Corvani (pattern analysis, the scholars who find things in records nobody else noticed), Hobgoblins (administrative and cataloguing infrastructure), Kitsune (acquisition network), Geliss (cross-cultural research, field scholarship)  
**Succession relevance:** Controls knowledge — historical, legal, and artifact. The King knows things about every succession claim that the claimants don't know about themselves. Has not used that knowledge yet. Is in no hurry. The knowledge is the weapon and it only fires once.

## Collective Political Position

Five client kingdoms. Five different readings of the succession crisis. Five different leverage positions. None has declared.

The succession claimants have been working the five kingdoms for two years. The results:

- **Stormbreak** — courteous, practical, focused on the Eye and the breakwaters
- **A'Kavah** — gracious, feeding everyone, scheduling nothing
- **San Tavar** — warm, completely non-committal in ways that take three days to fully realize were non-committal
- **Tzafan** — direct, asking about northern policy, receiving inadequate answers
- **Kol Idic** — scholarly, helpful, suggesting the credentialing process for relevant documentation access

The claimant who cracks the five kingdoms first — who gives one of them a compelling enough reason to declare — changes the shape of the crisis. The other four will be watching which wave breaks first.

**The ratification mechanism:** Imperial succession law requires three of five client kings to ratify any named heir. Constitutional concession from a weaker reign — in stable times a formality, currently the structural heart of the crisis. The minimum winning coalition is three. Every claimant's campaign is a race to three kings, and San Tavar is almost certainly the deciding third vote for any claimant who assembles the first two.

**The dissolution provision:** A separate constitutional mechanism for a childless emperor with no confirmed heir. Unanimous vote of all five kings formally dissolves the empire — each kingdom returns to full independence, clean and legal.

**The treason clause:** A dissolution vote that fails is treason for every king who voted in favor. The defecting king who voted against backs a claimant by default and inherits the traitors' kingdoms as the prize. Failed dissolution doesn't mean the empire continues unchanged — it means the kings who voted for it lose everything.

**The Prisoner's Dilemma:** Each king runs the same calculation. All five coordinating on dissolution wins for everyone. One defector makes four others traitors and wins catastrophically. The coordination requires absolute mutual trust the five kings do not have — and the conversation required to build it is itself documentable treason if anyone defects afterward. San Tavar's Queen is the only player with enough intelligence about all five calculations to assess whether unanimity is achievable. That assessment is the most valuable piece of information in the empire. What she plans to do with it is not known.
