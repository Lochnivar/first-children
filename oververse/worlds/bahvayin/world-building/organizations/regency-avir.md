# The Regency of Avir

**Working name:** Grimport  
**Ruler:** Regent TBD  
**Related:** [Political Hierarchy](political-hierarchy.md), [Client Kingdoms — Kol Idic](client-kingdoms.md#kol-idic), [Southern Way Culture](../cultures/southern-way-culture.md), [Client Kingdoms — A'Kavah](client-kingdoms.md#akavah)

## Overview

The Regency of Avir sits in the southern inner Akhan, fog belt adjacent, between Kol Idic and the Southern Way mouth. On imperial documents it is Avir — *avir*, air and atmosphere, named by someone who visited once in good weather and left before they understood what they were looking at. On charts, in logs, in the mouths of every sailor who has ever made the approach by sound: Grimport.

The fog does not lift. Not fully. Not permanently. There are days when it thins enough to see across the harbor and residents remark on it. There are nights when the visibility drops to arm's length and the foghorns run continuous and the fishing fleet navigates entirely by sound and memory. Both conditions are normal. The duchy has built its entire operational culture around the fog's permanence rather than waiting for it to clear.

The foghorn runs all day and all night. It has run continuously for as long as anyone has records. It is the heartbeat of Grimport — functional navigation infrastructure that became cultural identity that became something closer to a living thing. Visitors complain for three days. Then they stop noticing. Former residents who have moved to the inner Akhan islands wake up at 3am in quiet harbors and stare at the ceiling and cannot explain why they feel wrong.

If the foghorn stops, something is wrong. Not storm-wrong. Something else.

## The Fishing Fleet

Grimport is the empire's largest fishing operation. The fog belt waters are extraordinarily productive — the same cold upwelling that generates the fog drives nutrient-rich deep water to the surface, creating fisheries that have been feeding the inner Akhan for two centuries. The duchy doesn't grow anything. It pulls protein from water that nobody else has the navigation culture to work effectively.

**The fleet's scale:** Not one fleet — a permanent rotating cycle of vessels in different phases of their operation. Some out in the deep fog belt. Some returning with catch. Some unloading at the docks. Some being provisioned for the next run. Some being repaired. The harbor is never still. The foghorns that guide vessels in and out run on a schedule the harbor masters maintain like a liturgy.

**Navigation by sound:** Grimport's fishing fleet operates in conditions that would strand most inner Akhan sailors. The navigational toolkit is acoustic — the foghorns, the bell buoys marking hazards and channels, the specific calls that fleet vessels use to communicate position in zero visibility. A Grimport fisher can locate themselves in the fog by sound alone. This is not instinct — it is trained capability, passed from experienced crew to new crew over years of fog work. Outsiders who try to crew Grimport vessels without this training are a navigational liability.

**The Aubrei integration:** Fog belt Aubrei are embedded in the fishing fleet at every level — not as a separate navigation caste but as crew who grew up in the fog and stayed. The distinction between "Grimport Aubrei" and "Grimport Human" is less meaningful than the distinction between "experienced fog crew" and "everyone else." The fleet's accumulated knowledge of where the fish are, where the dangerous currents run, and which fog banks will kill you lives in the crew culture, not in any chart.

**Protein security:** The inner Akhan eats Grimport fish. Not all of it — A'Kavah's agricultural output feeds the bulk of the population — but the fishing catch is the empire's primary non-agricultural protein source and a substantial portion of it comes through Grimport. This is not leverage the duchy advertises. It is leverage that exists regardless.

## Gnoll Culture

Grimport is Gnoll-dense in a way that has produced a distinct cultural variant — not Gnolls who happen to be in Grimport, but Grimport Gnolls, a thing unto themselves.

The fog, the noise, the permanent low-visibility conditions, the close quarters of fishing vessels on long runs, the communal dependency that fog work requires — all of this selects for Gnoll cultural strengths and has amplified them into something that looks faintly unhinged from outside. The pack cohesion that makes Gnolls effective on long patrols makes them effective on fog fishing runs for the same reasons. The noise tolerance that other species find remarkable in Gnolls is not merely tolerated in Grimport — it is considered correct. The foghorn is always running. The harbor is always loud. Anyone who needs quiet has come to the wrong city.

**"We're all a little mad here"** is not a complaint — it is a statement of identity delivered with complete equanimity. Grimport Gnolls have looked at their environment, looked at the effect it has on outsiders, and concluded that the outsiders' reaction says more about the outsiders than about Grimport. The fog is fine. The noise is fine. The work is hard and the conditions are difficult and this is home and frankly they can't sleep without the foghorn.

**The philosophical position:** Grimport culture has made a collective choice about its relationship with discomfort. Not denial — the fog is real, the danger is real, the attrition of fog fishing work is real. Genuine reframing. The thing that drives visitors mad is the thing that makes the culture work. That choice extends to everything: the visibility, the noise, the danger, the smell. All of it metabolized into identity rather than endured.

**The Mishmarine connection:** Mishmarine Gnolls and Grimport Gnolls recognize each other immediately. One group left for the outer rim. The other stayed in the fog. Both choices are completely comprehensible to both of them. Port reunions between Mishmarine vessels and Grimport fishing fleet are loud, warm, and memorable. The non-Gnoll crew members of both institutions have learned to find somewhere else to be.

## The City of Grimport

The city itself is built for the fog. Architecture that assumes low visibility — buildings close together, the streets narrow enough that you can navigate by touch in the worst conditions, landmarks chosen for sound and texture rather than appearance. The harbor infrastructure is acoustic engineering as much as physical engineering — the placement of foghorns, bells, and sound-reflecting surfaces has been refined over two centuries into a system that guides vessels in conditions that should make harbor approach impossible.

**The docks:** Always active. Always loud. The smell of fish is not background — it is the atmosphere. Visitors who comment on the smell are gently informed that they will stop noticing it. This is not entirely true but it becomes more true over time.

**The Regent's court:** The most entertainingly direct court in the empire. No elaborate protocol — not because the duchy lacks sophistication but because protocol that requires seeing clearly across a room has limited utility in a city where the fog sometimes gets indoors. Audiences are loud. Someone is always tracking the foghorn schedule. The Regent conducts formal imperial correspondence in correct register and returns to Grimport and immediately becomes a different person.

**The foghorn schedule:** Maintained by the harbor masters as a functional document that has accumulated ritual significance. Which horn sounds when. The sequence that means incoming fleet. The sequence that means hazard. The sequence that means the harbor mouth is closed for emergency maintenance — which has happened four times in recorded history and each time caused significant cultural disruption among residents who had never heard silence before.

## Political Position

Avir's political position in the succession crisis is shaped by a simple fact: the inner Akhan eats its fish. The duchy has no fleet that matters militarily, no archives, no fabrication capacity, no outer water patrol function. What it has is protein supply to a significant portion of the empire's population, produced in conditions nobody else can replicate, distributed through trade networks that depend on Grimport's docks functioning.

The Regent is not a political sophisticate by inclination. The succession crisis has been managed with the same approach Grimport applies to fog: it's there, you navigate around it, you keep working. The duchy will declare for whoever is most likely to leave it alone and maintain the trade routes that move the catch to market.

Every claimant delegation that has visited has found the experience difficult to characterize in their subsequent reports. The meetings were productive. The city was disorienting. The foghorn never stopped. They are not sure what they committed to, if anything.

**The fog belt Aubrei variable:** The Aubrei behavioral change on the southern outer boundary has reached Grimport through the fishing fleet before it reached any imperial institution. Grimport Aubrei crew members who work the fog belt edge have noticed. They have told their captains. The captains have noted it as a navigational consideration. Nobody has framed it as political intelligence because it arrived as a fishing report.

## Story Entry Points

**The incoming fleet** — a fishing vessel returning through the fog with something in the catch that shouldn't be there. Not a sea creature. Something else. The crew navigating the question of what to do with it while navigating the harbor approach by foghorn.

**The new arrival** — a character from the inner Akhan posting to Grimport for the first time. The three days of complaint. The gradual acclimatization. The moment they realize they've stopped noticing the foghorn, which is either adaptation or the beginning of becoming a Grimport person.

**The foghorn stops** — short, contained, ground-level crisis. Whatever caused it, whatever it means, the entire city reacting to silence it has never experienced. The harbor master who has to fix it before the fleet tries to make approach.

**The fishing report** — a Grimport Aubrei captain filing an anomaly report about something she saw on the fog belt edge. Not intelligence. A fishing report. The person at the harbor master's office who reads it and realizes it is something else entirely.
