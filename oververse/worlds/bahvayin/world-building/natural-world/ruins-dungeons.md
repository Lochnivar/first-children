# Ruins & Dungeons

**Category:** Natural World  
**Related:** [The Mageocracy](../history-events/mageocracy.md), [Pre-Death Artifacts](../magic-systems/artifacts.md), [Death of Magic](../magic-systems/death-of-magic.md)

## Overview

The mageocracy left behind physical infrastructure across Bahvayin — towers, dungeons, administrative facilities, experimental sites. Most are ruins. Some are not. The distinction between "safely ruined" and "unsafely present" is not always visible from outside.

---

## Towers

The mageocracy's towers were power infrastructure — magical anchors, administrative centers, regional control points. When the Death of Magic hit, the active magical systems failed. The towers themselves — stone, wood, brick — did not.

**Current states:**

*Fully ruined* — structure collapsed or so degraded as to be non-functional. Safe to enter with normal physical precautions. Potentially valuable for pre-Death artifacts, mageocracy-era records, and architectural information.

*Structurally intact, magically dead* — the most common category. The building stands. Nothing magical functions inside it. Previous occupants left quickly; what they left behind has been there for generations. Variable levels of prior looting.

*Partially active* — rare. Some towers retain residual magical function in specific systems that the Death of Magic did not fully drain. What functions is unpredictable. The active systems do not necessarily match the tower's original purpose. A tower built for weather observation might have a partially functional containment system in the basement for reasons nobody currently alive can explain.

*Occupied* — some towers have been claimed and occupied by post-mageocracy inhabitants. These range from hermit scholars to organized groups to succession claimant staging areas. Occupied towers are not open to casual exploration.

## Dungeons

The more dangerous legacy. The mageocracy's dungeons served as laboratories, prisons, experimental containment, and in some cases as housing for the Orcus genus used as security and labor.

**The Orcus problem:** When the Death of Magic hit, behavioral control of Orcus genus failed. Goblins, Orcs, Trolls, and Ogres that had been under magical supervision became unsupervised. Some dungeons were evacuated. Some were sealed with the Orcus still inside. Some were neither — the mageocracy's people simply left when things went wrong.

Orcus genus that has been in sealed dungeons for generations has had time to:
- Stabilize into functional social structures without external management
- Exhaust whatever food sources existed in the dungeon and find alternatives
- Develop relationships with the dungeon's environment, including any remaining artifacts
- In some cases, find exits the original architects didn't intend to be exits

**Current dungeon states:**

*Sealed and holding* — containment enchantments (pre-Death artifacts) are still functioning. What's inside stays inside. For now. The enchantments are degrading on the same long timescale as all pre-Death artifacts.

*Sealed and leaking* — containment has partially failed. Things are getting out. This has been ongoing in some cases for decades. The local populations around leaking dungeons have adapted to the situation with varying degrees of success.

*Unsealed and cleared* — someone went in and dealt with what was inside. Usually imperial military expeditions from the early empire period. The "cleared" designation in imperial records does not always mean what it says.

*Unsealed and present* — the dungeon is accessible. What's inside is unclear without investigation. May be empty, may be occupied by Orcus or something else, may contain artifacts, may contain all of the above in combination.

*Unknown status* — the mageocracy's records of its own facilities are incomplete. Some dungeons were never officially documented. Some are in locations that make them difficult to survey. Some are known to exist from secondary sources but have never been investigated in the post-Death period.

## Experimental Sites

The mageocracy ran experiments. Some experiments had dedicated sites beyond the standard tower/dungeon infrastructure. These sites are the least documented and the most variable in current state.

**What might be at an experimental site:**
- Pre-Death artifacts related to the experiment's purpose
- Records that survived because they were physically robust rather than magically protected
- Whatever the experiment was doing, in whatever state it was in when the Death of Magic hit
- Biological experiments that survived the Death of Magic because their subjects were physical, not magical

The engineer-priests' catalogue of Storm Gate surge events includes debris that appears to have originated from outer-ocean experimental sites. This is Senior Council restricted information.

## The Northern Rim "Reading Stones"

The suppressed oral traditions of the northern rim cultures reference objects from the predecessor civilization era — before the mageocracy — described as "reading stones." The traditions are fragmentary. What they suggest:

- Objects, possibly stone, possibly not, that provided access to information
- Associated with sites that predate the mageocracy
- Some traditions suggest the reading stones are still present at specific locations
- The mechanism by which they provided access to information is not described in surviving tradition fragments

If reading stones exist, they are pre-pre-Death artifacts of unknown capability and unknown origin. They predate any framework the current world has for understanding magical objects. They may interact with residual ability in ways that differ from mageocracy-era artifacts.

They may not exist. The oral traditions are fragmentary and have been degraded by suppression. The northern rim elders who hold the fragments do not know how much to trust them.

## Notes for Authors

- Dungeons are not generic adventure locations. Each has a history — what it was built for, who built it, what happened to it during the Death of Magic, what has happened since.
- Orcus genus that has been isolated for generations is not the same as freshly deployed Orcus adversaries. They have culture. They have social structure. They have history. Whether that makes them more or less dangerous depends on the dungeon.
- Pre-Death artifacts found in ruins are archaeological objects, not loot. Their purpose may not be obvious. Their effects may not be safe. Their value may be primarily informational rather than functional.
- Sealed dungeons with failing containment enchantments are time-sensitive story elements. What's inside has been there for generations and is getting out.
