# Pre-Death Artifacts

**Category:** Magic Systems  
**Related:** [Death of Magic](death-of-magic.md), [Residual Ability](residual-ability.md), [Ruins & Dungeons](../natural-world/ruins-dungeons.md)

## What They Are

Pre-Death artifacts are objects enchanted before the Death of Magic. They retain their enchantment as frozen states — the magic was locked into physical matter before the world-substrate changed. New enchantment is impossible. These objects are the only functioning magic in the current world.

They are not magic items in the sense of convenient tools with defined effects. They are **archaeological artifacts that happen to still work**. Their creators are dead. The traditions that made them are extinct. The technical knowledge required to fully understand what they do and why is partially or entirely lost.

---

## Categories

**Instruments** — the most practically significant category. Measurement devices, detection tools, analytical instruments enchanted to remain precisely calibrated. The engineer-priests' tidal pressure gauges are the clearest example: pre-Death instruments that produce reliable readings centuries after their creators died, calibrated to standards that no current craftsman can replicate or verify.

What makes them valuable is not that they are magical but that they are *precise* in ways that current technology cannot achieve. The enchantment is not the point — the calibration is.

**Weapons and armor** — the most sought-after category. Pre-Death weapons and armor that retain enchantments providing enhanced durability, effect, or capability. These are fought over. Wars have started over single items. The empire's legal restrictions on artifact possession are aimed primarily at this category because armed individuals with pre-Death weapons represent a genuine security threat.

**Knowledge objects** — books, scrolls, and recording devices that retain enchantments allowing access to information that could not otherwise be preserved. Some contain the mageocracy's technical knowledge; some contain historical records that predate current documentation; some contain knowledge whose significance is not understood because the context in which it was created no longer exists.

**Infrastructure remnants** — the mageocracy's towers and dungeons contain artifact-scale enchantments built into their architecture. Most are non-functional — the Death of Magic took the active systems while leaving the physical structures. Some retain partial function in ways that are unpredictable and occasionally dangerous. The engineer-priests have at least one instrument that falls into this category: functional, outputs reliable, exact mechanism unknown.

**Unknown category** — objects with enchantments whose original purpose is lost. They do something. What they do may not be obvious. What they were designed to do may be different from what they currently do given centuries of use without proper maintenance or understanding.

## Properties

**They degrade, but slowly.** Pre-Death artifacts are not permanent. The frozen enchantments are losing coherence on a very long timescale. Some artifacts have already failed — simply stopped working without apparent cause. Others appear stable. The rate of degradation is not uniform and is not well understood. Some scholars believe the rate is accelerating; others believe the stable artifacts are stable indefinitely.

**They cannot be repaired.** When a pre-Death artifact's enchantment fails, it fails. There is no restoration process. The knowledge required to re-enchant doesn't exist. This irreplaceability is why they are so politically contentious.

**They interact with residual ability.** Individuals with residual magical ability sometimes interact with pre-Death artifacts in unusual ways — activating effects that should require active magical input, reading information that shouldn't be accessible, or triggering dormant functions. This interaction is not well understood. The empire finds it alarming.

**They are not safe.** Pre-Death artifacts were designed and maintained by people who understood what they were doing. The current world contains people who possess these objects without that understanding. The mageocracy's containment enchantments in sealed dungeons are pre-Death artifacts. Some are still functioning. Some have partially failed. "Partially failed" in the context of containment enchantments is a specific and serious problem.

---

## Legal Status

**Imperial law:** Significant artifacts are legally the empire's property regardless of who currently possesses them. Possession without imperial license is illegal. Enforcement is inconsistent — the empire cannot effectively monitor artifact possession across its fragmented territories.

**Practical reality:** The black market is robust. Wealthy houses possess artifacts and do not report them. The imperial licensing system is used primarily to legitimize possession after the fact for items that have become impossible to conceal.

**The succession crisis effect:** As imperial authority fragments, artifact law becomes increasingly theoretical. Succession claimants are quietly encouraging their supporters to not report significant finds until the political situation clarifies. The artifacts that surface during the succession crisis's disorder will not surface through legal channels.

## Notable Examples — Story Resources

**The engineer-priests' instruments** — several pre-Death measurement devices in active, maintained use. The most significant is an instrument whose function is not fully understood by the current Senior Council but whose outputs have proven reliable for three centuries. What it is actually measuring, and why it was built to measure it, is an open question.

**The sealed dungeon containment enchantments** — mageocracy-era containment systems. Some are holding. Some have failed. The distinction between these two states is not always visible from outside the dungeon. See [Ruins & Dungeons](../natural-world/ruins-dungeons.md).

**The northern rim oral tradition references** — the suppressed oral traditions mention objects from the predecessor civilization era that predate the mageocracy entirely. If these objects exist and have survived, they are pre-pre-Death artifacts of unknown capability and unknown origin. The traditions describe them as "the reading stones." Nobody knows what that means.
