# The Death of Magic

**Category:** Magic Systems  
**Related:** [The Mageocracy](../history-events/mageocracy.md), [Pre-Death Artifacts](artifacts.md), [Residual Ability](residual-ability.md)

## What Happened

Magic stopped working.

Not instantly. Not completely. Not everywhere simultaneously. Over a generation, magical ability became unreliable, then unpredictable, then absent. The process took long enough that people who grew up with functional magic lived to see it fail. Long enough for institutions to collapse slowly rather than catastrophically. Long enough to be genuinely, inexplicably terrible.

This is the Death of Magic. The mageocracy's power base evaporated. The Orcus genus that had been under behavioral control via magic lost their handlers. The non-magical populations discovered that the ruling class's primary advantage had disappeared. The towers and dungeons went dark.

---

## What Mortals Believe

No mortal explanation for the Death of Magic has survived as consensus. Various traditions hold:

**Divine judgment:** Tsapadon withdrew his favor from a civilization that had used magical power in ways that offended the cosmic order. Shamur balanced a ledger that had been in deficit. Dinath collected a debt.

**Natural exhaustion:** Magic was a resource, not a permanent feature of the world, and the mageocracy used it until it was gone. This position is held by scholars who find it unsatisfying but cannot identify the mechanism of the alternative.

**Cyclical theory:** Magic ebbs and flows on a long cycle. The tide went out. Some scholars who hold this theory believe the tide could come back in. This is the most dangerous belief from the imperial religion's perspective, and the empire has periodically suppressed it.

**Predecessor civilization theory:** The predecessor civilization did something — built something, destroyed something, triggered something — that changed the world's fundamental relationship with magic. The mageocracy was the last flowering before the change fully manifested. This theory requires explaining what the predecessor civilization did and why, which nobody can.

All of these explanations are wrong in the same direction — they assume the cause was internal and mortal.

## What Actually Happened (FC Layer — Not Story Content)

The Death of Magic was a FC course correction. Magic had become the dominant variable in Bahvayin's species interaction experiment. The experiment was measuring integration, segregation, and conflict patterns between diverse species. When magical ability replaced species traits as the primary axis of power organization, the experiment's independent variable had been contaminated.

The FC running Bahvayin suppressed magic at the world-substrate level — a quantum manipulation affecting the world's ambient conditions rather than individual beings. From their perspective it was a programming decision. From the mortal perspective it was inexplicable catastrophe.

*This is authorial background. It never appears in mortal-level stories.*

## Current State of Magic

**New enchantment:** Impossible. The substrate suppression is complete for active magic. No practitioner, no matter how talented, can produce new magical effects. This has been consistent for the entire post-mageocracy period.

**Pre-Death artifacts:** Still functional. Objects enchanted before the Death retain their enchantment as frozen states — the magic was locked into physical matter before the substrate changed. These are the only functioning magic in the current world. See [Pre-Death Artifacts](artifacts.md).

**Residual individual ability:** The substrate suppression affects the world's ambient magical potential. Individual lineages that carried magical ability before the Death may produce descendants with fragments of that ability — the substrate suppression doesn't reach into bloodline genetics. These individuals cannot create new magic, but some can interact with pre-Death enchantments in unusual ways, perceive magical residue, or in rare cases produce effects that should not be possible. See [Residual Ability](residual-ability.md).

## Social and Political Consequences

**Institutional allergy:** The current empire and its predecessor cultures developed a strong political and social allergy to magical practice. This is both rational caution — magical practitioners could theoretically destabilize a post-magic power structure — and cultural memory of living under magical rule. It has calcified into doctrine.

**Artifact politics:** Pre-Death artifacts are legally restricted in most jurisdictions, practically irreplaceable, and extremely valuable. The empire claims regulatory authority over significant artifacts. Enforcement is inconsistent. The black market is robust.

**The cyclical theory risk:** If the tide-goes-out theory is correct, magic could return. The empire's political structure is built on the assumption that it won't. The imperial religion's position on this question is officially that magic's absence is permanent divine arrangement. Scholars who publish cyclical theory arguments have careers that end abruptly.
