## World at a Glance

Bahvayin (*bah-VAH-yin*) is an atoll surrounding the Akhan Sea — the inner sea itself approximately 1.75x the size of the Mediterranean, the atoll rim extending further beyond. A maritime empire is dying — too many heirs, not enough throne. The Eye of Tsapadon is coming. The breakwater islands are being sabotaged. The engineer-priests have the math but not the political access. The emperor has divine right and no engineering knowledge. Nobody is paying attention to what comes through the Storm Gate during the surge.

**Authorial register:** Ground level. Stories follow common folk, junior functionaries, third sons, dock workers, the people far below the succession crisis who feel every consequence of it. The meta-narrative is assembled from fragments, never explained from above.

## Naming Conventions

**World name:** Bahvayin (*bah-VAH-yin*) — stress on second syllable. Formal usage. Common: "the Vah" or simply Bahvayin depending on register.

**Inner sea:** The Akhan Sea — formal. The Akhan — common usage.

**The three straits:** The Storm Gate, The Desert Road, The Southern Way. All take "the" as article. Sailors use the full names; no common abbreviations established yet.

**Deity names:** Shamur, Tsapadon, Yameth, Mothar, Kadoru, Nurathi, Azaron, Dinath. Semitic-adjacent roots, not directly Canaanite/Mesopotamian. Background texture — never front-line actors.

**Imperial title:** *Sheltering Hand of Tsapadon* — full formal usage. Shortened in common speech to "the Hand" or "the Sheltering Hand."

## Authorial Notes

- FC experimenters are unnamed and uncharacterized. Their presence is felt only in the world's design — the geography, the species distribution, the Death of Magic course correction. They do not appear in stories.
- The pantheon exists as background infrastructure. Gods are invoked by mortals, argued about, gone to war over. They do not appear.
- The meta-narrative (dying dynasty, Eye cycle, breakwater sabotage) is the container. Stories live inside it at ground level.
- Magic items are archaeological artifacts — studied, hoarded, occasionally activated by accident. Not common equipment.
- The "thar be dragons" outer ocean is real. Do not send characters out there casually.
