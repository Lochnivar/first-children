## Overview

Bahvayin (*bah-VAH-yin* — from Semitic roots *bavah* + *ayin*, meaning "pupil of the eye") is a known world in Hypatia. From space it looks exactly like an eyeball — the atoll rim as iris, the inner Akhan Sea as pupil, the three straits as imperfections in the iris. The mortals have no concept of an outside to look from.

**FC Premise:** An integration/segregation experiment — maximum racial diversity seeded into a closed geographic system with distinct ecological niches and limited exit points. The question: given enough pressure and time, do diverse species integrate, segregate, or destroy each other? The experimenters are unnamed and remain offstage permanently. The mortals have no idea they are in an experiment.

**First Speech naming:** *Bahvayin* derives from triconsonantal roots carried into the proto-language as an FC fingerprint. Mortals use the word without knowing they are describing what their world looks like from outside.

## Framework Elements Demonstrated

### World Creation
- **General Rule:** First Children can create worlds for any purpose; legality determined by peer judgment
- **Bahvayin Expansion:** Created as a controlled experiment in species interaction under geographic pressure; experimenters unnamed and offstage

### Genetic Repository / Panspermia
- **General Rule:** FC seed worlds from the genetic repository; worlds may include all, some, or none of the salad bar
- **Bahvayin Expansion:** Maximum species diversity deliberately seeded — the breadth of the salad bar is the experiment's independent variable

### Death of Magic (World-Specific Event)
- **General Principle:** FC may modify world conditions mid-experiment when variables become confounding
- **Bahvayin Expansion:** A high-magic mageocracy era was terminated by FC intervention (the Death of Magic) when magical ability displaced species interaction as the dominant organizing variable. Pre-Death magic items persist as frozen artifacts; new enchantment is impossible.

### Mortal Theology as FC Fingerprint
- **General Principle:** Mortals construct theology from observable phenomena without access to cosmological truth
- **Bahvayin Expansion:** The imperial religion is built around Tsapadon the Storm Watcher — divine attention rather than divine wrath. The mortals named their mega-storm cycle the Eye of Tsapadon without knowing their world literally looks like an eye from above.

## World Characteristics

### Physical
- Hemisphere-spanning atoll surrounding the Akhan Sea (inner sea)
- Three straits breaching the rim: The Storm Gate (east), The Desert Road (west-northwest), The Southern Way (southwest)
- Zonal rim habitation: storm coast (east), desert (west), cold temperate (north), warm fog belt (south)
- Outer ocean: genuinely dangerous, held by Lesser Dragons — "thar be dragons" is accurate

### Population
- Maximum species diversity — full genetic repository salad bar deliberately seeded
- Species distributed by ecological niche logic, not randomly
- Humans are the majority generalist population across most regions
- See [Species Distribution](bahvayin/world-building/races/distribution.md) for full placement

### Magic
- **Current era:** Low magic — the Death of Magic event terminated the mageocracy era
- **Pre-Death artifacts:** Still functional as frozen enchantments; irreplaceable, hoarded, fought over
- **Residual ability:** Some individuals born with fragments of pre-Death capability; politically persecuted
- **Engineer-priests:** Appear magical through genuine expertise, pre-Death instruments, and carefully maintained mystique — not actual magic

### Status
- Declining maritime empire in active succession crisis
- Eye of Tsapadon mega-storm cycle approaching — engineer-priests have been sounding the alarm for a generation
- Breakwater island sabotage suspected but not provable through legal means
- Political disaggregation underway as imperial legitimacy fractures

## The Empire

**Structure:** Constellation of relationships — crown colonies, client kingdoms, treaty ports, rim protectorates — bound by two centuries of strategic intermarriage. Not a monolithic state; a web of mutual obligation that functions like an empire without quite being one.

**Imperial Title:** *Sheltering Hand of Tsapadon* — the emperor as mortal administrator maintaining the correct procedural relationship between the cosmic bureaucracy and Akhan civilization. Theological and political legitimacy are inseparable.

**Succession Crisis:** Strategic intermarriage worked too well. Every powerful house was given a stake in the system; every stake is now a claim. The founding bloodline has thinned. The empire accumulated cousins instead of enemies. Dinath's scales are genuinely balanced — the goddess of contracts will not tip them for political convenience.

**The Control Valve:** The empire has historically used Storm Gate breakwater maintenance as leverage against island lords — redirecting budgets, arranging accidents, letting surge through as plausible-deniability punishment. The current emperor has reached for this tool during the Eye's approach cycle.

## Key Concepts

**The Eye of Tsapadon** — the mega-storm cycle. Not divine wrath; divine attention. Tsapadon looks away most of the time. The Eye is his periodic gaze falling directly on Bahvayin. Surviving the Eye is not victory over the god — it is being found worthy of continued existence.

**The Engineer-Priests** — multi-species coalition at the Storm Gate maintaining the breakwater islands. Their civilization is the engineering. Their "magic" is genuine expertise dressed as divine mystery: hydraulic calculations, eight centuries of tidal records, pre-Death instruments only they can read, and a Sungrun who starts screaming three days before any instrument moves.

**The Death of Magic** — the FC course correction that ended the mageocracy. From mortal perspective: inexplicable catastrophe. From FC perspective: a confounding variable was removed. Left behind: ruins, sealed dungeons with Orcus genus that lost their handlers, pre-Death artifacts that still function, and a civilization with strong institutional allergy to magic.

**The Predecessor Question** — deep history holds unresolved inconsistencies. The oral traditions of rim cultures remember things the empire has suppressed without fully understanding why those traditions feel threatening.
