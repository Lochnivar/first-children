# Kan-Ji (Scriptmancy)

**Status:** Canon — Oververse Magic System  
**Origin World:** Iolanu (Empire Age)  
**Current Distribution:** Iolanu (suppressed) + select worlds via accidental Oververse propagation  
**Practitioners called:** Scriptmages; Kan-ji practitioners; rune-writers  
**Related:** [quantumancy.md](quantumancy.md), [../rules/school-framework-anchors.md](../rules/school-framework-anchors.md), [../worlds/iolanu/world-building/magic-systems/quantum_abilities.md](../worlds/iolanu/world-building/magic-systems/quantum_abilities.md)

## Overview

Kan-ji is a school of Quantumancy — a cognitive framework that allows a mortal with Quantumancer talent to impose structure on quantum chaos without fracturing. It is not the only possible framework, but it is the only one with documented cross-world distribution, and its spread is the result of an accident rather than design.

The school's defining characteristic is externalization: where other frameworks exist entirely within the practitioner's mind, Kan-ji practitioners build their cognitive framework into physical script. The runes are not the magic. They are a map of the mind that is about to perform magic — and any mind trained to read that map can complete what another mind began.

---

## Origin: Iolanu, Empire Age

Kan-ji developed during the height of the Iolanu Empire among practitioners of the Quantumancer fragment. The precise origin is debated by the few scholars who know it existed; the most defensible account is that it emerged from the Thohalla tradition — the first people, who carried the quantum gift from Iolanu's earliest generations and accumulated centuries of pedagogical practice around making it survivable.

The core problem Kan-ji solved was transmission. A Quantumancer without a framework is a catastrophe waiting to happen — the unfiltered quantum layer destroys mortal minds that try to hold it directly. The Thohalla had developed internal cognitive disciplines for managing this, but internal disciplines die with their practitioners and transmit imperfectly across generations. A master could describe their framework in words; the student still had to reconstruct it from nothing inside their own mind.

Kan-ji's innovation was to make the framework visible. If the internal cognitive structure could be reflected in physical strokes — if the architecture of a controlled quantum collapse could be written down — then transmission became documentation. The rune is the framework, made legible.

## How It Works

### The Rune

A Kan-ji rune is built in stages. The practitioner constructs an intricate pattern of strokes, each stroke reflecting a component of the quantum manipulation being structured. The manipulation's parameters — scope, target, nature of the collapse — are encoded in the pattern. The rune grows toward completion as the practitioner works.

The rune is left incomplete. A final stroke is withheld.

### The Final Stroke

The final stroke does not cast the spell. It completes the framework.

When a trained Scriptmage draws the final stroke, two things happen simultaneously: the physical pattern closes, and their internal cognitive framework — the one they have trained for years to hold — locks into alignment with what is written. At that intersection of physical completion and mental engagement, the quantum collapse fires.

The stroke without the framework is meaningless. An untrained person completing the rune adds ink. There is no quantum event because there is no mind behind the action capable of engaging with what the rune describes. The rune does not trigger. It completes — and completion is cognitive, not physical.

### Who Can Cast

Any practitioner trained in Kan-ji can complete another Scriptmage's rune — provided they genuinely understand what the rune is doing. The rune is a map; the caster must be able to read the map completely. Partial understanding produces partial or corrupted completion: the quantum collapse fires on an incomplete framework, with results that range from diminished effect to catastrophic misfire.

This is the school's most dangerous failure mode and the subject of its most serious pedagogy: a student who thinks they understand a rune is more dangerous than a student who knows they don't.

### Preparation vs. Casting

The difficult work happens before the moment of casting. A Scriptmage constructing a complex rune may spend hours, days, or in exceptional cases weeks building the framework into script. The casting itself — the final stroke — takes a moment.

This separation defines the school's tactical character. Scriptmages in conflict do not cast; they deploy. The fight was already fought during preparation.

Prepared runes are physical objects. They can be stored, carried, distributed to other trained practitioners, and used at need. They can also be stolen, burned, soaked, or lost. The school's relationship to its own materials is a persistent practical and strategic concern.

### The Rune as Fingerprint

Because the strokes reflect an internal framework, and internal frameworks vary between minds, different Scriptmages draw slightly different runes for equivalent effects. Not randomly — the variations are systematic, reflecting genuine differences in how individual practitioners have organized the same knowledge. Masters leave recognizable traces in their script. Lineages and teaching traditions develop identifiable styles. In principle, a sufficiently expert analyst can identify a rune's author or training lineage from the stroke patterns alone.

## Suppression on Iolanu

When the Church of Malachor consolidated authority over Iolanu's spiritual and magical life, Kan-ji was suppressed alongside every other school of Quantumancy. The Church's position was consistent: quantum abilities are the province of the divine, not mortal craft, and any organized mortal framework for their use is heresy.

The practical consequence was the destruction of the school's institutional structures — the academies, the documented rune libraries, the master-apprentice lineages. Individual practitioners were hunted. Surviving rune collections were burned. Within two generations of active suppression, Kan-ji ceased to exist as a living tradition on Iolanu.

The deepest irony of the Church's success: suppressing the frameworks that made Quantumancy survivable accelerated Quantumancer extinction more efficiently than persecution alone could have. Quantumancers without frameworks do not last. The Church may not have understood what it was doing, but the effect was total.

What survives on Iolanu are fragments — scattered runes in private collections whose owners do not know what they are, and the extremely rare practitioner who has reconstructed pieces of the school independently and in secret.

## Accidental Distribution: Nala's Botched Commit

Nala's intention was documentation.

Working against Malachor's operation on Iolanu, she gathered evidence of what he had cultivated and suppressed — among other things, the Kan-ji tradition, which she recognized as a genuine mortal achievement being erased by her brother's Church. Her intent was to commit this material to the Hypatia repository: to preserve it as a record in the Library, available for her own reference and as documentation of what had been built and destroyed on Iolanu.

She did not check the distribution scope before pushing.

The commit propagated. Not universally — the Hypatia repository routes pushes according to compatibility criteria that Nala neither examined nor overrode — but to a selection of worlds whose substrates met whatever threshold the system applied.

What she did not realize until after: the committed package included more than the school frameworks. Bundled within the archived materials was Malachor's amygdala modification — the Homo Quantus bootjack that makes quantum talent expression possible in biological organisms. She had committed the user manual and the firmware simultaneously. The frameworks arrived on receiving worlds. So did the latent genetic potential for quantum talent expression, seeded across every species present, since the modification targets biological structures that all repository organisms share.

This was not an "everyone is a wizard" event. The modification is genetic potential — like eidetic memory, carrying the capacity and expressing it are different things. The overwhelming majority of organisms on affected worlds carry the modification silently, across generations, without any sign of it. Talent expression is rare, variable, and surfaces in a small percentage of individuals who have no name for what is happening to them, no tradition to explain it, and no pedagogical infrastructure to make it survivable.

The frameworks arrived. The masters did not. The modification arrived. The context did not.

Nala discovered the scope of what had happened sometime after the fact. She did not roll it back.

### What Worlds Received It

The distribution criteria are not fully documented. Observable patterns suggest:
- Worlds with existing QWAN infrastructure or compatible quantum substrates were prioritized
- Worlds under active First Child management with locked magical parameters were excluded or minimally affected
- Worlds in earlier developmental stages received stronger propagation than mature worlds with established quantum equilibria

The result is a scattered distribution: some worlds received the full Quantumancer activation plus usable Kan-ji frameworks; some received activation without coherent frameworks; some received frameworks without sufficient activation to use them; some received nothing detectable.

### The Activation Problem

On worlds where the amygdala activation propagated without corresponding framework transmission — or where the frameworks arrived without the pedagogical lineage needed to interpret them — the result is mortals with latent Quantumancer access and no reliable way to use it safely. They do not know what they have. They may never know. If they stumble into using it without a framework, the haze awaits.

On worlds where both arrived intact, a tradition can rebuild — if the right practitioners find the templates and can interpret them. This is not guaranteed. A rune library without a master is a map in a language with no speakers.

## Oververse Status

Kan-ji is the only mortal-developed quantum framework with documented cross-world existence. It is also the only such framework whose cross-world existence was not intended by any First Child.

First Children aware of the accidental distribution have varied reactions. Some are indifferent. Some are interested. Malachor, if and when he becomes aware of the full scope of what escaped from Iolanu, will have opinions.

The school is most fully alive in the worlds where both the activation and the frameworks arrived intact and were found by practitioners capable of reconstructing the tradition. In those places, Scriptmages practice without knowing they are practicing something that died on the world that made it.
