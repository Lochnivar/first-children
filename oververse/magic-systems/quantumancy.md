# Quantumancy

**Status:** Framework Element - Universal  
**Source:** Extracted from Iolinus examples  
**Related:** [First Children](first-children.md), [../FRAMEWORK.md](../../FRAMEWORK.md)

## Overview

Quantumancy is the full spectrum of quantum manipulation abilities possessed only by First Children. First Children have full Quantumancy—effectively all abilities at once, not split into fragments. Mortals may manifest fragments of these abilities, appearing as separate, limited powers that vary by world. The specific way these abilities are split or manifested is world-specific.

## Full Quantumancy (First Children Only)

First Children possess full Quantumancy—effectively all abilities at once, not split into fragments. The split into separate abilities (like the 5 on Iolinus) is an implementation limitation imposed on mortals by world creators, not how First Children experience Quantumancy.

**Note:** The 5 abilities listed below (Quantumancers, Readers, Weavers, Echoes, Nullwraiths) are how they manifest on Iolinus after Malachor's split. First Children have all these capabilities combined seamlessly:

1. **Quantumancers** - Manipulate quantum reality, collapse possibilities into reality
2. **Readers** - Read quantum states (truth/current reality)
3. **Weavers** - Manipulate quantum threads (reality weaving, health assessment)
4. **Echoes** - View past events (quantum historical record)
5. **Nullwraiths** - Nullify quantum effects (anti-magic, invisible to quantum perception)

### Capabilities
- Complete control over quantum manipulation
- All abilities work together seamlessly
- No limitations or costs that mortals experience
- Can use abilities without risk of madness or detection issues

### Core Limitation: The Persistence Problem

**Without Anchors:**
- Quantumancy lets First Children collapse quantum probabilities into desired realities (rewriting existence at the fundamental level)
- **This requires constant, persistent enforcement from the wielder**
- Let focus slip, and reality snaps back to its default probabilistic determinism (like a rubber band returning to shape)
- Prolonged exposure without proper control drives mortals mad (the "haze")—they can't sustain the mental load without fracturing

**Why Mortals Go Mad:**
- They see all possible realities simultaneously (quantum haze)
- Probability fields unfiltered—"maddening, confusion flicker of possibilities"
- Mind cannot process infinite maybe
- Perception becomes torture
- Even death doesn't save them—souls still perceive quantum haze after death

### Anchors as Offload Mechanisms

**What Are Anchors:**
- Stable artifacts or constructs that "hold" the quantum probability collapse in place
- Offload the persistence requirement, freeing the wielder from constant effort
- Allow permanent changes without endless personal maintenance

**How They Work:**
- Without anchor = must constantly enforce (rubber band effect if focus slips)
- With anchor = anchor maintains the collapse, wielder can focus elsewhere
- Anchors aren't foolproof: they can be disrupted, destroyed, or overloaded, causing partial or total reversion

**Scaling Across Worlds:**
- Different First Children/worlds may use different anchor types:
  - Artifacts (the Godstone is Iolinus's specific implementation—other worlds might have similar concepts but different artifacts)
  - Networks of living souls
  - Cosmic lattices
  - Nothing at all (for impermanent changes)
- Some worlds may teach mortals to craft small personal anchors for their quantum fragments (more stable abilities)
- Worlds without intervention (like Earth, a "control" world in Hypatia with no magic and minimal First Children involvement) have no overt anchors—any quantum bleed-through is fleeting

### Other Limitations
- Still bound by natural laws of the Prime Author
- Must operate within framework of reality
- Direct actions may be limited or detectable on created worlds
- Life force binding situations create constraints

## Fragmented Quantumancy (Mortals)

Mortals may manifest fragments of Quantumancy - typically as separate, limited abilities:

### The Five Fragments

#### 1. Quantumancers (Active/Future)
- **Function:** Collapse possibilities into reality, manipulate probability
- **Ability:** See "ghost images" of potential futures, choose which becomes real
- **Mechanics:** More probable = easier, less probable = harder
- **Cost:** Quantum haze drives them insane (see all possible realities simultaneously)
- **Limitation:** Can't hide use - reality changes visibly
- **Rarity:** Extremely rare, functionally extinct on many worlds

#### 2. Readers (Passive/Present/Truth)
- **Function:** Read current Prime (reality as it IS)
- **Ability:** See truth/falsehood, understand actual state, ground others in reality
- **Mechanics:** Can't manipulate, just observe
- **Limitation:** Passive observation only
- **Rarity:** Rare but concealable

#### 3. Weavers (Passive/Present/Health)
- **Function:** See quantum connections between people/things, assess health/damage of threads
- **Ability:** Care about HEALTH not truth (strong vs. weak, whole vs. broken, healthy vs. toxic)
- **Mechanics:** Guide Quantumancers to "healthy" collapses, enable repair of quantum fabric
- **Limitation:** Can't manipulate alone, need Quantumancer
- **Rarity:** Rare, concealable if careful

**Key Distinction from Readers:**
- Reader: "Is this connection TRUE?" (truth/falsehood)
- Weaver: "Is this connection HEALTHY?" (strong/weak, whole/broken)
- Different questions, different information, both valuable

#### 4. Echoes (Passive/Past)
- **Function:** View past events (rewind quantum like VCR)
- **Ability:** Experience historical record, witness what actually happened
- **Mechanics:** NOT changing it, just viewing
- **Limitation:** Passive viewing only, cannot edit past
- **Rarity:** Very rare, endangered on many worlds

#### 5. Nullwraiths (Outside/Negation)
- **Function:** Invisible to quantum perception, project field outside quantum
- **Ability:** Can't be detected by quantum abilities, block/suppress nearby quantum powers
- **Mechanics:** Completely passive (most don't know what they are)
- **Limitation:** Passive only, no active quantum manipulation
- **Rarity:** Possibly common but unknown (most don't know they have this ability)

## Power Combinations

### Quantumancer + Weaver = Repair
- Quantumancer = power without wisdom (dangerous)
- Weaver = wisdom without power (limited)
- Together = can repair quantum damage healthily
- Demonstrates how fragments can combine for greater effect

## World-Specific Manifestations

### Iolinus Example
- Five separate abilities manifest in mortals
- All persecuted by Church and mobs
- Active powers (Quantumancers) functionally extinct
- Passive powers can hide and survive
- Quantum abilities are genetic/natural manifestation

### Variations Across Worlds
- Different worlds may manifest quantum abilities differently
- **Different numbers of fragments:** Other worlds may have different numbers of fragments (there might be more than 5 total across all worlds, but not on Iolinus)
- **Different splits:** The way abilities are split is world-specific—each world creator may impose their own implementation
- Some worlds may call them different names
- Persecution levels vary by world (from accepted to hunted)
- Rules and limitations may differ
- Some worlds may not have mortal quantum abilities at all
- **Iolinus is one specific implementation:** The 5 abilities on Iolinus are Malachor's implementation, not a universal standard

## Rules and Guidelines

### What Must Remain Consistent
- First Children have full Quantumancy (all abilities combined, effectively all abilities at once—not split into fragments)
- Mortals have fragmented versions (if they manifest at all)
- The way abilities are split/fragmented is world-specific—imposed by the world creator (e.g., Malachor imposed the 5-split on Iolinus)
- There might be more than 5 fragment types across all worlds, but the 5 on Iolinus are Iolinus-specific
- Quantum abilities relate to reality manipulation at fundamental level
- Abilities have rules and limitations
- Mortal abilities are less powerful than First Children's

### What Can Vary
- How many fragments exist (five is Iolinus-specific—there might be more than 5 across all worlds, but not on Iolinus)
- How abilities are split (imposed by world creator—Malachor chose 5 for Iolinus)
- What each fragment does (details can vary)
- Naming conventions (quantum-adjacent, magic, etc.)
- Persecution levels (from accepted to hunted)
- Detection methods and visibility
- Power combinations and interactions
- Genetic vs. learned vs. granted

## Story Implications

### For First Children
- Full power but constrained by natural laws
- Can intervene but must be careful
- Direct action may be detectable or limited
- Life force binding creates hostage situations

### For Mortals
- Limited power but can be significant
- Must hide abilities in many worlds
- Risk of madness, detection, persecution
- Abilities can drive conflict and plot
- Can be tools for liberation or oppression

## Notes

- Quantumancy is the universal force, but manifestations vary
- Iolinus shows one way quantum abilities can manifest
- Other worlds may have completely different systems
- The key is that they all relate to quantum-level reality manipulation
- Framework allows for creative variation while maintaining core concept
