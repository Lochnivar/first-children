# Character Types

**Status:** Framework Element - Narrative Framework  
**Category:** Narrative / Character Types  
**Related:** [Story Types](story-types.md), [Themes](themes.md), [../FRAMEWORK.md](../../FRAMEWORK.md)

## Overview

The framework supports various character types that populate stories across Hypatia. These types provide structure while allowing for individual variation and development.

## First Children

### Characteristics
- The gods of individual worlds or stories
- Possess full Quantumancy
- Immortal and ancient
- Vastly powerful but bound by rules

### Story Roles
- Can be protagonists (rare, but possible)
- Often antagonists (common)
- Supporting characters
- Background presence
- Mentioned but not seen

### Story Functions
- Provide powerful forces in stories
- Create conflicts and stakes
- Represent different perspectives
- Drive plot through their actions

## Transferred Souls

### Characteristics
- Souls transferred from other worlds
- Provide outside perspective
- Agents of change
- Bridge between worlds/cultures

### Story Roles
- Often protagonists or major characters
- Provide unique perspective
- Drive liberation or change
- Create cultural bridges

### Story Functions
- Bring outside knowledge
- Enable liberation operations
- Create identity questions
- Provide different viewpoint

## Mortals

### Characteristics
- Native inhabitants of worlds
- May have quantum abilities (fragments)
- The "hostages" or "freed" depending on world status
- Central characters in most stories

### Story Roles
- Usually protagonists
- Central focus of most stories
- Drive plot through their actions
- Represent different perspectives

### Story Functions
- Ground stories in relatable experience
- Provide emotional core
- Drive conflict and growth
- Represent various world perspectives

## Character Combinations

### Mixing Types
- Stories can feature combinations of types
- Different perspectives create interesting dynamics
- Type interactions drive plot
- Framework supports diverse combinations

### Supporting Characters
- Any type can serve as supporting characters
- Framework doesn't limit character roles
- Flexibility in character usage
- Creative approaches encouraged

## Development Guidelines

### Individual Variation
- Characters are individuals, not just types
- Personality and motivation drive characters
- Types provide structure, not limitation
- Creative character development encouraged

### Framework Compliance
- Characters must respect framework rules
- Type characteristics are guidelines, not strict rules
- Individual variation is expected
- Framework supports diverse characters

## Notes

- Character types are flexible categories
- Individual characters can vary significantly
- Framework provides structure, not limitation
- Creative character development is encouraged
