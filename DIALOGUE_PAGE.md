# Corvus morticus — Corvani / Crows / Blackwings

**Taxonomy**  
- Genus: Corvus  
- Species: Corvus morticus  
- Self-name: Corvani (or simply "the Corv" in casual speech)  
- Human nickname (Manchester/local): Crows, Blackwings, Roof-Watchers  
- Derogatory/affectionate: "The Death Birds," "Morticus" (shortened when annoyed)  
- Origin of nickname: "Because the fuckers are always talking about death" — common human complaint

**Origin & Arrival**  
- Independent generalist species (like Testudo chelonari, Loxodonta pachi, Manis quedari)  
- Naturally evolved (or panspermia-seeded) on a world of high predation, shifting seasons, and complex social networks  
- Survived their own trial by **observing, remembering, and never being the first to act**  
- Arrive on Earth during Prep Year via Platform 934 (Manchester terminus) or smaller rifts  
- Gravitate to high ground: rooftops, overpasses, water towers, old mill chimneys — their natural habitat in a fragmented urban landscape

**Physical Appearance**  
- Height: 5–6 ft tall when upright (often perch or lean forward)  
- Jet-black feathers with subtle iridescent sheen (blue-green-purple in direct light)  
- Large, intelligent black eyes with faint silver rim  
- Prominent, curved beak (long, strong, slightly hooked — used for precise manipulation, defense, feeding)  
- Powerful wings (flight capable, prefer gliding/short bursts)  
- Taloned hands/feet (grasping, climbing, delicate work)  
- Clothing: Minimal — dark cloaks or hooded wraps (practical for weather, blending into night rooftops)  
- Overall vibe: Tall, dark, watchful bird-people who loom like omens when they descend — but the help is real, quiet, and reliable

**Species-Wide Trait: Asperger's-Lite (Universal Corvid Characteristic)**  
- Hyper-literal communication (say what you mean, expect same)  
- Death-framed worldview (measure everything against mortality)  
- Pattern recognition obsession (see patterns everywhere, must analyze)  
- Brutal honesty (lying is inefficient and confusing)  
- Detail fixation (notice and mention EVERYTHING)  
- Social obliviousness (miss emotional subtext entirely)  
- Delayed emotional processing (feel deeply, but later, privately)  

This trait is **not** a disorder — it's the species norm. Other species find it strange; corvids find other species strange. Among themselves: perfect clarity, total understanding.

**Natural Gravitation: Healers / Chain-Menders / Memory Surgeons**  
Why they end up in medicine (60–70% of corvids):  
- Death-framing? Essential for triage, prognosis, treatment.  
- Brutal honesty? Patients need accurate information.  
- Pattern recognition? Diagnosis is pattern matching.  
- Detail obsession? Symptoms are details; details save lives.  
- Emotional distance? Can work on dying patients without breaking down.  
- Memory? Remember every case, every treatment, every failure.  

Other professions tolerate corvid traits; medicine **requires** them.  
"I could be a merchant. But merchants want me to lie about product quality. That's exhausting.  
Medicine? Medicine wants EXACTLY what I naturally do: truth, patterns, details, death awareness.  
So I became a healer. It's... efficient."

**Healing Methods (Practical & Themed)**  
- **Chain-Mending**: Use recall to guide survivors through re-linking lost blocks ("Tell me what you remember. I saw it happen. Let me fill the gap.").  
- **Haze Filtration**: Use beak ruff + herbal salves (traded from Pachi or scavenged) to reduce haze intake.  
- **Conversion Quarantine**: Can't reverse, but can "quarantine" the monster-pattern and rebuild the original chain around it (leaves scars, but preserves self).  
- **Plague/Disease Response**: Spot outbreaks from above, deliver warnings or remedies via dropped packets. "The sickness is in the water. Boil it. I remember how it spreads."  
- **Emotional / Social Healing**: Act as mediators — their memory ensures fair retellings of disputes ("I remember what was said. No lies.").

**Agency & Inner Lives**  
- Full lives beyond healing: family roosts in high places, storytelling rituals (recounting the day's observations to reinforce their chains), territorial disputes over prime perches.  
- Conflicts: Some resent the burden of remembering others' pain ("I carry too many ghosts"). Others embrace it ("We are the ones who don't forget. That's our gift.").  
- Insecurity: Unlike Pristails, they don't need crowds — they need **accuracy**. A wrong memory is their haze: "If I forget, who will remember?"

**Manchester Context**  
- Arrive mid-Prep Year via Platform 934.  
- Claim rooftops, overpasses, high medians as territory.  
- Become known as "the Blackwings" or "the Roof-Watchers."  
- Humans seek them for haze treatment or lost memories: "The Crows remember when no one else does."

**Quote (Typical Corvus healer)**  
"I remember your brother. Two days ago. Scar on left cheek. Looked back once. Afraid.  
Tell me his name. I’ll keep it for you.  
If you survive, come back. I’ll give him back."

This is the Corvus morticus in action: not loud, not needy — just **there**, holding the pieces humans can’t hold themselves.
