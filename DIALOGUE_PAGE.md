**Session Summary: Iolanu Culture Development — Spartan-Devotional Fusion**

**Context**
Working on cultures for the Iolanu world within the Hypatia multiverse. The Wrinomo (Alpine guide × Mongol steppe) was shared as a reference example of successful cultural fusion work.

**The Culture Being Developed**
A Spartan-based culture that retains strong devotion to Malachor while surrounding cultures have drifted into neo-agnosticism. Key design constraints:

- Malachor is their god but largely absent ("lost the server")
- This culture maintained fervor while others let go
- Early biblical Judaism is already in use elsewhere — cannot be the second fusion element
- Not filling a specific narrative niche — needs to feel like a complete people

**Core Design Decisions Reached**
- The Spartan martial discipline functions as **religious practice and devotion** rather than state function — they train because it's covenant faithfulness, not political requirement
- Malachor's silence reads internally as a **test** rather than abandonment
- **Gender split** as the primary structural solution:
  - Males: public covenant, martial discipline, pseudo-evangelical exterior, the visible "we are still here" declaration
  - Females: living covenant, warmth, actual cultural transmission, domestic devotion
  - Both equally devoted — split is between **demonstration and inhabitation** not sacred and secular
- Terrain negotiable — likely highlands or defensible peninsula to mirror the cultural siege mentality posture

**Current Decision Point**
Evaluating three candidates for the female-sphere fusion culture:

- **Bedouin** — hospitality as sacred obligation, oral poetic tradition, honor culture
- **Armenian** — survival identity through catastrophe, craft as sacred preservation, diaspora coherence
- **Georgian** — supra feast tradition as theological transmission, polyphonic music, warrior-sophistication coexistence

Georgian currently the leading candidate because the supra tradition directly solves the interior life problem — the feast becomes liturgy, the toastmaster a theological role, warmth as genuine devotion rather than softness.

**Not Yet Resolved**
- Which fusion culture to commit to
- How women relate to Malachor's absence specifically
- Class structure, naming conventions, material markers
- Geographic specifics
