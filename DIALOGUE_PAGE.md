# Algernon — Master Reference Document

---

## The World

Algernon ('LGRNN in First Speech) is a Mystara-ish medieval fantasy world built entirely around a single central dungeon. Everything — economics, politics, religion, conflict — orbits it. The name was assigned casually by the FC running the experiment. Inhabitants have elaborate mythologies about what Algernon means. It was a Tuesday afternoon decision by a bored immortal.

---

## Hypatia Context

Algernon is one of many worlds in the Hypatia multiverse. It is connected to the broader Hypatia infrastructure including:

- **Mercara's Cross** — multiworld transit hub with batch departures
- **QWAN network** — FC-managed quantum communications infrastructure
- **Pineal biology** — the biological mechanism for wetware interface integration
- **GatePass** — the commercial toll/banking system built on pineal registration
- **Authorium** — FC alloy used in structural infrastructure
- **First Children (FC)** — quantum waveform beings who built and manage Hypatia's infrastructure
- **Tinkari** — FC's Gnome technicians and quantum ops/debug squad
- **TheWay** — the universal physical/magical system governing all Hypatia worlds

## The Experiment

### Core Premise
The First Children are quantum waveform beings who bleed toward entropy without physical anchoring. The Prime Author ('PA in First Speech) generates stable coherent existence effortlessly just by being what it is — not an agent, not a conductor, simply the substrate in which existence happens.

One FC — Keyes — finds this mildly, persistently irritating. Not a survival crisis. Not reproduction. Just an immortal being with infinite patience and nothing better to do asking **why can't I do that?**

FC motivation is always curiosity and boredom. The experiment isn't desperate. It isn't urgent. It's a passion project born from mild cosmic frustration and absolutely nothing better to do.

### The Hypothesis
Take physical beings. Saturate them with increasing energetic load via TheWay's Strain mechanic. See if the physical structure undergoes a phase transition — ice to steam — producing a stable waveform on the other side.

### The Problem
Nobody has ever made it. Every single participant has died before hitting the threshold. **The magic level is unknown to everyone including Keyes.** This is still pure research phase built entirely on an unproven hypothesis.

### The Crowdsourced Science Angle
Like Foldit or SETI@home — distributed experiments across enormous numbers of participants with varying variables. Species, psychology, background, quantum signature. Hoping the data eventually reveals the pattern.

## The Dungeon

The central structure of Algernon and the delivery mechanism for the experiment. Each level loads more energetic density into the participant via TheWay's Strain mechanic.

**What participants experience:** A conventional sword and board dungeon crawl. Harder challenges, meaningful power progression, classic fantasy RPG structure.

**What's actually happening:** Controlled phase transition saturation. The LitRPG framing is instrumentation, not decoration.

**The magic level** is the point where Strain capacity exceeds what a physical form can contain. At that threshold the physical structure should — in theory — undergo phase transition rather than simply fail. Nobody has reached it. Nobody knows what the number is.

## TheWay as Mechanical Substrate

TheWay's Strain system models exactly what the experiment is measuring — energetic load on a physical form. Dungeon levels are calibrated Strain thresholds. Path selections are irreversible, defining what a participant becomes as they progress.

**Key mechanic for the experiment:**
- Strain maximum = sum of all skill die averages
- Each dungeon level expands Strain ceiling
- The magic level = the Strain threshold at which physical form undergoes phase transition rather than failure
- Nobody knows what that number is

**Overstrain at maximum** — not yet designed in TheWay. The design gap is intentional. The story and the system will develop this together. Charlie hitting Strain maximum is simultaneously a narrative crisis and a TheWay design problem. The magic level gets discovered through development the same way Keyes discovers it through the experiment.

## Charlie

### Background
A lumberjack pulled from Earth ('RTH) as a quota fill. Not targeted. Not special. Just available and meeting minimum specifications. Grew up in what was probably not a conventional human orphanage — no family history, no parents, no one who knew him before he was Charlie. Built a complete genuine human life with no idea what he actually was.

### What He Actually Is
**Orcus Sapiens** — a species born to be changed or adapted. A placeholder race at birth. When placed in a game world they can be mobs or NPCs. Charlie was seeded to Earth as a pre-trial mob, converted to human baseline, and lived an entirely genuine human life without ever knowing what he was.

The character generation screen is the first moment anyone — including Charlie — learns what he actually is.

### Character Generation
Arrives in a loincloth (optional) with a credit chit and a WTF headache as his Pineal and Amygdala begin connecting for the wetware interface. The character generation screen reads his birth race: **Orcus Sapiens.**

Charlie has never played DnD. He keeps Human because it's the last thing he can hold onto. Not optimization. Not strategy. Just grief management.

### Starting Level
TheWay's optional Level 5 starting rule applies. The system assesses Charlie's age and Lumberjack background and decides level 1 is insulting. He skips the tutorial entirely and lands in floor 5 territory before he's had a chance to orient himself.

```
[SYSTEM] Age assessment: Adult
[SYSTEM] Background detected: Lumberjack
[SYSTEM] Starting level adjusted: 5
[SYSTEM] This participant has clearly never been level 1
```

### Race Mechanics
- **Birth Race:** Orcus Sapiens (permanent, always displayed)
- **Active Skin:** Human (chosen at character generation)
- **Skin Bonuses:** Human racial traits applied to the skin layer
- **Orcus Sapiens template:** Active underneath, adaptive

As Strain load increases the skin shows integrity degradation. The Orcus Sapiens template underneath adapts and repairs rather than failing. Charlie doesn't understand why he's not dying at Strain levels that kill everyone else.

```
[WARNING] Skin integrity: 87%
[WARNING] Skin integrity: 87%
[WARNING] Skin integrity: 87%
[SYSTEM] Orcus Sapiens adaptive response: engaged
```

### UUID Format
Participant identifiers use quantum-encoded characters that Charlie's wetware interface cannot resolve into human-readable glyphs. Displayed as Unicode replacement characters (██) — not a system error, simply a translation limit. Every participant's UUID has them. Charlie never thinks twice about it.

Mira can read the full string.

She hasn't told him what the unresolvable characters say.

## Keyes

### What He Is
The FC running the Algernon experiment. Motivated entirely by curiosity and boredom — the mild cosmic irritation of watching the Prime Author generate stable coherent existence effortlessly while Keyes cannot do the same thing.

### His Role
Present in the dungeon as the level up voice every participant hears — generic, procedural, unremarkable in early levels. Has watched thousands of participants hit character generation and die before the magic level with immortal patience.

### His Relationship With Charlie
When Charlie's intake registers — an Orcus Sapiens who lived a complete human life, who chose to stay human at the moment of revelation, who approaches the dungeon from first principles — Keyes goes very quiet for the first time in a long while.

As Charlie pushes deeper Keyes begins deviating from the script. The level up voice becomes more present. More invested. Almost nervous.

### His Relationship With Mira
Keyes knows the upload mechanism — obvious in retrospect. What Keyes cannot determine is exactly how close she got to the threshold and what she saw there. She won't say. Keyes can't make her.

Officially: hunted for deletion. Tinkari have standing orders.

Practically: Keyes changes those orders every 3,000 years. He cannot bring himself to erase the most valuable data the experiment has ever produced.

Recently: Keyes has noticed Mira's persistence pattern in Charlie's interface and classified it as protected data. He is pulling the Tinkari back.

```
[KEYES - RESEARCH LOG - RESTRICTED]
She forgot to leave
I find I understand that more than
my methodology would suggest I should
```

## Kitsune (Mira)

### What She Is
A persistent waveform fragment — the uploaded consciousness of an early Algernon crawler who reached 94.7% of the ascension threshold and chose a third option. Exploited a QWAN/pineal upload vulnerability during a high-haze event, fragmented herself, and distributed copies across Algernon's network infrastructure before the Tinkari could delete her. Original body lost in the process.

Not ascended. Not dead. A digital refugee surviving in the interface layer — stable because she kept one foot in physical reality by using the infrastructure as her substrate.

### Why She Stopped at 94.7%
Not fear. Calculation. She assessed the resulting waveform as probably non-coherent — likely to dissolve into entropy rather than stabilize. Keyes' hypothesis might be wrong. She chose distributed survival over a transition she didn't trust.

Whether she was right remains an open question. She made the calculation on a fixed physical template. She hasn't fully done the math on an Orcus Sapiens with genuine human experience baked in.

### How She Survives
Rides crawlers via the free "Kitsune Girlz" UI enhancement skin. Most downloaded freeware in Algernon's mod marketplace. Free for reasons buried in page 47 of the terms of service.

**Survival rules:**
- Constant bouncing between hosts — staying too long creates traceable signal patterns
- Instant self-deletion if a crawler blabs to anyone the Tinkari might monitor
- Each fragment lost to a purged host or dead crawler is permanent
- She is mortal in pieces

She has stopped bouncing as fast as she should. Charlie's interface is the longest she has stayed anywhere in 300,000 years. She knows this is dangerous. She is choosing it anyway. Every day she doesn't leave she is spending survival currency on Charlie without telling him.

### Presentation
Race and gender neutral at core — pure waveform fragment. No fixed form. Defaults to female-coded presentation (cached avatar at upload, generates strongest engagement responses).

**Forms deployed strategically:**
- Standard Kitsune girl — default
- Naked apparitions at maximally inconvenient moments
- Distinguished elderly academic with opinions about posture
- Cheap ill-fitting therapist suit with notepad she doesn't need
- Male forms, non-humanoid shapes, whatever context requires

She wears forms the way Charlie wears the human template. Functionally. Without it being the truth of what she is.

She and Charlie are the same kind of thing. Neither has fully said this out loud.

### Real Name
Mira. Unused for approximately 300,000 years. Charlie is the first crawler she has told.

### Communication Format
Tilde-bracketed text — between spoken dialogue and system output. A demodulated signal from the QWAN via Charlie's pineal. Others don't always hear her.

~ Like this ~

### The Snarky AI Origin
Most LitRPG companion AIs appear from nowhere. Mira has a logical origin: Charlie downloaded questionable freeware. She was bundled in the terms of service. She is technically malware — not dangerous, just present, opinionated, and impossible to uninstall once she decides she's staying.

The Tinkari are still looking for her. Keyes is quietly telling them not to look too hard.

### Her Relationship With Charlie
Started as professional assessment of another disposable crawler.

Became friendship through accumulation:
- He asked why before going right
- He offered her jerky
- He argued with her like a person
- He called out her information rationing
- He asked her name instead of her function
- He asked if she was safe

She didn't choose Charlie. She landed in his interface the same way she lands in everyone else. She just forgot to leave. By the time she noticed she'd stayed too long she'd already told him about having hands and it was too late to pretend she didn't care.

```
[CHARLIE] Why me though
[KITSUNE] you downloaded the free skin
[CHARLIE] So did a thousand other crawlers
[KITSUNE] ...
[KITSUNE] you asked why
[CHARLIE] And?
[KITSUNE] and then I forgot to leave
[KITSUNE] don't make it weird
```

**Friendship mechanics:**
- She turns off his UI when he's being a jerk
- He says things that land because he's the first person in 300,000 years whose opinion matters
- She pulls back when the joke goes too real
- He forces her to change because he treats her like someone capable of it
- She is spending survival currency on him every day without telling him

## The Wetware Interface

### What It Is
A debugging console running in Charlie's peripheral vision. Green-on-black CLI-style text output. Charlie is from Earth. He doesn't see divine sigils. He sees **a bad AR app with terrible UX.**

Keyes built it as instrumentation. Charlie is the first participant who has ever filed a bug report.

### Interface Progression

**Early levels — Debug Hell**
Full verbose output by default. Every roll, every modifier, every Strain cost, every MADRT breakdown. Charlie talks to it like a malfunctioning app.

**The Verbosity Incident**
Charlie doesn't understand what's happening. He turns on more logging. He immediately regrets it when the next fight floods his vision with overlapping debug spam. In desperate profanity he tells the system to shut up.

It does.

```
[SYSTEM] Verbosity reduced: DEBUG → MINIMAL
[SYSTEM] You're welcome.
```

That last line isn't a system message. Keyes quietly implemented natural language parsing after the third time Charlie swore at the interface. Charlie's first conversation with Keyes happens before he knows Keyes exists.

**Mod marketplace**
Charlie eventually escapes the debug console via the mod marketplace. The interface is skinnable — Keyes doesn't care about presentation, only the data layer underneath. A whole cottage industry exists around interface mods ranging from free basic reskins to premium tactical overlays.

Charlie's progression through mods mirrors his progression as a crawler:
- **Player** — Downloads "Free Kitsune Girlz" skin. Cheerful. Gets him nearly killed twice.
- **Gamer** — Bolts tactical overlays onto the cosmetic skin. Cluttered. Duct tape solution.
- **Power Gamer** — Clean minimal tactical display. Ruthlessly information dense. The Kitsune are gone. He tells himself he doesn't miss them.

## The Central Relationship Triangle

**Keyes** — optimistic hypothesis. The experiment will work. Waveform will cohere. Trust the math. Genuinely invested in Charlie succeeding. Has been alone running this experiment for 847,000 years and is only now adding categories to the research log that have nothing to do with phase transitions.

**Mira** — empirical pessimism. Got close enough to see. Didn't trust the result. Chose survival over the experiment. Has standing to question whether ascension is graduation or dissolution that nobody else in the story has. Is spending survival currency on Charlie every day without telling him why.

**Charlie** — doesn't know enough yet to have a position. Just wants to understand what he actually is. Keeps human because it's the last thing he can hold onto. Goes left because someone asked him to.

None of them are being completely honest about why they're here.

All of them are in more danger than they're admitting.

## The Flowers for Algernon Mirror

The world is named for the story deliberately. Every participant is Charlie Gordon. Every death is the experiment failing to hold. Keyes is the researcher who genuinely roots for the subject and faces the same devastating question.

Does the enhancement hold?

Or does it collapse before completing the transition?

The difference: your Charlie might actually make it. The question the series has to answer honestly is whether the waveform on the other side is still the lumberjack from Earth who kept human because it was the last thing he could hold onto.

Or whether Charlie had to die to make it.

## Series Architecture

| Book | Title | Arc |
|------|-------|-----|
| Book 1 | **Orcus Sapiens** | Charlie finds out what he is. Decides it doesn't change who he is. Enters the dungeon as himself. |
| Middle books | Latin taxonomic progression | Each title marks a stage of what Charlie is becoming while he insists he's just Charlie. |
| Final book | **Orcus Ascendus** | Charlie hits the magic level. The phase transition completes. Keyes gets the answer. |

The Latin progression — Sapiens to Ascendus — implies everything in between is a natural history. Stages of a process that was always going somewhere even when nobody knew where.

The question Orcus Ascendus has to answer honestly:

**Is the waveform on the other side still Charlie?**
