## Overview

Hegemon's game mechanics demonstrate how First Children influence and control territories through quantum manipulation, working within physical laws while shaping mortal civilizations.

## Influence & Control

### First Children Methods

**Quantum Fragment Bestowal:**
- Grant abilities to chosen champions/bloodlines
  - **Weaver fragments:** Army mobilization, terrain alteration
  - **Nullwraith fragments:** Defensive anti-magic strongholds
  - **Reader fragments:** Strategic intelligence, prophecy
  - **Quantumancer fragments:** Direct reality manipulation (rare, dangerous)
  - **Echo fragments:** Historical knowledge, tactical advantage

**Framework Alignment:**
- Mortals receive fragmented quantum abilities (framework rule)
- First Children grant fragments (framework capability)
- All abilities are quantum manipulation (framework requirement)

**Subtle Manipulation:**
- Stabilize friendly territories (boost coherence)
- Destabilize enemy territories (induce haze-like instability)
- Influence weather, crop yields, resource discovery
- Shape probability during key moments (battles, negotiations)

**Framework Alignment:**
- Quantum manipulation operates within physical laws (framework constant)
- Patterns can be influenced (framework capability)
- Probability manipulation is quantum (framework principle)

**Champion Cultivation:**
- Identify promising mortals and grant power
- Multiple First Children may compete for same champion's loyalty
- Champions become legendary figures in mortal history

**Framework Alignment:**
- First Children can identify and empower mortals (framework allows)
- Competition for champions demonstrates First Children conflicts (framework pattern)

## Conquest Dynamics

### Territory Control

**Determined by Mortal Civilization:**
- First Children cannot simply "claim" territory without mortal presence
- Conquest happens through real wars, real casualties
- Victory means cultural/political dominance, not just military
- Mortal control determines territorial ownership

**Framework Alignment:**
- First Children work through mortals (framework allows)
- Real consequences exist (framework truth)
- Mortal agency matters (framework allows)

### Resource Allocation

**Working Through Anchors:**
- First Children work through existing quantum anchors in territories
- Can boost productivity, spawn minor rift creatures as allies
- Limited by physical laws - cannot create from nothing
- Must work with what quantum substrate provides

**Framework Alignment:**
- Anchors enable influence (framework function)
- Physical laws cannot be violated (framework constant)
- Rift spawns are coherent energy patterns (framework truth)

### Probabilistic Outcomes

**Quantum Uncertainty:**
- Quantum uncertainty prevents total First Children control
- Battles, plagues, discoveries have inherent randomness
- Even gods cannot violate quantum mechanics
- Strategic planning vs. chaos management

**Framework Alignment:**
- Quantum uncertainty provides inherent limits (framework principle)
- Physical laws cannot be violated (framework constant)
- Even First Children have limits (framework truth)

## Cycles & Momentum

### Temporal Structure

**Normal Time Flow:**
- Time flows normally (no turns in literal sense)
- Major celestial events mark strategic "phases"
- First Children may plan around these natural cycles
- Mortals experience as omens, religious calendars

**Framework Alignment:**
- Time flows normally (framework constant)
- Celestial events are natural phenomena (framework allows)
- Mortals interpret through cultural lenses (framework pattern)

### Victory Conditions

**To Be Determined:**

- Cultural dominance?

- Victory triggers consequences but not world destruction?

**Framework Alignment:**
