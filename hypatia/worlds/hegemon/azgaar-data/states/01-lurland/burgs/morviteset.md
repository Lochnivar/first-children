# Morviteset

**Type:** Generic | **Population:** 1.75 | **Culture:** Valthir

Morviteset is a logging settlement in the deep interior forests of Lurland, accessible only via river during spring melt and autumn rains. The settlement exists to harvest **old-growth timber**—massive trees that take centuries to mature and produce wood of exceptional quality. These trees cannot be replaced within human lifetimes; once cut, they're gone.

The settlement operates on a seasonal cycle. During spring melt, logs cut over winter are floated downriver to sawmills near the coast. During summer, loggers push deeper into the forest, marking trees for next year's cutting. Autumn sees the actual harvest—trees felled, limbed, and hauled to river staging areas. Winter is spent in camp, maintaining equipment and waiting for spring.

Morviteset is less a city than a **permanent camp**—a collection of log barracks, tool sheds, and storage buildings designed to house sixty loggers during the working season. Come winter, population drops to a dozen caretakers who maintain buildings and guard stockpiled equipment. The settlement has no families, no children, no permanent population beyond those caretakers.

**The Lumber Concern:** Morviteset is owned and operated by the Djugardi Lumber Concern, a consortium of coastal merchant families that holds royal charter for interior timber rights. The loggers are wage workers, paid in silver and fed from company stores. This makes Morviteset an anomaly in Valthir culture—a place where men work for wages rather than working land they own.

**Environmental Destruction:** Morviteset represents slow-motion environmental catastrophe. The old-growth forest it harvests has stood for over a thousand years, a complex ecosystem that cannot regenerate quickly. The Lumber Concern claims to practice "sustainable harvesting," but everyone knows this is fiction—they're cutting trees faster than new ones can grow, extracting wealth that will not exist for their grandchildren.

**Labor Unrest:** The loggers know they're destroying their own future. As the old-growth forests recede, the settlement must push deeper into increasingly dangerous territory. Every year, Morviteset operates farther from Djugardi, making supply runs more expensive and hazardous. Every year, the Lumber Concern's profit margin shrinks. Eventually—perhaps in a decade, perhaps in two—there will be no more trees worth cutting, and Morviteset will be abandoned.

The loggers joke grimly that they're cutting down their own jobs. Some have begun organizing, discussing demands for profit-sharing or ownership stakes. The Lumber Concern has responded by hiring private security, ostensibly to protect against "bandits." Everyone knows what the guards are really there to prevent.

**Strategic Interest:** Morviteset's isolation makes it an ideal location for discrete activities. Smugglers use the timber roads; fugitives hide in logging camps; occasionally, very discrete business arrangements are conducted in the deep forest where no one keeps records. The Lumber Concern officially sees nothing, knowing that their timber operation benefits from the same isolation that attracts less savory enterprises.
