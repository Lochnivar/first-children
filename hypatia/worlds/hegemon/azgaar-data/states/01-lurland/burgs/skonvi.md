# Skonvi

**Type:** Naval | **Population:** 1.37 | **Culture:** Valthir

Skonvi is barely a city—more a fortified anchorage where Valthir fishing families shelter their boats during storm season. Yet it has held city status for two centuries, maintained through a legal fiction: as long as Skonvi has a resident priest and a market held at least once per month, it remains technically urban and thus entitled to certain legal protections under Lurland law.

The settlement consists of perhaps forty permanent structures, mostly stone boathouses built to withstand winter storms, with living quarters built above. During fishing season, Skonvi's population swells to several hundred as boats from throughout the Grand Duchy use it as a staging point for deep-sea expeditions. Come winter, it shrinks back to a skeleton crew of caretakers and the handful of families who refuse to leave regardless of weather.

What makes Skonvi strategically interesting is its **sheltered deepwater anchorage**—one of the few places along this stretch of coast where large ships can safely anchor during storms. Foreign merchants know Skonvi well; it's where you go when northern weather catches you unexpectedly. The local families charge exorbitant fees for anchorage, fresh water, and supplies, but no one complains—the alternative is drowning.

**Economic Role:** Skonvi serves as Lurland's unwitting intelligence gathering post. Every foreign captain who shelters here talks to the locals, and the locals talk to each other, and eventually all that talk makes its way to Djugardi. The Grand Duke's spymaster considers Skonvi more valuable than a network of professional informants—sailors tell fishermen things they'd never tell officials.

**The Skonvi Custom:** It's traditional for anyone who shelters at Skonvi during a storm to leave a small carved token—usually a piece of wood with the ship's name and date. These tokens are nailed to the rafters of the largest boathouse, creating a ceiling thick with hundreds of wooden markers from ships across the known world. To find your ship's token from a previous visit is considered extraordinarily good luck.
