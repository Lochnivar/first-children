# Djugardi (Capital)

**Type:** Naval | **Population:** 12.27 | **Culture:** Valthir

The seat of the Grand Duke of Lurland, Djugardi sits on a deep natural harbor where cold northern currents meet warmer coastal waters, creating fishing grounds of legendary productivity. The city is built in the traditional Valthir pattern—a great stone mead hall at the center, surrounded by concentric rings of family steadings, each marked by boundary stones placed by ancestors centuries ago.

What makes Djugardi unusual for a Valthir settlement is its openness to the sea. Most Valthir build inward, toward forests and valleys; Djugardi builds outward, its stone piers extending into waters that freeze solid four months of the year. The harbor is kept navigable through backbreaking labor—crews work day and night during winter to break ice, maintaining channels that allow the Grand Duchy's fishing and trading fleet to operate year-round.

The Grand Ducal Hall is less palace than fortified warehouse, its stone walls three meters thick, its cellars capable of storing grain for the entire city through a three-year siege. This is not paranoia but memory—Djugardi has been besieged four times in its history, and each time the city simply waited, fishing through ice holes and eating preserved cod, until besiegers gave up and went home.

**Strategic Importance:** Djugardi controls access to Lurland's interior waterways. Any power seeking to influence the Grand Duchy must either win Djugardi's cooperation or bypass it entirely—and there is no easy bypass.

**Relationship to Strategists:** The city's stubborn resistance to manipulation is legendary. Attempts to foment political change in Djugardi tend to fail—not because the population is particularly loyal to their Grand Duke, but because change itself is viewed with suspicion. A Strategist seeking to move Djugardi must think in generations, not years.
