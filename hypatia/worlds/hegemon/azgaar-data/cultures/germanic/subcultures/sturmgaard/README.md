## Narrative Description

**"The Storm Fortresses"**

Where the Valthir hold, the Sturmgaard **build**. Centered in the great river basins where trade routes converge, Sturmgaard culture revolves around their famous fortified cities—massive stone constructions that blend fortress, market, and temple into single integrated complexes. A Sturmgaard city is less a collection of buildings than a machine for commerce and war, its walls thick enough to house workshops, its towers serving as granaries and treasuries.

The Sturmgaard emerged from the collapse of an earlier empire (whether they were conquerors or inheritors remains disputed), and they retain an institutional memory of organized governance that sets them apart from other Germanic peoples. They have written law codes, professional administrators, and a merchant class that operates across cultural boundaries. A Sturmgaard trader in Veridian markets is a common sight; a Veridian trader in Sturmgaard is a rarity—the Sturmgaard prefer others to come to them.

Their moderate expansionism reflects a calculated approach to growth. Sturmgaard cities expand by absorbing surrounding villages into their economic orbit, offering protection and market access in exchange for taxes and military service. When they do establish new cities, it is always at strategic chokepoints—river fords, mountain passes, harbor mouths. Each new foundation is planned years in advance, with surveyors, engineers, and diplomat-priests laying groundwork before the first stone is laid.

**Military Character**: The Sturmgaard field the most professional armies in the Germanic world. Their hessen shields (large rectangular shields that can interlock) allow for complex formations that lesser warriors cannot maintain. More importantly, they have **military academies**—institutions where the sons of merchants and magistrates learn siegecraft, logistics, and combined-arms tactics. A Sturmgaard army might be smaller than its opponents, but it will be better supplied, better positioned, and fighting according to a plan formulated months earlier.

**Cultural Tensions**: The Sturmgaard's organized, urban character creates friction with both the Valthir (who see them as abandoning ancestral ways) and the Eldermark (who see them as lacking ambition). Within Sturmgaard society, tension simmers between the old fortress-nobility and the rising merchant class—the former hold military power, the latter hold economic power, and the laws that govern their relationship are increasingly strained.

**Relationship to the Strategists**: The Sturmgaard are **aware**. Not necessarily of specific Strategists or their nature, but of patterns in history that suggest non-human intelligence at work. Their academies include scholars who study "the invisible hand"—apparent coincidences and unlikely events that shape history. Whether this awareness makes them better or worse pieces in the game is unclear; some Strategists avoid them, others find their predictable responses useful, and a few may be attempting direct contact through intermediaries.
