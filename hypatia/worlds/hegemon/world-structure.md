## Overview

Hegemon's structure is organized around territories as coherent quantum energy patterns, with natural borders formed by quantum instabilities. The world functions as a living game board where strategic manipulation operates through the quantum substrate.

## Geographic Organization

### Territory System

**Structure:**
- World divided into distinct regions (exact count TBD - enough for strategic depth)
- Each territory is a **coherent quantum energy pattern** with defined borders
- Natural borders formed by:
  - Unstable rift zones (chaotic energy patterns as chokepoints)
  - Quantum flux barriers (requiring anchors to cross safely)
  - Geographic features reinforced by quantum stability

**Framework Alignment:**
- Territories are coherent energy patterns (framework truth)
- Rift zones are quantum instabilities (framework concept)
- Anchors enable safe passage (framework function)

### Territory Types

**Stable Cores:**
- High-coherence regions, easier to hold and develop
- Strong quantum pattern stability
- Centers of civilization and power

**Flux Borders:**
- Contested zones with quantum instability
- Patterns shift and change
- Strategic chokepoints

**Rift Strongholds:**
- Naturally fortified by dangerous energy patterns
- Difficult to attack but also difficult to hold
- Natural defensive advantages

**Resource Nodes:**
- Rich in quantum-manifestable materials
- Strategic economic value
- Targets for conquest

**Anchor Points:**
- Strategic locations with powerful existing anchors
- Enable greater First Children influence
- Key to territorial control

**Framework Alignment:**
- All territory types operate within quantum foundation (framework constant)
- Anchors enable influence (framework function)
- Quantum stability varies (framework allows)
