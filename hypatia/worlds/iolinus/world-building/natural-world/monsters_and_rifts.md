# Monsters and Rifts

**Category:** World-Building / Threat / Cosmology
**Related Concepts:** The Shattering, Quantum Fabric, Main Rift, Mobile Rifts, Thohalla Riftwalkers
**Related Factions:** Thohalla (hunt mobile rifts), Locquodian Order (opposes reunification that might affect rifts)

## Overview
Monsters are not natural creatures, but horrors spawned directly from quantum rifts—tears in reality created by the Shattering. They are quantum fluctuations made manifest, "living quantum noise." The world is mostly city-states because wandering hordes of monsters will devour (sometimes literally) any unprotected settlement. This monster pressure, combined with the Locquodian Order's political fragmentation, prevents the formation of larger political entities.

## The Main Rift

### Origin
- Created by the Shattering event when ancient Quantumancers attempted to destroy the Godstone
- A massive, permanent tear in the quantum fabric of reality
- Likely located where the Godstone once stood, or in a central, remote location

### Physical Description
- Massive, permanent tear in reality
- Unpredictable edges that "flap around unpredictably"
- Quantum fluctuations manifest physically from this tear
- An existential threat to Iolinus

### Effects
- Unpredictable edges cause quantum fluctuations to manifest physically
- Spawns horrors/monsters constantly
- Makes reality unstable around it
- Cannot be sealed or collapsed by mortals (Thohalla tried and failed)
- Would require the Godstone to be intact to seal

### Current Status
- Unfixable by mortal means
- Constantly spawning monsters
- Creating secondary mobile rifts
- Malachor is distracted managing the rift damage

## Mobile Rifts

### Origin
- Smaller, unpredictable tears spawned by the main rift
- Secondary tears that move around randomly
- Appear and disappear unpredictably

### Physical Description
- Smaller than the main rift
- Temporary, mobile
- Unpredictable in location and timing

### Effects
- Cause local reality breakdown
- Spawn monsters/horrors locally
- Create dangerous, mobile hazards
- Can appear anywhere, anytime

### Thohalla's Mission
- The Thohalla people (Riftwalkers) actively hunt down and collapse/seal these mobile rifts
- Constant battle of damage control
- Rift-sealing is a cultural duty and survival mechanism
- Different skill levels (Rift-Sealer Levels 1-10)

## Monsters

### Origin
- **Not natural creatures:** They are quantum fluctuations made manifest
- **"Living quantum noise":** Manifestations of quantum chaos from rifts
- **Spawned from rifts:** Both main rift and mobile rifts constantly generate them
- **Symptoms, not cause:** Monsters cannot be eliminated unless the main rift is sealed

### Nature
- Spawned directly from quantum rifts
- Quantum fluctuations made manifest
- Their devouring nature might be an attempt to become "real" or simply the nature of quantum chaos
- Not biological creatures with life cycles - they are reality breakdown made physical

### Characteristics
- Ubiquitous threat (everywhere because main rift constantly spawns them)
- Can appear anywhere (mobile rifts spawn them unpredictably)
- Endless generation (cannot be eliminated at the source unless main rift sealed)
- Sometimes literal devouring (will devour settlements)

### Examples
- Various types of horrors and monsters spawned from quantum chaos
- The "polite monster" (psychology-based threat, not just physical)
- The "calm monster" (appears peaceful but dangerous)

## Impact on World Structure

### City-States Dominance
- Wandering hordes of monsters devour unprotected settlements
- Makes wandering around outside city-states a risky proposition
- People have adapted to traveling (e.g., Thohalla caravans as mobile fortresses)
- Combined with Locquodian Order's fragmentation efforts, prevents larger political entities

### Travel and Trade
- Travel is dangerous but happens
- Thohalla caravans are mobile fortresses that can fortify themselves every night when they stop
- Some cultures have adapted (e.g., Orindi adapted to both monsters AND magnetic hazards)
- Monster-infested wilderness creates natural barriers

### Settlement Patterns
- Only fortified city-states survive
- Unprotected settlements are devoured
- Creates fragmented political landscape
- Natural barrier to empire-building (alongside Locquodian Order)

## Thohalla and Riftwalking

### Cultural Duty
- Thohalla people are Riftwalkers
- Actively hunt and seal mobile rifts
- Rift-sealing levels (1-10) indicate skill
- Cultural identity tied to this duty

### Methods
- Hunt down mobile rifts
- Collapse/seal them using quantum techniques or ritual
- Damage control mission
- Cannot seal the main rift (too large, requires Godstone)

### Significance
- Useful regardless of god's attention (Malachor's silence doesn't stop the work)
- Protects settlements from mobile rift threats
- Reduces monster spawning (temporarily, until next mobile rift appears)

## Relationship to The Shattering

### The Shattering Created This
- Ancient Quantumancers' attempt to destroy the Godstone created the main rift
- The main rift spawns mobile rifts
- Mobile rifts and main rift spawn monsters
- All of this is a consequence of the Shattering event

### Current State
- Main rift is unsealable without the Godstone
- Mobile rifts continue to spawn
- Monsters continue to be generated
- This is the "wound" in reality that cannot be healed by mortals

## Strategic Importance

### For Malachor
- Distracted managing rift damage
- Must maintain quantum focus to prevent worse
- Limited in actions due to constant damage control
- Wants Godshards reunited (might help seal rift, but also restore his power)

### For Nala's Apostasy
- Monsters and rifts create chaos and distraction
- Allows Apostasy to operate beneath notice
- But also threatens all life on Iolinus
- May need Godshards to seal rift (conflict with preventing Malachor's return)

### For Locquodian Order
- Monster threat helps enforce city-state fragmentation (natural barrier)
- But also threatens all settlements
- Would oppose anything that might worsen the rift situation
- Likely opposes reuniting Godshards (could restore God-King, but also might seal rift)

## Notes
- Monsters are symptoms of the rift problem, not the root cause
- Cannot be hunted to extinction (constantly regenerated from rifts)
- Main rift is the existential threat
- Mobile rifts are manageable (Thohalla seal them)
- Monster pressure + Locquodian Order = city-state political structure
