# Malachor and the Ebon Throne

**Category:** Religion / Belief System (Iolinus-Specific)  
**Framework Reference:** [Marcionite Theology](../../../../cosmology/marcionite-theology.md)  
**Related Organizations:** Church of Malachor (the priesthood is part of this church), Knights of the Ebon Throne  
**Related Characters:** Axiarch Solon (Knight of the Ebon Throne)  
**Related Files:** [Zeta/Malachor Naming](zeta_malachor_naming.md), [Zeta, God of Iolinus](zeta_god.md)

## Overview
Malachor (also known as Zeta in earlier ages) is a god served by the Knights of the Ebon Throne, an order within the priesthood of the Church of Malachor. The faith emphasizes the sacred number five and the five aspirations that guide the faithful. The name "Malachor" is the current age's name for the god; "Zeta" was used in earlier ages, representing the progression through different "ages of man."

## The Sacred Number Five

The number five is central to Malachor's faith and appears throughout the world:
- **Days of the week:** Five days
- **Months:** Fifteen months (three fives)
- **Fingers and toes:** Five on each hand and foot
- **Church structure:** Five orders
- **Apostasy:** Also has five orders (mirroring the church structure)

**Philosophy:** "Anything less than five is seen as incomplete, unworthy."

## The Five Aspirations

1. **Dominion**
2. **Plenty**
3. **Surrender**
4. **Ascent**
5. **Cleansing**

These aspirations are enumerated as the core principles of Malachor's faith. The faithful "taste each one like a pleasant drug on the tongue."

## Knights of the Ebon Throne

### Philosophy
- Knights are above petty emotions
- They take satisfaction only in jobs well-done in glory to Malachor
- Faith gives peace; purpose gives strength
- Service to Malachor is the highest calling

### Practices
- **Truth Extraction:** Knights are ordained to harvest truth from the guilty
- **Punishment:** Punishment is Malachor's right, not the Knight's pleasure
- **Methodology:** Those who delight in pure butchery have lost sight of their true purpose
- **Afterlife:** Once the questioned die, Malachor extracts whatever information he wants; the torment that awaits under Malachor's stern gaze is severe

### Interrogation Methods
Knights trained in interrogation may:
- Use healer's instruments to prolong life during questioning
- Train with the best healers to ensure subjects survive until full truth is extracted
- Grant "release" only after extracting complete and accurate truth
- View release as "illusionary and transitory" given what awaits under Malachor

## The Apostasy

There is an "apostasy" with five orders that mirrors the church structure. This suggests a heretical or schismatic movement that has adopted the same five-fold structure as Malachor's church.

## Notes
- Malachor doesn't speak directly to lowly Knights, even those of high rank (Axiarch)
- The final accounting to Malachor is what matters; secrets lost to the living are still accessible to the god
- The faith emphasizes service and purpose over personal desires
- The sacred number five structures both the church and its opposition
- The priesthood is part of the Church of Malachor
- There is no "Maneth priesthood" - it is simply the priesthood of the Church of Malachor
- The Church of Malachor and Maneth culture are completely separate entities with no inherent connection
