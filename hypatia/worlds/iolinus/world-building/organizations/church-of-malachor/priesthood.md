# Church of Malachor Priesthood Hierarchy

**Category:** Organization / Religion  
**Related Organizations:** Church of Malachor  
**Related Characters:** Jason Lochnivar (connected to the priesthood), Axiarch Solon (Knight of the Ebon Throne)

## Overview
The priesthood of the Church of Malachor maintains a strict hierarchical structure, with a clear distinction between training ranks (Novate through Praeparch) and power-tier ranks (Terarch and above). The transition to Terarch represents a fundamental shift: authority becomes irrevocable, legal and fiscal burdens attach, political exposure increases, and binding obligations are required.

## Tiered Structure (Linear, Lowest → Highest)

### Training and Mentorship Sequence
- **Novate** — initiate servitor and basic catechumen training
- **Novarch** — junior cleric; recordkeeping, errands, supervised rites
- **Acolyarch** — senior acolyte; trains novices, enforces ritual precision
- **Cantarch** — parish-level priest; public worship, local stewardship
- **Praeparch** — steward; manages resources and audits; final mentorship gate

### Power-Tier Ranks (True Power)
- **Terarch** — first power-tier post; tribunal commander and enforcer
- **Axiarch** — provincial governor and institutional architect
- **Hierarch** — doctrinal authority and legal arbiter
- **Exarch** — supreme head and final political authority

## What Changes at the Terarch Threshold

### Authority Becomes Irrevocable
Appointments carry independent command: orders issued by Terarchs are executed without supervisory micromanagement.

### Legal and Fiscal Burdens Attach
Terarchs control detention, punishment, and sanctioned force; their decisions create official records with legal weight.

### Political Exposure Increases
Actions by power-tier officers can trigger public crises, succession questions, and factional responses.

### Binding Obligations Required
Promotion into Terarch requires formal oaths, public registration, and at least one binding sponsorship that cannot be withdrawn without tribunal action.

## Promotion Requirements to Attain Terarch (Auditable Checklist)

1. **Time in rank minimum:** configurable field (default 3 years as Praeparch)
2. **Service record:** quantified entries; minimum successes in resource stabilization, ritual administration, or parish governance
3. **Sponsorship:** two sponsors from the immediate upper tier; at least one must be an Axiarch or Hierarch
4. **Ratified action:** documented completion of a sanctioned enforcement task or administrative consolidation (case ID, witnesses, outcome)
5. **Examination:** written doctrinal/legal exam; competency score threshold
6. **Public oath and registry:** sworn before a Hierarch with entries into the central ledger and two notarized witnesses
7. **Noctarch vet:** background clearance for secrets exposure (pass/fail flag)
8. **Appeal window:** 14 days for petitions against the promotion; appeal requires formal tribunal docket

## Transition Ritual and Paperwork Blueprint

### Ritual Name: The Binding of Office

**Minimal Steps:**
1. Public citation of service record
2. Readout of ratified action
3. Sponsor testimonies
4. Recitation of oath
5. Administrative sealing of ledger entry
6. Issue of insignia (iron gauntlet or tribunal ring)

**Auditable Fields Produced:**
- Candidate ID
- Time-in-rank
- Sponsor IDs
- Case IDs of ratified actions
- Exam certificate serial
- Oath transcript hash
- Ledger seal stamp
- Noctarch clearance stamp

**Consequences Recorded:**
Authority scope, limits, and any attached monitoring conditions (e.g., probationary oversight period).

## Example Career Arcs and Implications

### Stabilizer Arc
Novate → Novarch → Acolyarch → Cantarch → Praeparch → Terarch

Moves from service to resource mastery to enforcement; strong in logistics and law.

### Confidant Arc
Novate → Novarch → Acolyarch → Cantarch → Praeparch → Axiarch (delayed Terarch) → Hierarch

Skips early enforcement focus; becomes a policy architect with later political leverage.

### Shadow Arc
Novate → Novarch → Acolyarch → Cantarch → Noctarch (as a side appointment during Praeparch) → Praeparch → Terarch

Noctarch service provides blackmail leverage and secrecy skills that make this Terarch exceptionally effective and politically dangerous.

## Story and Mechanical Hooks

### Auditable Scandal
A Terarch's ratified action later shows forged witnesses; the ledger hash reveals tampering and triggers an investigation.

### Sponsorship Politics
Losing a sponsor late in the checklist delays promotion and exposes candidates to rival appointment.

### Probation Monitoring
Newly minted Terarchs may have a temporary oversight clause in their ledger entry that Noctarchs and Axiarchs can invoke.

### Hidden Magic Liability
A Noctarch clearance flags a candidate as having secret contacts with a mage; the file is sealed but can be leaked to ruin careers.

## Orders and Affiliations

### Knights of the Ebon Throne
A specific order within the priesthood of the Church of Malachor. Knights of the Ebon Throne serve Malachor and are above petty emotions, taking satisfaction only in jobs well-done in glory to their god. They are known for their dedication to extracting truth and serving Malachor's will.

## Notes
The training sequence (Novate through Praeparch) produces fully formed officers through mentorship and gradual responsibility. Terarch and every rank above it represent the onset of "true power" with permanent authority, public responsibilities, and binding political consequences.
