## One-Line Summary

**Liberation fantasy** — Earth souls transplanted into Iolinus to free the world from Malachor, a god who holds all life hostage. Iolinus is a bootleg of Earth—Malachor ripped it off thousands of years ago, too lazy to create; the bootleg proved he was too immature. Almost exclusively human. Five persecuted quantum powers. Godstone shattered into seven Godshards. Nala's Apostasy vs. Church of Malachor. Grounded tone, found family, morally grey.

## The Gods

### Malachor (Zeta)
- **Same entity** — Zeta = earlier name, Malachor = current (age progression)
- **Creator** of Iolinus; created against peer judgment (judged too immature)
- **Personality:** Immature, spoiled brat; clever; lacks compassion, lacks desire to gain wisdom
- **Life force binding:** Tied to Iolinus. If he dies, all life on Iolinus dies. Hostage mechanism.
- **Invulnerability:** Cannot be killed without killing everyone. If threatened, he'll abandon his playground.
- **Post-Shattering:** Lost direct God-King power; still powerful but constrained; distracted managing rift damage

### Nala
- **Liberator** — Malachor's sister, fighting to free Iolinus (benevolence open)
- **Plan:** Break mortals from Malachor's grasp → evacuate → then Malachor can be punished. *Or* the liberation is the experiment.
- **Limitation:** Can only speak to souls *outside* Iolinus. On Iolinus, cannot talk directly or assist—too risky.
- **Methodology:** Quiet, hidden effort. Build momentum beneath Malachor's notice. Agents = Earth souls.
- **Moral complexity:** Influenced mad Quantumancers to shatter Godstone—used suffering minds as weapons. Not pristine savior.
- **Open question:** Pissed-off sister running chaos-tolerance experiment? How much chaos can a closed system take before it breaks? Iolinus = the closed system.
- **Benevolent framing:** She presents as benevolent to Earthers she recruits—that's the face she shows to get willing participants. The framing is from the recruits' perspective.
- **Agents remember:** Greyrose tattoos mark her followers. Most of the conversation with Nala is forgotten (too dangerous).

## World Origin & Population

- **Bootleg of Earth** — Malachor ripped it off thousands of years ago, too lazy to do his own work. Rearranged continents, added Godstone/Shattering, reskinned, claimed it.
- **What pissed off the FC** — Not just that he created against judgment, but that he couldn't be bothered to create at all. The bootleg was proof he was too immature.
- **Almost exclusively human** — No Panspermia species. Earth souls (from the original) return to free the copy.
- **Geography:** Two massive continents straddling equator; mountain ranges, lush vegetation, large desert; other lands on far side (not important to current conflict)

## The Shattering & Godshards

### What Happened
- Mad Quantumancers (influenced by Nala) tried to destroy Godstone to escape eternal quantum haze
- **Result:** Shattered into 7 Godshards instead of destroying. Created main rift. Malachor's God-King power broken.
- **Wound:** Massive rip in quantum fabric; spawns monsters; cannot be sealed by mortals

### Godstone (Original)
- Malachor's quantum anchor + embodiment of life-force binding
- Enabled God-King rule without constant focus
- Shattering wounded the bond itself—not just broke the anchor

### Seven Godshards
- Scattered; locations unknown
- Each holds partial anchor; connected to main rift
- **Strategic stakes:** Reuniting = Malachor restores power (bad) but seals rift (good). Destroying = Malachor never regains power (good) but rift permanent (bad). Terrible choices.
- **Factions seeking:** Malachor, Apostasy, Locquodians, Church, others

## Five Quantum Powers (All Persecuted)

| Power | Function | Visibility | Status on Iolinus |
|-------|----------|------------|-------------------|
| **Quantumancers** | Collapse possibilities into reality | Can't hide—reality changes visibly | 5–10 alive, hiding in terror; functionally extinct |
| **Readers** | See truth/falsehood, current Prime | Seem insightful; plausibly deniable | Hundreds hiding |
| **Weavers** | See health of quantum connections | Safe if just viewing | Dozens (uncertain) |
| **Echoes** | View past events (rewind like VCR) | Internal; looks like daydreaming | Dozens, endangered; Gabby is one |
| **Nullwraiths** | Invisible to quantum; block nearby powers | Completely passive; most don't know | Thousands? Solon confirmed |

**Quantumancer + Weaver = Repair** — Together can repair quantum damage. Apostasy mission includes healing post-Shattering damage.

**Persecution:** Church (institutional), Locquodian Order, superstitious mobs. Hide abilities or die.

## Key Organizations

### Church of Malachor
- Dominant religious institution; enforces orthodoxy
- Doctrine: quantum-gifted caused Shattering; must be eliminated
- Inquisitors, informant networks, priests report unusual events
- Greyrose discovery = death sentence

### Nala's Apostasy
- Hidden rebellion; liberation effort
- Greyrose bearers = dedicated to Nala; can be trusted
- Earth souls as agents with outside perspective
- Must stay quiet until strong enough

### Locquodian Order
- **Mission:** Prevent any central world empire. No God-King return. Enforced fragmentation.
- Formed after Shattering; response to God-King era
- Prevents political unification (along with monster pressure)
- May guard/hide Godshards to prevent reassembly
- Complex relationship with Apostasy (accidental allies and enemies)

## Greyroses & Soul Transfer

- **Physical:** Actual gray rose embedded in flesh (not tattoo). Stem between breasts. Placement varies (chest traditional; belly for operational concealment—e.g., Loisa).
- **Functions:** Mark of Apostasy membership; Nala's interface to Earth souls; blood purification; suicide mechanism (toxin bulb if ruptured)
- **Soul transfer:** Earth souls inhabit prepared Iolinus bodies. Laila gave her body for Gabby. Donor letters tradition.
- **Threat:** Chance Hodgkins (disembodied Quantumancer) can hijack Greyroses—possess bodies through connection

---

## Rifts & Monsters

- **Main rift:** Massive, permanent tear; spawns monsters constantly; cannot be sealed by mortals
- **Mobile rifts:** Smaller tears; move unpredictably; Thohalla hunt and seal these
- **Monsters:** Quantum fluctuations made manifest; "living quantum noise." Not natural creatures.
- **Political effect:** Monster pressure + Locquodian Order = city-state fragmentation (no larger entities)

## Author Notes (Background Canon — Not Yet Reader-Facing)

**Malachor/Moloch:** Name is Moloch-adjacent. Framework Demiurge = Moloch-like, not Yahweh. Marcion/Gnostics misidentified.

**Impersonation:** Malachor has impersonated other gods (including Yahweh) to get mortals to do his bidding. Biblical episodes may reflect this.

**Overthrow = FC tired of griefing:** The "overthrow of the Demiurge" is the other FC (including Earth FC) finally fed up with Malachor's griefing. Nala's liberation = the guild booting the griefer.

**Nala's benevolence open:** She's not necessarily the benevolent liberator. She's the pissed-off FC sister who's angry Malachor ripped off Earth, possibly running her own experiment: how much chaos can a closed system take before it breaks itself. Iolinus = the closed system. **The benevolent framing is from the Earthers' perspective:** she presents herself as benevolent to encourage willing Greyrose recruits. The face she shows to get volunteers. Use as background; reader-facing TBD.
