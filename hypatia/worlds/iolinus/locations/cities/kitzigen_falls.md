## Overview
Kitzigen Falls is both the name of the seven-tiered waterfall and the suspended city built in, on, and around it. A single name for both the waterfall and the city that lives inside its mist. The Kitzengai simply say "Kitzigen" and everyone knows you mean the falls, the bridges, the paper-boat funerals, and the place where the mistfae are born from rainbows.

## Physical Description

### The Falls
- **Veilfall Cascades:** A tiered sequence of seven massive waterfalls
- **Height:** Drop more than a kilometre from cloud forest to mist-shrouded gorge
- **Location:** The Kitzengai city is built in, on, and around these cascades

### The City
- **Name:** Kitzigen Falls (same name for city and waterfall)
- **Structure:** Houses are suspended on cedar-and-silk cables between the falls, swaying gently in the perpetual updraft
- **Streets:** Rope bridges and water-slick stone stairs that vanish into spray
- **Nickname:** Outsiders call it "the city that lives in a waterfall"
- **Kitzengai Correction:** "No. The falls live in the city."

## The Mistfae

### Nature
- **Origin:** Born from the waterfall vapour itself
- **Formation:** You can sometimes see them forming like silver foxes out of rainbows at dawn
- **Appearance:** Glowing mist-foxes, the size of a spaniel
- **Behavior:** Can trot across open air, leap into cascades with souls in their jaws

### Cultural Significance
- **Soul Couriers:** The mistfae carry souls after death
- **No Fear:** No one fears the mistfae - they fear never seeing it again, because that would mean the soul was rejected and lost forever
- **Children's Warning:** Children are told never to whistle near the falls after dark; the mistfae might think you're calling for an early ride

## Funeral Rites

### Paper Boat Funerals
- **Location:** Funerals are held on the lowest platform
- **Process:** 
  - The body is placed in a paper boat
  - The mistfae arrives visibly to everyone for once
  - The boat is released over the final drop
- **Belief:** The Kitzengai believe the soul rides the last fall with its courier and is reborn wherever the water next touches earth
