# AI Refresher — Algernon World

Use this as a quick context block when asking AI tools to generate scenes, outlines, or continuity checks for Algernon.

## One-Paragraph Snapshot

Algernon is a medieval-fantasy world built around a central dungeon that functions as a First Child experiment in phase transition: can physical beings survive conversion into stable waveforms under increasing energetic load. Book 1 (`Orcus Sapiens`) follows Charlie, whose birth race is revealed as Orcus Sapiens at character generation; he chooses to remain human and enters the dungeon as "just Charlie." The core dramatic pressure is identity under load ("the burn"): the same Charlie-ness the experiment requires may be destroyed by the process itself, while Keyes shifts from generic level-up voice to deeply invested observer as Charlie pushes further than previous participants.

## Canon Anchors

- **World Function:** The dungeon is both setting and instrument.
- **Experiment:** Phase transition saturation under escalating energy.
- **Primary POV:** Charlie (third-person limited).
- **Aside POV:** Keyes (level-up/system voice; increasingly present).
- **Origin Twist:** Charlie is Orcus Sapiens by birth, human by choice.
- **Core Question:** If ascension succeeds, is the waveform still Charlie?

## Character Guidance

### Charlie
- No genre savviness; solves problems from first principles.
- Emotional drive is to hold onto what he knows is real.
- Choosing human is identity-preserving, not optimization.

### Keyes
- Starts procedural and scripted, becomes personal and invested.
- Scientific motive and emotional stake converge around Charlie.

### Mira (Kitsune)
- Persistent waveform fragment surviving in interface infrastructure.
- Reached 94.7% threshold and chose distributed survival over uncertain coherence.
- Serves as empirical skepticism against ascension optimism.

## Tone and Style Guidance

- Use system/interface text for contrast, not as a dominant mode.
