# Premise, Tone & Perspective — Algernon

**Status:** Skeleton — In Development

## Premise

An Orcus Sapiens who lived a complete human life — and chose to stay human at the moment of revelation — walks into a dungeon that is actually an FC experiment in phase transition. The thing the experiment needs to work (identity, Charlie-ness) is the same thing that gets burned away as the energy load increases. The FC running the experiment has been waiting for someone who gets far enough that the conversation becomes real.

## Tone

- **Emotional core:** Everyone is dying in the dark looking for a number nobody knows
- **Central relationship:** Keyes roots for Charlie; scientifically invested; faces the Flowers for Algernon question
- **Not:** Punishment, rescue, cosmic morality play
- **Is:** Quality assurance on identity; can it survive the process that requires it?

## Perspective

- **Primary POV:** Charlie (third-person limited)
- **Aside POVs:** Keyes (level-up voice; becomes more present as Charlie progresses)
- **Charlie:** No genre savviness; first principles; holds onto what he knows is real
- **Keyes:** Generic → present → invested → almost nervous as Charlie pushes deeper

## Open Question

If the phase transition completes and Charlie ascends — is the waveform on the other side still Charlie? Or did Charlie have to die to make it?
