## Overview

Algernon is a Mystara-ish medieval fantasy world built entirely around a single central dungeon. Everything — economics, politics, religion, conflict — orbits it. The First Child Keyes runs an experiment: can physical beings undergo a phase transition to stable waveforms under increasing energetic load? Nobody has ever made it. Charlie — an Orcus Sapiens who lived a complete human life and chose to stay human at character generation — might be the first.

**A comprehensive world-building reference for the Algernon novel series.**

## Framework Elements Demonstrated

- **First Children:** Keyes (experiment operator) — present as level-up voice
- **World Creation:** Algernon created by Keyes for pure research
- **Quantum Foundation:** Phase transition (physical → waveform) via TheWay Strain mechanic
- **Soul Transfer:** Quota fills, black market souls from other Hypatia worlds (Earth)
- **Orcus Sapiens:** Pre-trial conversion to human baseline; Charlie's origin

## Core Concepts

- **The Experiment:** Phase transition saturation — can physical beings become stable waveforms?
- **The Dungeon:** Delivery mechanism; each level loads more energetic density
- **TheWay Mechanics:** Strain, power progression — instrumentation for the experiment
- **TheWay Interface:** Console-debugging aesthetic (log lines, data dumps, internal IDs) — not polished game UI. Wetware (Pineal + Amygdala). Some learn player-based mod overlay. See [world-building/theway-interface.md](world-building/theway-interface.md)
- **Charlie:** Orcus Sapiens, quota fill, chose human at character gen, no genre savviness
- **Keyes:** FC as level-up voice; roots for Charlie; the enhancement (identity) may not survive the process

## Emotional Core

Everyone in the story — Players and Keyes alike — is dying in the dark looking for a number nobody knows.
