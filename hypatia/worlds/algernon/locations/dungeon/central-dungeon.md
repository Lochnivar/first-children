# The Central Dungeon

**Type:** Dungeon — Experiment Delivery Mechanism  
**Significance:** Everything in Algernon orbits the dungeon. Economics, politics, religion, conflict.

## Overview

The dungeon is the delivery mechanism for Keyes's experiment. Each level loads more energetic density into the participant. Players experience a completely conventional sword-and-board dungeon crawl — harder challenges, meaningful power progression via TheWay mechanics. What's actually happening is controlled phase transition saturation. The LitRPG framing is instrumentation, not decoration.

## Surface Experience
- Conventional dungeon crawl
- Sword and board combat
- Power progression via TheWay
- Harder challenges at deeper levels

## Underlying Reality
- Controlled phase transition saturation
- Energetic density increases per level
- Physical structure → waveform (hypothesis)
- Magic level unknown to everyone including Keyes

## Participant Access
- Character generation at entry
- Quota fills, serious contenders, tourists, black market souls
- Everyone dies before the threshold (so far)

## TheWay Interface
See [TheWay Interface](../../world-building/theway-interface.md). Participants see console-debugging output (log lines, data dumps, internal IDs), not polished game UI. Wetware (Pineal + Amygdala) renders stat screens, character gen, inventories, level-up prompts. Splitting headache on first connection. Some learn to layer a player-based mod overlay for a more polished view.
