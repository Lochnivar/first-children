# Hegemon

**Status:** Framework-Level Summary - Proposed World (Concept Development)  
**Category:** Contested/Collaborative Creation  
**Related:** [World Creation](../mechanics/world-creation.md), [First Children](../cosmology/first-children.md), [../FRAMEWORK.md](../../FRAMEWORK.md)

## Overview

Hegemon is a proposed world that demonstrates **collaborative world creation** devolving into competitive strategic conquest. Similar to Stratara in structure but focused on geopolitical manipulation rather than Risk-style territory control. The world functions as a living game board where multiple First Children test strategies of influence, control, and conquest through mortal civilizations.

**Core Theme:** Power, strategy, and the moral cost of treating civilizations as game pieces.

## Framework Elements Demonstrated

### Collaborative Creation
- **Multiple First Children:** 4-6 First Children siblings collaborated to create Hegemon jointly
- **Ownership Questions:** The collaboration raises questions of "whose world is it?"—demonstrating how collaborative creations can become contested
- **Competition Escalation:** What began as an experiment in "controlled imbalance" devolved into ongoing competition among creators

### Peer Judgment
- **External Observation:** Other First Children observe with mixed reactions
- **Moralistic Gray Areas:** Debate whether this violates moralistic rules about mortal treatment
- **Potential Liberation:** If too destructive, could trigger liberation operation—external First Child might smuggle souls from other worlds
- **Legal Gray Area:** No clear Prime Author prohibition, but peer judgment matters

### Quantum Foundation
- **Territories as Energy Patterns:** Each territory is a coherent quantum energy pattern with defined borders
- **Influence Through Quantum:** First Children work through quantum manipulation within physical laws
- **Mortals Perceive as Fate:** Quantum patterns mortals experience as fate/fortune
- **No Literal Game Board:** Living world where quantum substrate enables strategic manipulation
