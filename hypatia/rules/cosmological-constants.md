# Cosmological Constants

**Status:** Framework Element - Universal Rules  
**Category:** Rules / What Must Remain Consistent  
**Related:** [../FRAMEWORK.md](../../FRAMEWORK.md)

## Overview

These are the cosmological constants that must remain consistent across all worlds and stories in Hypatia. These rules cannot be violated and form the foundation of the framework.

**Note:** The framework contains the **general/universal** rules. Individual worlds can **expand** these rules with specific details or provide **exceptions** that work within these general rules, but they cannot contradict these constants.

## Cosmological Constants

### The Prime Author
- Prime Author (also called "Big 'G' God" in older terminology) is the true creator
- Created reality, established the quantum substrate, and anchored foundational coherence permanently. Big G is the only entity capable of creating truly permanent coherence
- Created a cohort of First Children (many siblings—the exact number is intentionally left as a gray area). Not all First Children create worlds—some may never create worlds
- Established **physical laws** (fundamental laws of reality)
- **Presence is structural**—distributed and embedded in the substrate itself. Best understood as the concert hall rather than a performer within it. The continued existence and coherent operation of the universe is proof of sustained presence. "Present" vs. "absent" is a category error—beings looking for a person when the presence has become structural
- Does not enforce moralistic rules—these are First Children constructs

### First Children
- First Children have Quantumancy (full quantum manipulation)
- They are immortal and ancient
- They can create worlds (legally or illegally)
- They are bound by natural laws and prohibitions

### Quantum Foundation
- Quantum manipulation is a fundamental force across worlds
- **Core Limitation:** Without anchors, requires constant enforcement—let focus slip and reality snaps back
- **Anchors:** Stable artifacts/constructs that offload persistence, allowing permanent changes
- Mortals may have fragmented quantum abilities (driven mad by "haze" if they can't sustain mental load)
- **All magic is quantum manipulation** - All "magic" in Hypatia ultimately boils down to quantum manipulation
- **Magic systems are comprehension tools** - "Magic" as mortals understand it are schools/methods that let the mortal mind comprehend something way above their abilities
- Different worlds may present quantum manipulation as different "magic" systems (spells, rituals, schools, etc.), but they're all ultimately quantum manipulation
- Manifestations vary by world but foundation is consistent

### Note on Life Force Binding
- Life force binding is **NOT a universal framework constant**
- It is **Iolinus-specific**—a tool created by Malachor for his situation
- Other worlds may have similar mechanisms, but none have been created yet
- This is an example of world-specific elements, not a universal framework rule

### Soul Transfer
- Soul transfer requires mechanisms (greyrose or equivalent)
- Transfers are limited in scope
- Operational security may require memory suppression
- Souls retain identity/personality

### World Creation Legality
- Legality is determined by **First Children peer judgment** (not Prime Author laws)
- There are no explicit Prime Author prohibitions about creating worlds
- First Children judge each other's readiness/maturity to create worlds
- Creating against peer judgment (judged too immature, etc.) is "against prohibition"
- Peer judgments and enforcement attempts have consequences, but are complicated by practical constraints (especially life force binding)

## Moral Framework Constants

### Complexity
- No simple good vs. evil
- Liberation is complex and morally grey
- First Children vary in morality and methods
- Mortals are often caught in the middle

### Operations
- Hidden operations are necessary for liberation
- Direct intervention is often impossible or dangerous
- Careful planning and gradual action required
- High stakes create moral complexity

## What This Means

### For New Worlds
- Must follow these constants
- Cannot violate fundamental rules
- Can vary in how constants manifest
- Must respect framework boundaries

### For Stories
- Stories must work within these constants
- Conflicts arise from working within these rules
- Constants create dramatic tension
- Framework provides consistency

## Notes

- These constants are non-negotiable
- Individual worlds can vary in details but not fundamentals
- Constants create framework boundaries
- Working within constraints creates interesting stories
