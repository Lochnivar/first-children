# First Children (Little 'g' Gods)

**Status:** Framework Element - Universal  
**Source:** Extracted from Iolinus examples (Nala, Zeta/Malachor)  
**Related:** [Quantumancy](../magic-systems/quantumancy.md), [World Creation](../mechanics/world-creation.md), [../FRAMEWORK.md](../../FRAMEWORK.md)

## Overview

First Children are the cohort created by the Prime Author—many siblings (the exact number is intentionally left as a gray area). They are ancient, immortal beings with powers beyond mortal comprehension. Each can author complete worlds from nothing, and they possess full Quantumancy. However, not all First Children create worlds—some may never create worlds, focusing on other activities or remaining uninvolved. Worlds may be shaped by one sibling alone (most common), by several in collaboration (possible but not necessarily common—raises questions of ownership/control, "whose world is it?"—potential fuel for conflicts), or become contested when visions clash.

**Conceptual Metaphor: Librarians and Authors**
- First Children are the **Prime Author's first creations (siblings)** who **live in the Library (Hypatia)**
- They can **access and author the Books (worlds)**—reading them, interacting with them, and creating new ones
- They inhabit the library space itself, between and around the books
- Their ability to author books gives them godlike power—working alone, in collaboration, or in conflict when visions clash

## Nature and Origin

### What They Are
- The first children/creations of the Prime Author (Big 'G' God)
- They refer to the Prime Author as "The Author" in their cosmology
- Glorified waveforms (patterns of coherent energy) that are sentient and godlike—cannot be truly destroyed but can be reformed (effectively the same thing)
- Possess full **Quantumancy** - the complete spectrum of quantum manipulation abilities
- Essentially powerful beings (aliens/dimensional entities) who assume deistic identities

### Background Detail
- The "what they really are" is background world-building only
- For story purposes, they are simply gods with godly powers
- Their true nature/origin is out of scope for individual stories
- The framework allows for many First Children, not just those encountered in Iolinus

## Powers: Quantumancy

**Full Quantum Manipulation:**
- All five quantum abilities combined in one being:
  1. **Quantumancers** - Manipulate quantum reality (collapse possibilities into reality)
  2. **Readers** - Read quantum states (past/present/future truth)
  3. **Weavers** - Manipulate quantum threads (reality weaving, health assessment)
  4. **Echoes** - Create quantum echoes (view past events)
  5. **Nullwraiths** - Nullify quantum effects (anti-magic, invisible to quantum perception)

- Only First Children possess full Quantumancy
- Mortals may manifest fragments of these abilities (persecuted on many worlds)

See [Quantumancy](../magic-systems/quantumancy.md) for detailed breakdown.

## Characteristics

### Nature: Glorified Waveforms
- **Fundamental nature:** First Children are glorified waveforms—patterns of coherent energy at the quantum layer (consistent with "Everything is energy. Everything is quantum.")
- **Sentient and godlike:** Despite being energy patterns, they possess consciousness and godlike powers
- **Cannot be truly destroyed:** As energy patterns, they can't be eliminated entirely
- **Can be reformed:** They can be reshaped/reformed, which is effectively the same as destruction for practical purposes
- Reformation can reshape their personality, memories, powers, or fundamental structure

### Immortality and Vulnerability
- **Persistent existence:** Have existed since the early universe as coherent energy patterns
- **Cannot be killed through normal means:** No traditional death/destruction
- **Can be reformed:** Can be reshaped beyond recognition or functionality (practical destruction)
- **Reformation = effective destruction:** From a practical standpoint, reforming a First Child is equivalent to destroying them
- Liberation operations can "punish" creators by reforming them
- Can assume different identities/forms
- Can change names over time (different "ages of man")
- Possess vast knowledge and experience (unless reformed)

### Personality Variation
- Range from wise and compassionate to immature and cruel
- Each First Child has their own motivations and goals
- Some respect the laws/judgments of the Prime Author and peers, others violate them
- Can be allies, enemies, or indifferent to each other
- Personality drives their actions and conflicts

### Relationships and Hierarchy
- **All First Children are technically siblings** (they all share the same creator, the Prime Author)
- **Equality implication** - As siblings, there's a nominal equality between them
- **But some are "more equal than others"** - Despite being siblings, there are power/hierarchy differences (likely related to age, experience, influence, world creation, or other factors)
- **Social orders** - Still undetermined, will be developed as needed for stories
- **Relationships vary** - First Children can be allies, enemies, indifferent, or have complex relationships

### Limitations
- Bound by **physical laws** established by the Prime Author (fundamental laws of reality, immutable constants)
- Bound by **moralistic rules** (First Children constructs)—First Children are responsible for violations and enforcement
- Cannot violate physical laws (these are immutable)
- May violate moralistic rules, but face peer judgment and enforcement attempts
- Must operate within the framework of reality
- Direct actions in mortal realms may be limited or detectable
- Protective mechanisms (like life force binding in Iolinus) can create hostage situations that limit direct intervention

## Known Examples

### Nala (Iolinus Example)
- **Role:** Liberator
- **Personality:** Compassionate, wise, pragmatic
- **Mission:** Freeing worlds from cruel creators
- **Limitations:** Freedom of action on created worlds is limited; can only speak directly to souls outside of creator's awareness
- **Methods:** Hidden operations, careful planning, uses agents with outside perspective

### Zeta/Malachor (Iolinus Example)
- **Role:** Creator/Antagonist
- **Personality:** Immature, spoiled, cruel, but clever
- **Actions:** Created Iolinus against peer judgment (other First Children judged him too immature), bound life force to creation
- **Methods:** Uses life force binding (Iolinus-specific tool) as hostage mechanism to avoid punishment

## World Creation

First Children can create worlds/realms:
- **Solo Creation:** One First Child creates alone (most common)
- **Collaborative Creation:** Multiple First Children can collaborate (possible but not necessarily common)
  - Raises questions of ownership/control ("whose world is it?")—potential source of conflict
  - No personality stereotypes—any First Child could potentially collaborate depending on circumstances
  - Protective mechanisms (if any) would be more complex with multiple creators
  - Can become contested when visions clash or ownership is disputed
- Creation is possible—legality determined by peer judgment
- Creating against peer judgment (e.g., judged too immature) is "against prohibition"
- There are no explicit Prime Author laws prohibiting creation—it's a First Children peer judgment system
- Worlds may have protective mechanisms (like life force binding in Iolinus, which is Iolinus-specific)
- Each world may have different rules, physics, and magic systems

See [World Creation](../mechanics/world-creation.md) for details.

## Relationships

### With Other First Children
- **All First Children are technically siblings** (they all share the same creator, the Prime Author)
- **Equality implication** - As siblings, there's a nominal equality between them
- **But some are "more equal than others"** - Despite being siblings, there are power/hierarchy differences (likely related to age, experience, influence, world creation, or other factors)
- **Social orders** - Still undetermined, will be developed as needed for stories
- May have complex relationships (allies, enemies, indifferent, etc.)
- Can be in conflict or cooperation
- May have shared or opposing goals
- Interactions are limited by hostage situations and natural laws

### With Mortals
- **First Children control what mortals know** - They ultimately control what happens on their creations, including mortal awareness of the greater cosmology
- **Mortal awareness varies by world design:**
  - **Open worlds:** Some First Children are open about the cosmology (mortals know about Hypatia, First Children, etc.)
  - **Closed worlds:** Some First Children keep mortals ignorant, hiding the truth about the greater reality
  - **Active encouragement:** Some First Children, like Malachor on Iolinus, actively encourage belief that they are the only god
- May see mortals as pawns, children, or tools
- May care deeply about mortal welfare
- May use mortals for their own purposes
- Direct intervention may be limited or dangerous

### With Prime Author (The Author)
- Created by the Prime Author
- First Children refer to the Prime Author as "The Author" in their cosmology
- Bound by **physical laws** (from Prime Author) and **moralistic rules** (First Children constructs)
- Physical laws cannot be violated (they are immutable constants of existence)
- May respect or violate First Children moralistic rules, facing peer judgment and enforcement
- Prime Author's presence is structural—embedded in the substrate (not an active character)

## Story Roles

First Children can play various roles in stories:

- **Protagonist** (rare, but possible) - Rarely the focus, but can be
- **Antagonist** (common) - Creator holding world hostage, cruel ruler
- **Supporting character** - Liberator, guide, background presence
- **Background presence** - Mentioned but not seen, influence felt
- **Mentioned but not seen** - Part of cosmology but not directly involved

## Rules and Guidelines

### What Must Remain Consistent
- First Children have Quantumancy (full quantum manipulation)
- They are immortal and ancient
- They *can* create worlds (legally or illegally), but not all First Children do—some may never create worlds
- They are bound by physical laws (from Prime Author) and moralistic rules (First Children constructs)
- First Children are responsible for violations and enforcement of their moralistic rules
- Protective mechanisms (like life force binding in Iolinus, which is Iolinus-specific) may create hostage situations

### What Can Vary
- Personality and motivations
- Methods and approaches
- Specific goals and conflicts
- Relationships with other First Children (allies, enemies, indifferent, etc.)
- Power and influence levels (despite nominal equality as siblings)
- Involvement level in stories (from active to background)

## Notes

- Details about "what they really are" are background only, out of scope for stories
- Stories focus on their actions and character, not their origins
- Multiple First Children exist - Iolinus only shows two examples
- Each story can introduce new First Children with different personalities and goals
