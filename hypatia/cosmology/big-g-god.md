# The Prime Author (True Creator)

**Status:** Framework Element - Universal  
**Category:** Cosmology / Deity  
**Related:** [First Children](first-children.md), [../FRAMEWORK.md](../../FRAMEWORK.md)

**Also Known As:** Big 'G' God (older terminology)

## Overview

The Prime Author (also called "Big 'G' God") is the true creator who created reality and a cohort of First Children (many siblings), then withdrew forever. This is a **deistic** creator—a "watchmaker" who created the universe, wound it up, and stepped away permanently. **The Prime Author is not coming back—they have left the library (Hypatia) permanently.** The Prime Author is not actively involved in the affairs of individual worlds or conflicts between First Children.

**Terminology Note:** "The Author" is the term First Children use to refer to the Prime Author in their cosmology. Different worlds and mortals may have their own conceptions of the Prime Author, or none at all. First Children control what mortals know on their creations—some worlds are open about cosmology, some closed, some actively encourage creator-as-only-god belief. For example, mortals on Iolinus have no concept of the Prime Author and believe Malachor (Zeta) is the only god (Malachor actively encourages this).

**In the First Speech:** The Prime Author created hypatia (simultaneously "library" and "creator") and the First Children, then withdrew forever.

**Conceptual Metaphor: The Author (Purely Conceptual)**
- The Prime Author can be conceptually understood as **"The Author"** (First Children's term) who created Hypatia (conceptually, "the Library")
- Hypatia can be conceptually understood as containing all worlds (conceptually, "Books") within it—this is a mental model, not literal structure
- The Prime Author established the foundational rules and structure, created the First Children (siblings), then withdrew forever
- The Prime Author does not actively intervene in the stories being told within the worlds—withdrawal is permanent and final (will not return)
- **Note:** The Library metaphor is purely conceptual—it's the closest that First Children or any mortal mind can approach understanding the true nature of Hypatia
- Mortal awareness of the Prime Author varies: First Children control what mortals know on their creations. Some worlds are open about the cosmology, some are closed, and some (like Iolinus) actively encourage belief that the creator is the only god

## Nature

- **Status:** True deity (big 'G' God)
- **Relationship to Universe:** Creator of all existence
- **Philosophy:** Deistic—created, wound up, stepped away
- **Involvement:** Not actively involved in world events
- **Relationship to First Children:** Creator of all First Children (many siblings—the exact number is intentionally left as a gray area), then withdrew forever. Not all First Children create worlds—some may never create worlds.

## Relationship to First Children

- **First Children** are the cohort created by the Prime Author—many siblings
- They can author complete worlds from nothing
- They have the power of **Quantumancy** (all five quantum abilities combined)
- The Prime Author established **physical laws** (fundamental laws of reality) before withdrawing forever
- **Moralistic rules** are First Children constructs—the Prime Author does not establish moral prohibitions

**Note for Stories:** Details about what First Children "really are," their origins, or other background mechanics are out of scope for the reader—they are simply gods with godly powers in the story.

## Role in Stories

- **Background presence, not an active character**—withdrew permanently and will not return
- Represents the "true" god versus imperfect creators
- Source of physical laws (fundamental laws of reality) but does not enforce moralistic rules (First Children constructs)
- Provides the cosmological "why" behind conflicts
- Not a character in stories—distant, uninvolved, and permanently absent
- Will never intervene, return, or re-engage with creation

## Theological Framework

The Prime Author serves as the foundation for understanding conflicts:
- The Prime Author is the true creator, but withdrew forever (deistic, permanent withdrawal)
- First Children are siblings with godly powers who can author worlds
- Prohibitions come from the Prime Author (or its natural laws) before withdrawal
- Violations of these prohibitions create conflicts, but the Prime Author is absent from them

## Notes

- The Prime Author is not a character in stories—withdrew forever after creation
- All action happens at the First Children (siblings) level
- Prohibitions against creating worlds come from the Prime Author (or its natural laws) before withdrawal
- Details about what First Children "really are" are out of scope—they are simply gods with godly powers who can author worlds
