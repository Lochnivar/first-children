## The Substrate — The Prime Author ("Big G")

The universe is fundamentally quantum in nature. Existence consists of coherent waveforms — entities are not "solid" things but stable self-reinforcing patterns of quantum probability. Physical reality is not an intrinsic property of matter but an emergent quality of relationship — something becomes physical through its entanglement and interaction with surrounding waveforms. The more interactions constrain a waveform, the more "solid" and real it becomes. Physicality is defined by the capacity to be interacted with.

The Prime Author created the universe, established this quantum substrate, and anchored the foundational coherence permanently. Big G is the only entity capable of creating truly permanent coherence — existence that requires no maintenance or reinforcement.

Big G is now structural rather than actively interventionist — best understood as the concert hall rather than a performer within it. His presence is not absent but distributed and embedded in the substrate itself. The continued existence and coherent operation of the universe is proof of His sustained presence. Arguments about whether He is "present" or "absent" are category errors — beings looking for a person when the presence has become structural. The FC, Dragons, mortals, and everything else exist within Him without necessarily recognizing it as such.

## The First Children (FC) — Little "g" Gods

The Prime Author's first experiment into sentient coherent waveforms. The First Children are non-physical beings of enormous power whose primary capability is quantumancy — the ability to collapse quantum probabilities into specific outcomes. They are conductors of reality rather than participants in it.

**Key limitation:** The FC cannot create permanent coherence. Anything they create from scratch is subject to entropy and will dissolve back into quantum noise unless they consciously reinforce and maintain the waveform through active intention. This makes large scale or complex creation enormously costly in terms of sustained attention and will. A FC who stops focusing on a creation loses it.

**Character:** The FC are generally intellectually mature but emotionally and morally immature. They are obsessively analytical and experimental. They tend to treat their creations as objects and experimental subjects rather than beings with intrinsic worth. None are categorically demonic or angelic — morality among them is highly subjective and individual. More FC lean toward "good" than "evil" but even benevolent FC relate to their creations with an irreducible alien quality. Even the most empathetic FC cannot fully bridge the gap between their nature and mortal experience — their care is genuine but their understanding is always approximate.

**Social regulation:** FC self-regulate through peer judgment rather than any absolute moral framework. "Evil" FC such as Malachor are designated renegades primarily for flouting the consensus of their peers rather than for specific harm to creations. The peer judgment system has failure modes and does not always resolve cleanly.

## The Greater Dragons — Second Children

The Prime Author's second experiment — this time into self-sustaining physical coherence reinforced by interaction with the physical substrate itself. The Dragons are physical beings whose coherence is maintained not by conscious will but by their entanglement with physical reality.

**Trade-off:** Dragons have far less raw quantumancy power than the FC but are genuinely self-sustaining and robust. Their physical entanglement anchors them without requiring constant conscious maintenance — but that same entanglement constrains them. They are bound by the interactions that sustain them in a way the FC are not.

The FC are powerful but dependent on active engagement to maintain their creations. The Dragons are independent but limited in power. This is a fundamental trade-off baked into the cosmology — independence costs power, power costs independence.

**Character:** Dragons are generally loners with their own agendas. They are wiser than the FC in ways earned through the humility that physical limitation teaches. They do not have the power to upset or threaten their FC siblings and exist somewhat outside FC social dynamics — not subjects like mortals, not players in FC political drama, but something orthogonal to both. They occupy a unique vantage point understanding both the non-physical and physical worlds but have little incentive to share that perspective.

## Mortal Life and the Physical Universe

The FC learned from studying their Dragon siblings how physical anchoring creates self-sustaining coherence. They applied this principle to their own creations — building physically anchored beings and environments that maintain their own coherence through mutual physical interaction without requiring constant FC attention.

The primary motivation was practical: maintaining non-physical creations at scale is unsustainably costly in attention and will. Physical anchoring delegates coherence maintenance to the physical interactions themselves. The physical universe is essentially infrastructure — a persistence layer that runs itself.

**Mortality** is the cost of imperfect anchoring. The FC approximated but could not fully replicate Big G's permanent coherence. Mortal waveforms are genuinely self-sustaining for a time but entropy ultimately wins. Death is the signature of a created thing that is almost but not quite permanently anchored.

## The Genetic Repository

The FC are obsessively organized and not inherently wildly creative. Rather than each FC independently inventing lifeforms, they maintain a communal open-source genetic repository of mortal lifeforms that all FC borrow from, fork, modify, and contribute back to. This explains biological consistency across different FC domains — convergent design rather than convergent evolution.

Truly novel creatures are rare. Most lifeforms have repo ancestry if traced far enough. The repo has no governance structure — it is genuinely open source, sprawling, and occasionally contradictory, with emergent results nobody specifically designed.

**Two purpose-built utility genera:**

*Genus Homo* — Designed as a general staff template. Homo races are forks optimized for specific administrative or functional roles. Examples include Homo Sylvanius (elves) — forked for administrative tasks, with patience, long memory, and organizational capability as designed features rather than mere cultural traits. Homo Sapiens is the generalist fork — the Jack of All Trades template deployed when maximum adaptability across unpredictable parameters is needed rather than specific optimization.

*Genus Orcus* — Designed as a communal adversary template. When an FC needs an opposition race for their experiment they fork Orcus and customize for their specific parameters. Orcus variants differ widely across FC domains depending on what kind of adversary was needed. The adversarial relationship with Homo races is contextual and designed-in rather than inherent or universal.

## Summary of Core Principles

The foundational tension of this cosmology is the trade-off between power and independence, and between permanence and flexibility. Big G solved both problems by becoming the substrate itself. The FC have power but not independence from the cost of sustained will. The Dragons have independence but not power. Mortals have neither power nor permanence but exist freely within the substrate Big G established — enabled by His structural presence, shaped by FC intention, and running on their own coherence until entropy claims them.
