# Expansion Guidelines

**Status:** Framework Element - Guidelines  
**Category:** Guidelines / How to Expand  
**Related:** [../FRAMEWORK.md](../../FRAMEWORK.md), [Rules](../rules/cosmological-constants.md)

## Overview

These guidelines explain how to expand Hypatia by creating new worlds and stories while maintaining framework consistency. They explain what can vary and how to work within the framework.

## Framework vs. World Details

### The Framework: General/Universal Rules
The framework contains the "all or general" things that apply universally:
- Cosmological constants (Big 'G' God, First Children, quantum foundation)
- Universal mechanics (soul transfer, world creation)
- Fundamental principles (prohibitions, moral framework, story patterns)
- Universal races, themes, and character types

### World Details: Expansions or Exceptions

**Expansions:**
World-specific details that add more depth and specificity **within** the framework rules:
- Framework says "mortals may have fragmented quantum abilities" → World expands with specific abilities, names, rules
- Framework establishes soul transfer mechanics → World expands with specific mechanism (greyrose, amulet, ritual)
- Framework provides story patterns → World expands with specific plot details, characters, conflicts

**Exceptions:**
World-specific variations that work **within or modify** general rules:
- Framework establishes universal principles → World may have unique cultural interpretations
- Framework provides patterns → World demonstrates a variation of those patterns
- Framework sets constraints → World finds creative ways to work within those constraints

**Key Point:** World details must respect framework constants but can expand or provide exceptions for world-specific elements.

## What Can Vary

### World-Specific Elements
- **Magic systems:** Quantum abilities may manifest differently, be called different names, have different rules
- **Technology levels:** From primitive to advanced, varies by world
- **Cultures and societies:** Unlimited variation in cultural expression
- **Religions and beliefs:** Diverse religious systems and practices
- **Geography and physics:** Different world structures, physics (within reason)
- **Naming conventions:** Different naming systems and conventions
- **Races:** May include any races from framework or create new ones

### Story-Specific Elements
- **Conflict types:** Liberation, creation, discovery, exploration, etc.
- **Character focus:** Mortals, First Children, transferred souls, or combinations
- **POV structure:** Any narrative structure
- **Tone:** Can vary (though often grounded)
- **Specific plots and arcs:** Unlimited plot possibilities
- **Themes:** Can emphasize different themes while respecting framework

## Creating New Worlds

### Essential Questions
1. Who created this world? (First Child or natural? Solo or collaborative?)
2. If collaborative: How many First Children? What's the ownership/control arrangement? Potential for conflict? (Note: Collaboration is possible but not necessarily common—depends on individual First Children and circumstances, not personality stereotypes)
3. Was creation made with peer approval, or against peer judgment (judged too immature/not ready)?
4. Are there protective mechanisms? (hostage situations, binding mechanisms, etc.? Note: Life force binding is Iolinus-specific—other worlds may have different mechanisms or none)
5. What is the status of quantum abilities? (accepted, persecuted, hidden?)
6. Are there transferred souls? (from where, why?)
7. **What is mortal awareness of the greater cosmology?** (Open about Hypatia/First Children, closed/ignorant, or actively encourages creator-as-only-god belief like Iolinus? First Children control what mortals know on their creations.)
8. What is the primary conflict or story focus?
9. How does this world relate to others (if at all)?

### Magic System Guidelines
- Must relate to quantum manipulation (all magic is quantum manipulation—magic systems are comprehension tools for mortals)
- Can manifest differently than Iolinus examples
- May be called different names
- Should have rules and limitations
- May be persecuted, accepted, or hidden
- Must respect quantum foundation

### Character Guidelines
- Mortals are usually the focus
- First Children can appear but are powerful
- Transferred souls provide outside perspective
- Character relationships are central
- Found family is a common theme

## Creating New First Children

### Characterization
- Each First Child has a distinct personality
- Motivated by their own goals (not just "good" or "evil")
- Possesses full Quantumancy
- Bound by natural laws (cannot violate fundamental prohibitions easily)
- Can create worlds (legally or illegally)
- May assume different identities/names

### Roles They Can Play
- Protagonist (rare, but possible)
- Antagonist (common)
- Supporting character
- Background presence
- Mentioned but not seen

### Relationships
- May have complex relationships with other First Children
- May care about mortals or see them as pawns
- May respect or violate the Big 'G' God's laws
- May be allies, enemies, or indifferent

## Connection Points

### Ways Stories Might Connect
- Shared First Children (appearing in multiple stories)
- Cross-world characters (soul transfers, travelers)
- Shared organizations (if they span worlds)
- References to other worlds/stories
- Cameo appearances
- Shared themes without direct connection

### Levels of Connection
- **Easter Eggs:** Subtle references, names, hints
- **Cameos:** Characters appearing briefly
- **Shared Organizations:** Groups spanning worlds
- **Direct Connections:** Characters crossing between stories
- **Meta-Narrative:** Overarching plot across multiple stories

## Expansion Principles

### Consistency First
- Framework constants must be respected
- Variations must work within framework rules
- Creative variation is encouraged within boundaries
- Framework provides foundation, not limitation

### Gradual Expansion
- Start with framework-compliant worlds
- Build connections gradually
- Let connections emerge naturally
- Framework supports expansion

### Documentation
- Document how new worlds conform to framework
- Create consistency check documents
- Update framework if intentional expansion
- Track changes and decisions

## Notes

- Guidelines provide flexibility within framework rules
- Expansion is encouraged and supported
- Framework provides structure, not limitation
- Creativity thrives within consistent boundaries
