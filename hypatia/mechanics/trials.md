# Trials

**Status:** Framework Element - Universal  
**Category:** Mechanics / World Operations  
**Related:** [World Creation](world-creation.md), [../FRAMEWORK.md](../../FRAMEWORK.md)

## Overview

A **Trial** is the world-to-dungeon transformation that a world undergoes. Earth will undergo this process in the book. Some worlds have already been through their trial—they have experienced this transformation and exist in its aftermath.

**Narrative focus:** The story is about **humans surviving in a drastically changed world**—adaptation, community, re-linking chains, holding median camps, dealing with walls and conversions and warlords. FC creators are background cosmology (cameos at most), not central characters.

**Note:** Trials are distinct from "Dungeon" worlds (e.g., Delverium). A Dungeon world is a world type built around gamified dungeon mechanics. A Trial is a *process* or *transformation* that a world goes through—the before-and-after of that transformation.

## Key Concepts

- **Trial** = world-to-dungeon transformation process
- Some worlds have completed their trial; others (like Earth) have not yet undergone it
- Trial worlds = worlds that have been through or are undergoing this transformation
- Distinct from Dungeon worlds (which are a world type, not a transformation)

**Generalist failure (background cosmology):** Generalist variants typically fail under trial pressure. Specialists (Pachi, Sahirans, etc.) survive; generalists (Camelus sapiens, Elephas sapiens, previous Homo sapiens deployments) tend to collapse. Earth's crawl is high-stakes for sapiens—but the narrative focus is human survival, not FC vindication.

## Framework Implications

- Trial veterans (species or individuals who have survived trials) may have specific experience and perspective
- Trial worlds may share certain characteristics post-transformation
- Earth's trial is a central narrative element—the story of humans in a changed world

## Earth Trial Structure

Earth's trial uses a specific dungeon topology: roads create corridors with walls; spaces between are rooms; gaps at intersections allow access. See **[Earth Trial Structure](earth-trial-structure.md)** for full mechanics (Year of Preparation, corridor hierarchy, plazas, waterways, wall extent).
