## Definition

**WARNING: Not for staff deployment. Adversary use only.**

Genus Orcus is a designed adversary template—created by First Children specifically as monster stock for trials. NOT FC Staff. Orcus species are training opponents, adversary populations, "mob spawns" for trials. They are designed obstacles to be overcome. The fundamental horror: they are created only to be killed. Self-aware enough to know it. Trapped in their role by biological imperative.

### Base Framework Characteristics
- Aggressive by design (biological imperative to fight)
- Resilient but killable (challenging but beatable)
- Fast reproduction (replace casualties quickly)
- Recognizable as hostile (green skin, tusks, brutish)
- Poor organization (deliberately—internal fighting keeps them weak)
- "If they worked together, they'd be unstoppable. Good thing they won't."

### The Fundamental Horror
- Created only to be killed
- Designed to lose (eventually)
- Self-aware enough to know this
- Cannot escape biological imperatives
- Cannot choose peace (biologically driven to attack)
- Reproduce just to maintain enemy populations
- Eternal cycle of violence and death

## Species Expressions

See individual species files: [Goblins](goblins.md), [Orcs](orcs.md), [Trolls](trolls.md), [Ogres](ogres.md).

### Orcus minor - Goblins (Swarm Mobs)
**Adversary designation:** Low-level swarm enemies  
**Physical traits:** 3-4 feet tall, wiry, quick, expendable. Sharp teeth, large eyes (low-light vision). Green-grey skin. High reproduction rate.  
**Purpose in trials:** Tutorial enemies, ambush attackers, swarm tactics, loot rewards.  
**Design cruelty:** Designed to be killed en masse. Know they're weak, constantly flee. "We are prey. That is our purpose."  
**Personality:** Cruel, cowardly individually; brave in groups; cunning in petty ways; treasure-obsessed; cackling, chaotic.  
**Expected lifespan:** 2-6 months

### Orcus sapiens - Orcs (Standard Combat)
**Adversary designation:** Mid-tier combat opponents  
**Physical traits:** 6-7 feet tall, heavily muscled. Green skin, prominent tusks. High pain tolerance, fast healing (minor wounds). Aggressive disposition.  
**Purpose in trials:** Primary adversary, territory holders, boss fights (chieftains, warlords), tactical challenges.  
**The tragedy:** Smart enough to know they're designed to lose. Organized enough to try fighting back. Strong enough to win sometimes. But ultimately designed to be overcome. "We fight. We lose. We die. We are replaced. This is the cycle."  
**Personality:** Brutal, straightforward. Respect strength (hierarchical). Combat-focused. Surprisingly organized (militaristic).  
**Expected lifespan:** 2-5 years

### Orcus gigantus - Trolls (Elite Encounters)
**Adversary designation:** Elite/boss enemy type  
**Physical traits:** 8-10 feet tall, massive build. Enhanced regeneration (regrow limbs slowly). Extremely tough hide. Green-brown skin. Can eat almost anything.  
**Purpose in trials:** Boss encounters, bridge guardians, area denial. Teaching moment (learn exploitable weakness—fire/acid).  
**Design features:** Near-immortal (hard to kill permanently). Regenerate in place. Slow but unstoppable. Weakness to fire/acid (rewards knowledge).  
**The awareness:** Live long enough (centuries) to get very smart. Know they're unkillable... until someone learns fire. Watch generation after generation die to the same weakness. Can't escape their nature. "We are eternal... until we're not. We guard... because we must."  
**Personality:** Slow-thinking, patient. Solitary. Territorial. Mournful, depressed (centuries of same role).  
**Expected lifespan:** Centuries (regenerative)

### Orcus magnus - Ogres (Tank Enemies)
**Adversary designation:** Heavy/siege enemy type  
**Physical traits:** 10-12 feet tall, enormously strong. Huge appetite (high caloric need). Yellowish-green to brown skin. Not very bright. Surprisingly fast when motivated.  
**Purpose in trials:** Heavy damage output, huge health pool, environmental hazards (can be tricked, redirected), siege encounters.  
**Design features:** Maximum strength, demolition capability. Slow (telegraphed attacks). Stupid (exploitable). Food-motivated (can be distracted).  
**The simplicity:** Too stupid to fully understand their purpose. Just hungry, angry, fighting. "Ogre hungry. Ogre smash. Ogre die. Ogre... confused."  
**Personality:** Simple, food-motivated. Easily confused/angered. Strong but lazy (need supervision) May refuse to fight if overfed (hunger is primary motivator).  
**Expected lifespan:** 5-10 years

## The Moral Horror

**Orcus species are sentient victims:**
- Self-aware, know what they are
- Understand their purpose (to be killed)
- Can't escape (biological imperative to fight)
- Orcs: smart enough to hate it
- Trolls: aware enough to mourn it
- Goblins: too numerous to matter individually
- Ogres: too simple to fully comprehend

**Parallel to humans:**
- Humans: Homo sapiens (designed as staff)
- Orcus: designed as adversaries
- Both created for FC purposes
- Both trapped by design
- Neither chose their role

**The impossible choice:**
- Killing Orcus = self-defense (they attack)
- But they can't not attack (designed that way)
- Are you murdering victims or defending yourself?
- Mercy = death, cruelty = life
- No good answer

## Post-Trial Question

**What happens to Orcus after trial ends?**

**Repository recommendation:** "Terminate all specimens post-trial (humane methods recommended)"

**The problem:**
- Can't leave them alive (will attack)
- Can't imprison them (cruel)
- Can't "cure" them (biology, not choice)
- Must kill them all
- They know this

**Orc acceptance:** "We know. When trial ends, we end." "Better than endless cycle." "At least make it quick."

## Thematic Role

**Orcus represent:**
- Designed suffering (created only to be killed)
- Parallel victimhood (like humans, trapped by design)
- Moral complexity (self-defense vs. murder of victims)
- The cruelty of FC creation (entire species exists to suffer)

**Together with Halflings:** Full spectrum of FC creation—some created to help, some to harm. All trapped by their design. Humans fit somewhere in between.

## Repository Entry Summary

**Ethical Considerations:**
- Subjects experience suffering
- Self-aware of adversarial role
- Cannot escape biological imperatives
- High mortality rates inherent to purpose
- **Recommended:** Minimize deployment time
- **Alternative:** Use non-sentient constructs (lower engagement but less ethical concern)

**Known Issues:**
- Orcs sometimes organize beyond design parameters (rebellion risk)
- Trolls develop depression (centuries of same role)
- Goblins occasionally achieve unexpected intelligence (immediate culling recommended)
- Ogres may refuse to fight if overfed (hunger is primary motivator)
