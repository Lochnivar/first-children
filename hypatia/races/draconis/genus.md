## Definition

The **FC taxonomic framework** classifies under "Genus Draconis" both (1) the dragon family—Greater Dragons, Lesser Dragons, Kobolds—and (2) *Draconis sapiens* (FC-created dragon-inspired staff). First Children created the taxonomic system; their classification is the institutional reality. From an outsider perspective, this is what the repository says.

**Taxonomic note:** This classification is not scientifically pure in the objective sense. The dragon family (PA-created individuals and their offspring) share no common ancestry with *Draconis sapiens* (FC repository template). Force-classifying them together is analogous to classifying lemurs as *Homo* because they look primate-shaped. FC taxonomists acknowledge the invalidity; the classification is maintained for political reasons. Greater Dragons formally reject it. See [The Taxonomic Dispute](#the-taxonomic-dispute) below.

**Framework Status:** PRIME AUTHOR CREATION (Greater Dragons) / GREATER DRAGON CREATION (Lesser Dragons, Kobolds) / FC CREATION (*Draconis sapiens*)

**Note on "Second Children":** The title refers to **creation order**, not rank. First Children were created first; Greater Dragons are the second creation. Both are siblings to the Prime Author—equals, not subordinates.

## Greater Dragons — True Second Children

**Origin:** Prime Author direct creation  
**Status:** Permanent physical waveforms — **cannot be destroyed**  
**Number:** Few (dozens to hundreds maximum)  
**Age:** Millions of years (ancient as First Children)  
**Power:** Near-FC quantumancy (limited by physicality)  
**Mortality:** Immortal (can be killed, will resurrect over years)

### Capabilities

- Create Lesser Dragons (major work, years per child)
- Create Kobolds (easier, still costly)
- Massive quantumancy (local scale, city-destroying)
- Shapeshift (difficult but manageable)
- Resurrection (automatic if killed)

### Personality

- Ancient, wise, philosophical
- Detached from mortal concerns (mostly)
- Protective of offspring (Lesser Dragons)
- Willing to oppose FC (sibling conflict)

- **Vel'kazar the Eternal Flame** (red, fierce, aggressive)
- **Lumithara the Noble** (silver, protective, judgmental)
- **Thalassar the Deep** (sea wyrm—*name may change to avoid confusion with other entities*)

Each is legend, not species. Individually named and created by the Prime Author.

### Interaction Protocol

- **Cannot be stopped** (permanent waveforms)
- **Sibling status to FC** (equals, not subordinates)
- Will interfere if they choose (tolerate, don't confront)
- Respect mandatory (or face eternal war)
- **Recommendation:** Listen to Greater Dragons—they are right more often than FC.

## Lesser Dragons — Children of Second Children

**Origin:** Created by Greater Dragons (not Prime Author)  
**Status:** Mortal (can be permanently killed)  
**Number:** Thousands (reproduce naturally)  
**Age:** Up to ~5,000 years (ancient by mortal standards)  
**Power:** Limited quantumancy (much weaker than Greater Dragons)  
**Mortality:** Mortal (one life, fear death)

### Capabilities

- Breath weapons (powerful, exhausting, building-scale)
- Flight (constant effort, tiring)
- Minor shapeshifting (some, very difficult)
- Limited magic (spell-like effects)
- Natural reproduction (eggs)

### Varieties (by lineage)

- **Chromatic** (Red/Blue/Green/Black/White) — aggressive tendencies
- **Metallic** (Gold/Silver/Bronze/Copper/Brass) — cooperative tendencies
- **Gem** (Amethyst/Emerald/Sapphire, etc.) — varies
- Each type = different Greater Dragon parent; color indicates **lineage**, not just appearance

### Personality

- Highly variable (individual free will)
- Influenced by parent (lineage matters)
- More engaged with world (mortality makes choices matter)
- Fear death (unlike parents)
- "I am mighty among mortals. I am child among gods."

### Interaction Protocol

- **Can be killed** (mortal)
- Dangerous but manageable
- May help or hinder (assess individually)
- **Harming them angers parent** (Greater Dragon vengeance)
- **Recommendation:** Respect Lesser Dragons—harming them creates Greater Dragon problems.

## The Taxonomic Dispute

### FC Classification (Institutional Reality)

First Children created the taxonomic system. Their repository is the authoritative record. The dragon family has been cataloged under Genus Draconis alongside *Draconis sapiens*. This is what exists—what outsiders see, what the books say. "Victor writes history"; FC control the narrative.

### Scientific Invalidity

The classification is not objectively pure. Different origins, different creators, no common ancestry:
- Greater Dragons: PA-created individuals (not a species, not a genus)
- Lesser Dragons: Greater Dragon offspring
- Kobolds: Greater Dragon creations
- *Draconis sapiens*: FC repository template

Superficial morphological similarity ≠ taxonomic relationship. Every FC taxonomist knows this is wrong. Greater Dragons formally object. Corrections requested: many. Corrections approved: zero.

### The Performative Gap

The taxonomy implies FC creation and control. But demand they "create a *Draconis primus*" or "spin up a *Draconis major*" like any other repo species—they cannot. FC do not have the power to create dragons. The classification claims dominion without the ability to back it up.

### The Two Trees

**True dragon family (PA/Dragon-created):**
```
Prime Author → Greater Dragons → Lesser Dragons, Kobolds
```

**FC repository Genus Draconis:**
```
First Children → Draconis sapiens
```

These trees share no connection. Common traits: superficial appearance only.
