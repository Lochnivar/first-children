# Ursine Marshals & Rangers

**Status:** Framework Element - Universal Race  
**Category:** Races / Species  
**Related:** [Genus Ursus](genus.md), [Legal Systems](../../cosmology/legal-systems.md), [Races README](../README.md)

## Overview

Ursine are bear-based anthropomorphic people from the FC genetic repository. Their traits—solitary, territorial, independent, strong—naturally suit law enforcement. No designated role; they became marshals and rangers because it *works*, not because they were designed for it. In a cosmos with no galactic government and no unified law, Ursine marshals are the peak of what's available: professional, impartial, effective.

## Physical Characteristics

- **Height:** 6–8 feet (varies by variant)
- **Weight:** 300–600 lbs
- **Variants:** Grizzly (largest, most territorial), Polar (swimmers, rare), Black (smaller, most adaptable)
- **Lifespan:** 200–400 years

## Behavioral Traits (Not Designed, Just Natural)

- **Solitary** — Work alone; minimal social needs; "I like you. From a distance."
- **Territorial** — "This is mine. For now. Then it's not."
- **Independent** — "You hire my expertise, not my obedience."
- **Protective** — Contracted duty is sacred; defend the vulnerable
- **Practical** — "Use what you have. Don't waste. Keep it simple."

## The Marshal Role

**Contracted law enforcement in alien jurisdictions.** Enforce local law, catch criminals, resolve disputes, maintain order. Authority granted by contract—arrest, investigation, use of force, judicial power (if contracted). Typical terms: 3 months–2 years, $3,000–12,000/month. Either side can terminate with notice.

**NOT:** Permanent residents, badge-wearing officials, followers of orders. "I enforce YOUR law. If your law is evil, I don't take contract."

## The Ranger Role

**Wilderness law enforcement.** Patrol vast territories; no specific laws—just "keep the peace." No formal authority—"I'm ranger because I say I'm ranger. Disagree? Prove it." Cache system: marked locations for supplies, message drops, meeting points. Completely independent.

## Why Settlements Hire Ursine

- **Impartial** — No local ties, no species bias
- **Cross-cultural** — Know many species' legal traditions; can create hybrid systems
- **Effective** — Crime drops 70–90% in contracted territories
- **Temporary** — Bridge to stability, not permanent solution

**Compared to alternatives:** Militia (untrained), vigilantes (mob rule), nothing (chaos). Ursine marshal: professional, impartial, effective. Expensive but worth it.

## Professional Code

- Enforce contracted law (won't enforce slavery, genocide, child harm)
- Be the law, not above it
- Impartial enforcement (rich and poor equal)
- Proportional force (match threat level)
- Complete contract (don't abandon mid-term)
- Leave it better ("I'm bridge. Not destination.")

## On Earth (Post-Trial)

- Small population (50–100 total); high demand; premium pay
- **Alien districts:** Enforce local alien law; bridge to human authorities
- **Frontier settlements:** Mixed populations; need neutral arbiter
- **Wilderness:** Rangers patrol between settlements; cache system

## Weaknesses

- **Social:** Crowds exhausting; small talk painful; politics incomprehensible
- **Bureaucracy:** Paperwork minimal; committees impossible
- **Permanence:** Can't settle; can't build community—"I'm bridge. Not foundation."
- **Methods:** Do job their way or walk; can seem stubborn

## Story Role

Background presence, occasional focus. Shows decentralized law reality, cross-species legal complexity, frontier justice. Human perspective: learning that decentralized IS the system; adapting expectations; appreciating what Earth had (planetary unity was rare).
