# Aubrei (Testudo aubreii)

**Status:** Framework Element - Universal Race  
**Category:** Races / Species  
**Related:** [Genus Testudo](genus.md), [Chelonari](chelonari.md), [Races README](../README.md), [../../FRAMEWORK.md](../../../FRAMEWORK.md)

## Overview

Aubrei are tortoise-based anthropomorphic people—naval-themed Testudo, cousins to the Chelonari. They share the same core genus traits: shell-backed, long-lived, patient, slower of movement and thought. The key difference: Aubrei are more aggressive. Their culture and identity center on the sea—ships, naval tradition, maritime life. Same family, different temperament.

## Universal Characteristics

### Physical Attributes
- Shell-backed, can retreat into shell (same as Chelonari)
- 300-500 year lifespans
- Slow-moving, methodical
- Naval adaptations vary by world (webbed digits, salt tolerance, etc.)

### Emergent Tendencies
- Patient, shell-backed, long-lived → same Testudo baseline
- More aggressive → tends toward naval combat, ship defense, maritime protection
- Naval culture → often sailors, shipwrights, coastal guardians—traits suit the sea, not a designated role

### Culture
- Maritime tradition, naval identity
- Ships and the sea as central to who they are
- Often coastal or island settlements

### Personality
- Patient but with an edge—willing to act when provoked
- Protective of their ships and crews
- "We carry shells. We also carry teeth."

## Variations Across Worlds

- Naval traditions and ship types vary
- Degree of aggression relative to Chelonari may differ
- Relationship to Chelonari (cousins, trade partners, or distant) varies by world
