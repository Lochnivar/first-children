# Dwarves

**Status:** Framework Element - Universal Race  
**Category:** Races / Species  
**Related:** [Races README](README.md), [../FRAMEWORK.md](../../FRAMEWORK.md)

## Overview

Dwarves are a sturdy, hardy intelligent race known for their craftsmanship, mining, and strong communities. Typically shorter than humans but broader and more physically resilient, dwarves often form close-knit societies focused on skill, tradition, and craftsmanship. Their mastery of stonework, metalwork, and engineering is legendary across many worlds.

## Universal Characteristics

### Physical Attributes
- **Lifespan:** Long-lived (typically 200-400 years, varies by world)
- **Size:** Shorter than humans but broader and more robust
- **Build:** Strong, stocky, physically resilient
- **Stamina:** Exceptional endurance and physical toughness

### Cognitive Abilities
- **Craftsmanship:** Natural talent for making things
- **Engineering:** Strong understanding of construction and mechanics
- **Memory:** Long memories, especially for craft knowledge
- **Focus:** Can concentrate on detailed work for extended periods

### Social Characteristics
- **Community:** Strong sense of community and loyalty
- **Tradition:** Value tradition, lineage, and craft mastery
- **Society:** Often form tight-knit communities (clans, holds, etc.)
- **Reproduction:** Moderate population growth

## Framework Role

### Master Craftspeople
- Represent skill, craftsmanship, and quality
- Often create the finest weapons, armor, and structures
- Value mastery and tradition in their work
- Create interesting dynamics with other races through trade

### Underground/Stone Dwellers
- Often associated with mountains, underground, or stone
- May have natural affinity for mining and construction
- Could have specialized abilities related to stone or metal
- Create distinct cultures based on their dwellings

## Variations Across Worlds

### Physical Variations
- Height and build vary by world
- Some worlds: very short and broad
- Other worlds: moderately shorter than humans
- Facial hair, coloration, and features vary

### Cultural Variations
- Mountain dwarves, hill dwarves, city dwarves, etc.
- Different cultural focuses (mining, trading, engineering, etc.)
- Clan structures and social organization vary
- Values and traditions differ by world

### Magical Variations
- Some worlds: resistance to magic or special magical properties
- Other worlds: no special magical connection
- May interact with quantum abilities differently
- Could have earth- or metal-related abilities

## Story Implications

### As Characters
- Provide skilled craftspeople or engineers
- Represent tradition and community values
- Can serve as mentors in craftsmanship or combat
- Create interesting conflicts between tradition and innovation

### As World Elements
- Can populate mountain holds or underground cities
- Create trade networks through their craftsmanship
- Provide technological or architectural elements
- Add depth through different cultural values

## Iolinus Status

Current Iolinus documentation does not mention dwarves. This framework element is available for other worlds or future Iolinus expansion.

## Notes

- Framework provides common baseline characteristics
- Individual worlds can vary significantly in implementation
- Dwarves can fill various story roles depending on world needs
- Craftsmanship focus creates interesting narrative opportunities
