# The Hypatia Framework

**The collected worlds shaped by the First Children after the withdrawal of the Prime Author.**

**In the First Speech:**  
*hypatia* — simultaneously "library" and "creator."

**Purpose:** This document establishes the cosmological framework for Hypatia, the over-universe (Library) in which individual stories (like Iolinus) take place. Similar to Brandon Sanderson's Cosmere, Hypatia allows for multiple worlds (Books), stories, and interconnected narratives while maintaining consistent universal rules and metaphysics.

**Status:** Framework Document - For Universe Expansion  
**Over-Universe Name:** Hypatia  
**Last Updated:** December 2024

---

## Core Cosmology

**The Deepest Truth: Everything is energy. Everything is quantum.**

This is the absolute bedrock of Hypatia's cosmology—beautiful in its simplicity and terror. There's no "other" realm. No separate spirit world. No elemental planes. No divine essence outside the rules. It's all one vast, indifferent quantum sea.

- The **Prime Author** created reality and a cohort of **First Children** — many siblings (the exact number is intentionally left as a gray area) — then withdrew forever. Not all First Children create worlds—some may never create worlds.
- Each First Child *can* author complete worlds from nothing (but not all do—some First Children may never create worlds).
- Worlds may be shaped by one sibling alone (most common), by several in collaboration (possible but not necessarily common—raises questions of ownership/control, "whose world is it?"—potential fuel for conflicts), or become contested when visions clash.
- Mortal awareness of the greater reality (hypatia, the First Children, the Prime Author) varies widely by world and by design. First Children ultimately control what happens on their creations—some worlds are open about the cosmology, some are closed, and some (like Iolinus) actively encourage belief that the creator is the only god.
- **Everything—mortal bodies, ghosts, spirits, rift spawns, magic, technology, souls, even the First Children themselves—are patterns of coherent energy at the quantum layer.** The First Children are the only ones who can swim in the quantum sea without drowning (most of the time).

## Conceptual Foundation: The Library Metaphor

**Note on the Metaphor:** The Library metaphor is **purely conceptual**—it's not a literal description of Hypatia's structure. However, it's the closest that First Children or any mortal mind is going to approach understanding the true nature of Hypatia. It serves as a useful approximation tool for comprehension and communication.

**Terminology Note:** In framework documentation, we refer to **"Prime Author"** (also called "Big 'G' God") as the actual being/concept. **"The Author"** is how First Children conceptualize and refer to this being in their cosmology. Different worlds and mortals may have completely different conceptions of the Prime Author, or none at all—for example, mortals on Iolinus think Malachor (Zeta) is the only god and have no concept of the Prime Author.

**The Author and the Library (Conceptual Framework):**
- **Prime Author** (Big 'G' God) = The actual being who created everything, then withdrew forever (framework truth)
- **"The Author"** = First Children's term/concept for the Prime Author (in-world terminology)
- **Hypatia** = Conceptually understood as "The Library" containing all existence (from First Speech: simultaneously "library" and "creator")—this is a mental model, not literal structure
- **Worlds** = Conceptually understood as "Books" within the library, each telling their own story—this is how First Children may think about them, not a literal description
- **First Children** = The Prime Author's first creations (siblings) who conceptually "inhabit the library" and can "author new books/worlds"—this metaphor helps them organize their understanding

This metaphor provides a useful conceptual framework: The Prime Author created the Library (Hypatia, conceptually) and the First Children (siblings), then withdrew. The First Children refer to the Prime Author as "The Author" in their cosmology. They may think of themselves as inhabiting a library and can conceptually "open, read, and even create new books" (worlds). This mental model helps them understand and work with worlds, even though the metaphor is an approximation, not literal truth. Mortals on different worlds may have their own conceptions of the Prime Author, or (like Iolinus) may be completely unaware and think their local First Child is the only god.

## Framework vs. World Details

**The Framework = General/Universal Rules**

This framework document and the `hypatia/` directory contain the **"all or general"** things that apply universally across Hypatia:
- Cosmological constants (Prime Author, First Children, quantum foundation)
- Universal mechanics (soul transfer, world creation)
- Fundamental principles (prohibitions, moral framework, story patterns)
- Universal elements (races, themes, character types)

**World Details = Expansions or Exceptions**

Individual world documentation (in `worlds/[world-name]/`) contains world-specific details that are either:

- **Expansions:** Adding more depth and specificity **within** the framework rules
  - Example: Framework says "mortals may have fragmented quantum abilities" → Iolinus expands this with specific five abilities (imposed by Malachor when he brought Quantumancy into Iolinus) (Quantumancers, Readers, Weavers, Echoes, Nullwraiths) and their detailed rules
  - Example: Framework establishes soul transfer mechanics → Iolinus expands with greyrose-specific implementation details
  - Example: Framework provides liberation operation patterns → Iolinus expands with specific characters, plots, and conflicts

- **Exceptions:** World-specific variations that work **within or modify** general rules
  - Example: Framework establishes universal principles → World may have unique cultural interpretations of those principles
  - Example: Framework provides patterns → World demonstrates a variation of those patterns
  - Example: Framework sets constraints → World finds creative ways to work within those constraints

**Key Principle:** World details must respect framework constants but can expand or provide exceptions for world-specific elements. The framework provides the foundation; worlds build upon it with their unique details.

## Core Cosmological Structure

### The Prime Author (The Watchmaker)

**Nature:**
- The true, deistic creator of the entire universe (formerly called "Big 'G' God")
- Created existence and a cohort of First Children (many siblings), then withdrew forever
- Not actively involved in world events—withdrawal is permanent and final (will not return)
- Established the fundamental **physical laws** of reality
- Does not establish moralistic rules—these are First Children constructs

**Relationship to Creation:**
- Creator of all First Children (the siblings)
- Established **physical laws** that govern reality (fundamental laws of existence)
- Does not establish moralistic rules—these are First Children constructs
- Provides the cosmological framework but doesn't intervene directly—has withdrawn permanently and will not return
- The universe operates according to laws set in motion at creation

**Role in Stories:**
- Background presence, not an active character—withdrew permanently after creation and will not return
- Represents the "true" creator versus the First Children who came after
- Source of the moral framework that First Children may violate
- Provides the cosmological "why" behind conflicts, but is absent from them

## The First Children (Little 'g' Gods)

### Nature and Origin

**What They Are:**
- The first children/creations of the Prime Author—many siblings
- Glorified waveforms (patterns of coherent energy) that are sentient and godlike, with powers beyond mortal comprehension—cannot be truly destroyed but can be reformed (effectively the same thing)
- Each can author complete worlds from nothing
- Each possesses **Quantumancy** - the full spectrum of quantum manipulation abilities

**World Creation:**
- Worlds may be shaped by one sibling alone
- Worlds may be shaped by several siblings in collaboration
- Worlds may become contested when sibling visions clash
- Each world can have different rules, physics, and magic systems

**Background Detail:**
- The "what they really are" is background world-building only
- For story purposes, they are simply gods with godly powers
- Their true nature/origin is out of scope for individual stories
- The framework allows for many First Children (siblings), not just those encountered in Iolinus

### Powers: Quantumancy

**Full Quantum Manipulation:**
- All quantum abilities combined in one being (effectively all abilities at once, not split):
  1. Quantumancers - Manipulate quantum reality
  2. Readers - Read quantum states (past/present/future)
  3. Weavers - Manipulate quantum threads (reality weaving)
  4. Echoes - Create quantum echoes (duplications/reflections)
  5. Nullwraiths - Nullify quantum effects (anti-magic)
- Only First Children possess full Quantumancy
- Mortals may manifest fragments of these abilities (persecuted on many worlds)

### Characteristics

**Nature: Glorified Waveforms:**
- First Children are glorified waveforms—patterns of coherent energy at the quantum layer (consistent with "Everything is energy. Everything is quantum.")
- Sentient and godlike despite being energy patterns
- Cannot be truly destroyed (as energy patterns), but can be reformed
- Reformation is effectively the same as destruction for practical purposes—can reshape personality, memories, powers, or fundamental structure

**Immortality and Vulnerability:**
- Cannot be killed through normal means (no traditional death)
- Have existed since the early universe as persistent coherent energy patterns
- Can be reformed beyond recognition or functionality (practical destruction)
- Liberation operations can "punish" creators by reforming them (effectively destroying them)
- Possess vast knowledge and experience (unless reformed)
- Can assume different identities/forms

**Personality Variation:**
- Range from wise and compassionate to immature and cruel
- Each First Child has their own motivations and goals
- Some respect the laws of the Prime Author, others violate them
- Can be allies, enemies, or indifferent to each other

**Relationships and Hierarchy:**
- All First Children are technically siblings (they all share the same creator, the Prime Author)
- There's an equality implication—as siblings, there's nominal equality between them
- But some are "more equal than others"—despite being siblings, there are power/hierarchy differences (likely related to age, experience, influence, world creation, or other factors)
- Social orders are still undetermined and will be developed as needed for stories

**Limitations:**
- Bound by **physical laws** (from Prime Author) and **moralistic rules** (First Children constructs)
- Cannot violate physical laws (these are immutable constants of existence)
- May violate moralistic rules, but face peer judgment and enforcement—First Children are responsible for violations and enforcement
- Must operate within the framework of reality
- Direct actions in mortal realms may be limited or detectable

## The Multiverse Structure

### Multiple Worlds and Realms

**World Creation:**
- First Children can create worlds/realms
- **Solo Creation:** One First Child creates alone
- **Collaborative Creation:** Multiple First Children can collaborate, but this raises questions of ownership/control ("whose world is it?")—potential fuel for conflicts and future stories
- Legality is determined by First Children peer judgment (not Prime Author laws)
- Creating against peer judgment is "against prohibition" (e.g., judged too immature to create)
- Worlds can be bound to their creators through life force connections (raises complications for collaborative worlds)
- Each world may have different rules, physics, and magic systems
- Collaborative worlds can become contested when visions clash or ownership is disputed

**Known Worlds:**

**Iolinus:**
- Created by Zeta/Malachor (a First Child) against explicit prohibition
- Two massive continents straddling the equator
- Features: Mountain ranges, lush vegetation, large desert
- Life force bound to creator - if creator dies, all life dies
- Currently the focus of a liberation conflict
- Magic system: Quantum-adjacent abilities (fragmented versions of Quantumancy)

**Other Lands on Iolinus:**
- Mentioned: "A few other lands on the far side" (not important to current conflict)
- Suggests Iolinus itself may have multiple regions/cultures

**Earth:**
- One of the worlds in Hypatia (not external)
- A "control" world with no magic and very little hands-on involvement by any First Children
- Source of "Earth souls" transported to other worlds (like Iolinus)
- There's a hint that Marcion of Sinope's theology might have some validity regarding Earth's status

**Potential Other Worlds:**
- Framework allows for unlimited world-building
- Each world could be a different story/series
- Worlds may or may not be aware of each other
- Connection points could be discovered or created

## Universal Forces and Rules

### Quantumancy as Universal Force

**The Quantum Foundation:**
- Quantum manipulation is a fundamental force across worlds
- **All "magic" in Hypatia ultimately boils down to quantum manipulation**
- **Magic systems are comprehension tools** - "Magic" as mortals understand it are schools/methods that let the mortal mind comprehend something way above their abilities
- First Children have full access (Quantumancy)
- Mortals may manifest fragments (the way these are split is world-specific—Iolinus has 5 because Malachor imposed that split when bringing Quantumancy into Iolinus)
- These abilities may be persecuted, accepted, or hidden depending on the world
- Different worlds may present quantum manipulation as different "magic" systems (spells, rituals, schools, etc.), but they're all ultimately quantum manipulation
- **Coherent Energy Patterns:** All existence (physical bodies, ghosts, spirits, etc.) are patterns of coherent energy at the quantum layer—ghosts/spirits are looser, non-corporeal patterns (see [hypatia/metaphysics/coherent-patterns.md](hypatia/metaphysics/coherent-patterns.md))

**Core Limitation - The Persistence Problem:**
- Quantumancy lets First Children collapse quantum probabilities into desired realities (rewriting existence)
- **Without an anchor, this requires constant, persistent enforcement**—let focus slip and reality snaps back (rubber band effect)
- This is why prolonged exposure drives mortals mad (the "haze")—they can't sustain the mental load without fracturing
- **Anchors** (stable artifacts/constructs) offload the persistence, allowing permanent changes without endless personal effort
- Different worlds/First Children may use different anchor types (artifacts, networks, lattices, or nothing for impermanent changes)

**World-Specific Variations:**
- Each world may have different manifestations of quantum abilities
- Some worlds may call them different names
- Persecution levels vary by world
- Rules and limitations may differ

### Soul Transfer Mechanisms

**Cross-World Soul Transportation:**
- First Children (or their agents) can transport souls between worlds
- Mechanism: Greyrose marks/interface allow soul transfer
- Limited in scope - cannot transport many souls without detection
- Used strategically for missions and liberation efforts

**Rules and Limitations:**
- Requires willing donors or prepared bodies
- The greyrose (or equivalent) makes the transfer possible
- Recipients may lose memories of the transfer process (operational security)
- Souls retain their original identity/personality
- Body adaptation required

**Purpose:**
- Provides "outside perspective" agents
- Breaks mortals free from creator control
- Enables liberation missions
- Allows for cross-world characters

### Life Force Binding (Iolinus-Specific Example)

**Note:** Life force binding is NOT a universal framework element. It is Iolinus-specific—a tool created by Malachor for his situation. Other worlds may have similar mechanisms, but none have been created yet.

**The Iolinus Mechanism:**
- Malachor bound his life force to Iolinus
- If Malachor dies, all life on Iolinus dies
- Creates invulnerability through hostage-taking
- Prevents direct intervention by other First Children
- Can only be broken by freeing the souls/life from the binding

**Ethical Implications (Iolinus Example):**
- Used by Malachor to avoid punishment
- Makes liberation difficult and dangerous
- Requires careful, hidden operations
- Creates moral complexity in conflicts

**Framework Note:** This demonstrates how a First Child might create a mechanism to protect themselves, but life force binding itself is not a universal framework rule. Other worlds may have different protective mechanisms or none at all.

## Physical Laws vs. Moralistic Rules

### Physical Laws (Prime Author)
- Fundamental laws of reality established by the Prime Author before withdrawal
- Quantum mechanics, reality structure, natural forces
- These are immutable constants of existence
- Not enforced—they simply exist as the framework of reality

### Moralistic Rules (First Children Constructs)
- The "Rules" are a First Children construct
- Peer judgments, prohibitions, and moral frameworks created by First Children themselves
- First Children are responsible for both violations and enforcement
- No Prime Author enforcement—the Prime Author withdrew forever and doesn't enforce moralistic rules

## World Creation and First Children Judgment

### What Determines "Legal" vs. "Prohibited" Creation

**First Children Peer Judgment:**
- The legality of world creation is purely a **First Children judgment call** (a moralistic rule, not a Prime Author law)
- There are no explicit prohibitions from the Prime Author about creating worlds
- First Children judge each other's readiness/maturity to create worlds
- **"Legal" creation** = Created with approval/consent of other First Children, or at least not judged as problematic
- **"Against prohibition"** = Creating despite negative judgment from other First Children (judged as too immature, not ready, etc.)

**Iolinus Example:**
- Other First Children judged that Zeta/Malachor (before he became Malachor) was too immature to create his own world
- He created Iolinus anyway, against their judgment—hence "against prohibition"
- The "prohibition" was a First Children moralistic judgment, not a Prime Author law

**Enforcement:**
- **First Children enforce their own moralistic rules** - They are responsible for violations and enforcement
- Other First Children may attempt to enforce peer judgments (limited by hostage situations)
- Peer judgments and enforcement have consequences, but are complicated by practical constraints
- The Prime Author does not enforce these moralistic rules—they are purely First Children constructs

### Consequences of Creating Against Peer Judgment

**For World Creators:**
- Shame and disapproval from other First Children who judged them unready
- Potential enforcement attempts by peers (complicated by hostage situations)
- Liberation movements to free the bound life
- Long-term conflicts with other First Children who disagree with the creation

**For Bound Worlds:**
- Life held hostage
- Cannot be freed without careful operation
- Must rely on hidden liberation efforts
- Risk of complete destruction if creator is threatened

## Rules for Expansion

### What Must Remain Consistent

**Cosmological Constants:**
- Prime Author is the true creator (created reality and First Children, then withdrew forever)
- First Children have Quantumancy (full quantum manipulation)
- Mortals may have fragmented quantum abilities
- Life force binding is NOT a universal constant—it is Iolinus-specific (Malachor's tool)
- Soul transfer requires mechanisms (greyrose or equivalent)
- Violations of prohibition have consequences

**Moral Framework:**
- No simple good vs. evil
- Liberation is complex and morally grey
- First Children vary in morality and methods
- Mortals are often caught in the middle
- Hidden operations are necessary for liberation

### What Can Vary

**World-Specific Elements:**
- Magic systems (quantum abilities may manifest differently)
- Technology levels
- Cultures and societies
- Religions and beliefs
- Geography and physics (within reason)
- Naming conventions

**Story-Specific Elements:**
- Conflict types (liberation, creation, discovery, etc.)
- Character focus (mortals, First Children, transferred souls)
- POV structure
- Tone (can vary, though often grounded)
- Specific plots and arcs

### Connection Points

**Ways Stories Might Connect:**
- Shared First Children (appearing in multiple stories)
- Cross-world characters (soul transfers, travelers)
- Shared organizations (if they span worlds)
- References to other worlds/stories
- Cameo appearances
- Shared themes without direct connection

**Levels of Connection:**
- **Easter Eggs:** Subtle references, names, hints
- **Cameos:** Characters appearing briefly
- **Shared Organizations:** Groups spanning worlds
- **Direct Connections:** Characters crossing between stories
- **Meta-Narrative:** Overarching plot across multiple stories

## World-Building Guidelines

### Creating New Worlds

**Essential Questions:**
1. Who created this world? (First Child or natural?)
2. Was creation legal or against prohibition?
3. Is life force bound to creator? (hostage situation?)
4. What is the status of quantum abilities? (accepted, persecuted, hidden?)
5. Are there transferred souls? (from where, why?)
6. What is the primary conflict or story focus?
7. How does this world relate to others (if at all)?

**Magic System Guidelines:**
- Must relate to quantum manipulation in some way
- Can manifest differently than Iolinus examples
- May be called different names
- Should have rules and limitations
- May be persecuted, accepted, or hidden

**Character Guidelines:**
- Mortals are the focus (usually)
- First Children can appear but are powerful
- Transferred souls provide outside perspective
- Character relationships are central
- Found family is a common theme

### Creating New First Children

**Characterization:**
- Each First Child has a distinct personality
- Motivated by their own goals (not just "good" or "evil")
- Possesses full Quantumancy
- Bound by natural laws (cannot violate fundamental prohibitions easily)
- Can create worlds (legally or illegally)
- May assume different identities/names

**Roles They Can Play:**
- Protagonist (rare, but possible)
- Antagonist (common)
- Supporting character
- Background presence
- Mentioned but not seen

**Relationships:**
- May have complex relationships with other First Children
- May care about mortals or see them as pawns
- May respect or violate the Prime Author's laws (established before withdrawal)
- May be allies, enemies, or indifferent
