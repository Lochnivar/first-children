## Terminology Consistency

### Q1: Prime Author vs. Big 'G' God vs. "The Author"
**RESOLVED**

**Answer:** 
- **"Big 'G' God" / "Prime Author"** = The actual concept/being that exists in reality (framework truth)
- **"The Author"** = How First Children conceptualize/refer to this being in their cosmology (in-world term used by First Children)
- **Different worlds/mortals** = May have completely different conceptions, or none at all
  - Example: Iolinus mortals don't know about Prime Author - they think Malachor (Zeta) is THE god
  - Different worlds could have different names/concepts for the Prime Author, or be unaware entirely

**Framework Documentation Standard:**
- Use **"Prime Author"** (or "Big 'G' God") in framework documentation to refer to the actual being/concept
- Note that "The Author" is the First Children's term for this concept
- World-specific documentation may use different terminology as appropriate to that world's understanding

**Files to update:**
- Clarify in framework docs that "The Author" is First Children terminology
- Note that mortals on different worlds may have no conception of Prime Author
- Update Iolinus docs to note that mortals there don't know about Prime Author

## World Creation Rules

### Q2: What makes world creation "legal" vs. "against prohibition"?
**RESOLVED**

**Answer:** The legality of world creation is purely a First Children judgment call. There are no explicit prohibitions from the Prime Author. First Children judge each other's readiness/maturity to create worlds. 

**Examples:**
- Iolinus: Other First Children judged that Zeta/Malachor (before he became Malachor) was too immature to create his own world. He created it anyway, against their judgment—hence "against prohibition."
- Legal creation would mean either: created with approval/consent of other First Children, or at least not judged as problematic/immature by peers.

**Framework Update:**
- "Prohibition" = First Children's judgment that a sibling is not ready/mature enough
- "Against prohibition" = Creating despite negative judgment from other First Children
- "Legal" = Created with approval, or at least without negative judgment from peers
- No explicit Prime Author laws prohibiting creation—it's a peer judgment system

**Files to update:**
- Clarify that prohibitions are First Children judgments, not Prime Author laws
- Update world creation documentation
- Update Iolinus example to reflect that other First Children judged Zeta/Malachor as too immature

### Q3: Relationship between life force binding and prohibition violations
**RESOLVED**

**Answer:** Life force binding is a separate mechanism from prohibition violations. It's not inherently a violation itself, but a tactic that can be used with any world creation (legal or illegal). The prohibition is about peer judgment of readiness/maturity—life force binding is a separate hostage mechanism.

**Updated Understanding:**
- Life force binding = Separate mechanism that can be used with any creation
- Not inherently a violation—it's a tactic/choice
- Commonly used with prohibited creations as a way to avoid enforcement (hostage mechanism)
- But could theoretically be used with legal creations too (though may be judged problematic by peers)

**Files Updated:** Framework documentation clarified that life force binding is separate from prohibition/judgment system.

**Framework Understanding:**

- **Not Necessarily Common:** Collaboration is possible but not necessarily common—depends on individual First Children and circumstances, not personality stereotypes
- **Ownership Question:** This raises the question of "whose world is it?" - a potential source of conflict
- **Story Potential:** Collaborative creations can become contested later when visions clash or ownership is disputed
- **Can Become Contested:** A world that starts as collaborative can become contested when:
  - Visions clash during creation
  - Ownership disputes arise later
  - Collaborators have falling out
  - External factors intervene
- **No Personality Stereotypes:** Any First Child could potentially collaborate depending on situation—no predetermined traits

**Examples of Potential Conflicts:**

- What happens if collaborators disagree on direction?
- Can one collaborator bind life force to a collaborative world?

## Quantumancy and Magic

### Q12: Quantumancy fragments - always five?
**RESOLVED**

**Answer:** The split into 5 quantum-adjacent powers is Iolinus-specific, imposed by Malachor when he brought Quantumancy into Iolinus. First Children don't have the split—they effectively have all 5 abilities at once (full Quantumancy). There might be more than 5 fragments across all worlds, but not on Iolinus.

**Framework Understanding:**
- **First Children:** Have full Quantumancy—all abilities combined, not split (effectively have all 5 at once)
- **The 5 split is Iolinus-specific:** The split into 5 quantum-adjacent powers was imposed by Malachor when he brought Quantumancy into Iolinus
- **There might be more than 5 fragments total:** Across all worlds, there could be more than 5 fragment types, but not on Iolinus
- **Other worlds:** May have different numbers or types of fragments, different splits, or different implementations entirely
- **Iolinus implementation:** The 5 abilities (Quantumancers, Readers, Weavers, Echoes, Nullwraiths) are Malachor's specific implementation for Iolinus

**Core Mechanic:**
- Quantumancy requires constant enforcement without anchors (rubber band effect)
- Anchors offload persistence, allowing permanent changes
- This explains why mortals go mad (the "haze")—can't sustain mental load
- Different worlds may use different anchor systems

## Structural and Organizational

### Q16 & Q17: Single Point of Truth with World-Based Exceptions/Expansions
**RESOLVED**

**Unified Answer:** The framework establishes a single point of truth with clear hierarchy and organization. World-specific content provides exceptions or expansions within the framework.

**Document Hierarchy:**

1. **FRAMEWORK.md (Root) = PRIMARY SOURCE OF TRUTH**
   - Comprehensive overview of all universal framework elements
   - Single authoritative reference for framework rules and cosmology
   - Contains all essential framework information in one place
   - **Purpose:** Be the definitive reference—anyone reading this should understand the complete framework
   - **Status:** Complete, comprehensive, authoritative

2. **oververse/ = DETAILED DOCUMENTATION (Expansions/References)**
   - Detailed, expanded explanations of framework elements
   - Cross-referenced navigation and organization
   - Provides depth and context beyond FRAMEWORK.md
   - **Purpose:** Serve as detailed reference, cross-reference hub, and organized categorization
   - **Relationship to FRAMEWORK.md:** Expands upon but never contradicts FRAMEWORK.md
   - **Status:** Supplementary detailed documentation

3. **oververse/worlds/[world]/ = WORLD-SPECIFIC EXCEPTIONS/EXPANSIONS**
   - World-specific details that expand or provide exceptions to framework rules
   - Must conform to framework but can add world-specific depth
   - Documents how the world demonstrates, expands, or provides exceptions to framework rules
   - **Purpose:** Show how individual worlds work within the framework
   - **Status:** Subordinate to framework, provides world-specific examples

**Key Principles:**

- **Single Source of Truth:** FRAMEWORK.md is the authoritative reference
- **No Contradictions:** oververse/ files expand but never contradict FRAMEWORK.md
- **Clear Hierarchy:** Framework → Detailed Docs → World Docs
- **World Exceptions/Expansions:** Worlds can expand or provide exceptions (documented) within framework rules

**Information Flow:**

- **Framework Rules:** Defined in FRAMEWORK.md (primary), detailed in oververse/ (supplementary)
- **World Details:** Expansions/exceptions documented in oververse/worlds/[world]/ (subordinate)
- **Updates:** Changes to framework rules go to FRAMEWORK.md first, then reflected in oververse/ files

**Organization Standards:**

- All framework universal elements: FRAMEWORK.md (primary) + oververse/ (detailed)
- All world-specific elements: oververse/worlds/[world]/
- Cross-references: oververse/ files link to FRAMEWORK.md as primary source
- No duplicate authoritative statements: Only FRAMEWORK.md states rules authoritatively

**Resolution:**
- FRAMEWORK.md = Comprehensive primary source of truth (not just overview)
- oververse/ = Detailed supplementary documentation (expansions, cross-references, organization)
- Worlds = Subordinate exceptions/expansions within framework rules
- Clear separation: Framework (universal) vs. World (specific exceptions/expansions)

**Implications:**

- The metaphor helps First Children organize their understanding and communicate about worlds
- Mortals may never even reach this level of conceptualization (depends on world design)

- The metaphor provides a useful framework for readers and creators, even if it's not literally accurate

**Usage:**

### Q20: Can First Children die or be destroyed?
**RESOLVED**

**Answer:** First Children are glorified waveforms that are sentient and godlike. They can't be truly destroyed, but they can be reformed, which is basically the same thing.

**Framework Understanding:**
- **Nature:** First Children are waveforms—patterns of coherent energy at the quantum layer (consistent with "Everything is energy. Everything is quantum.")
- **Sentient and godlike:** They possess consciousness and godlike powers despite being energy patterns
- **Cannot be truly destroyed:** As energy patterns, they can't be eliminated entirely
- **Can be reformed:** They can be reshaped/reformed, which is effectively the same as destruction for practical purposes
- **Reformation = destruction:** From a practical standpoint, reforming a First Child is equivalent to destroying them

**Implications:**
- Liberation operations can "punish" creators by reforming them (effectively destroying them)
- Life force binding doesn't make First Children invulnerable to reformation
- Reformation could reshape their personality, memories, powers, or fundamental structure
- This aligns with the deepest truth of Hypatia—everything is energy patterns that can be reshaped
- First Children's apparent "immortality" is actually persistent coherent energy patterns that can be reformed

**Terminology:**
- "Immortal" = Persistent coherent energy patterns that can't be truly eliminated
- "Reformed" = Reshaped/reconfigured—effectively the same as destruction
- "Destroyed" = Reformed beyond recognition or functionality (practical destruction)

## The Deepest Truth of Hypatia (Locked as Canon)

**Everything is energy. Everything is quantum.**

This is the absolute bedrock of Hypatia's cosmology—beautiful in its simplicity and terror.

- **Mortal bodies?** Coherent energy patterns bound tightly by forces, pretending to be solid for a lifetime.
- **Ghosts and spirits?** The same patterns, just looser—echoes that haven't fully dissipated.
- **Rift spawns?** Raw quantum collapses, some stable enough to persist, most unraveling like half-remembered dreams.
- **Magic, technology, souls, gods themselves?** All manipulations or expressions of the same underlying quantum layer.
- **Even the First Children**—for all their godlike spark—are just more complex, self-sustaining waveforms dancing on the same substrate.

**There's no "other" realm. No separate spirit world. No elemental planes. No divine essence outside the rules.**

It's all one vast, indifferent quantum sea, and the First Children are the only ones who can swim in it without drowning (most of the time). Malachor didn't add something foreign when he bound his life force or split the disciplines—he just rearranged the waves into patterns that pleased him.

**The Moral Question:**
When everything—lives, worlds, souls—is just energy waiting to be shaped or scattered… what does it mean for a childlike being to have the only hands big enough to play with it all?

**No duality. No escape. No higher court.**

Just patterns, coherence, and the fleeting preferences of those who can make the patterns hold.

The cosmology is complete, consistent, and haunting.
